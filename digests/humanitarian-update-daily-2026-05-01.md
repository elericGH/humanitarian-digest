# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Friday, 1 May 2026
*Produced Friday, 1 May 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The Australian Council for International Development (ACFID) has published an analysis of the US-Iran conflict and its humanitarian implications, noting that Australian NGO members are tracking the situation closely as ceasefire arrangements hold but remain fragile. ACFID's Consolidated Middle East Humanitarian Crisis Appeal remains active, with member organisations continuing to mobilise resources for affected populations across Iran, Lebanon, and the broader region. ([ACFID – Middle East Consolidated Appeal](https://acfid.asn.au/consolidated-appeal-middle-east-humanitarian-crisis/); [ACFID – US-Iran Conflict Analysis](https://acfid.asn.au/acfid-analysis-the-us-iran-conflict/))

Australia is a key contributor to Pacific humanitarian response capacity. The World Food Programme's Pacific Islands Country Brief for April 2026 notes that successive cyclones during the month disrupted infrastructure across the Pacific, with WFP deploying emergency telecommunications and coordination support. In Tonga, WFP completed a disability-focused emergency survey, and WFP is assisting Fiji to create a Social Protection Helpline scheduled to launch by end of April 2026. Australia's ongoing support for Pacific preparedness and logistics training was highlighted, with WFP and SPC launching the Pacific's first accredited humanitarian supply chain and logistics training programme. ([WFP Pacific Islands Country Brief, April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/fiji/wfp-pacific-islands-country-brief-april-2026))

Australian agencies are engaged in the humanitarian response to Tropical Cyclone Maila in Papua New Guinea, where a coordinated national, provincial, and international response has been mobilised under the leadership of the PNG Government through the NDC-NEOC. The cyclone caused significant damage across Milne Bay Province, East New Britain Province, the Autonomous Region of Bougainville, and parts of West New Britain, affecting low-lying island and coastal communities reliant on subsistence livelihoods. ([ReliefWeb – PNG Cyclone Maila Sitrep #05, 27 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/papua-new-guinea-severe-tropical-cyclone-maila-disaster-management-team-situation-report-no-05-27-april-2026))

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Papua New Guinea continues to grapple with overlapping humanitarian challenges. The PNG Humanitarian Snapshot as of 30 April 2026 reflects the compound impact of Cyclone Maila alongside ongoing conflict-related displacement in Enga Province. An April 2026 conflict and context analysis for Enga Province has been released, underscoring the complexity of the humanitarian situation in the Highlands region where tribal conflict continues to drive displacement and constrain access to services. ([ReliefWeb – PNG Humanitarian Snapshot, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/papua-new-guinea-humanitarian-snapshot-30-april-2026); [ReliefWeb – Enga Province Conflict Analysis, April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/enga-province-papua-new-guinea-conflict-and-context-analysis-april-2026))

The Federated States of Micronesia is responding to Typhoon Sinlaku, with the Pacific Humanitarian Team (PHT) publishing Situation Report No. 01 on 28 April 2026. Coordination efforts are being led by the State Government, with support from the National Government's Emergency Management Division and an integrated OCHA/UNDAC team. PHT partners are on the ground in Chuuk, supporting assessments, provision of non-food items, relief distribution, and sector-specific services. A robust response is required to avoid a humanitarian catastrophe. ([ReliefWeb – FSM Typhoon Sinlaku PHT Sitrep #01, 28 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/micronesia-federated-states/federated-states-micronesia-typhoon-sinlaku-pacific-humanitarian-team-pht-situation-report-no-01-28-april-2026))

The Philippines Red Cross activated an Early Action Protocol (EAP) for typhoon preparedness, with an activation report published on 30 April 2026 covering outcomes and expenditures since the November 2025 activation. Tonga is managing an ongoing dengue fever outbreak, with Situation Report #12 issued on 30 April 2026 noting 45 cumulative confirmed cases, with DENV-2 as the active serotype. No dengue-related deaths have been recorded, and no cases required hospital admission during the latest reporting period; the majority of cases are among males aged 11–14 years on Tongatapu Island. ([ReliefWeb – Philippines Typhoon EAP Activation, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/philippines-typhoon-early-action-protocol-activation-operation-no-mdrph550-30-april-2026); [ReliefWeb – Tonga Dengue Sitrep #12, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/tonga/tonga-dengue-fever-outbreak-situation-report-12-30th-april-2026-ento))

The East Asia Forum published analysis on 30 April 2026 examining ASEAN's need to advance RCEP implementation, and on 26 April explored the case for a renewed Australia–Japan compact for comprehensive security — both pieces relevant to the evolving regional governance landscape that underpins humanitarian coordination and regional stability. ([East Asia Forum – ASEAN and RCEP, 30 April 2026](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/04/30/asean-must-give-rcep-a-chance/); [East Asia Forum – Australia-Japan Security Compact, 26 April 2026](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/04/26/australia-and-japan-need-a-new-compact-for-comprehensive-security/))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

The World Health Organization released its sixth Global Situation Report on the Middle East Escalation of Conflict on 30 April 2026, noting that ceasefire arrangements involving Iran and Lebanon continue to hold but the situation remains fragile. Health systems are being impacted by shortages of medicines and supplies, with closure of essential and maternal health services having severe consequences on women and children's health. WHO is monitoring displacement patterns and sustaining coordination with partners on WASH, infection prevention, and mental health for displaced populations. ([ReliefWeb – WHO Middle East Escalation Sitrep #6, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/world-health-organization-middle-east-escalation-conflict-global-situation-report-6-30-april-2026))

ICRC President Mirjana Spoljaric visited Tehran on 30 April, warning that any return to war would be catastrophic for millions of civilians. "Six weeks of hostilities have pulled Iran and the wider Middle East into patterns of warfare that have proved devastating for civilians and the infrastructure they rely on to survive," President Spoljaric said, adding that the ceasefire has widened the window to scale up humanitarian assistance. The ICRC has brought additional staff into Iran and delivered relief items and medical supplies, with more on the way. ([ReliefWeb – ICRC Iran President Statement, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/iran-icrc-president-says-return-war-would-be-ruinous-civilians))

As World Immunization Week concludes, MSF issued an urgent call on 30 April 2026 for donors and diplomatic actors to increase flexible funding and pressure warring parties in Sudan to end administrative obstruction and unblock humanitarian delivery routes. MSF teams treated at least 13,000 measles cases in Darfur alone in the first four months of 2026, responding to six outbreaks across South, North, and West Darfur. "Drastic funding cuts and deliberate bureaucratic obstruction by the warring parties are blocking lifesaving vaccines and medicines from reaching people in need timely," said MSF vaccination advisor Miriam Alía. ([ReliefWeb – MSF Sudan Vaccination Crisis, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/three-years-outbreaks-everywhere-msf-urges-end-barriers-boost-sudans-vaccination-programs))

South Sudan faces a credible risk of famine in four counties across Upper Nile and Jonglei States under a worst-case scenario, according to the latest IPC analysis highlighted in Humanitarian Update #18 of 30 April 2026. A surge in fighting since late 2025 has displaced large numbers of people; on 26 April clashes between the SSPDF and SPLA-IO displaced civilians in Longochuk County, Upper Nile State. Nationwide, 31.7% of assessed health facilities are non-functional, affecting an estimated 9.8 million people. Japan contributed USD 713,333 to WFP Cameroon to provide food assistance to 22,600 vulnerable people in the Far North and Adamawa regions, where 2.9 million people face acute food insecurity between June and August 2026. ([ReliefWeb – South Sudan Humanitarian Update #18, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-humanitarian-update-18-30-april-2026); [ReliefWeb – Japan/WFP Cameroon, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/cameroon/japan-boosts-life-saving-food-assistance-funding-crisis-threatens-600000-people-cameroon))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

MSF marked the approaching tenth anniversary of UN Security Council Resolution 2286 on the protection of medical care in conflict, issuing a statement on 30 April 2026 calling on states to act on their commitments. In 2025, WHO's Surveillance System for Attacks on Health Care reported 1,348 attacks on medical facilities resulting in 1,981 deaths. "What was once considered exceptional has now become commonplace," said MSF International President Dr Javid Abdelmoneim, citing airstrikes on hospitals in Syria and Yemen, shellings in Ukraine and Gaza, a drone strike on a hospital in Myanmar, and attacks on ambulances in Cameroon, Haiti, and Lebanon over the past decade. ([ReliefWeb – MSF Resolution 2286 Statement, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/msf-attacks-healthcare-medical-staff-deserve-more-empty-words))

Colombia's Humanitarian Country Team issued a statement on 30 April 2026 regarding the escalation of violence and grave humanitarian deterioration in the southwest of the country, calling for urgent action to protect civilians caught in renewed fighting. The statement reflects ongoing concern among international humanitarian actors about access constraints and the safety of displaced populations in the region. ([ReliefWeb – Colombia HCT Statement, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/colombia/comunicado-de-prensa-pronunciamiento-del-equipo-humanitario-pais-sobre-la-escalada-de-violencia-y-el-grave-deterioro-humanitario-en-el-suroccidente-de-colombia-30042026))

The International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies (IFRC) published a final report on 29 April 2026 for its Multi-Country Response in Ethiopia, Sudan, and Djibouti, closing out the Ethiopia crisis operation (MDRTIGRAY). The report documents the humanitarian consequences of the Tigray conflict and the response carried out over the course of the operation. The Quarterly Mixed Migration Update for Asia and the Pacific (Q1 2026) was also released, tracking migration trends and protection concerns across Afghanistan and the broader region. ([ReliefWeb – Ethiopia/Sudan IFRC Final Report, 29 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ethiopia/multi-country-response-ethiopia-sudan-and-djibouti-ethiopias-crisis-and-its-humanitarian-consequences-ndeg-mdrtigray-final-report-29-april-2026); [ReliefWeb – Mixed Migration Asia-Pacific Q1 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/quarterly-mixed-migration-update-asia-and-pacific-quarter-1-2026))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

A magnitude 5.7 green-level earthquake struck Papua New Guinea on 30 April 2026 at 13:27 UTC, at a depth of 10 km, with approximately 80,000 people in the MMI ≥ III shaking zone. GDACS assessed this as a green (low humanitarian impact) alert. The earthquake compounds existing humanitarian pressures from Tropical Cyclone Maila in the same country. ([GDACS – PNG Earthquake, 30 April 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1538279))

GDACS is tracking orange-level drought alerts in two regions: one covering Angola, Democratic Republic of Congo, Republic of Congo, Gabon, and Zambia (ongoing since early February 2026), and another covering Argentina and Uruguay (ongoing since late January 2026). A green flood alert is active in Bangladesh and another in Peru; multiple green-level wildfire notifications are active across various Australian states. No active cyclones, tsunamis, or volcanic eruptions are currently rated orange or red by GDACS. ([GDACS – Angola/DRC Drought](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018344); [GDACS – Argentina/Uruguay Drought](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018327))
