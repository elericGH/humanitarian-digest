## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa
The US-Iran conflict escalated sharply on 9 July. Al Jazeera's [live coverage](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/9/iran-war-live-one-killed-as-us-bombs-bushehr-chabahar-bandar-abbas-jask) reports US air strikes on the Iranian port cities of Bushehr, Chabahar, Bandar Abbas and Jask, with at least one person reported killed, after Tehran struck targets in several GCC states and Jordan. The strikes came on the day Ayatollah Khamenei was buried in Mashhad, and Iran has vowed "continued resistance." Ports of this kind are import lifelines for food, fuel and medicine, so sustained strikes carry significant humanitarian risk for civilians across southern Iran and the wider Gulf.

Later on 9 July, further [explosions were reported across southern Iran](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/9/explosions-heard-in-iran-as-us-military-denies-involvement), including near Iranshahr airport; a US defence official denied US involvement, and one analyst told Al Jazeera it is "certainly possible" Israel was behind the blasts. A same-day [explainer](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/9/how-strait-of-hormuz-dispute-led-to-latest-us-iran-cycle-of-fighting) traces the escalation to clashing interpretations of vague Strait of Hormuz language in the US-Iran memorandum of understanding — with obvious flow-on risks to global fuel supply chains, including the Pacific.

In Gaza, [Israeli attacks killed six people in 24 hours despite the ceasefire](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/9/israeli-attacks-on-gaza-kill-10-people-in-24-hours-despite-ceasefire) (Al Jazeera, 9 July). Meanwhile the Norwegian Refugee Council and Shelter Cluster warn that [Israel's shelter-material restrictions are forcing nearly one million people — about 170,000 households — to endure summer heat in tents](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/gaza-israels-shelter-restrictions-force-nearly-million-people-endure-summer-heat-tents-nrc), with another 5,000 households sleeping outdoors and 52,000 in overcrowded shelters (featured on ReliefWeb's latest headlines this morning; exact publication time could not be independently verified).

In Syria, authorities say they have [arrested the cell behind the recent "terrorist bombings" in Damascus](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/9/syria-says-cell-behind-terrorist-bombings-in-damascus-arrested) (Al Jazeera, 9 July), which had injured at least 18 people near the Tourism Ministry earlier in the week.

On food security, the new FAO-WFP [Hunger Hotspots outlook (June–November 2026)](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/hunger-hotspots-fao-wfp-early-warnings-acute-food-insecurity-june-november-2026-outlook) warns that worsening hunger puts 13 hotspots at significant risk, with Sudan, South Sudan, Yemen and Palestine remaining of highest concern and northeast Nigeria and Somalia joining the top tier as acute hunger risks deteriorate towards catastrophic levels (featured on ReliefWeb's latest headlines this morning; exact publication time could not be independently verified).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World
Two weeks after the 7.2 and 7.5 magnitude earthquakes that struck Venezuela on 24 June, Al Jazeera published a 9 July [long-form report on families searching for the missing](https://www.aljazeera.com/features/longform/2026/7/9/a-quest-for-closure-in-search-of-the-missing-after-venezuelas-earthquakes), documenting the scale of unresolved loss as search-and-rescue transitions to recovery.

Ukraine's Humanitarian Coordinator Matthias Schmale [strongly condemned another wave of Russian attacks on energy infrastructure](https://reliefweb.int/updates), which killed and injured civilians and left thousands exposed as critical services fail (statement at the top of ReliefWeb's updates feed this morning).

[Mexico will seek US criminal complaints over the ICE-related deaths of its citizens](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/9/mexico-to-seek-us-criminal-complaints-over-ice-related-deaths-of-citizens) (Al Jazeera, 9 July), escalating a consular-protection dispute with Washington.

FAO and WFP have launched a [joint $202 million appeal to protect 8.8 million people from extreme weather events](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/bracing-el-nino-fao-and-wfp-launch-joint-appeal-protect-88-million-people-extreme-weather-events) ahead of El Niño-driven climate shocks threatening food security and livelihoods across the most vulnerable regions (featured on ReliefWeb's latest headlines this morning; exact publication time could not be independently verified).
