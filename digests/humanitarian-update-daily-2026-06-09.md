## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The 8 June 2026 magnitude 7.8 earthquake off Mindanao in the southern Philippines triggered a Pacific-wide tsunami advisory that included Australia's eastern and northern coastlines. The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS) issued the alert for nine provinces and for international coastlines including Japan, Indonesia and Australia; the warning was lifted by mid-afternoon local time after waves of approximately 1 metre were recorded in Sarangani province. The event is recorded as ongoing and represents a background pressure on agricultural communities and emergency food-security services. ([GDACS, 9 June 2026](https://gdacs.org/default.aspx))

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

A magnitude 7.8 earthquake struck 32 km offshore west of Maasim, Sarangani province, Mindanao, at 7:37 am local time on 8 June 2026. The highest reported shaking intensity was VIII (very destructive) in General Santos City. As of the first flash update, 19 people had been confirmed dead, 12 remained missing, and 134 were injured — figures considered provisional pending full assessment. Homes, government buildings, hospitals and schools have been damaged or destroyed, and road and bridge damage is constraining access to some communities. General Santos International Airport was temporarily closed and has since resumed at limited capacity. More than 138 aftershocks, including multiple events above magnitude 5.5, were recorded through 8 June. ([OCHA Flash Update No. 1, 8 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/philippines-78m-sarangani-earthquake-flash-update-no-1-8-june-2026))

The earthquake struck on the first day of the new Philippine school term, affecting more than 3.2 million learners and forcing the suspension of classes in over 6,200 public and private schools pending safety assessments. Save the Children, which has operated in the Philippines since 1981, confirmed its teams were assessing the situation and mobilising a response focused on education, health and child protection. President Ferdinand Marcos Jr. suspended classes in affected areas and called for immediate coastal evacuations. ([ReliefWeb / Save the Children, 8 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/philippines-earthquake-first-day-back-school-affects-3-million-students-rescue-efforts-underway))

Severe monsoon flooding in Kerala, India continues to intensify, with the India Meteorological Department issuing red alerts for three districts — Idukki, Kottayam and Pathanamthitta. Schools have been shut and seven deaths have been recorded since the flood event began on 28 May. The event remained active as of 7–8 June with heavy rainfall continuing. ([GDACS Flood Event 1103917, 8 June 2026](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103917&episodeid=2&eventtype=FL))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

OCHA's Director of Crisis Response, Edem Wosornu, briefed the UN Security Council on Afghanistan on 8 June 2026, painting a deteriorating picture: 4.7 million people are now at IPC Phase 4 (severe food insecurity), a 50 per cent increase compared to the same period last year, and 3.7 million children are facing acute malnutrition. A brief ceasefire in April followed fighting that had erupted along the Afghanistan-Pakistan border in late February, displacing over 100,000 people and cutting off 17,000 families in Nuristan Province from all aid for nearly two months. The continued border closure is disrupting humanitarian supply chains, particularly for food security and nutrition programmes. ([ReliefWeb / OCHA Security Council Briefing, 8 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/ms-edem-wosornu-director-crisis-response-division-ocha-behalf-mr-tom-fletcher-under-secretary-general-humanitarian-affairs-and-emergency-relief-coordinator-briefing-security-council-afghanistan-8-june-2026))

The Ebola outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo is expanding rapidly. A third-edition analysis published on ReliefWeb in June 2026 reports confirmed cases rising from 282 to 515 between 30 May and 6 June, now spanning 25 affected health zones with Ituri province as the epicentre. Weak contact tracing, active insecurity, population movements, community resistance and misinformation are all constraining the response. The outbreak is also restricting humanitarian access and cross-border movement, compounding an already severe crisis in eastern DRC. ([ReliefWeb / OCHA, June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/ebola-outbreak-expanding-health-crisis-geographically-differentiated-impacts-third-edition-june-2026))

The Lebanon Health Cluster and World Health Organization published Emergency Situation Report Issue 14 on 8 June 2026, documenting the ongoing health sector emergency in Lebanon. The report reflects continuing strain on health services and infrastructure in a country managing multiple concurrent crises. ([ReliefWeb / WHO & Health Cluster, 8 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-health-sector-emergency-situation-report-issue-14-june-08-2026))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

OCHA Acting Assistant Secretary-General Indrika Ratwatte briefed the UN Security Council on Ukraine on 8 June 2026, reporting that at least 30 civilians had been killed and more than 200 injured across Ukraine between 6 and 8 June alone. The scale and intensity of attacks on major urban centres is increasing, with strikes hitting Dnipro, Kyiv and other cities. Communities are still dealing with the consequences of the 2 June attack on Kyiv — described as one of the most significant attacks on the capital since February 2022 — which struck hospitals, schools, humanitarian personnel and a UN office. Front-line communities continue to face civilian casualties, infrastructure damage, and displacement. ([ReliefWeb / OCHA, 8 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/mr-indrika-ratwatte-acting-asg-humanitarian-affairs-behalf-mr-tom-fletcher-usg-humanitarian-affairs-and-emergency-relief-coordinator-briefing-security-council-humanitarian-situation-ukraine-8-june-2026))

A new EU-funded international consortium has launched coordinated humanitarian operations to support civilians within 50 km of Ukraine's frontlines. The four-organisation consortium — Johanniter International Assistance (Germany), EMERGENCY (Italy), UK-Med (United Kingdom) and Eleos Ukraine — will deliver primary healthcare, mental health and psychosocial support, energy resilience for health facilities, mobile medical units in Kharkiv and Zaporizhzhia, and targeted evacuations. The initiative prioritises older people, persons with disabilities and those excluded from other assistance. ([ReliefWeb / Johanniter, 8 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/ukraine-eu-funded-partnership-boosts-health-protection-and-evacuations-near-frontlines))

The Global Humanitarian Policy Forum 2026 has released its attendees' briefing packet on "Navigating Global Risks: Priorities for the Future of Humanitarian Action," providing a strategic framework for discussion on current humanitarian challenges and reform priorities. ([ReliefWeb, June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/global-humanitarian-policy-forum-2026-attendees-briefing-packet-navigating-global-risks-priorities-future-humanitarian-action))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

The dominant sudden-onset event of the past 24 hours is the **M7.8 earthquake and tsunami off Mindanao, Philippines** (7 June 23:37 UTC / 8 June 07:37 local). GDACS rates this ORANGE (medium-to-significant humanitarian impact), with approximately 3.6 million people exposed in the MMI ≥ VII zone. A tsunami alert was issued across the Pacific and lifted by mid-afternoon. 19 confirmed dead, 12 missing, 134 injured as of the first flash update; figures will rise as assessments continue. ([GDACS EQ 1544720 / OCHA Flash Update, 8 June 2026](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1544720&episodeid=1710268&eventtype=EQ))

**Tropical Storm CRISTINA-26** (GDACS ORANGE) formed in the eastern Pacific on 8 June, tracking toward Honduras, El Salvador, Nicaragua and Guatemala with maximum sustained winds of 93 km/h and forecast rainfall of up to 738 mm. El Salvador has high humanitarian vulnerability; GDACS rates the humanitarian impact potential at medium. ([GDACS TC 1001275, 8 June 2026](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001275&episodeid=3&eventtype=TC))

**Tropical Storm BORIS-26** (GDACS GREEN) formed on 8 June off Mexico's southern Pacific coast, approximately 85 miles south-east of Acapulco, with winds of 74 km/h. The storm brings flood and mudslide risk to Guerrero state on Mexico's Pacific coast. ([GDACS TC 1001274, 8 June 2026](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001274&episodeid=7&eventtype=TC))

A **M6.1 earthquake struck off northwestern Cuba** on 8 June at 18:00 UTC, described in regional media as the strongest to hit the area in nearly 150 years. GDACS rates it GREEN (low direct impact), but the event was felt across Cuba, Florida, Mexico and Belize, and prompted precautionary building closures in Florida. ([GDACS EQ 1544981, 8 June 2026](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1544981&episodeid=1710576&eventtype=EQ))

**Kerala, India flooding** continues with three-district red alert and seven deaths recorded — see Tier 1 above for detail. ([GDACS FL 1103917](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103917&episodeid=2&eventtype=FL))
