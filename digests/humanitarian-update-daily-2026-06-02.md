## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Lebanon continued to generate significant humanitarian attention on 1 June 2026 with the formal launch of an International Humanitarian Alliance in support of the Lebanese people, bringing together international partners to coordinate the crisis response ([ReliefWeb, 1 Jun](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/comprometidos-con-el-libano-lanzamiento-de-la-alianza-humanitaria-internacional-en-apoyo-al-pueblo-del-libano)). A Lebanon Health Sector Emergency Situation Report (Issue #13) was also published, documenting the ongoing health system strain ([ReliefWeb, 1 Jun](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-health-sector-emergency-situation-report-issue-13-june-01-2026)).

The Democratic Republic of Congo faces a dual burden: a joint WHO/Government declaration confirmed an active Ebola (Bundibugyo strain) epidemic ([ReliefWeb, 1 Jun](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/declaration-conjointe-du-gouvernement-de-la-republique-democratique-du-congo-et-de-loms-concernant-lepidemie-de-maladie-virus-ebola-causee-par-la-souche-bundibugyo)), while a funding status overview for West and Central Africa shows the 2026 regional humanitarian response remains significantly underfunded ([ReliefWeb, 1 Jun](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/west-and-central-africa-2026-regional-funding-status-general-overview-1-june-2026)). South Sudan faces elevated Ebola virus disease importation risk, with health cluster partners mapping potential corridors as of May 2026 ([ReliefWeb, 1 Jun](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-evd-potential-importation-corridors-and-health-cluster-partners-presence-may-2026)).

Iran's humanitarian situation has deteriorated severely following the US-Iran conflict, with millions of refugees and displaced persons now in crisis, according to a situation report published 1 June 2026 ([ReliefWeb, 1 Jun](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/iran-war-has-devastated-life-millions-refugees-and-displaced)). Chad's latest humanitarian bulletin covering March–April 2026 also documents ongoing needs in the Sahel region ([ReliefWeb, 1 Jun](https://reliefweb.int/report/chad/tchad-bulletin-humanitaire-mars-avril-2026)), and Mozambique's daily cholera bulletin for 31 May 2026 indicates continued disease spread ([ReliefWeb, 1 Jun](https://reliefweb.int/report/mozambique/boletim-diario-da-colera-data-31052026)).
