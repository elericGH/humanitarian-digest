## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano remains on alert**
Mayon Volcano in Albay Province, southern Luzon, has maintained Alert status following a significant eruption on 2 May 2026 that produced pyroclastic density currents and rockfalls. The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS) issued daily 24-hour observation bulletins throughout 25–29 May, indicating continued volcanic unrest. Exclusion zones remain in force around the volcano's flanks, displacing farming communities dependent on the fertile slopes. No mass casualty update was published in the text-based ReliefWeb bulletins for this period, but the sustained alert level signals that the hazard has not abated.

Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/mayon-volcano-summary-24hr-observation-29-may-2026-1200-am-entl

**Indonesia — Ongoing floods across Java and Greater Jakarta**
Indonesia continued to record flood events across Java and Sumatra throughout the week, with disaster authorities confirming fresh flooding in Greater Jakarta on 29 May 2026. The cumulative toll for the ongoing Java flood sequence — tracked by GDACS since 2 March — stands at 19 deaths and 2,311 displaced persons. The ASEAN AHA Centre's Week 21 situation report (18–24 May) documented 50 discrete disaster events across Southeast Asia, with major episodes in East Java (Pasuruan Regency and City), Sabah (Malaysia), Mindanao and Zamboanga (Philippines), and multiple provinces in Thailand. The breadth of concurrent events illustrates the pressure on national disaster management systems across the sub-region heading into the June–August monsoon peak.

Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/indonesia/asean-weekly-disaster-update-week-21-18-24-may-2026

**Japan — Typhoon JANGMI-26**
Typhoon JANGMI tracked into Japan between 27 and 31 May with maximum sustained winds of 139 km/h (Category 1 equivalent), exposing approximately 1.8 million people to Category 1 or higher winds and generating storm surge up to 0.8 m. Japan's high disaster preparedness capacity meant GDACS assigned a Green alert overall, but the system produced intense rainfall across affected prefectures. This is the Pacific typhoon season's most significant system so far in the 2026 season.

Source: https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001272&episodeid=19&eventtype=TC

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

### Gaza

The week of Eid Al Adha (26 May) brought no respite for Palestinian civilians. OHCHR documented at least 26 people killed by Israeli attacks during the Eid holiday period, including six women and seven children, as military operations pushed deeper into the strip. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/attacks-gaza-over-eid-kill-26-israeli-forces-push-deeper-strip]

OCHA's weekly situation report describes the territory as "heavily fragmented," with movement between Gaza and the West Bank blocked entirely, and movement within each area further restricted by military divisions and closed zones. Humanitarian partners reported it "extremely difficult and unsafe" to deliver staff and critical supplies, as new waves of displacement began before previous ones had ended. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/opt-humanitarian-situation-report-25-may-2026]

UNICEF Communication Specialist Salim Oweis, briefing in Geneva, described children as "trapped in an endless cycle of suffering," with parents unable to obtain basic wound care for injured children due to the total collapse of health and supply systems. UNICEF is calling for immediate and unconditional humanitarian access to avert preventable deaths. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/i-try-clean-her-wounds-my-daughter-screams-agony-dire-conditions-trap-children-gaza-endless-cycle-suffering]

### Lebanon

A new escalation of hostilities saw Israeli airstrikes across Nabatiyeh, South Lebanon, and Choueifat (Mount Lebanon) on and around 28 May. As of that date, 127,714 people (33,731 families) were sheltering in 631 collective shelters — a figure on a clear upward trend. Since hostilities began on 2 March 2026, the Lebanese Ministry of Public Health reports 3,324 killed and 10,027 injured, with 182 documented attacks on healthcare facilities resulting in 125 deaths and 311 injuries among health workers and patients. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-flash-update-30-escalation-hostilities-lebanon-28-may-2026]

### Sudan

Sudan's conflict — now past its second anniversary — continues to produce one of the world's worst displacement and hunger emergencies. WFP's May country brief reports more than 740,000 refugees have fled into neighbouring countries, with a further 185,000 internally relocated to safer areas. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/wfp-sudan-country-brief-may-2026]

Over Eid, Life for Relief and Development published a field assessment describing Sudan's humanitarian collapse in stark terms: millions displaced, hunger rising sharply in camps and conflict zones, and families unable to observe basic Eid traditions amid catastrophic deprivation. Humanitarian organisations report growing operational challenges as access restrictions tighten. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/life-relief-and-development-intensifies-its-efforts-combat-famine-sudan-escalating-hunger-and-poverty-place-humanitarian-organizations-growing-challenges]

A joint atrocity alert (No. 484) covering Sudan and the Occupied Palestinian Territory highlighted ongoing violations of protected zones and international humanitarian law in both contexts, calling for accountability mechanisms. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/atrocity-alert-no-484-sudan-israel-and-occupied-palestinian-territory-and-protected-zones]

### Democratic Republic of Congo — Ebola Outbreak

The Bundibugyo Ebola Virus Disease outbreak has escalated into a multi-country concern. WHO's Situation Report #2 classifies DRC, Uganda, South Sudan, Burundi, and Rwanda as Priority 1 countries for response, with Angola, CAR, Ethiopia, Kenya, Tanzania, and Zambia designated Priority 2 for readiness. IOM has expanded population mobility mapping to support cross-border surveillance. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/multi-country-bundibugyo-ebola-virus-disease-outbreak-situation-report-2]

UNICEF's first international shipment of emergency Ebola response supplies arrived in Ituri province during the week — a logistics milestone in what is one of DRC's most conflict-affected and access-constrained provinces. The shipment includes personal protective equipment, infection prevention materials, and community protection kits for frontline health workers and affected communities. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/unicefs-first-international-shipment-emergency-ebola-response-supplies-arrives-ituri]

### South Sudan

WFP scaled up emergency food and nutrition assistance in Akobo East, where conditions have reached IPC Phase 5 (Catastrophe/Famine). Hundreds of thousands are facing the highest level of food insecurity, with operations severely hampered by insecurity, infrastructure damage, and the onset of the rainy season. WFP issued an urgent call for unimpeded humanitarian access and sustained donor funding to prevent mass mortality. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/wfp-scales-emergency-response-akobo-catastrophic-hunger-and-malnutrition-deepen]

### Yemen

WFP's food security projection through September 2026 warns that price fluctuations and above-average temperatures are negatively impacting household incomes and food access across Yemen, with large parts of the country projected to remain in Crisis (IPC Phase 3) or worse. Import dependencies, currency depreciation, and conflict-linked disruptions compound the outlook with no clear improvement on the horizon. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/yemen-key-message-update-may-september-2026-price-fluctuations-and-above-average-temperatures-negatively-impact-incomes]

UNFPA published a human interest report on women receiving treatment for obstetric fistula — a condition almost entirely preventable with skilled birth attendance — highlighting how Yemen's collapsed health system continues to cause severe, long-term harm to women's health and dignity. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/i-am-reclaiming-my-health-my-dignity-and-my-life-women-healing-obstetric-fistula-yemen-enar]

### Syria

UNFPA's situation report for February–April 2026 shows scale-up of sexual and reproductive health (SRH) and gender-based violence (GBV) services, with mobile and static teams reaching over 33,000 people with SRH services and nearly 37,000 with GBV services in April alone. UNFPA is requesting US$7.5 million for a six-month response covering escalating needs in Aleppo and north-east Syria, including from population influxes arriving from Lebanon. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/syrian-arab-republic/unfpa-syria-situation-report-15-february-30-april-2026]

### Somalia

A CERF-funded programme in Somalia is providing integrated maternal health services to reach women in remote communities where fewer than one in three mothers give birth with a trained health worker present. The programme combines skilled birth attendance, nutrition support, and GBV referral pathways as a one-stop model designed for contexts where health system infrastructure is sparse. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/one-door-many-solutions-saving-lives-somalia-cerf]

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Global — World Hunger Day: Hunger doubles in a decade**
Marking World Hunger Day on 28 May, WFP and Action Against Hunger published a global assessment confirming that hunger has doubled over the past decade. Sudan and Gaza are cited among the most acute current crises globally. The report frames the trend as driven by the convergence of climate shocks, conflict, and economic instability, and calls on donor governments to significantly increase humanitarian food funding. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/world/world-hunger-day-urgent-global-action-needed-hunger-doubles-in-a-decade]

**China — Jiangsu Province floods**
Flooding in Jiangsu Province peaked in late May with GDACS recording 18 deaths and more than 23,000 displaced. Copernicus EMS GloFAS confirmed the flood extent as of 27 May 2026. [Source: https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103883&episodeid=7&eventtype=FL]

**Türkiye — Samsun and Kars Province floods**
Flooding struck multiple Turkish provinces, with the Havza district of Samsun Province hit by overflowing rivers in mid-May and fresh flooding in Kars Province confirmed by GloFAS on 27 May 2026. Three deaths and 98 displaced were recorded. [Source: https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103845&episodeid=10&eventtype=FL]

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Typhoon JANGMI-26 — Japan (27–31 May 2026)**
Category 1-equivalent typhoon with 139 km/h sustained winds and 1.8 million people in the wind exposure zone. Maximum storm surge 0.8 m; heavy rainfall forecast 595–981 mm. GDACS Green alert (high national coping capacity). [Source: https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001272&episodeid=19&eventtype=TC]

**M6.9 Earthquake — Northern Chile (25 May 2026)**
Deep-focus earthquake (101 km depth) near Atacama/Antofagasta region. Approximately 160,000 people exposed to MMI VI shaking; depth significantly reduced surface impact and structural damage risk. GDACS Green alert. [Source: https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1542497&episodeid=1707803&eventtype=EQ]

**Jiangsu Province Floods — China (ongoing, peaking late May)**
18 deaths; 23,112 displaced. GloFAS-confirmed event extent as of 27 May 2026. GDACS Green alert. [Source: https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103883&episodeid=7&eventtype=FL]

**Java & Greater Jakarta Floods — Indonesia (ongoing since March, latest episode 29 May)**
Cumulative 19 deaths, 2,311 displaced. Greater Jakarta flooding confirmed 29 May; rainy season onset compounding risk for remaining months. [Source: https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103786&episodeid=56&eventtype=FL]

**Mayon Volcano — Philippines (ongoing since 2 May)**
Sustained Alert status with daily PHIVOLCS bulletins throughout 25–29 May. Pyroclastic hazard remains active; exclusion zones in force. [Source: https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/mayon-volcano-summary-24hr-observation-29-may-2026-1200-am-entl]

**Samsun/Kars Floods — Türkiye (ongoing, latest episode 27 May)**
3 deaths, 98 displaced; rivers overflowing in Havza and Kars districts; fresh GloFAS confirmation 27 May. GDACS Green alert. [Source: https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103845&episodeid=10&eventtype=FL]
