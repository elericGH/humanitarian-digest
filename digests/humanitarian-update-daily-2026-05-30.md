# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Saturday, 30 May 2026
*Produced Saturday, 30 May 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia's aid sector faces mounting pressure as global crises intensify. ACFID welcomed the 2025–26 Federal Budget's commitment to protect the aid program — including increased investment in the Indo-Pacific and support for Australian NGOs — but warned that Australia's ODA as a percentage of GNI remains at a record low, and stronger investment is needed in an increasingly unstable world ([ACFID, 12 May 2026](https://acfid.asn.au/federal-budget-holds-for-aid/)).

ACFID analysis highlights that the humanitarian fallout from the US-Iran conflict is already being felt across the Middle East, Africa, and Asia, with flow-on impacts in the Indo-Pacific. ACFID called for urgent Australian diplomatic and aid leadership to meet rapidly rising needs ([ACFID, 23 April 2026](https://acfid.asn.au/development-in-reverse-far-reaching-and-long-lasting-impact-of-us-iran-conflict-already-being-felt/)).

GDACS notes an ongoing low-level drought in Australia (Green alert) updated 29 May 2026, consistent with dry conditions affecting parts of eastern and southern Australia ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018457)).

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Bangladesh is experiencing its most significant measles outbreak since 2005. Between 15 March and 14 April 2026, authorities reported 19,161 suspected cases and 2,897 laboratory-confirmed cases across 91% of districts, with 166 suspected deaths — exposing serious governance and health system failures with implications for regional disease surveillance ([East Asia Forum, 27 May 2026](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/05/27/bangladeshs-measles-outbreak-lays-bare-governance-failures/)).

OCHA's weekly Asia-Pacific humanitarian snapshot (19–25 May 2026) reported flash floods and landslides in Chongqing, China, displacing 697 people with 1,444 more relocated and a Level-IV emergency response activated. In Afghanistan, flash floods struck Laghman Province on 22 May killing five people including a Danish Refugee Council staff member, with further flooding in Baghlan, Takhar, and Badakhshan provinces ([OCHA/ReliefWeb, 28 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/asia-and-pacific-weekly-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-19-25-may-2026)).

Myanmar continues to face compounding crises. An ACAPS thematic report released 29 May documents widespread psychological trauma from displacement, economic deterioration, and climate hazards, with approximately 3.7 million people internally displaced as of March 2026 and critical gaps in mental health and psychosocial support service delivery ([ACAPS/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/myanmar-drivers-influencing-mental-health-and-psychosocial-needs-thematic-report-29-may-2026)).

Vietnam faces a severe oil price shock compounded by structural supply constraints as conflict in the Middle East reverberates through global oil markets. Domestic crude output is projected to fall significantly over 2026–30, with flow-on humanitarian risks for Vietnam's poorest communities ([East Asia Forum, 29 May 2026](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/05/29/the-oil-shock-tests-vietnams-overstretched-economy/)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Gaza/Occupied Palestinian Territory:** OHCHR condemned a surge in Israeli airstrikes over the Eid al-Adha period beginning 26 May, killing at least 26 Palestinians — including six women and seven children — in a single day. Since a ceasefire was announced, Israeli forces have killed 922 Palestinians, bringing the total death toll since 7 October 2023 to nearly 73,000. OHCHR stated attacks continue to violate international humanitarian law ([OHCHR/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/attacks-gaza-over-eid-kill-26-israeli-forces-push-deeper-strip)).

UNICEF described children in Gaza as trapped in an endless cycle of suffering, with parents going weeks without adequate food, water, or medicine, and children enduring untreated wounds. UNICEF called for immediate, unimpeded humanitarian access and an end to the blockade ([UNICEF/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/i-try-clean-her-wounds-my-daughter-screams-agony-dire-conditions-trap-children-gaza-endless-cycle-suffering)).

**Lebanon:** OCHA Humanitarian Coordinator Imran Riza issued an urgent Eid al-Adha statement condemning escalating hostilities in Lebanon, with displacement orders affecting communities in Tyre, Nabatieh, and areas south of the Zahrani River. Riza called for the protection of civilians and immediate humanitarian access ([OCHA/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/statement-humanitarian-coordinator-lebanon-imran-riza-civilians-bear-brunt-escalating-hostilities-and-displacement-orders-lebanon-enar)).

**Sudan:** WFP's May 2026 country brief confirms Sudan remains the world's largest humanitarian crisis. Donors pledged over USD 1.5 billion at the Berlin Conference, but a UNHCR truck carrying emergency shelter for 1,300 families was destroyed in a drone attack in North Darfur in April. The Global Centre for the Responsibility to Protect's weekly Atrocity Alert flagged Sudan — alongside Gaza — as a critical atrocity risk, with warring parties increasingly deploying drones in populated areas ([WFP/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/wfp-sudan-country-brief-may-2026); [GCR2P/ReliefWeb, 27 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/atrocity-alert-no-484-sudan-israel-and-occupied-palestinian-territory-and-protection-civilians)).

**DRC/Uganda — Ebola:** An Ebola Bundibugyo virus disease outbreak spanning DRC and Uganda has triggered a multi-agency international response. The World Bank Group announced swift financing support and Direct Relief has pivoted existing medical supply partnerships to Ebola-specific support. The EU's DG ECHO issued its daily situation map tracking geographic spread ([Direct Relief/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/ebola-outbreak-2026-questions-and-answers-direct-reliefs-dr-jeffrey-samuel); [World Bank/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/response-ebola-outbreak-democratic-republic-congo-drc-and-uganda)).

**South Sudan:** WFP has scaled up its emergency food and nutrition response in Akobo East, one of only four counties in South Sudan at risk of famine (IPC Phase 5 — Catastrophe). WFP Country Director Mutinta Chimuka warned that insecurity, infrastructure damage, and the onset of the rainy season are severely hampering operations ([WFP/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/wfp-scales-emergency-response-akobo-catastrophic-hunger-and-malnutrition-deepen)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Haiti:** Armed attacks in the Cator/Nan Chapelle locality of Petite Rivière de Bayonnais on 26 May displaced approximately 1,103 persons (266 households), all seeking refuge in Gonaïves. Haiti currently has 1.47 million people internally displaced due to persistent gang violence ([IOM/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/haiti/haiti-emergency-tracking-tool-94-displacement-following-armed-attacks-gonaives-artibonite-department-26-28-may-2026)).

OCHA's weekly Latin America and Caribbean update notes that 2.6 million people in Ecuador face IPC Phases 3–4 food insecurity between April and June 2026, and 1,200 people were displaced or confined by escalating violence in Antioquia, Colombia. The approaching 2026 Atlantic hurricane season poses additional risk across the Caribbean ([OCHA/ReliefWeb, 29 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/haiti/latin-america-caribbean-weekly-situation-update-29-may-2026)).

On World Hunger Day, Islamic Relief warned that global hunger levels have doubled over the past decade, with the 2030 goal of ending hunger rapidly becoming unachievable without renewed urgent government commitment ([Islamic Relief/ReliefWeb, 28 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/world-hunger-day-urgent-global-action-needed-hunger-doubles-decade)).

**Yemen:** UNFPA documented the stories of women in Yemen being treated for obstetric fistula — a debilitating childbirth complication — in Aden and Al Hudaydah Governorate. One woman, Safiy (28), lived five years with an untreated condition after severe birth complications, unable to access care due to Yemen's destroyed health system ([UNFPA/ReliefWeb, 25 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/i-am-reclaiming-my-health-my-dignity-and-my-life-women-healing-obstetric-fistula-yemen-enar)).

**Pakistan:** FEWS NET's special report flags Pakistan's acute vulnerability to intersecting shocks: weather extremes tied to the Indus River system, economic instability, rapid population growth (over 241 million), and cross-border instability, with rural areas facing persistent food insecurity ([FEWS NET/ReliefWeb, 26 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/pakistan/pakistan-special-report-may-26-2026)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Tropical Cyclone JANGMI-26 — Japan (Green Alert):** Tropical Storm JANGMI-26 was active in the Northwest Pacific from 27–29 May 2026 with maximum wind speeds of 148 km/h, affecting Japan. An estimated 1.668 million people were exposed to Category 1+ winds. Japan's vulnerability rating is Low and the alert level is Green ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=TC&eventid=1001272)).

**Drought — Orange Alert — Central/Southern Africa:** GDACS maintains an Orange-level drought alert covering Angola, DRC, Republic of Congo, Gabon, and Zambia, indicating moderate humanitarian impact potential ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018344)).

**Drought — Orange Alert — Madagascar:** An active Orange-level drought alert for Madagascar reflects conditions that pose moderate risk for already food-insecure communities in the south ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018431)).

**Flood — Vietnam (Green Alert):** GDACS issued a Green flood alert for Vietnam on 27 May 2026, consistent with pre-monsoon seasonal patterns ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103896)).

**Earthquake — Western Indian-Antarctic Ridge (Green Alert):** Two magnitude 6.0 earthquakes struck the remote Western Indian-Antarctic Ridge on 27 May 2026. No populated areas affected; no humanitarian impact expected ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1542829)).
