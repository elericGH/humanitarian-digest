## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The [Emergency Action Alliance (EAA)](https://emergencyaction.org.au) currently has two active fundraising appeals running simultaneously for Australian member organisations: the **Middle East Humanitarian Emergency** and the **Myanmar Earthquake** response. EAA members are on the ground providing food, shelter, water, and emergency supplies to affected populations in both crises, with families continuing to flee escalating violence in the Middle East and earthquake-affected communities in Myanmar still requiring urgent support.

Australia's drought emergency continues to worsen, with [GDACS tracking an Australian drought alert now in its 13th consecutive week](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018457&episodeid=3). Additionally, GDACS recorded two separate wildfire notifications in Australia on 20–21 May 2026, both receiving green-level alerts.

A [Pacific fuel price crisis documented by ANU researchers (published via ReliefWeb)](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/when-price-fuel-becomes-price-childhood-pacific-families-crisis) carries significant implications for Australian-funded Pacific humanitarian operations. Fuel prices across the Pacific have surged 20–70% in key nations; the Marshall Islands has declared a 90-day state of economic emergency, Tuvalu declared a state of emergency amid daily power blackouts, and fuel supplies washed away during Tropical Cyclone Maila in Solomon Islands and Papua New Guinea have not been replenished, directly impeding Australian-supported disaster response logistics. The Asian Development Bank projects this inflationary pressure will persist across the Pacific through 2026.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Solomon Islands — Tropical Cyclone Maila (ongoing):** The Pacific Humanitarian Team's [Situation Report No. 4 (19 May, published 22 May)](https://reliefweb.int/report/solomon-islands/tropical-cyclone-maila-pacific-humanitarian-team-situation-report-no4-19-may-2026) confirms 161 affected communities assessed in Western Province alone, with 31,000 people requiring shelter support across Western and Choiseul Provinces. Eighty-four schools have been disrupted, affecting more than 11,000 children, while 41 health facilities are only partially functional. Approximately 180 expectant mothers urgently need access to safe delivery services, and widespread WASH system damage is increasing the risk of waterborne disease outbreaks. A further hazard is emerging as flooding erodes soil and exposes unexploded ordnance (UXO) across affected areas. CERF has allocated USD 2.5 million for urgent life-saving assistance, and the response is now shifting from immediate relief toward stabilisation and early recovery — though significant gaps remain.

**Federated States of Micronesia — Typhoon Sinlaku:** The [Pacific Humanitarian Team Situation Report No. 3 (15 May 2026)](https://reliefweb.int/report/micronesia-federated-states/federated-states-micronesia-typhoon-sinlaku-pacific-humanitarian-team-pht-situation-report-no-03-15-may-2026) shows the second round of food and water distribution is now underway across all of Chuuk State following the April typhoon. The Government of FSM has released its Initial Damage Assessment and the Joint Damage Assessment (JDA) with the United States has concluded — a prerequisite for unlocking Compact of Free Association (COFA) recovery funds. Cold-chain disruptions continue to threaten medical supply integrity, with 65% or more of assessed health facilities rated as high priority for intervention. Presidential and State Declarations of Emergency remain in place until October 2026.

**Myanmar — Compounded crisis:** [OCHA's Asia-Pacific Weekly Snapshot (5–18 May 2026, published 21 May)](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-weekly-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-5-18-may-2026) documents severe ongoing violence: in Magway Region, armed clashes since early May have displaced approximately 5,000 people from 15 villages, reportedly killing 8 civilians and destroying around 300 homes; airstrikes and drone attacks in Mandalay's Ngazun Township on 7 May killed 3 civilians and injured 4; and military operations in Sagaing Region on 8–9 May killed 9 civilians including a child. In Rakhine State, clashes displaced roughly 3,000 people in Kyaukpyu Township. As of December 2025, Myanmar has 3.7 million internally displaced persons, and the 2026 UN Humanitarian Response Plan projects 16 million people requiring assistance this year.

**Rohingya Crisis — Bangladesh:** The UN and partners are appealing for [USD 710.5 million under the 2026 Joint Response Plan for Rohingya Humanitarian Crisis](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/un-and-partners-appeal-usd-7105-million-meet-critical-needs-rohingya-refugees-and-bangladeshi-host-communities-enbn) (published 20 May). The plan covers 1.2 million Rohingya refugees in Cox's Bazar and Bhasan Char, including 150,000 newly arrived since early 2024. This year's appeal is 26% lower than the previous year — a deliberately hyper-prioritised, scaled-down request driven by funding fatigue — yet needs continue rising as Myanmar's conflict drives further displacement.

**DPRK — Spring Drought:** OCHA's Asia-Pacific snapshot also flags a severe spring drought underway in North Korea, with early April rainfall recorded at just 14.3 mm. Persistent high-pressure systems and high temperatures are threatening food security. The Government has mobilised a multi-sectoral response including water transport for irrigation, though access constraints severely limit independent verification of conditions on the ground.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**DRC — Ebola Bundibugyo Outbreak (MAJOR DEVELOPING STORY):** The 17th Ebola outbreak in DRC's history is rapidly escalating. The outbreak was declared on 15 May 2026 in Ituri Province (Bundibugyo strain), and WHO declared a **Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC)** on 17 May. As of 19 May, figures stood at 536 suspected cases, 105 probable, 34 confirmed, and **134 deaths**. There is no approved vaccine or specific treatment for the Bundibugyo strain. The outbreak is spreading to Bunia, Goma, Butembo, and Katwa, with one confirmed case already detected in Uganda. An [IOM Multi-Country Situation Report No. 1 (published 23 May)](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/iom-multi-country-ebola-bundibugyo-outbreak-situation-report-no-1-15-21-may-2026) identifies seven WHO Priority 1 countries (DRC, Uganda, South Sudan, Burundi, Rwanda) and eight Priority 2 countries, with 3.59 million IDPs in eastern DRC — 98% displaced by conflict — posing extreme cross-border transmission risks.

A [High-Level Ministerial Meeting in Kampala (22–23 May, communiqué published 25 May by WHO)](https://reliefweb.int/report/uganda/high-level-ministerial-meeting-cross-border-coordination-ebola-disease-outbreak-caused-bundibugyo-virus) brought health ministers of DRC, Uganda, and South Sudan together with WHO and Africa CDC. The outbreak has been simultaneously classified as a PHEIC and a **Public Health Emergency of Continental Security**. Ministers committed to harmonised border screening, cross-border surveillance, expanded lab capacity, and community engagement — though resourcing gaps remain substantial given the outbreak context.

**Occupied Palestinian Territory — West Bank:** [OCHA's humanitarian situation report (25 May 2026)](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/opt-humanitarian-situation-report-25-may-2026) documents an average of 6 settler attacks per day against Palestinian communities in 2026 — over 870 attacks across 220+ communities since 1 January — including arson attacks on a mosque, homes, farmland, and vehicles. In the reporting week of 12–18 May, Israeli forces and settlers killed 5 Palestinians including 1 child and injured approximately 60 others including 6 children. Israeli authorities have also directed rapid implementation of demolition orders against Khan al Ahmar, placing approximately 4,000 Bedouin people across 18 communities at immediate risk of forced displacement.

**Yemen — Food Security:** The [IPC Acute Food Insecurity Snapshot for March–December 2026 (published 22 May)](https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/yemen-government-controlled-areas-ipc-acute-food-insecurity-snapshot-march-december-2026-enar) finds 1 in 2 people in government-controlled areas facing high levels of acute food insecurity. Five million people are in IPC Phase 3 (Crisis) or worse, with 1.4 million in Phase 4 (Emergency). The situation is projected to deteriorate further during the June–September lean season, when 5.4 million are expected to face Phase 3+ conditions including 1.6 million in Phase 4. Underlying drivers include macroeconomic collapse, localised violence, and total dependence on external humanitarian aid.

**Sudan — Refugee Flows into Chad:** UNHCR published [two coordination documents on 25 May](https://reliefweb.int/report/chad/unhcr-emergency-situation-chad-update-arrivals-sudan-25-may-2026) tracking continued refugee arrivals from Sudan into eastern Chad. The Sudan crisis remains one of the world's largest displacement emergencies, with cross-border flows continuing unabated. UNHCR's East Chad Coordination Office is managing an active relocalisation programme for new arrivals.

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Haiti — MSF Hospital Evacuation, Cité Soleil:** Intense fighting between rival armed groups erupted in Cité Soleil and Croix des Bouquets on 10 May 2026. [MSF's hospital in Cité Soleil was overwhelmed](https://reliefweb.int/report/haiti/haiti-msf-forced-evacuate-its-hospital-cite-soleil-and-temporarily-suspend-activities-there) with 800+ civilians seeking refuge and 40+ gunshot wound patients treated within 12 hours, before MSF was forced to evacuate and suspend operations entirely after a security guard was hit by a stray bullet inside the compound. No hospital now remains open in the Cité Soleil area, leaving tens of thousands of displaced people with no medical access.

**UN Secretary-General Report — Protection of Civilians (published 25 May):** The annual [UN SG report on Protection of Civilians in Armed Conflict (S/2026/390)](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/protection-civilians-armed-conflict-report-secretary-general-s2026390) finds that in 2025 "the gap between global commitments to protect civilians in armed conflict and the reality faced by civilians widened further." The report flags rising attacks on medical personnel and facilities (the 10th anniversary year of UNSCR 2286), two simultaneous famines, and the expanding use of AI, drones, and ICT on battlefields in ways that increase risks to civilians.

**Colombia — Humanitarian Needs Assessments:** OCHA published comprehensive humanitarian needs and priorities factsheets for 13 Colombian departments on 25 May 2026, covering armed conflict, displacement, food insecurity, and access constraints across the country's conflict-affected regions including Nariño, Chocó, Arauca, and Norte de Santander. [Example factsheet (Nariño)](https://reliefweb.int/report/colombia/colombia-ficha-de-necesidades-y-prioridades-humanitarias-narino-2024-2025).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Afghanistan — Flash Floods (active):** Heavy and sustained rainfall has triggered flash floods across 16 provinces including Helmand, Herat, Uruzgan, Kabul, and Kandahar. An [IFRC/Afghan Red Crescent situation report (published 25 May)](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/afg-flood-05-2026-afghanistan-flash-flood-may-2026-2026-05-23) confirms 1,401 affected families, 28 deaths, 23 injuries, 4 missing, 1,401 damaged or destroyed homes, 660 livestock lost, and 1,754 jeribs of agricultural land damaged. Priority needs are emergency shelter, food, multipurpose cash, and livestock support. Afghan Red Crescent teams are conducting field assessments.

**Indonesia — Earthquakes (ongoing seismic activity):** A M5.6 earthquake struck Indonesia on 25 May 2026 at 20:39 UTC ([GDACS green alert](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1542484), 3,000 people in MMI IV). This follows a M5.9 earthquake on 22 May ([GDACS green alert](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1541847), 200,000 in MMI IV+). Indonesia's ongoing flood situation ([GDACS active since 21 May](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103786&episodeid=51&eventtype=FL)) continues concurrently.

**Philippines — Earthquakes and Flooding (active):** A M5.5 earthquake struck the Philippines on 24 May ([GDACS green alert](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1542305&episodeid=1707602&eventtype=EQ), 7,000 in MMI V), and active flooding has been ongoing since 23 May ([GDACS green alert](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103880&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL)).

**Papua New Guinea — Earthquake:** A M5.7 earthquake struck Papua New Guinea on 22 May ([GDACS green alert](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1541910&episodeid=1707144&eventtype=EQ), 10,000 in MMI IV).

**Malaysia — Floods (ongoing):** Flooding continues in Johor and Kedah states; 359 people evacuated across three evacuation centres as of 22 May ([GDACS report](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103884)). More rainfall is forecast across Peninsular Malaysia.

**India — Severe weather (ongoing):** Severe thunderstorms, hailstorms, and floods across multiple states since 13 May have resulted in at least **122 fatalities** (primarily in Uttar Pradesh, Prayagraj district), 59 injuries, 227 damaged homes, and approximately 23,600 affected people in Assam due to floods ([OCHA Asia-Pacific snapshot](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-weekly-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-5-18-may-2026)).

*No active tropical cyclones or tsunami warnings as of 26 May 2026 AEST.*
