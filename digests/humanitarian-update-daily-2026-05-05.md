## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

An ongoing drought condition across parts of eastern Australia continues to be tracked by GDACS (event DR 1018457). The Australia-2026 drought began in late February 2026 and has now persisted for over ten weeks, affecting approximately 32,139 km² and classified as a mild to severe agricultural drought event. Drought indicators recorded by the [Global Drought Observatory (JRC)](http://edo.jrc.ec.europa.eu/gdo/php/index.php?id=2001&bbox=150,-31,152,-29) suggest anomalies consistent with a GDACS Green-level humanitarian impact, though extended dry conditions have implications for rural communities and food production in the region.

No acute emergency response announcements from ACFID, DFAT, or the Emergency Action Alliance were accessible this cycle (sources unreachable — see footer). Australians monitoring the humanitarian sector are encouraged to check [acfid.asn.au](https://www.acfid.asn.au), [dfat.gov.au](https://www.dfat.gov.au), and [emergencyaction.org.au](https://emergencyaction.org.au) directly for the latest announcements.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano Eruption (Active):** Mount Mayon in Albay Province, southern Luzon, produced a major pyroclastic density current on 2 May 2026 following a lava-flow collapse along the Mi-isi Gully. The eruption also triggered rockfalls and ashfall across municipalities including Guinobatan, Camalig, and Ligao City. [GDACS reports](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1000139&episodeid=1&eventtype=VO) that more than 30,000 families across 87 barangays have been affected, with over 14,000 individuals evacuated to centres and approximately 200,000 people living within the ashfall footprint. Alert Level 3 (magmatic unrest) remains in effect as of 4 May, and the 6-km Permanent Danger Zone remains strictly prohibited. The Smithsonian/USGS Weekly Volcanic Activity Report (published 30 April) noted ongoing lava effusion, PDCs, and daily volcanic tremor since at least 21 April.

**Philippines — M6.0 Earthquake, Eastern Samar:** A magnitude 6.0 tectonic earthquake struck Eastern Samar at 14:09 local time on 4 May 2026, at a depth of approximately 56–73 km. [GDACS assessed the event](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1538909&episodeid=1703833&eventtype=EQ) as low humanitarian impact with an exposed population of around 3,000 in MMI VI shaking intensity. The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS) confirmed the epicentre approximately 20 km northeast of San Julian town, with no reports of casualties as of 5 May.

**Micronesia / Northern Mariana Islands — Super Typhoon Sinlaku Recovery:** Super Typhoon Sinlaku (maximum sustained winds 287 km/h, Category 5) made landfall over Tinian Island in the Northern Mariana Islands on 14 April 2026, causing widespread damage across Guam, the Northern Mariana Islands, and Chuuk State (Federated States of Micronesia). [GDACS records](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001270&episodeid=44&eventtype=TC) that Chuuk experienced displacement of approximately 100 people and serious structural damage across seven islands (Weno, Tamatam, Piherarh, Onoun, Uman, Parem, and Fanapanges). Recovery and Copernicus Emergency Management Service (EMSR872) activation were still active as of 29 April 2026. The Virtual OSOCC had an open coordination page as of 14 April noting an active international response.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Kenya — Floods and Landslides (Ongoing):** Heavy rainfall has affected multiple Kenyan counties since 21 April 2026, causing the Tana River and other waterways to overflow. [GDACS confirmed as of 4 May](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103849) 18 fatalities — nine in the Eastern region, three in Central, and two each in Coast, Nairobi, and Rift Valley. Media report approximately 54,000 households affected. The affected counties include Tana River, Mombasa, Kwale, Tharaka Nithi, Elgeyo-Marakwet, Kiambu, and parts of the Rift Valley. The Kenya Meteorological Department has warned that rainfall will persist across several parts of the country over the coming days, and GDACS forecasts continued heavy rain over western, southern, and coastal Kenya through the next 96 hours.

**Multi-country — Active Flood Events in Africa:** GDACS is also tracking active flood events as of 3–5 May 2026 in the Democratic Republic of Congo, South Africa, and Algeria, all listed as newly activated events. Detailed impact assessments from these events were not yet publicly available at the time of publication. The DRC in particular has a history of high vulnerability to seasonal flooding, and observers should watch for developing situation reports via [ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int) and [OCHA](https://www.unocha.org).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Brazil — Ongoing Major Flooding (Orange Alert):** Brazil is facing compounding flood crises. The longer-running southern Brazil flood (GDACS Orange alert FL 1103757) that began in early February has resulted in 102 deaths and 27,866 displaced people across Minas Gerais, Rio de Janeiro, and São Paulo states, according to [GDACS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103757&episodeid=30&eventtype=FL). Separately, a new flooding episode has struck north-eastern Brazil (Pernambuco and Paraíba states) since 30 April, with [as of 4 May](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103852) six additional deaths and approximately 3,300 displaced across Recife, Olinda, Condé, João Pessoa, and Campina Grande. Forecasters warn that very heavy rainfall will continue in north-eastern Brazil over the next 96 hours.

**Tajikistan — Flash Floods and Mudflows:** Recent heavy rainfall in Kulob City (Khatlon Region) and Isfara City (Sughd Region) has caused floods and mudflows resulting in casualties and infrastructure damage. According to [GDACS](https://gdacs.org/), IFRC data reports three deaths, two persons missing, five injured, approximately 4,450 people affected, and more than 650 displaced. Several houses have been damaged. No significant rainfall is forecast in the affected regions until 7–8 May, when moderate to heavy thunderstorms are expected to return.

**Peru — Huánuco Department Flooding:** Flooding has been ongoing in Huánuco Department, Peru, from 28 April through 5 May 2026, with [GDACS recording one confirmed fatality](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103854&episodeid=4&eventtype=FL). Local disaster authorities are monitoring the situation. The Awajún communities of the Peruvian Amazon have deployed an innovative early-warning app ("Jempe Etsejui") that provides real-time alerts for emergencies and environmental crimes, according to regional media reports.

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano (Active, Alert Level 3):** The ongoing eruption of Mayon continues to pose immediate danger to communities within and around the 6-km Permanent Danger Zone in Albay Province. The VEI 4 eruption on 2 May generated pyroclastic density currents — fast-moving avalanches of hot volcanic material — and ashfall affecting multiple municipalities. Approximately 1.39 million people live within 30 km of the summit and 5.3 million within 100 km. PHIVOLCS maintains Alert Level 3 and advises all residents and civil aviation to observe exclusion zones. See [GDACS event VO 1000139](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1000139&episodeid=1&eventtype=VO) for continuous updates.

**Philippines — M6.0 Earthquake, Eastern Samar (4 May):** Low humanitarian impact assessed; no reported casualties. See [GDACS event EQ 1538909](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1538909&episodeid=1703833&eventtype=EQ).

**Brazil — Ongoing Orange-Alert Floods:** The multi-month flooding crisis across Brazil (102 deaths, 27,866 displaced) continues, with new flooding in the north-east adding to the toll. This is the most significant ongoing multi-province disaster event globally by death toll in the current 24-hour cycle. See [GDACS event FL 1103757](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103757&episodeid=30&eventtype=FL).

**Kenya — Active Floods (18 deaths):** Heavy rains forecast to persist through the coming days across multiple counties. See [GDACS event FL 1103849](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103849).
