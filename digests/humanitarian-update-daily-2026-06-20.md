## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Eastern Australia continues to grapple with a drought now in its 17th week. The [Eastern Australia-2026 drought](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=6&eventtype=DR) has been tracked by GDACS since late February 2026, affecting approximately 159,232 km² with drought indicators showing anomalies typical of mild to severe events and minor impact for agricultural production. The situation is compounded by the broader context of a developing El Niño, with the UN Secretary-General and meteorological agencies warning that a potentially powerful "Godzilla" El Niño event could be forming in the Pacific — an event that historically worsens drought conditions across eastern and southern Australia. [Forecasters](https://www.aa.com.tr/en/environment/-godzilla-el-nino-scientific-warning-or-media-hype/3972261) note the risk of unusual and potentially impactful weather changes, though near-record global grain stocks may provide some buffer to food security impacts in the near term.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

A wide-area drought across South-East Asia — tracked as the [South-East Asia-2026 event](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018545&episodeid=6&eventtype=DR) — is now in its 21st week, covering an area of approximately 365,168 km² across China, Indonesia, Cambodia, Laos, Malaysia, the Philippines, Thailand, and Vietnam. GDACS rates the impact as minor for agricultural drought, but the duration (120+ days as of 30 May 2026) and geographic breadth across multiple food-producing nations warrants continued monitoring, particularly given the potential for a developing El Niño to extend or intensify dry conditions in parts of the region.

Tropical Cyclone [MEKKHALA-26](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001277&episodeid=5&eventtype=TC) was active in the western Pacific as of 19 June 2026, reaching Category 4 intensity with maximum sustained winds of 213 km/h. GDACS classified the storm with a green (low humanitarian impact) alert as of the most recent advisory period (18–19 June), with the storm tracking offshore and no population in Category 1 or higher wind zones. The storm is being monitored by JTWC and multiple numerical models. Humanitarian-focused Pacific sources (Pacific Disaster Center, East Asia Forum, The Diplomat) were unreachable during this run.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Multiple large forest fires continue to burn in the Democratic Republic of Congo. The most significant active event, [GDACS WF 1028934](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1028934&episodeid=5&eventtype=WF), has been burning since 9 June 2026 across 12,843 hectares with 264 people estimated in the burned area. GDACS lists six separate DRC wildfire events active or recently active as of 19 June 2026, with total burned areas across those events exceeding 80,000 hectares. All are rated green (low humanitarian impact) based on affected population and vulnerability assessments, but the cumulative scale and ongoing nature of these fires in a country with one of the world's largest protracted displacement situations merits attention. The [Angola/Gabon/Congo drought](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018344&episodeid=14&eventtype=DR) is also in its 27th week in the sub-region.

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

A cluster of significant earthquakes struck Russia's Kamchatka Peninsula on 19 June 2026 UTC, with a [M6.0 at 06:51 UTC](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1547395&episodeid=1713223&eventtype=EQ) followed closely by a [M6.6 at 06:52 UTC](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1547396&episodeid=1713228&eventtype=EQ) and a M5.8 shortly after. A small tsunami wave was modelled at up to 0.16 m near the coast. GDACS assessed all three events as low humanitarian impact (green alert) given the sparse population in the affected area and Russia's INFORM coping capacity rating. The Kamchatka branch of the Russian Academy of Sciences' Geophysical Survey confirmed the quakes.

Tropical Storm ARTHUR-26 — the [first named storm](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001276&episodeid=8&eventtype=TC) of the 2026 Atlantic hurricane season — made landfall in the United States between 16–18 June 2026, causing flash flooding and at least [one fatality in San Antonio, Texas](https://www.archyde.com/tropical-storm-arthur-causes-flooding-and-one-death-in-san-antonio/). The storm reached 74 km/h with a maximum storm surge of 1.6 m and carried up to 522 mm of projected rainfall in affected zones. GDACS rated it green given low US vulnerability ratings.

Forecasters and climate scientists are increasingly discussing the potential formation of a powerful El Niño event later in 2026. [Near-record global wheat and rice reserves](https://www.thecattlesite.com/news/record-grain-stocks-seen-cushioning-blow-from-super-el-nino) are described as providing a potential buffer against the worst food security impacts if the event materialises, though key agricultural regions in South Asia and sub-Saharan Africa remain at elevated risk from El Niño-associated drought and flood patterns.

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Russia — Kamchatka earthquake swarm (19 June 2026):** Three earthquakes struck the Kamchatka Peninsula on 19 June 2026 within minutes of each other — M6.0 (06:51 UTC), M6.6 (06:52 UTC), and M5.8 (07:52 UTC). All epicentres were located off the eastern coast near 52.7°N, 160.5°E at shallow to moderate depth (10–28 km). A small tsunami was modelled (max 0.16 m) but no Pacific-wide alert was issued. Humanitarian impact is assessed as low by GDACS. Sources: [GDACS EQ 1547395](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1547395&episodeid=1713223&eventtype=EQ), [GDACS EQ 1547396](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1547396&episodeid=1713228&eventtype=EQ).

**Western Pacific — Tropical Cyclone MEKKHALA-26 (active):** Category 4 cyclone with 213 km/h sustained winds remains active in the western Pacific as of 19 June 2026 18:00 UTC, tracking offshore with no populated areas in the Category 1+ impact zone per current modelling. Continues to be monitored. Source: [GDACS TC 1001277](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001277&episodeid=5&eventtype=TC).
