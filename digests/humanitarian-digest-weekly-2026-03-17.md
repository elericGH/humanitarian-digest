## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Myanmar: UN Expert Warns of Crossroads as Airstrikes Kill Civilians (March 13–14)**
On 13 March, a UN human rights expert warned that Myanmar stands at a "crossroads," with escalating military violence and deepening humanitarian needs pushing millions deeper into crisis more than five years after the coup. The following day, Myanmar's military conducted an airstrike on a crowded market in Let Pan Hla village, Mandalay Region, killing at least 27 civilians — including children as young as two — and destroying homes. OCHA's most recent Myanmar Humanitarian Update (No. 51, 9 March) reports that nearly one-third of the population — approximately 18 million people — require humanitarian assistance, with 12 million facing acute hunger. A separate expert analysis from Mizzima News (13 March) warned that the escalating Middle East war risks dramatically deepening Myanmar's already severe crisis by further diverting global donor attention and funding. [UN News – Myanmar Crossroads (13 March)](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167132) | [OCHA Myanmar Update No. 51 (9 March)](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/myanmar-humanitarian-update-no51-9-march-2026) | [Mizzima – Mideast War and Myanmar (13 March)](https://eng.mizzima.com/2026/03/13/32130)

**Humanitarian Networks & Partnerships Week Concludes (March 11–12)**
The hybrid phase of the Humanitarian Networks and Partnerships Week 2026 concluded this week in Geneva, with March 11 focused on the "humanitarian reset" — a proposed systemic overhaul of the global aid model in response to major funding contractions, the collapse of USAID, and geopolitical shifts. Pacific and Asian civil society organisations called for structural shifts toward local humanitarian leadership and more flexible funding streams. [UNDP – HNPW 2026](https://www.undp.org/geneva/events/humanitarian-networks-and-partnerships-week-2026)

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Lebanon: Displacement Approaches One Million as Conflict Deepens (March 11–16)**
A week of deteriorating conditions saw Lebanon's displacement crisis escalate rapidly. On 11 March, Al Jazeera reported the UN warning of a widening crisis as Israeli attacks had displaced 816,000 people; by 13 March, IOM reported the figure had surged to nearly one million, and launched an emergency appeal for USD 19 million. A Washington Post investigation (14 March) found hundreds of overcrowded shelters with inadequate sanitation, with many families still sleeping in cars or on roadsides. An Al Jazeera analysis on 16 March reported that Israeli forces appear to be using continued occupation of southern Lebanese territory as a negotiating tool, raising fears that displacement — and the humanitarian emergency — will be prolonged. Lebanon was already hosting 1.5 million Syrian refugees and had 4.1 million people in humanitarian need before the escalation. [Al Jazeera – 816,000 Displaced (11 March)](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/11/un-warns-of-widening-crisis-as-israeli-attacks-displace-750000-in-lebanon) | [IOM – Nearly One Million Displaced (13 March)](https://www.iom.int/news/nearly-one-million-displaced-lebanon-iom-appeals-usd-19-million) | [Washington Post – Lebanon Shelter Crisis (14 March)](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/03/14/lebanon-israel-hezbollah-displacement-shelter/59deab16-1f6c-11f1-a29c-fd43da9a479a_story.html) | [Al Jazeera – Occupation as Leverage (16 March)](https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2026/3/16/in-lebanon-israel-is-using-occupation-as-negotiating-tool-say-analysts)

**Gaza: Crossings Remain Largely Closed, Medicines Running Out (week of March 11)**
UNRWA's Situation Report #212, published 11 March and covering the period 4–10 March, confirms that all Gaza crossings except Kerem Shalom remain closed, with severely inadequate volumes of aid entering. Some 46% of essential medicines and 66% of medical consumables are currently out of stock. UNRWA has now recorded 391 of its own staff killed in Gaza since the war began — 310 agency personnel, plus 81 support workers. Airstrikes, shelling, and gunfire continued across North Gaza, Gaza City, Bureij, and Khan Younis during the reporting week. The lack of medical evacuation routes and restricted fuel access continues to cripple health services. [UNRWA Situation Report #212 (11 March)](https://www.unrwa.org/resources/reports/unrwa-situation-report-212-humanitarian-crisis-gaza-strip-and-occupied-west-bank)

**Sudan: 200+ Civilians Killed by Drone Strikes Since March 4 (March 12–14)**
The UN human rights chief Volker Türk stated on 13 March that he was "appalled" by the devastating surge in drone attacks on civilian areas of Sudan, with more than 200 civilians reported killed by drone strikes since just 4 March. A Washington Post report (12 March) documented strikes in Darfur, while a CBC report described at least 17 civilians killed — including young girls — in a strike near a White Nile school. In West Kordofan, at least 152 civilians were reportedly killed by Sudanese army drone strikes in recent weeks, including 50 when a market and hospital were struck simultaneously. Sudan urgently called on the UN Security Council to condemn the RSF drone attacks on cities including El Obeid, Kosti, and Ed Damazin. [UN News – Sudan Drone Attacks (13 March)](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167125) | [OHCHR – Türk Statement (13 March)](https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-releases/2026/03/sudan-turk-appalled-surge-deadly-drone-attacks-civilians-kordofan-and-white) | [Washington Post – Sudan Darfur Strikes (12 March)](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/03/12/sudan-darfur-war-army-drones-kordofan/06299b14-1e51-11f1-a29c-fd43da9a479a_story.html)

**South Sudan: Power-Sharing Collapse Drives Mass Displacement (March 12–13)**
OCHA's Jonglei State Conflict Flash Update No. 12 (13 March) reports 267,000 people displaced across Jonglei, Lakes, Upper Nile, and Central Equatoria states as the collapse of the country's power-sharing agreement drives fresh violence. An estimated 100,000 civilians fled across the border into Ethiopia from Akobo County after government forces ordered civilians and aid agencies to vacate ahead of military operations on 6 March. The main water-pumping station in Akobo has been looted and destroyed, leaving tens of thousands without safe water during mass displacement. The New Humanitarian's analysis (12 March) links the violence directly to the breakdown of the 2018 peace accord between the government and main opposition. [OCHA – Jonglei Flash Update No. 12 (13 March)](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-conflict-jonglei-state-flash-update-no-12-13-march-2026) | [The New Humanitarian – Power-Sharing Collapse (12 March)](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/news-feature/2026/03/12/south-sudan-power-sharing-collapse-drives-violence-mass-displacement)

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Global Aid Funding Crisis: One Year On From USAID Dismantlement**
The week following the one-year mark of USAID's dismantlement (GBH reported 9 March on its devastating ongoing impact) continues to define the global funding landscape. A *Lancet* study published in February 2026 projects at least 9.4 million additional deaths by 2030 if current aid funding trends continue. Compounding US cuts, the UK, Germany, and Canada have announced further ODA reductions. The IRC has warned that the cuts are placing millions in peril with no substitution in sight. [GBH – One Year After USAID Cuts (9 March)](https://www.wgbh.org/news/local/2026-03-09/a-year-after-usaid-cuts-local-groups-say-impact-on-humanitarian-work-has-been-devastating) | [CNN – The Lancet Study](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/04/world/lancet-usaid-global-aid-cuts-intl) | [IRC – US Aid Cuts Putting Millions in Peril](https://www.rescue.org/article/us-aid-cuts-put-millions-peril)

**Ukraine & Afghanistan: Ongoing High-Need Crises**
OCHA's 2026 Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan for Ukraine requires US$2.3 billion to support 4.1 million people, with front-line communities bearing the brunt of continued strikes. In Afghanistan, Australia committed $50 million in humanitarian assistance on 29 January 2026 for food security, health, and gender protection — as 21.9 million people (45% of the population) require assistance in 2026 and acute food insecurity has doubled year-on-year. [OCHA Ukraine HNRP 2026](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/ukraine/ukraine-humanitarian-needs-and-response-plan-2026-january-2026-enuk) | [OCHA Afghanistan HNRP 2026](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-humanitarian-needs-and-response-plan-2026-december-2025)
