## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Myanmar Earthquake Response (Week 3): Funding Gap Deepens**
The death toll from the twin 7.7-magnitude earthquakes that struck central Myanmar on March 28 has now exceeded 3,600, with more than 5,100 injured and 116 missing. More than 17.2 million people — including 2.7 million children — live in affected areas and urgently need food, water, healthcare, and emergency shelter. Despite a Flash Appeal for $275 million, only $34 million has been disbursed, leaving a critical funding gap as rescue operations wind down and relief transitions to recovery. [OCHA Myanmar Update No. 51](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/myanmar-humanitarian-update-no51-9-march-2026) | [PBS News – Myanmar death toll](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/myanmars-deadly-earthquake-death-toll-exceeds-3500-as-rescue-efforts-wind-down) | [UNICEF – Children at Risk](https://www.unicef.org/press-releases/millions-children-grave-risk-following-myanmars-deadliest-earthquake-decades)

**Humanitarian Networks & Partnerships Week (March 10–12)**
The hybrid phase of the Humanitarian Networks and Partnerships Week 2026 concluded this week, centred on the theme of a "humanitarian reset" — a proposed systemic overhaul of the global aid system in response to funding contractions and shifting geopolitics. With USAID dismantlement now a year old, discussions focused on how the sector adapts, with Pacific and Asian civil society voices pushing for greater local leadership. [UNDP – HNPW 2026](https://www.undp.org/geneva/events/humanitarian-networks-and-partnerships-week-2026)

**Asia-Pacific: World's Most Disaster-Prone Region Faces Overlapping Crises**
UNICEF's East Asia and Pacific Situation Report highlights that the region — home to 580 million children and adolescents — continues to face overlapping climate, conflict, and health emergencies. Extreme weather events in 2025 affected tens of millions, and ongoing conflict in Myanmar compounds existing disaster vulnerability. [UNICEF EAP Humanitarian Situation Report](https://www.unicef.org/media/179106/file/East-Asia-and-Pacific-Region-Humanitarian-Situation-Report-No.2-(End-of-Year)-31-December-2025.pdf.pdf)

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Gaza: Crossing Closures Deepen Crisis Amid Regional Escalation**
All crossings into Gaza except for Kerem Shalom have been closed since February 28, following Israeli and US military operations against Iran. With only approximately 200 trucks entering Gaza daily — versus the 600 needed — critical shortages of food, medicine and fuel are intensifying. Some areas are receiving just two litres of drinking water per person per day, while 46% of essential medicines and 66% of medical consumables are out of stock. The UN Secretary-General has urgently called on Israel to reopen all crossings. [OCHA Gaza Humanitarian Situation Report – 6 March 2026](https://www.ochaopt.org/content/humanitarian-situation-report-6-march-2026) | [Al Jazeera – Gaza Food Prices](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/10/gaza-food-prices-soar-as-border-closures-deepen-shortages-amid-iran-war) | [Al Jazeera – UN Warning](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/3/un-chief-warns-of-israeli-made-humanitarian-crisis-in-gaza-amid-war-on-iran)

**Lebanon: Over 830,000 Displaced as Conflict Escalates**
Since fresh hostilities erupted on March 1, more than 830,000 people have been displaced across Lebanon — including approximately 200,000 children — as Israeli airstrikes and evacuation orders swept more than 100 towns and villages in the south. All 399 government-opened shelters are reported to be at or near full capacity, with many families sleeping in cars or on roadsides. Lebanon was already hosting 1.5 million Syrian refugees before this latest escalation, and over 4.1 million people (70% of the population) were already in humanitarian need. [UN News – Lebanon Displacement](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167098) | [Washington Post – Lebanon Shelter Crisis](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/03/14/lebanon-israel-hezbollah-displacement-shelter/59deab16-1f6c-11f1-a29c-fd43da9a479a_story.html) | [OHCHR – Displacement Orders](https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-briefing-notes/2026/03/lebanon-israeli-blanket-displacement-orders-bring-more-misery)

**Sudan: World's Largest Displacement Crisis Deepens**
Now in its third year of civil war, Sudan remains the world's largest displacement crisis with 33.7 million people requiring humanitarian assistance. The World Food Programme reports 24.6 million people face acute hunger, with 2 million at or near famine conditions. Fighting has intensified in Kordofan, where drone strikes are striking markets, health facilities and residential areas on an almost daily basis. Aid access continues to be deliberately obstructed by both parties. [UNHCR Sudan 2026 Appeal](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/unhcr-sudan-situation-appeal-2026) | [PBS – Sudan 3-Year Anniversary](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/show/after-3-years-of-relentless-war-sudan-faces-worlds-worst-humanitarian-crisis) | [HRW World Report 2026 – Sudan](https://www.hrw.org/world-report/2026/country-chapters/sudan)

**South Sudan: 9.3 Million People Need Humanitarian Assistance**
South Sudan faces a multidimensional crisis driven by armed conflict, intercommunal violence, extreme climate shocks, and economic collapse. An estimated 9.3 million people require humanitarian assistance, including 7.7 million who are food insecure during lean months. Humanitarian operations continue to be hampered by attacks on aid workers and movement restrictions. [Al Jazeera – South Sudan Humanitarian Crisis](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/2/10/humanitarian-crisis-deepens-as-south-sudan-violence-surges) | [IOM South Sudan Crisis Response Plan 2026](https://crisisresponse.iom.int/response/south-sudan-crisis-response-plan-2026)

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Ukraine: $2.3 Billion Needed as Conflict Enters Fourth Year**
OCHA's 2026 Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan for Ukraine requires US$2.3 billion to support 4.1 million prioritised people, focused on front-line response, newly displaced populations, and emergency support for those affected by ongoing strikes. Eight humanitarian workers were killed and 47 injured in 2025, with no signs of improvement ahead. [OCHA – Ukraine HNRP 2026](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/ukraine/ukraine-humanitarian-needs-and-response-plan-2026-january-2026-enuk)

**Afghanistan: Acute Food Insecurity Rising Sharply**
With 21.9 million people (45% of the population) requiring humanitarian assistance in 2026, Afghanistan remains one of the most severe crises globally. Acute food insecurity has sharply worsened — 17.4 million people are projected to face food insecurity this year, including 4.7 million in IPC Phase 4 (Emergency), more than double last year's figure. The return of more than 2.61 million Afghans from Iran and Pakistan in 2025 is placing enormous additional strain on communities and services. [OCHA – Afghanistan HNRP 2026](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-humanitarian-needs-and-response-plan-2026-december-2025)

**Global Aid Cuts: One Year On From USAID Dismantlement**
This week marked the one-year point since the Trump administration began dismantling USAID, with local humanitarian groups reporting devastating ongoing impacts. A new study in *The Lancet* projects at least 9.4 million additional deaths by 2030 if current funding trends continue. On February 3, the US Congress passed a $50 billion foreign aid bill, offering some hope of partial restoration. The cuts have been compounded by similar reductions announced by the UK, Germany, and Canada. [GBH – One Year On](https://www.wgbh.org/news/local/2026-03-09/a-year-after-usaid-cuts-local-groups-say-impact-on-humanitarian-work-has-been-devastating) | [CNN – Lancet Study](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/04/world/lancet-usaid-global-aid-cuts-intl) | [IRC – US Aid Cuts](https://www.rescue.org/article/us-aid-cuts-put-millions-peril)
