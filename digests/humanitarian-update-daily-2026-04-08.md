# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Wednesday, 8 April 2026
*24-hour scan | Australian Interest Priority*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Alerts

No new Australian priority releases in the past 24 hours. Australia's ongoing regional commitments remain active: the government continues to direct approximately 75% of its $5.1 billion development aid budget toward the Indo-Pacific, with standing Middle East humanitarian funding in place through the Emergency Action Alliance (EAA). The Foreign Minister's Declaration for the Protection of Humanitarian Personnel — co-signed by more than 100 countries — remains a key active commitment, backed by $15 million through OCHA, UN DSS, and the Red Cross/Red Crescent Movement.

## What's New Today

- **Lebanon** — Israel's ongoing military operations have now displaced more than 1.2 million people (one-fifth of the population), with nearly 1,500 killed since early March; only one-third of the $308.3M Flash Appeal has been funded. [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/7/how-israels-invasion-of-southern-lebanon-created-a-humanitarian-crisis) | [CARE](https://www.care.org/news-and-stories/april-2-2026-in-lebanon-one-month-into-the-escalation-in-lebanon-this-crisis-is-spiraling-toward-catastrophe/)

- **DRC — Mambasa Territory** — At least 30 civilians were killed in armed attacks in Bafwakoa on Wednesday, bringing the death toll there to over 100 since 11 March; nine health facilities have suspended operations and more than 50,000 people are displaced in the territory. [UN News / Global Security](https://www.globalsecurity.org/military/library/news/2026/04/mil-260402-unnews03.htm)

- **Afghanistan** — Compounding floods and a 5.8-magnitude earthquake across multiple provinces have killed at least 35 people and damaged or destroyed thousands of homes; India has sent a second humanitarian aid shipment in two weeks. [ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-severe-weather-floods-and-earthquake-update-media-gdacs-usgs-noaa-echo-daily-flash-7-april-2026) | [Ground News](https://ground.news/article/deadly-earthquake-and-floods-worsen-afghanistans-troubles_f91a04)

- **Asia-Pacific — Food Security** — OCHA warns that if the Middle East conflict persists and oil remains above $100/barrel, nearly 9.1 million additional people in Asia could face acute food insecurity; Afghanistan, Bangladesh, and Myanmar face compounding operational cost increases. [OCHA](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/asia-and-pacific-humanitarian-impact-middle-east-escalation-3-april-2026)

- **Pacific — Disease Surveillance** — The Pacific Public Health Surveillance Network updated its epidemic alert map on 7 April, flagging sustained vigilance for measles and polio across fragile Pacific health systems, with immunisation catch-up campaigns ongoing in high-risk settings. [ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/map/world/epidemic-and-emerging-disease-alerts-pacific-07-april-2026)

- **South Sudan** — UN Human Rights Council experts this week described South Sudan as heading toward a "catastrophic" crisis, with 267,000 people displaced in Jonglei state alone in 2026 and 300,000 learners affected by school disruptions. [fundsforNGOs / UN News](https://news.fundsforngos.org/2026/04/06/global-crisis-update-south-sudan-rights-who-opioid-guidelines-dr-congo-violence/)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

**Lebanon** is deteriorating rapidly — the destruction of key bridges in southern Lebanon has cut humanitarian access to over 150,000 people, and half of UNFPA-supported health facilities in conflict zones have closed. With the Flash Appeal only 33% funded, life-saving operations face severe gaps.
