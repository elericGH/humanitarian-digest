# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Sunday, 21 June 2026
*Produced Sunday, 21 June 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The Emergency Action Alliance (EAA) — the coalition of 15 leading Australian aid charities including ADRA Australia, CARE, Save the Children, and Oxfam Australia — continues its active [Middle East Crisis Appeal](https://emergencyaction.org.au/middle-east-appeal/) as conflict in Gaza, Lebanon, the West Bank and Syria intensifies. Australian NGO member organisations are scaling up responses, with EAA noting that "millions of people urgently need shelter, food and basic supplies" and that additional funding is critical. Australians can donate via the EAA centralised portal, which routes funds directly to members operating on the ground.

The EAA [Myanmar Earthquake appeal](https://emergencyaction.org.au/appeal/myanmar-earthquake/) also remains active, with member organisations providing food, shelter, safe water and emergency supplies more than six months on from the disaster. A six-month impact report was published alongside an updated appeal for continued support, reflecting the long recovery timelines typical of major seismic events.

GDACS has flagged a [17-week ongoing drought across Eastern Australia](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=6&eventtype=DR) as of 21 June 2026, classified as an ongoing hazard event. While this falls within domestic preparedness rather than overseas aid, it has implications for Australian government aid capacity, domestic food prices, and the broader Asia-Pacific food security picture that DFAT monitors closely.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

The [Philippines Earthquake and Tsunami disaster (June 2026)](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/eq-2026-000083-phl) remains an active, ongoing emergency per ReliefWeb's disaster tracking. The event continues to require international humanitarian response, with affected coastal communities still in need of shelter, WASH, and livelihoods support. This is a critical concern for Australia given its Pacific engagement commitments and proximity.

UNHCR welcomed a significant step toward refugee inclusion in Thailand, with a [press release on 17 June 2026](https://www.unhcr.org/news/press-releases/unhcr-government-issued-id-cards-mark-new-era-refugee-inclusion-and-self) announcing that government-issued ID cards now mark a "new era of refugee inclusion and self-reliance" for Myanmar and other refugees living in the country. The move allows refugees formal legal identity and access to services, and is seen as a model for the broader Asia-Pacific region.

GDACS also lists an ongoing [South-East Asia drought (21 weeks)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018545&episodeid=6&eventtype=DR) and the [Federated States of Micronesia Typhoon Sinlaku (April 2026)](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/tc-2026-000050-fsm) as still ongoing per ReliefWeb's disaster portal. The Micronesian response highlights the vulnerability of small island states and Australia's role as a first responder in the Pacific. GDACS further registered Super Typhoon MEKKHALA-26 at 213 km/h as of 19 June, a significant storm in the Northwest Pacific requiring close monitoring for potential landfall impacts.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Lebanon/Iran/Gaza:** Israeli strikes on Lebanon have killed at least 32 people, putting US-Iran peace talks in serious peril, [Al Jazeera reported on 20 June 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/6/20/us-envoy-headed-for-switzerland-israeli-strikes-on-lebanon-threaten-talks). US envoys Steve Witkoff and Jared Kushner are in Switzerland for technical talks following a postponed meeting, while Iranian Foreign Minister Araghchi has conditioned Iran's participation on a ceasefire in Lebanon. Overnight strikes on villages including Barish killed a family of four, and a Lebanese soldier was killed at Kfar Reman. The IRGC has separately announced closure of the Strait of Hormuz, a development with significant global supply chain implications. Meanwhile, [NRC and the Shelter Cluster report](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/gaza-israels-shelter-restrictions-force-nearly-million-people-endure-summer-heat-tents-nrc) that nearly one million people in Gaza — approximately 170,000 households — are living in tents as Israel's shelter restrictions prevent reconstruction heading into the summer heat.

**DRC/Uganda Ebola Outbreak:** UNHCR on 19 June issued an urgent warning that the [Ebola outbreak threatens displaced communities in DRC and beyond](https://www.unhcr.org/news/briefing-notes/unhcr-warns-ebola-outbreak-threatens-displaced-communities-drc-and-beyond), representing one of the most significant public health emergencies intersecting with a displacement crisis in the current period. The ICRC reported on 16 June that communities in the Kivus face [a double crisis of conflict and epidemic](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/im-already-injured-getting-ebola-doesnt-bear-thinking-about-kivus-face-double-crisis), with one patient quoted saying "I'm already injured. Getting Ebola doesn't bear thinking about." IRC has also flagged that [women and girls face a double threat](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/women-and-girls-face-double-threat-ebola-outbreak-response-must-address-both-irc) in the outbreak, as primary caretakers and those forced to travel further for water and resources.

**Food Security and Sudan:** A new [FAO-WFP Hunger Hotspots report](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/hunger-hotspots-fao-wfp-early-warnings-acute-food-insecurity-june-november-2026-outlook) warns that worsening hunger is putting 13 hotspots at significant risk through to November 2026, with Sudan, South Sudan, Yemen and Palestine as the highest-concern countries, and northeast Nigeria and Somalia newly joining the list at catastrophic risk levels. Separately, [more than 24 million people in the Sahel](https://reliefweb.int/report/mali/24-million-people-sahel-urgently-need-aid-and-world-needs-do-more) urgently need aid, with OCHA reporting violence spreading beyond traditional borders into coastal West Africa. In Somalia, MSF reports that [malnutrition admissions are overwhelming Baidoa Hospital](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/malnutrition-admissions-are-overwhelming-baidoa-hospital-somalia), with 1,400 patients admitted between March and May, and the facility pushed well beyond its 50-bed capacity even after expansion to 125 beds.

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**World Refugee Day (20 June 2026):** UNHCR's High Commissioner issued a [statement calling for the promise of safety and dignity to be renewed for a new generation](https://www.unhcr.org/news/speeches-and-statements/world-refugee-day-promise-safety-and-dignity-must-be-renewed-new) of refugees, noting that 41.6 million people worldwide are now refugees and that 68% are hosted in low- and middle-income countries. UNHCR simultaneously launched a [campaign rallying young people to stand with refugees](https://www.unhcr.org/news/press-releases/world-refugee-day-unhcr-rallies-young-people-stand-refugees-and-defend-asylum) and defend the right to asylum. Global Trends 2025 data released on 11 June revealed displacement figures as of end-2025.

**WFP Global Hunger Crisis:** WFP welcomed a major new US funding contribution to humanitarian operations at a moment when it described global hunger as reaching [record levels](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-welcomes-announcement-major-us-contribution-global-hunger-reaches-record-levels), with 318 million people facing acute hunger in 2025 and WFP requiring US$13 billion to reach the most vulnerable. The joint [FAO-WFP El Niño appeal](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/bracing-el-nino-fao-and-wfp-launch-joint-appeal-protect-88-million-people-extreme-weather-events) launched a $202 million request to protect 8.8 million people from extreme weather ahead of climate shocks threatening food security across the most vulnerable regions.

**Ukraine and the Global Peace Index:** Russia conducted new attacks on Ukraine's energy infrastructure, with the Humanitarian Coordinator strongly condemning strikes that killed and injured civilians and left thousands without power, per ReliefWeb updates. The newly released [Global Peace Index 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/global-peace-index-2026) — produced by the Institute for Economics and Peace — reveals that global peacefulness has deteriorated for the twelfth consecutive year, driven by a record-high number of interconnected conflicts that are increasingly difficult to resolve.

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Super Typhoon MEKKHALA-26** was tracked by GDACS at 213 km/h as of 19 June 2026, making it a significant Pacific weather event. The system is active and requires monitoring for potential landfall and humanitarian impact. [GDACS event page](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001277&episodeid=5&eventtype=TC).

**DRC Forest Fires:** Multiple large-scale forest fire events are ongoing in the Democratic Republic of Congo, with fires recorded on 19 June ranging from 10,000 to 14,500 hectares. These compound the existing Ebola and displacement crises in the country. [GDACS DRC fire events](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1028934&episodeid=5&eventtype=WF).

**Eastern Australia Drought:** GDACS lists an ongoing 17-week drought covering Eastern Australia, tracking as a persistent slow-onset hazard with implications for food production, water security, and community wellbeing. [GDACS drought page](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=6&eventtype=DR).

**Russia Earthquakes:** A cluster of earthquakes struck Russia on 19 June, including an M6.6 and M6.0, assessed by GDACS as Green alert events (limited impact on populated areas). No tsunami warning was issued. [GDACS event](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1547396&episodeid=1713228&eventtype=EQ).
