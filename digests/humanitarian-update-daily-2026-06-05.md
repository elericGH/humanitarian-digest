## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Japan — Severe Tropical Storm Jangmi (active):** Severe Tropical Storm Jangmi made landfall near Wakayama Prefecture on 3 June and tracked toward the Tokai and Kanto regions. Japanese authorities issued Level 4 evacuation warnings for river flooding in Tokyo and landslides across Chiba, Kanagawa, Shizuoka, and Mie prefectures, with up to 300mm of rainfall forecast in 24 hours. An emergency flood warning for the Kozagawa River was later downgraded as conditions improved ([OCHA Asia-Pacific Snapshot, 3 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-weekly-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-26-may-1-june-2026)).

**Myanmar — Ongoing conflict displacement:** Intensified military operations in Tanintharyi Region since mid-May have displaced more than 5,000 people from several villages, many now sheltering in forests with severe food and supply shortages. In western Bago Region, around 12,000 people remain displaced since April. A UNHCR map posted 4 June 2026 shows over 3 million people remain displaced since the February 2021 coup, and the Myanmar Health Cluster records 73 attacks on health care between 1 January and 31 May 2026 ([ReliefWeb, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/myanmar-emergency-overview-map-number-people-displaced-feb-2021-and-remain-displaced-01-jun-2026)).

**Sri Lanka — Dengue Early Action Protocol activated:** The Sri Lanka Red Cross Society activated its Simplified Early Action Protocol for dengue on 4 June after the National Dengue Control Unit confirmed a weekly threshold breach, triggering pre-emptive distribution of dengue prevention supplies ([IFRC, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sri-lanka/sri-lanka-simplified-early-action-protocol-activation-seap-seap2024lk01)).

**Papua New Guinea — Cyclone Maila recovery ongoing:** Tropical Cyclone Maila (Category 3, April 2026) continues to be listed as an active disaster affecting PNG and the Solomon Islands. The most recent UNCT PNG Disaster Management Team SitRep #8 (16 May 2026) and the PNG Humanitarian Snapshot (as of 29 May 2026) remain the latest published updates ([ReliefWeb PNG country page](https://reliefweb.int/country/png)).

**Pacific dengue outbreak:** An ongoing dengue outbreak affecting Tonga and at least nine other Pacific island nations continues to be listed as active, with the Pacific regional response coordinated through Fiji ([ReliefWeb Fiji country page](https://reliefweb.int/country/fji)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Lebanon — New ceasefire announced, humanitarian crisis deepens:** A new ceasefire between Israel and Lebanon was announced on 4 June 2026, but Islamic Relief warned it will only improve the humanitarian situation if fully adhered to. Up to 1.5 million people — around a quarter of Lebanon's population — remain displaced, with over 150,000 in severely overcrowded collective shelters and more than 42,000 homes destroyed. At least 3,433 people have been killed and over 10,300 injured since 2 March 2026 per Lebanon's Ministry of Public Health ([Islamic Relief, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-new-ceasefire-announcement-must-be-adhered-humanitarian-crisis-worsens)).

**Lebanon — Escalation continues, disease risk rising:** OCHA's Flash Update #31 (posted 3 June 2026) documents continued airstrikes and new displacement orders over South and Nabatieh governorates. Collective shelters in Saida are severely overcrowded, with 127,700+ people displaced in shelters. Acute watery diarrhoea cases rose from 504 per week (week 17) to 803 per week (week 20) ([OCHA, 3 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-flash-update-31-escalation-hostilities-lebanon-1june-2026)).

**Gaza — Attacks on health care continue:** Insecurity Insight recorded at least 27 attacks on health care in the occupied Palestinian territory between 13–26 May 2026. Since 7 October 2023, at least 3,291 attacks on health care have been documented, including more than 154 drone incidents affecting health workers and facilities. At least 61 maternal health services have been damaged or disrupted ([Insecurity Insight, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/attacks-health-care-occupied-palestinian-territory-13-26-may-2026)).

**WHO Middle East SitRep #10:** WHO's situation report covering 15 May – 2 June 2026 (posted 4 June) notes that 115 million people across the Middle East region — 48% of the global humanitarian total — are in need of aid. WHO's Middle East flash appeal is only 12% funded ($30.3M required). On 3 June, strikes on Kuwait Airport and diplomatic facilities resulted in 1 death and 63 injuries; the UN House hosting WHO's office was damaged by debris ([WHO, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/who-situation-report-no10-middle-east-conflict-15-may-2-june-2026)).

**Sudan — 19.5 million facing acute hunger:** A new IPC food insecurity analysis posted 4 June 2026 confirms that approximately 19.5 million people (41% of Sudan's population) face IPC Phase 3+ acute food insecurity through May 2026, including 135,000 in IPC Phase 5 (Catastrophe/starvation). 825,000 children are acutely malnourished, and 14 areas in North and South Darfur and South Kordofan are at risk of famine if conditions worsen. 40% of health facilities are non-operational ([IPC, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/sudan-ipc-acute-food-insecurity-analysis-l-february-2026-january-2027-published-3-june-2026)).

**Sudan — World's most neglected displacement crisis:** The Norwegian Refugee Council's 10th annual Neglected Displacement Crises report (posted 4 June) names Sudan as the world's most neglected displacement crisis, with over 9 million internally displaced and nearly 19.5 million facing hunger. The 10 most neglected crises are Sudan, DRC, Colombia, Yemen, Afghanistan, Honduras, Ecuador, Cameroon, Nigeria, and Mozambique. NRC Secretary General Jan Egeland stated: "Millions of people are being abandoned because we have chosen not to act" ([NRC, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/worlds-most-neglected-displacement-crises-2025-10th-edition)).

**DRC — Ebola Bundibugyo outbreak (17th outbreak, PHEIC declared):** The Bundibugyo ebolavirus outbreak declared on 15 May 2026 — for which no approved vaccine or treatment exists — has reached 321 confirmed cases with 48 confirmed deaths and 116 suspected cases under investigation across Ituri, North Kivu, and South Kivu provinces. Uganda has confirmed 15 cases and 1 death. WHO declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern within two days of confirmation, and Africa CDC has identified 10 countries at risk ([Data Friendly Space, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/democratic-republic-congo-ebola-outbreak-overview-context-vulnerabilities-and-response-4-june-2026)).

**DRC — Ebola impacts education sector:** As of 29 May 2026, 246 deaths had been reported from 1,139 suspected Ebola cases. The Education Cluster (Save the Children/UNICEF) has issued guidance for safe school exam operations in the context of the outbreak ([Education Cluster, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/country/cod)).

**South Sudan — Ebola preparedness and famine risk:** South Sudan's Senior Presidential Envoy visited the National Public Health Institute on 4 June to reinforce Ebola preparedness; no cases confirmed as of that date. Separately, FEWS NET warns that Emergency (IPC Phase 4) outcomes are expected to reach 34 counties by September 2026, with a credible famine risk in Akobo, Nyirol, Nasir, and Ulang counties. Violence has killed or displaced over 300,000 people in Jonglei and Upper Nile since January ([WHO, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/senior-presidential-envoy-backs-emergency-preparedness-south-sudan-amid-ebola-outbreaks-uganda-and-drc); [FEWS NET, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-key-message-update-fragile-gains-assistance-delivery-amid-lull-conflict-risk-famine-persists-may-september-2026)).

**Yemen — Near-half population facing acute hunger:** WFP, FAO, and UNICEF report that 47% of the population in government-controlled areas (approximately 5 million people) face IPC Phase 3+ acute food insecurity, including 1.4 million in Emergency conditions. This is projected to worsen to 51% by September 2026 as humanitarian food assistance declines sharply due to funding shortfalls. WHO is deploying mobile health teams to protect displaced Yemenis from malaria as the southwest monsoon season begins ([WFP, 3–4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/nearly-half-population-government-controlled-areas-yemen-face-acute-food-insecurity-humanitarian-support-sharply-declines)).

**Nigeria — Cholera outbreak accelerating:** International Medical Corps reports 6,078 suspected cholera cases and 65 deaths as of 3 June in Nigeria — a 288% increase since onset — spanning 138 settlements across 7 of 12 affected LGAs. Two Cholera Treatment Centers and 12 oral rehydration points are operational. IMC has deployed 218 volunteer community mobilisers across 4 LGAs. The Sallah period mass movement contributed to the spike ([IMC, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/nigeria/nigeria-cholera-outbreak-situation-report-2-june-4-2026)).

**Somalia — MPCA distributions ongoing:** ACTED is distributing multipurpose cash assistance to crisis-affected communities in Somalia, which continues to face an escalating multi-dimensional humanitarian crisis driven by climate shocks, disease outbreaks, and conflict ([ACTED, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/somalia-empowering-crisis-affected-communities-through-multipurpose-cash-assistance-mpca-support)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Colombia — Armed group conflict displaces thousands in Chocó:** OCHA's flash update on the Baudó sub-region of Chocó documents at least 2,728 people in prolonged confinement and mass displacement (552 families), and 12,605 people (3,133 families) facing mobility restrictions due to clashes between non-state armed groups. At least 89 ethnic communities — predominantly Indigenous and Afro-Colombian — face repeated victimisation including child recruitment, landmines, and targeted killings ([OCHA Colombia, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/colombia/colombia-flash-update-confinamientos-desplazamiento-masivo-restricciones-y-riesgos-en-la-subregion-del-baudo-choco-4-de-junio-de-2026)).

**Syria — Mass displacement from Lebanon continues:** WHO's Middle East SitRep #10 notes that more than 448,000 people have crossed from Lebanon into Syria since 2 March 2026. Syria is also reporting 545 measles cases since January 2026 (96 in the past two weeks) and more than 5,800 cutaneous leishmaniasis cases in Q1 2026 ([WHO, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/who-situation-report-no10-middle-east-conflict-15-may-2-june-2026)).

**Malawi — Worst food insecurity in 7 years:** IFRC reports that Malawi faced its worst food insecurity crisis in seven years during the 2025/2026 season, affecting over 4 million people, with an ongoing DREF operation providing emergency food assistance ([IFRC, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/country/mwi)).

**Ukraine — Rehabilitation coordination:** WHO published minutes from the Rehabilitation Technical Working Group meeting of 22 May 2026, covering coordination among MSF, GIZ, WHO, and others on post-conflict rehabilitation services ([WHO, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/country/ukr)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Japan — Severe Tropical Storm Jangmi (active):** STS Jangmi made landfall in Wakayama Prefecture on 3 June 2026, generating Level 4 evacuation warnings for flooding and landslides across multiple prefectures in the Kanto, Tokai, and Kinki regions. Up to 300mm of rainfall was forecast in 24 hours. An emergency flood warning for the Kozagawa River was later downgraded as conditions stabilised, but the storm's track toward densely populated areas warranted significant preparedness response ([OCHA, 3 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-weekly-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-26-may-1-june-2026)).

**DRC/Uganda — Ebola Bundibugyo PHEIC (ongoing):** 321 confirmed cases and 48 deaths in DRC; 15 cases and 1 death in Uganda. No approved vaccine or treatment for the Bundibugyo strain. WHO's PHEIC declaration (the highest level of international health alert) remains in force. 10 countries classified as at risk by Africa CDC ([Multiple sources, 4 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/democratic-republic-congo-ebola-outbreak-overview-context-vulnerabilities-and-response-4-june-2026)).
