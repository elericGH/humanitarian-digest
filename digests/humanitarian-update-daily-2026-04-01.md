## What's New Today

- **Sudan — War Enters Fourth Year** — Sudan's civil war approaches the three-year mark on April 15, with the UN Security Council warning of a "horrific milestone" marked by mass atrocities, confirmed famine in Al Fasher and Kadugli, and rising sexual violence. Over 33.7 million people — roughly two-thirds of the population — are in need, and the 2026 response plan has received only 5.5% of required funding. [UN Press Release](https://press.un.org/en/2026/sc16300.doc.htm) | [WHO](https://www.who.int/news/item/09-01-2026-sudan-1000-days-of-war-deepen-the-world-s-worst-health-and-humanitarian-crisis)

- **Lebanon — Displacement Surpasses 700,000** — Since resumption of hostilities on March 2, nearly 700,000 people have been displaced across Lebanon, with more than one million — approximately one in five residents — now affected by forced movement. Evacuation orders cover around 850 km² including large parts of Beirut's southern suburbs and the entire area south of the Litani River. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167098) | [UNICEF Flash Update](https://daleel-madani.org/civil-society-directory/unicef-united-nations-childrens-fund/press-releases/unicef-lebanon-humanitarian-flash-update-no-3-escalation-hostilities-12-march-2026)

- **Gaza / OPT — WFP Warns of Ration Collapse** — The World Food Programme warns that without consistent access through Kerem Shalom — the only remaining operational crossing — food rations for 1.3 million people could be cut to just 25% of daily requirements, reversing fragile post-ceasefire gains. Medical evacuations remain on hold. [UNRWA Sitrep #212](https://www.unrwa.org/resources/reports/unrwa-situation-report-212-humanitarian-crisis-gaza-strip-and-occupied-west-bank)

- **Myanmar — Conflict in Chin State, Ongoing Displacement** — Clashes between the Myanmar military and non-state armed groups in Chin State continue to force civilians to flee, with airstrikes killing civilians across multiple regions. An estimated 3.6 million people remain internally displaced nationally, with humanitarian funding at approximately one quarter of needs. [OCHA Asia-Pacific Snapshot](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-3-16-march-2026) | [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2026/03/27/a-year-on-from-myanmars-earthquake)

- **Asia-Pacific — Middle East Spillover Threatening Food Security** — The UN warns that ongoing Middle East hostilities are compounding humanitarian conditions across import-dependent Asia-Pacific nations. Benchmark urea fertiliser prices have risen approximately 25% since conflict escalated, while fuel costs are rising for Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, and the Philippines. Over 1.7 million Nepali migrant workers in the Gulf face heightened risks. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167167)

- **Global — One Year Since USAID Dismantlement** — February 2026 marked one year since the dismantling of USAID. A Lancet study published in February projects global aid cuts could lead to 9.4 million additional deaths by 2030. Documented impacts include HIV medication interruptions in Uganda and Tanzania, closure of food kitchens in Sudan, and a 62% increase in cholera in the DRC. [CNN / Lancet](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/04/world/lancet-usaid-global-aid-cuts-intl) | [IRC](https://www.rescue.org/article/us-aid-cuts-put-millions-peril)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

The UN Humanitarian Chief has warned that Lebanon risks becoming "the next Gaza," with displacement now exceeding 700,000 and the conflict showing no sign of de-escalation. Simultaneously, Sudan is entering its fourth year of war with famine now confirmed in multiple areas — yet the 2026 response plan remains critically underfunded at under 6% of requirements.
