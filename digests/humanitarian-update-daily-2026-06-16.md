*Produced Tuesday, 16 June 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

NOAA confirmed on 11 June 2026 that El Niño has officially arrived in the Pacific, with forecasts pointing to a potentially historically intense event by late 2026. Scientists estimate a 63% probability that the November–January peak will exceed 2°C above average — a threshold only breached three times since 1877 (1982–83, 1997–98, and 2015–16). Eastern Australia is already experiencing the downstream effects: GDACS has recorded a green-alert drought spanning approximately 159,232 km² of eastern Australia, active since late February 2026 — now entering its 16th consecutive week. Agricultural drought indicators show anomalies consistent with mild-to-severe conditions across the region. ([GDACS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=5&eventtype=DR); [Bangkok Post / AFP](https://www.bangkokpost.com/world/3269440/weather-pattern-el-nino-is-here-and-could-reach-historic-intensity))

Australian humanitarian agencies ACFID and DFAT did not publish new standalone statements within the past 24 hours. No new Emergency Action Alliance activation was detected. The broader El Niño forecast carries significant implications for Australia's aid commitments in the Pacific and Southeast Asia, where drought and flood risks are escalating simultaneously.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

The Philippines continues to reel from the M7.8 earthquake that struck southern Mindanao on 9 June 2026. The death toll has risen to at least 61, with 40 more people still missing. The Philippines environment department reported on 15 June that the quake raised the seabed by as much as 2 metres in affected areas, exposing coral reefs and damaging marine ecosystems. The European Union's Copernicus Emergency Management Service activated rapid mapping (EMSR882) to support response operations. A M5.5 aftershock struck the same region on 11 June, prompting a second Copernicus activation. GDACS assessed both events at green alert level (low humanitarian impact), but ground-level reporting indicates significant infrastructure damage and community displacement. ([GDACS EQ Report](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1545533&episodeid=1711196&eventtype=EQ); [Copernicus EMS EMSR882](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR882/download))

In Myanmar, localised flooding struck Shan State in early June 2026, claiming at least one life. The event was assessed by GDACS and Copernicus EMS GloFAS on 12 June. The flooding compounds an already severe humanitarian situation: the Middle East crisis-driven spike in food and fuel prices is placing new pressure on Myanmar households, with the EU announcing an EUR 8 million contribution to WFP in May to address deepening hunger in the country. ([GDACS FL Report](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103937&episodeid=4&eventtype=FL); [WFP Myanmar](https://www.wfp.org/news/middle-east-crisis-deepens-hunger-myanmar-eu-responds-eur-8-million-urgent-relief))

Across the broader Hindu Kush-Himalayan region, a new assessment released on 11 June warned of twin threats from the 2026 monsoon season: while overall rainfall is forecast below-normal (raising drought risk), intense localised downpours are expected to cause flash flooding in mountain communities. The report, released in Kathmandu, covers communities across Afghanistan, Pakistan, India, Nepal, Bhutan, Bangladesh, China, and Myanmar. South Asia monsoon communities face "serious hazards" from the dual risks. South-East Asia is also experiencing an ongoing GDACS drought event (green alert, 20 weeks). ([ENCA/AFP](https://www.enca.com/opinion/south-asia-monsoon-risks-both-floods-and-drought-experts))

The Federated States of Micronesia continues recovery from Typhoon Sinlaku (April 2026), with a Virtual OSOCC coordination page active through UNOCHA as of 14 April 2026. ([GDACS OSOCC](https://gdacs.org))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

WFP issued a stark warning on 5 June confirming that its earlier predictions of spillover hunger from the Middle East conflict have come true: new analysis shows deep and lasting food insecurity effects in some of the world's most vulnerable countries. WFP noted the crisis is pushing the poorest families further into hunger across the region and beyond. Lebanon remains the epicentre of displacement: as of 1 June, more than one million people remain displaced — three months into the conflict — while soaring prices and lost incomes are making food unaffordable for vulnerable families. WFP has scaled up its Lebanon response amid the ongoing crisis. ([WFP Middle East](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-warning-becomes-reality-millions-middle-east-crisis-pushes-poorest-families-further-hunger); [WFP Lebanon](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-scales-response-lebanon-displacement-crisis-enters-third-month-and-rising-costs-push-food))

In the Gaza Strip and Palestine more broadly, WFP's Acting Executive Director Carl Skau, who visited in January 2026, described the hunger situation as unprecedented: "I have never seen anything like it." WFP is continuing emergency operations in the territory amid severe access and funding constraints. ([WFP Palestine story](https://www.wfp.org/stories/i-have-never-seen-anything-it-reflections-worsening-hunger-crisis))

Somalia is facing its first confirmed famine risk since the 2022 crisis. A joint UN assessment from FAO, OCHA, UNICEF, and WFP released in May confirmed that 6 million people — 31% of Somalia's population — are in IPC Phase 3 or above as of April–June 2026. Approximately 1.9 million children are in the worst malnutrition crisis globally, with 493,000 facing severe acute malnutrition. Children in this category face 12 times the normal mortality risk. On 8 June, the African Development Bank, WFP, and UNOPS launched an USD 11.8 million climate resilience initiative for drought-affected Somalis. ([WFP Somalia](https://www.wfp.org/news/somalia-african-development-bank-group-wfp-and-unops-partner-build-resilience-drought-affected); [UN Somalia warning](https://www.wfp.org/news/joint-news-release-ochafaowfpunicef-un-agencies-warn-worsening-hunger-and-malnutrition-crisis))

Sudan's situation continues to deteriorate: 19.5 million people — two in five Sudanese — are in IPC Phase 3 or above, with 825,000 children at risk of death from severe malnutrition in 2026. The African Development Bank and WFP signed the USD 87 million BOOST project on 5 June to strengthen food systems for farmers in food-supply areas of the country. In the DRC, WFP is scaling up its Ebola emergency response in eastern provinces, working with WHO and government partners to contain an outbreak before it transforms into a regional humanitarian catastrophe. Large-scale forest fires are also burning in the DRC, with one GDACS-tracked fire burning 14,533 ha from 28 May to 12 June 2026, directly affecting nearly 6,000 people. ([WFP Sudan](https://www.wfp.org/news/risk-famine-continues-stalk-families-sudan-hunger-deepens); [WFP DRC Ebola](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-scales-emergency-ebola-response-eastern-drc-contain-risk-regional-crisis); [GDACS DRC Fire](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1028832&episodeid=4&eventtype=WF))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

South Sudan's Jonglei State is experiencing a compounding crisis: WFP scaled up its emergency response in Akobo East in late May, delivering food and nutrition assistance to hundreds of thousands of people facing catastrophic hunger (IPC Phase 5). Separately, UNHCR warned on 5 June of renewed insecurity and rising displacement in Jonglei, putting thousands at grave risk. Rainy season onset, infrastructure damage, and active insecurity are all hampering operations. ([WFP South Sudan](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-scales-emergency-response-akobo-catastrophic-hunger-and-malnutrition-deepen); [UNHCR Jonglei](https://www.unhcr.org/news/briefing-notes/renewed-insecurity-and-rising-displacement-south-sudan-s-jonglei-put-thousands))

In the United States, New Hampshire experienced heavy flooding in mid-June 2026, resulting in 2 deaths and 55 displaced people. The state's Division of Homeland Security and Emergency Management has requested FEMA conduct a Preliminary Damage Assessment. Separately, as of 15 June, approximately 20 million people across central and southern Texas, Louisiana, and Mississippi are under flood watches, with tropical downpours of 2–5 inches per hour forecast through at least Tuesday 16 June. ([GDACS US Flood](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103888&episodeid=17&eventtype=FL); [NBC News](https://www.nbcnews.com/weather/storms/millions-risk-dangerous-winds-hail-potential-tornados-rcna350089))

Afghanistan faces a converging malnutrition emergency. WFP navigated a 15,000 km detour through closed borders to deliver food to Afghan schoolchildren (story published 9 June), while Deputy Executive Director Carl Skau warned in May that Afghan mothers and children are bearing the brunt of a surge in malnutrition driven by economic distress, regional conflict, mass returns from Pakistan and Iran, and deep humanitarian funding cuts. UNHCR released its Global Trends 2025 report on 11 June, confirming that 41.6 million people worldwide are refugees, with 7 in 10 refugees now living in long-term displacement — prompting the UNHCR High Commissioner to call for renewed international push for durable solutions. ([WFP Afghanistan story](https://www.wfp.org/stories/15000-km-journey-how-wfp-navigated-closed-borders-deliver-food-afghan-schoolchildren); [UNHCR Global Trends](https://www.unhcr.org/global-trends))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Philippines M7.8 Earthquake (Active):** The 9 June 2026 earthquake in southern Mindanao remains the most significant active sudden-onset event globally. At least 61 people are confirmed dead, 40 are missing, and the seabed has been raised by up to 2 metres in coastal areas, causing marine ecosystem damage. Copernicus EMS rapid mapping (EMSR882) is active with multiple area-of-interest maps published. A M5.5 aftershock followed on 11 June. ([GDACS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1545533&episodeid=1711196&eventtype=EQ); [Copernicus](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR882/download))

**United States Flooding (Active):** Flooding is active across multiple US states. New Hampshire flooding (June 13–15) caused 2 deaths and 55 displacements; FEMA assessment is pending. As of 15 June, 20 million people in Texas, Louisiana, and Mississippi are under flood watches from an active low-pressure system. ([GDACS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103888&episodeid=17&eventtype=FL))

**Honduras Flooding (Recent):** GDACS recorded flooding in Honduras on 10 June 2026. Impact assessment is ongoing. ([GDACS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103928&episodeid=2&eventtype=FL))

**El Niño Arrival (Emerging):** NOAA officially declared El Niño on 11 June 2026 with a 63% probability of exceeding historic intensity thresholds by November 2026–January 2027. FAO, IFAD, and WFP have already convened a high-level roundtable on implications for Latin America, the Caribbean, and Southeast Asia. The Eastern Australia drought (16 weeks ongoing) is an early regional indicator. ([GDACS drought](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=5&eventtype=DR); [WFP El Niño](https://www.wfp.org/news/united-nations-latin-america-and-caribbean-faces-increased-food-insecurity-risks-due-el-nino))
