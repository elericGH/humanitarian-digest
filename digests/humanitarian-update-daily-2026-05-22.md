## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

GDACS is tracking three active Green-level forest fires in northern Australia as of 21 May 2026. The first fire, covering approximately 8,954 hectares in the Kimberley region of Western Australia (near coordinates 14°S, 127°E), was reported on 21 May 2026 and is classified as a Green alert with no reported population directly affected at this stage. ([GDACS WF1028722](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028722))

A second concurrent fire is burning approximately 6,513 hectares near Darwin in the Northern Territory (approximately 13°S, 131°E), also assessed at Green alert level with no population recorded in the immediately affected area. ([GDACS WF1028723](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028723))

A third fire was updated on 21 May at 19:00 UTC, burning approximately 5,115 hectares in northern Australia with a Green impact assessment. ([GDACS WF1028724](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028724)) While May marks the beginning of the dry season in northern Australia, all three events remain at the lowest GDACS alert tier, indicating limited expected humanitarian impact at this time. Monitoring continues.
