# 🌍 Weekly Humanitarian Digest — 29 June 2026 – 6 July 2026
*Produced Monday, 6 July 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The Venezuela earthquakes dominate the Australian sector's response agenda this week. ACFID's [Venezuela Earthquake Appeal page](https://acfid.asn.au/venezuela-earthquake-appeal/) (last updated 29 June) confirms that successive 7.2 and 7.5 magnitude earthquakes struck on 24 June, with the larger quake the largest recorded in the country, and consolidates Australian member appeals. The [Emergency Action Alliance](https://emergencyaction.org.au/venezuela-earthquake/) (page updated 3 July) reports widespread devastation, families sleeping outside amid continuing aftershocks, and severely disrupted water, food, electricity and medical services. EAA is "closely monitoring" and has published member appeal links — including [ADRA Australia's disaster response appeal](https://adra.org.au/project/disaster-response/), alongside Caritas Australia, Plan International Australia, Australia for UNHCR, Act for Peace and Baptist World Aid — while flagging that a centralised EAA appeal may follow.

ACFID has also issued an [urgent appeal on the DRC/Uganda Ebola outbreak](https://acfid.asn.au/act-now-to-contain-the-ebola-outbreak/) (updated 29 June), calling on Australians to act now to contain the outbreak and protect vulnerable communities. This sits alongside ACFID's ongoing [Consolidated Appeal for the Middle East Humanitarian Crisis](https://acfid.asn.au/consolidated-appeal-middle-east-humanitarian-crisis/), which remains EAA's headline appeal as the regional conflict intensifies, and a new [ACFID analysis of the US–Iran conflict](https://acfid.asn.au/acfid-analysis-the-us-iran-conflict/) examining implications for the sector.

Domestically, the Pacific Disaster Center is carrying an active [riverine flood warning west of Maryborough, Victoria](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=479927) — one to watch for Australian responders as the weather system develops.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

The most significant regional hazard is Super Typhoon Bavi. GDACS tracks [BAVI-26 at sustained winds of 278 km/h as of 5 July](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=18&eventtype=TC), and the Pacific Disaster Center has an active [warning for Super Typhoon Bavi](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=476585) with associated [flash flooding on Saipan and Tinian](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=479937) and [coastal flooding across Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=479631). This follows Typhoon Sinlaku's ongoing recovery in the Federated States of Micronesia ([ReliefWeb disaster page](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/tc-2026-000050-fsm)), underscoring a punishing typhoon season for the north Pacific. Two further storms, [MAYSAK-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001281&episodeid=13&eventtype=TC) and [DOUGLAS-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001280&episodeid=9&eventtype=TC), are also being tracked.

Seismic activity across the region remained elevated through the week: GDACS logged a [M6.2 in Indonesia (3 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549862&episodeid=1715971&eventtype=EQ), a [M6.1 (3 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549877&episodeid=1715989&eventtype=EQ) and [M6.0 (1 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549565&episodeid=1715647&eventtype=EQ) in Japan, a [M5.7 near Tonga (1 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549627&episodeid=1715727&eventtype=EQ) and a [M5.6 near Fiji (1 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549555&episodeid=1715635&eventtype=EQ). Response continues to the [Philippines earthquake and tsunami of June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/eq-2026-000083-phl), still classed as ongoing, with new [flooding in the Philippines reported 1 July](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103942&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL) and monsoon flooding also hitting [India (2 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103972&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL), [China (1 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103933&episodeid=12&eventtype=FL), [Japan (3 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103993&episodeid=2&eventtype=FL) and [Pakistan (29 June)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1104002&episodeid=1&eventtype=FL). PDC also holds an active [warning for Ambae volcano in Vanuatu](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=478521).

Regional tensions carry clear humanitarian undertones. [The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com) reports Thailand–Cambodia fighting has entered its second week with Bangkok spurning outside mediation, and that Philippine fishermen were injured in a skirmish with the Chinese Coast Guard near Sabina Shoal in the South China Sea. In Myanmar, the ICRC published a [FAQ on its work in Myanmar (30 June)](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/FAQ-icrc-work-myanmar) as it continues earthquake recovery and conflict response, while EAA's [Myanmar Earthquake appeal](https://emergencyaction.org.au/appeal/myanmar-earthquake/) remains open with a six-month report published.

East Asia Forum analysis this week highlights the region's exposure to the Iran war: EAF editors argue [keeping the Strait of Hormuz open is a priority for Asia (29 June)](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/06/29/keeping-the-strait-of-hormuz-open-and-free-to-shipping-is-a-priority-for-asia/), and a companion piece warns [Iran's claim over the Strait must not become a precedent (28 June)](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/06/28/irans-strait-of-hormuz-claim-must-not-become-a-precedent/), with proposed transit fees and security clearances threatening energy flows on which Asia-Pacific economies depend. A further piece notes [Southeast Asia hedging as trust in Washington wanes (1 July)](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/07/01/southeast-asia-hedges-as-trust-in-washington-wanes/).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

The Iran crisis reached a new inflection point. Al Jazeera's [live coverage (5 July)](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/5/iran-war-live-huge-crowds-mourn-khamenei-trump-vows-calm-during-funeral) reports huge crowds mourning Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei — assassinated on 28 February — during a multi-day state funeral running 4–9 July, even as Israel struck southern Lebanon. Analysts examine [the religious and political messaging of the funeral](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/5/what-is-the-religious-and-political-messaging-behind-khameneis-funeral) and the succession question. The ICRC has published a [FAQ on its work in Iran](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/FAQ-icrc-work-iran), and in Lebanon reports it is [supporting life-saving medical services during hostilities](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/lebanon-icrc-supporting-life-saving-medical-services-during-hostilities).

In Gaza, conditions continue to deteriorate as summer sets in. NRC and the Shelter Cluster report that [Israel's shelter restrictions are forcing nearly a million people — about 170,000 households — to endure summer heat in tents](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/gaza-israels-shelter-restrictions-force-nearly-million-people-endure-summer-heat-tents-nrc), with another 5,000 households sleeping outdoors. The ICRC's regional director warned on 30 June that [the suffering in Gaza is far from over](https://www.icrc.org/en/statement/suffering-gaza-far-over-icrc-regional-director), and the organisation flags a [deepening water crisis](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/gaza-water-every-drop-counts-summer-takes-hold). Al Jazeera reports a [Palestinian baby died in the West Bank after being blocked from urgent medical care (5 July)](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/5/palestinian-baby-dies-in-west-bank-after-israel-blocks-urgent-medical-care) and that the [jailed director of Gaza's Kamal Adwan hospital is in life-threatening condition (5 July)](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/5/jailed-gaza-hospital-chief-in-life-threatening-condition-rights-group-says). In Yemen, [Houthi rebels killed 16 government troops in Hodeidah in the fiercest clashes in years (5 July)](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/5/at-least-15-yemeni-government-troops-killed-in-hodeidah-fighting).

The DRC/Uganda Ebola outbreak remains a top-tier concern. The IRC warns that [women and girls face a double threat in the outbreak](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/women-and-girls-face-double-threat-ebola-outbreak-response-must-address-both-irc) as primary caretakers whose water-collection burdens raise exposure to violence, and WFP explains [how the outbreak is driving hunger across the region](https://www.wfp.org/stories/2026-ebola-outbreak-key-facts-symptoms-and-why-its-driving-hunger-africa). The [ReliefWeb disaster page](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/ep-2026-000071-cod) tracks the outbreak as ongoing.

Food insecurity indicators worsened across the continent. The FAO–WFP [Hunger Hotspots outlook for June–November 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/hunger-hotspots-fao-wfp-early-warnings-acute-food-insecurity-june-november-2026-outlook) warns of 13 hotspots at significant risk, with Sudan, South Sudan, Yemen and Palestine of highest concern and northeast Nigeria and Somalia joining the top tier. OCHA reports [more than 24 million people in the Sahel urgently need aid](https://reliefweb.int/report/mali/24-million-people-sahel-urgently-need-aid-and-world-needs-do-more) as violence spills into coastal West Africa. In Somalia, MSF says [malnutrition admissions are overwhelming Baidoa's Bay Regional Hospital](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/malnutrition-admissions-are-overwhelming-baidoa-hospital-somalia), which admitted 1,400 patients to its therapeutic feeding centre between March and May despite expanding from 50 to 125 beds. In Sudan, the ICRC reports [42,000 people assisted with food in Al Obeid (2 July)](https://www.icrc.org/en/news-release/sudan-42000-people-assisted-food-al-obeid), and WFP warns [conflict and shrinking assistance are driving northern Nigeria's hunger to crisis levels](https://www.wfp.org/news/conflict-and-shrinking-humanitarian-assistance-drives-northern-nigeria-hunger-crisis-levels).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

Venezuela's earthquake response scaled up sharply this week. UNHCR is [scaling up its response amid growing post-earthquake vulnerabilities](https://www.unhcr.org/news/briefing-notes/unhcr-scales-response-venezuela-amid-growing-post-earthquake-vulnerabilities), co-leading the emergency shelter response in a country from which almost 7 million people have already fled; its earlier briefing described [rushing support after the deadly earthquakes (26 June)](https://www.unhcr.org/news/briefing-notes/unhcr-rushing-support-venezuela-after-deadly-earthquakes). WFP is [ramping up food and other assistance](https://www.wfp.org/stories/wfp-ramps-food-and-other-assistance-venezuela-earthquake-response), and the ICRC has [mobilised a rapid response in support of the Venezuelan Red Cross (29 June)](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/venezuela-earthquake-icrc-mobilizes-rapid-response-support-venezuelan-red-cross). The EC/ECHO [daily map of the M7.5 event (2 July)](https://erccportal.jrc.ec.europa.eu/ECHOProducts//Maps#/maps/5714) and Copernicus rapid mapping continue to inform the response.

In Ukraine, the ICRC said on 3 July that [intensifying hostilities are claiming dozens of civilian lives and deepening trauma](https://www.icrc.org/en/statement/ukraine-intensifying-hostilities-claim-dozens-lives-deepen-trauma-civilians). Al Jazeera reports [Russia's advance has collapsed, with a claimed 40,000 Russian troops killed in June (3 July)](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/3/russian-advance-collapses-in-ukraine-as-anxiety-rises-in-moscow) and that [Moscow says Kyiv rejected a local ceasefire around Kostiantynivka (5 July)](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/5/russia-says-ukraine-rejects-local-ceasefire-in-dispute-over-kostiantynivka), leaving civilians in the contested pocket exposed.

Global-level reporting rounded out a sobering week. FAO and WFP launched a [joint US$202 million appeal to protect 8.8 million people from El Niño-driven extreme weather](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/bracing-el-nino-fao-and-wfp-launch-joint-appeal-protect-88-million-people-extreme-weather-events). The [2026 Global Peace Index](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/global-peace-index-2026) records a twelfth consecutive year of deteriorating global peacefulness, and NRC's [most neglected displacement crises report](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/worlds-most-neglected-displacement-crises-2025-10th-edition) places DR Congo on the list for a tenth straight year, alongside Sudan, Colombia, Yemen and Afghanistan.

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

Super Typhoon Bavi is the week's most significant new hazard: [278 km/h sustained winds per GDACS (5 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=18&eventtype=TC), with [flash flooding on Saipan and Tinian](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=479937) and [coastal flooding across Guam and the Northern Marianas](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=479631) already reported. The Venezuela M7.2/7.5 earthquake aftermath (24 June) remains an active emergency with [aftershocks and mass displacement](https://emergencyaction.org.au/venezuela-earthquake/). Notable earthquakes this week included [Indonesia M6.2 (3 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549862&episodeid=1715971&eventtype=EQ), [Japan M6.1 (3 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549877&episodeid=1715989&eventtype=EQ), [Tonga M5.7 (1 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549627&episodeid=1715727&eventtype=EQ) and [Fiji M5.6 (1 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549555&episodeid=1715635&eventtype=EQ). Monsoon and seasonal flooding hit [Japan](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103993&episodeid=2&eventtype=FL), [India](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103972&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL), [China](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103933&episodeid=12&eventtype=FL), [the Philippines](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103942&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL), [Pakistan](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1104002&episodeid=1&eventtype=FL) and [Ghana](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103954&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL). A [forest fire in Portugal reached 19,659 hectares (5 July)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1029260&episodeid=10&eventtype=WF).
