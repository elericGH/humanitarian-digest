# 🌍 Weekly Humanitarian Digest — March 24–30, 2026
*Produced 30 March 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

**🇦🇺 ACFID Testifies at Conflict Prevention Inquiry** — On 26 March, the Australian Council for International Development (ACFID) gave evidence at a parliamentary public hearing for the Joint Standing Committee on Foreign Affairs, Defence and Trade's [Inquiry into the role of Australia's international development program in preventing conflict](https://www.miragenews.com/acfid-to-testify-at-conflict-prevention-inquiry-1643453/). ACFID representatives were joined by Conciliation Resources and Action Aid Australia, highlighting on-the-ground peacebuilding experience across the region.

**🇦🇺 EAA Launches Middle East Joint Appeal** — Australia's Emergency Action Alliance (EAA) launched a joint Middle East Appeal on 17 March 2026, mobilising 15 charities — including Save the Children Australia, Plan International Australia, Oxfam Australia and CARE Australia — to raise funds for Lebanon, Syria, Gaza and the West Bank. The appeal responds to the rapidly escalating humanitarian crisis following military action against Iran. More at [emergencyaction.org.au](https://emergencyaction.org.au/).

**🇦🇺 Australia Announces $5 Million for Lebanon** — Foreign Minister [Penny Wong announced an additional AUD $5 million](https://www.foreignminister.gov.au/minister/penny-wong/media-release/further-humanitarian-assistance-lebanon) in lifesaving assistance for civilians impacted by the ongoing Lebanon conflict, expressing grave concern over the displacement of more than one million people.

**🇦🇺 Australia Commits AUD $16.5 Million for Rohingya** — Australia pledged multi-year funding (2026–2028) in partnership with UNFPA to address the [Rohingya humanitarian crisis in Bangladesh](https://www.bnionline.net/en/news/australias-aud-165-million-commitment-signals-lifeline-amid-deepening-rohingya-humanitarian), supporting nearly 1.2 million refugees.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Myanmar: Civilian Casualties Mount Amid Ongoing Civil War** — Conflict continued to ravage Myanmar this week, with [OCHA's 9 March Humanitarian Update](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/myanmar-humanitarian-update-no51-9-march-2026) documenting aerial drone strikes on a monastery sheltering displaced people, killing at least 11 civilians in Mandalay and Sagaing, and a further 19 civilians killed in southeastern Myanmar between 9–11 March. More than 16.2 million people — over 45% of Myanmar's population — are in need of lifesaving assistance, and WFP warns over 12 million face acute hunger in 2026. The [Asia and Pacific Regional Humanitarian Snapshot (3–16 March)](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-3-16-march-2026) underscores Myanmar as the region's deepest ongoing crisis.

**Rohingya in Bangladesh: Japan Pledges Support** — Japan pledged USD $2.6 million on 5 March 2026 for shelter materials, healthcare and clean cooking sets for Rohingya refugees in Bangladesh, where nearly 1.2 million people remain in Cox's Bazar — the world's largest refugee camp. Australia's own AUD $16.5 million UNFPA commitment reinforces international momentum. [UNHCR Rohingya Situation](https://data.unhcr.org/en/situations/myanmar).

**Pacific Islands Issue Advisories Amid Middle East Escalation** — Fiji, Solomon Islands and Vanuatu governments issued travel advisories for their nationals in the Middle East following US-Israeli airstrikes on Iran in early March, reflecting the broad regional ripple effects of the conflict. No new major natural disaster events were reported in the Pacific this week, though the region continues to face heightened climate and disaster risk affecting 580 million children and adolescents.

**Afghanistan: Underfunded Crisis Worsens** — Fighting along the Afghanistan-Pakistan border displaced nearly 66,000 people across eastern and southeastern provinces and damaged a 20-bed emergency hospital in Torkham, forcing WFP to suspend food assistance for around 160,000 people. The [2026 Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-humanitarian-needs-and-response-plan-2026-summary) is only 11% funded ($181 million of $1.7 billion needed). [Vatican News](https://www.vaticannews.va/en/world/news/2026-03/afghanistan-new-humanitarian-strain-crises-trigger-displacement.html).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Lebanon: Escalating War Creates Mass Displacement** — Since 2 March 2026, an [ongoing war between Israel and Hezbollah](https://www.anera.org/blog/lebanon-war-escalation-2026-what-happened-and-why-thousands-are-fleeing/) has killed more than 1,000 people and displaced nearly one million — 20% of Lebanon's population. More than 130,000 people have fled into Syria and UNICEF reports at least 91 children killed and 275 wounded as of 12 March. The UN Humanitarian Chief warned of fears that ["Lebanon could be the next Gaza."](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/03/18/fear-lebanon-could-be-the-next-gaza-un-humanitarian-chief-tells-euronews)

**Gaza: Ceasefire Fragile, Aid Access Severely Restricted** — Since October 2023, approximately 72,253 Palestinians have been killed and 171,912 injured in Gaza. The [OCHA Humanitarian Situation Report of 27 March 2026](https://www.ochaopt.org/content/humanitarian-situation-report-27-march-2026) notes that 46% of essential medicines are out of stock, all crossings except Kerem Shalom remain closed, and 1,697 Palestinians have been displaced in the West Bank already in 2026 — surpassing all of 2025. Settler attacks have reached more than six incidents per day.

**Sudan: Crisis Spills Across Borders** — The [IFRC warns](https://www.ifrc.org/press-release/ifrc-warns-sudan-crisis-spills-across-borders-humanitarian-crisis-worsens) that Sudan's conflict — now accounting for nearly 10% of global humanitarian needs — is overwhelming neighbouring countries. Almost 4.5 million Sudanese have now fled abroad to Chad, Egypt, Ethiopia, South Sudan and Uganda, while 9.5 million are internally displaced. The 2026 Sudan Regional Refugee Response Plan targets 5.9 million people across seven countries. Funding remains critically insufficient.

**DRC: M23 Shifts, Drone Strikes on Aid Workers** — Despite some M23 troop withdrawals near Lubero (23–24 March), [violence continues to intensify](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167204) across eastern DRC. 173 cases of conflict-related sexual violence were confirmed since December 2025. The UK raised alarm at the UN Security Council over [increasing drone strikes on civilians and humanitarian workers](https://www.globalsecurity.org/military/library/news/2026/03/mil-260326-ukfcdo01.htm). A Geneva Call MoU signed 27 March with AFC/M23 aims to reinforce humanitarian norms. More than 7 million people are internally displaced.

**Middle East War Threatens Regional Food Security** — The UN warns the Middle East war could push [an additional 45 million people into acute hunger](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167147) by price rises if conflict continues through June. Eastern Africa already has 36 million people at crisis food insecurity levels, compounded by drought in Somalia and La Niña floods in Southern Africa.

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Ukraine: Four Years On, Women Face Triple Crisis** — Four years after Russia's full-scale invasion, [UN Women reports](https://www.unwomen.org/en/news-stories/press-release/2026/02/trapped-by-war-energy-attacks-and-funding-cuts-women-and-girls-in-ukraine-face-a-deadly-triple-crisis) over 5,000 women and girls killed, 14,000 injured, with 2025 being the deadliest year. Women-led organisations face losing USD $52.9 million due to funding cuts, compounding the impact of ongoing energy and infrastructure attacks across Ukraine.

**Global Aid System: One Year Since USAID Dismantling** — One year on from USAID's dismantling, [a GBH report](https://www.wgbh.org/news/local/2026-03-09/a-year-after-usaid-cuts-local-groups-say-impact-on-humanitarian-work-has-been-devastating) documents devastating impacts on local humanitarian organisations worldwide. A Lancet study projects global aid cuts could cause [9.4 million additional deaths by 2030](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/04/world/lancet-usaid-global-aid-cuts-intl). The UK, Germany, Canada and France have also cut aid, with total global ODA projected to fall 17% from 2024 (OECD). Two-thirds of Americans — including nearly half of Republicans — oppose the cuts.
