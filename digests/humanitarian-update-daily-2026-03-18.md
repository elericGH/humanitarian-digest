## What's New Today

- **Middle East — Regional Spillover** — UN News' 17 March live briefing confirms the crisis is now rippling across the broader region: airspace closures are disrupting humanitarian flight rotations, gas flows into Syria have been interrupted, and UN humanitarian flights into Yemen are now grounded. OHCHR issued a statement saying the crisis "plays out worst fears" and that negotiations are "the only way out." Nearly 22 million people across the region are estimated to need humanitarian support. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167146) | [OHCHR](https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-briefing-notes/2026/03/middle-east-crisis-plays-out-worst-fears-talks-only-way-out)

- **DRC — Djugu Territory Escalation** — Armed group clashes in Ituri Province's Djugu Territory have killed at least 21 people since the start of March, with 10 civilians killed in a single attack near Bule locality. The Plaine Savo displacement site has nearly doubled in two months and now hosts 136,000 people; aid workers are struggling to reach those affected. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167125)

- **Sudan — Drone Strikes Continue** — Deadly drone attacks have been reported in North Darfur State this week, with civilians killed and injured. The city of Dilling (South Kordofan) remains isolated with access routes still cut off, pushing conditions to crisis levels. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167125)

- **Yemen — UN Operations Disrupted** — Middle East airspace closures have grounded UN humanitarian flights into Yemen, compounding the already severe funding crisis. Over 23 million people — more than half the country's population — are estimated to need humanitarian assistance in 2026, with the 2025 response funded at less than 25 per cent. [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/opinions/2026/3/14/hunger-is-looming-over-yemen-urgent-action-is-needed)

- **Myanmar — Conflict Reaches New High** — February 2026 was the deadliest month for clashes between anti-coup resistance groups since the 2021 military takeover, per ACLED's latest Asia-Pacific overview. Combined with the aftermath of the March 2025 earthquake, 2.6 million people remain prioritised for life-saving aid in 2026. [ACLED](https://acleddata.com/update/asia-pacific-overview-march-2026)

- **Papua New Guinea** — Intercommunal violence continues to flare, with 84,000 people internally displaced as of January 2026. New clashes reported in highland areas suggest that figure has grown further. [OCHA Asia-Pacific](https://www.unocha.org/asia-and-pacific)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

The Middle East regional war has now grounded UN humanitarian flight operations into Yemen — cutting off one of the few remaining supply corridors into a country already facing famine conditions. OHCHR warns the crisis is playing out "worst fears," with military options exhausted and ceasefire negotiations described as "the only way out." Simultaneously, eastern DRC's Ituri Province is experiencing a rapid escalation that has doubled displacement figures to 136,000 at a single site, with minimal international attention or funding.
