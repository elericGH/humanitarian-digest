## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Papua New Guinea — Cyclone Maila aftermath:** As of 8 May 2026, assessments across 230 wards in 28 local-level governments in five affected provinces confirm over 119,000 people affected by Severe Tropical Cyclone Maila, with more than 107,000 in need of humanitarian assistance and 49 reported fatalities. Priority needs are food, shelter, NFIs, WASH, and health services. Logistics remain severely constrained by damaged infrastructure and difficult sea conditions, with aid delivery relying on air and maritime transport. The National Disaster Centre is transitioning towards sub-nationally led coordination ([ReliefWeb / NDC, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/papua-new-guinea-severe-tropical-cyclone-maila-disaster-management-team-situation-report-no-07-08-may-2026)).

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano activity:** The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS) continued its 24-hour monitoring of Mayon Volcano on 11 May 2026, with observations issued at midnight. Mayon's current eruption episode (classified as a May 2026 event on ReliefWeb) is being tracked as an ongoing alert-level hazard for communities in Albay province ([ReliefWeb / PHIVOLCS, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/vo-2026-000065-phl)).

**ASEAN region — 45 disaster events in Week 19:** The ASEAN Coordinating Centre for Humanitarian Assistance (AHA Centre) recorded 45 disaster events across the region in the week of 4–10 May 2026, spanning floods, landslides, storms, and volcanic activity in Cambodia, Indonesia, Malaysia, the Philippines, Thailand, and Viet Nam. In the Philippines, an M6.0 earthquake was also recorded during the period. Persistent flooding continued in Malaysia's Perak and Kedah states ([ReliefWeb / AHA Centre, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/indonesia/asean-weekly-disaster-update-week-19-4-10-may-2026)).

**Bangladesh — Measles deaths exceed 400:** More than 400 people have died from confirmed or suspected measles in Bangladesh in the past two months, with government data estimating approximately 55,000 cases. Nine in ten cases are in children aged one to fourteen, with three-quarters in children under five. In Cox's Bazar — home to roughly one million Rohingya refugees — four suspected deaths and nearly 500 cases have been recorded. Save the Children is urging families to join an emergency vaccination campaign, supported by WHO and health authorities ([ReliefWeb / Save the Children, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/bangladesh-families-urged-vaccinate-children-number-suspected-measles-deaths-exceeds-400)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Gaza — One in five families surviving on one meal per day:** WFP's Palestine Emergency Response Situation Report #88 (covering 1–30 April 2026) reports that humanitarian and commercial inflows into Gaza remain well below population needs. One in five families is surviving on only one meal per day. While the reopening of the Zikim crossing in northern Gaza has improved goods flow, congestion and access restrictions continue to constrain cargo volumes. WFP is scaling up cash transfers and private-sector support including through bakeries and retailers, though fuel shortages are stifling commercial recovery ([ReliefWeb / WFP, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/wfp-palestine-emergency-response-external-situation-report-88-11-may-2026-reporting-period-1-30-april-2026)).

**Middle East — Strait of Hormuz crisis deepens regional food insecurity:** WFP's Middle East Regional Escalation Emergency Response Situation Report #10 (10 May 2026) warns that the ongoing Strait of Hormuz crisis is disrupting food systems, trade routes, and humanitarian access across the region. Rising fuel and food prices are reinforcing each other and constraining vulnerable households' access to basic needs. WFP is supporting over 33,000 refugees in Iran across 20 settlements; in Lebanon, 650,000 displaced people have received food and cash assistance to date ([ReliefWeb / WFP, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/wfp-middle-east-regional-escalation-emergency-response-external-situation-report-10-10-may-2026)).

**Somalia & Ethiopia — Drought and displacement push millions to the brink:** Four consecutive failed rainy seasons have driven a severe drought emergency across Somalia, with more than 6.5 million people — approximately one in four Somalis — now facing high levels of acute food insecurity under IPC classifications. More than two million people have been displaced. Somalia's federal government declared a national drought emergency in November 2025. As humanitarian funding collapses, the last remaining lifelines are at risk of being cut ([ReliefWeb, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/drought-and-displacement-push-millions-brink-somalia-and-ethiopias-somali-region)).

**Sudan — Relief supplies reach displaced families in Khartoum State:** Qatar Charity delivered food, shelter materials, and non-food items to approximately 400 displaced families from El Fasher and Greater Darfur who are now sheltering in Omdurman, Khartoum State. The intervention was coordinated through Sudan's Commission for Voluntary and Humanitarian Work to ensure aid reached the most vulnerable, including those fleeing ongoing conflict in Darfur ([ReliefWeb / Qatar Charity, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/qatar-charity-distributes-new-relief-supplies-displaced-families-sudan-enar)).

**DRC and West & Central Africa — Critical funding gap:** The 2026 Regional Funding Status overview for West and Central Africa (as of 11 May 2026) highlights a significant funding shortfall for humanitarian operations across the DRC and the wider region, with funding requirements for the regional humanitarian response plan substantially under-met at this stage of the year ([ReliefWeb, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/west-and-central-africa-2026-regional-funding-status-general-overview-11-may-2026)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Haiti — Ongoing gang violence displaces thousands:** The Latin America & Caribbean Weekly Situation Update (11 May 2026) reports that armed attacks in Artibonite Department on 2 May displaced approximately 4,400 people. Regional flooding risk is also elevated, with 1.5 million people exposed to flooding in Colombia during the April–June rainy season across 276 municipalities, with peak risk expected in May and June ([ReliefWeb / OCHA ROLAC, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/haiti/latin-america-caribbean-weekly-situation-update-11-may-2026)).

**Ukraine — Medical support continues through record cold winter:** AMDA's Emergency Relief Report #55 (11 May 2026) details ongoing medical operations through partner organisation Dynasty Medical Center in Kharkiv, eastern Ukraine. The past winter was one of the coldest on record; the centre provided medical services and warming spaces for civilians as central heating failed due to power outages from air raids. The centre also connected displaced people to heated apartments where homes were damaged ([ReliefWeb / AMDA, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/amda-emergency-relief-55-crisis-ukraine-11-may-2026)).

**Syria — Health conditions of returnees under scrutiny:** A new report on health conditions and experiences of returnees in Syria documents the significant medical and psychosocial challenges facing those returning home, amid infrastructure destruction and incomplete healthcare system recovery ([ReliefWeb, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/syrian-arab-republic/diagnosing-return-health-conditions-and-experiences-returnees-syria-enar)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano (ongoing):** Mayon Volcano in Albay province remains in an active eruption phase as of 11 May 2026. PHIVOLCS issued its standard 24-hour observation bulletin and the event is listed as an ongoing disaster on ReliefWeb, indicating continued alert status for surrounding communities ([ReliefWeb disaster page](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/vo-2026-000065-phl)).

**Papua New Guinea — Post-cyclone emergency (Cyclone Maila):** While Cyclone Maila has passed, its aftermath constitutes an active sudden-onset emergency with 119,000 people affected, 49 fatalities, and widespread infrastructure damage across five provinces as of 8 May 2026. Air and sea access remain constrained ([ReliefWeb / NDC, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/papua-new-guinea-severe-tropical-cyclone-maila-disaster-management-team-situation-report-no-07-08-may-2026)).

**ASEAN region — Active multi-hazard environment:** Forty-five discrete disaster events — including floods, landslides, storms, and volcanic activity — were recorded across ASEAN member states in the week of 4–10 May 2026, with several events still active as of the reporting date ([ReliefWeb / AHA Centre, 11 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/indonesia/asean-weekly-disaster-update-week-19-4-10-may-2026)).
