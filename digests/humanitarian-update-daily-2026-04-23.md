## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**New Zealand — Major Flooding and Landslides (22 Apr 2026).** The North Island of New Zealand has been hit by intense rain causing severe floods and landslides. The hardest-hit areas are the Manawatu-Whanganui region and Wellington, both under a local state of emergency. One person has died in the Karori suburb of western Wellington, many residents have been evacuated, and multiple cars have been submerged or swept away. Road closures from flash floods and landslides are widespread and schools across the region are closed. According to the National Emergency Management Agency, the state of emergency declared on 20 April continues for Ohura Ward in Manawatu-Whanganui as well as Wellington City and Wairarapa. Moderate to locally heavy rain and hail are forecast for the next 48 hours across most of the North Island. ([GDACS/ECHO, 22 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org))

**Typhoon SINLAKU-26 — Federated States of Micronesia (Active).** Tropical Cyclone SINLAKU-26, with maximum wind speeds of 287 km/h, struck the Federated States of Micronesia in mid-April and triggered a Copernicus Emergency Management Service activation (EMSR872). A Virtual OSOCC (On-Site Operations Coordination Centre) was opened on 14 April 2026 for "Federated States of Micronesia — Typhoon Sinlaku, April 2026." Copernicus satellite mapping of the affected areas of Micronesia is ongoing and imagery products have been released. This is one of the most intense Pacific cyclone events of the 2026 season. ([GDACS, Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001270&episodeid=44&eventtype=TC); [Copernicus EMS EMSR872](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR872/download))

**Japan — M7.4 Earthquake (20 Apr 2026).** A magnitude 7.4 earthquake struck Japan on 20 April at 07:53 UTC. Additional M5.6 earthquakes followed on 20 and 21 April. The seismic sequence is being monitored across the region. A separate M5.5 earthquake struck Timor-Leste on 21 April, and a M5.8 hit the Solomon Islands on 18 April, reflecting continued heightened seismic activity across the Pacific ring of fire. ([GDACS](https://gdacs.org))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Iran — US-Israel War, Ceasefire Fragile (23 Apr 2026).** Iranian forces have seized two vessels in the Strait of Hormuz, and Iran's Parliament Speaker Mohammad Bagher Ghalibaf accused the United States of using a port blockade to stall diplomatic talks. The fragile ceasefire established in late March remains in place but negotiations are described as stalled. Al Jazeera reports a preliminary death toll of approximately 3,375 people killed in Iran and 2,294 in Lebanon since the conflict began. On 22 April, Israeli strikes on Lebanon killed at least five people including a Lebanese journalist from the newspaper Al-Akhbar. ([Al Jazeera live updates, 23 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/4/23/iran-war-live-israel-kills-lebanese-journalist-tehran-us-talks-stalled))

**Sudan — Three Years of War, Record Hunger (14–22 Apr 2026).** April 2026 marks three years since Sudan's devastating civil war began. The WFP warned on 14 April that Sudan's people are being "abandoned and hungry," with millions without access to food, shelter or sanitation. Over one million Sudanese refugees in Chad face life-threatening cuts to food, water and healthcare due to funding shortfalls. The ICRC's President stated that "patterns of warfare that disregard human life and dignity cannot continue." In a significant development, the WFP announced on 9 April that its Sudan Country Office is returning to the capital Khartoum after three years — its former premises were looted and destroyed at the start of the war. ([WFP, 14 Apr 2026](https://www.wfp.org/news/three-years-war-sudans-people-abandoned-and-hungry); [ICRC, 15 Apr 2026](https://www.icrc.org/en/statement/icrc-president-patterns-warfare-disregard-human-life-and-dignity-cannot-continue-sudan))

**Lebanon — Displacement and Infrastructure Under Pressure (Apr 2026).** The ICRC has published new testimonies from Lebanese families displaced multiple times during the ongoing conflict, describing total loss of homes and community. ICRC is actively supporting water treatment facilities and life-saving medical services as Israeli hostilities continue to disrupt essential infrastructure. A Lebanese Red Cross volunteer was killed in March, prompting a joint ICRC-IFRC statement of outrage over the killing of humanitarian workers. ([ICRC, Apr 2026](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/lebanon-icrc-supporting-life-saving-medical-services-during-hostilities))

**Afghanistan — Humanitarian Assistance in Nuristan Province (21 Apr 2026).** The ICRC delivered humanitarian assistance to conflict-affected communities in Nuristan Province, Afghanistan. The operation reflects ongoing insecurity across the country following the Pakistan-Afghanistan conflict earlier in 2026. WFP is also maintaining a large-scale emergency response for communities in Afghanistan impacted by the conflict, adapting to violence and regional shipping disruptions caused by the Middle East crisis. ([ICRC, 21 Apr 2026](https://www.icrc.org/en/news-release/afghanistan-humanitarian-assistance-conflict-affected-communities-nuristan-province); [WFP, Mar 2026](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-delivers-lifesaving-assistance-families-caught-conflict-afghanistan))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Haiti — Over Half of Population Faces Acute Food Insecurity (22 Apr 2026).** New data shows more than half of all Haitians continue to face acute food insecurity. The United States announced a new contribution to WFP on 22 April to strengthen emergency food and nutrition assistance, including fortified and ready-to-use foods to prevent malnutrition among the most vulnerable. WFP had earlier urgently called for international support to protect "fragile gains" in the fight against hunger. ([WFP, 22 Apr 2026](https://www.wfp.org/news/new-contribution-united-states-strengthens-wfps-emergency-response-haiti))

**Brazil — Severe Flooding in Northern and Southern States (20–22 Apr 2026).** Since 20 April, heavy rainfall has battered northern Brazil — especially the city of Belém in the state of Pará — as well as the southern state of São Paulo. A state of emergency has been declared in Belém, where many homes are flooded and road transport is possible only by boat. In Sorocaba, around 600 families have been affected; in Ibiúna, nearly 100 houses were hit, displacing more than 600 people. Further moderate to heavy rain is forecast for northern Brazil. ([GDACS/ECHO, 22 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org))

**Ecuador — Ongoing Rainy Season Casualties (22 Apr 2026).** Heavy rainfall has affected most of Ecuador since early January, causing flooding and landslides. As of 22 April, the death toll has reached 17 fatalities, with 103,370 people affected, 270 houses destroyed and 30,933 houses damaged. The most affected provinces are Guayas, Los Ríos, Esmeraldas and El Oro. Two people remain missing in Pichincha province following severe weather on 16 April. ([GDACS/ECHO, 22 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org))

**Guatemala — Santiaguito Volcano Eruption (22 Apr 2026).** A volcanic eruption at Santiaguito Volcano in Quetzaltenango Department is generating one to two weak to moderate explosions per hour, with gas and ash columns rising over 800 metres above the crater rim, accompanied by pyroclastic flows and ejected rocks. Several people have been evacuated from the area around the crater. ([GDACS/ECHO, 22 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org))
