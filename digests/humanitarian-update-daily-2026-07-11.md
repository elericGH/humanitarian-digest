## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Fiji's escalating HIV outbreak is primarily a syringe-access crisis, argues a new analysis from ANU's Development Policy Centre: 48% of Fijians starting HIV treatment in 2024 named injecting drug use as their exposure route, yet pharmacies refuse to sell sterile syringes without prescription despite no legal basis. The piece, drawing on the WHO/UNDP rapid assessment led by UNSW's Kirby Institute, urges making syringes available over the counter as the fastest intervention ([Devpolicy Blog, 11 Jul 2026](https://devpolicy.org/fijis-hiv-crisis-is-a-syringe-crisis-and-the-fix-is-sitting-on-a-pharmacy-shelf-20260711/)).

New research published by the Development Policy Centre examines how polygyny undermines women's voice and agency in Papua New Guinea, Australia's largest aid recipient, with implications for gender-and-development programming in the country ([Devpolicy Blog, 10 Jul 2026](https://devpolicy.org/polygyny-weakens-female-voices-in-png-20260710/)).

The Australian Institute of International Affairs' weekly foreign affairs roundup (10 Jul) captures a consequential week for Australia's Pacific engagement: the PM's visit to Fiji and Solomon Islands with the signing of the Vuvale Union and Ocean of Peace Alliance treaties and launch of the Pacific Australia Skills program, entry into force of the PNG–Australia "Pukpuk" Mutual Defence Treaty, and the Australia–India Leaders' Summit in Melbourne ([AIIA Australian Outlook, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.internationalaffairs.org.au/australianoutlook/10-july-2026-the-week-in-australian-foreign-affairs/)).

No new humanitarian releases from ACFID, Emergency Action Alliance, or DFAT in the last 24 hours; the most recent were the Venezuela earthquake appeals and DFAT's $2 million Venezuela assistance package (29 Jun–3 Jul).

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Landslides triggered by Typhoon Bavi (local name Inday) and the enhanced southwest monsoon have killed at least 15 people in the southern Philippines: 10 in a farming village in Malapatan, Sarangani, and at least 5 in Calanogas, Lanao del Sur. Nearly 50,000 people are affected across four regions, with 1,777 families in 22 evacuation centres and PAGASA warning of continued widespread flooding including Metro Manila ([Al Jazeera, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/10/philippines-landslides-kill-15-as-typhoon-bavi-threatens-region)).

In the Marianas, the US declared a public health emergency for Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands following Super Typhoon Bavi's earlier devastation, pre-positioning federal medical teams in Guam and Saipan ([PACNEWS via Islands Business, 10 Jul 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-two-friday-10-july-2026/)). The US Coast Guard has reopened the Port of Guam to 24/7 commercial traffic, while assessment teams and relief water reached Rota, which took a direct Category 5 hit ([PACNEWS via Islands Business, 10 Jul 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-two-friday-10-july-2026/)).

In Fiji, 37 families from Nabavatu Village on Vanua Levu, displaced since cyclones Yasa and Ana in 2020–21, are preparing to move into cyclone-resistant permanent homes by September–October under the NZ$4.6 million Nabavatu Relocation Project, Fiji's first government-led planned relocation under its Standard Operating Procedures ([PACNEWS via Islands Business, 10 Jul 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-one-friday-10-july-2026/)). Separately, FAO and the Fiji Government signed a US$52.5 million forest landscape restoration agreement (US$29 million from the Green Climate Fund) to restore over 80,000 hectares and benefit nearly 197,000 Fijians ([PACNEWS via Islands Business, 10 Jul 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-two-friday-10-july-2026/)).

ASEAN foreign ministers will meet Myanmar's top diplomat in Bangkok on Sunday, the first in-person meeting since the 2021 coup, with cessation of violence and humanitarian assistance on the agenda, the Philippines announced as ASEAN chair ([Reuters via Interaksyon, 10 Jul 2026](https://interaksyon.philstar.com/politics-issues/2026/07/10/316223/asean-to-discuss-engagement-with-myanmar-at-special-meeting-philippines-says/)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

An Israeli drone attack on Kamal Adwan Hospital in Beit Lahiya, northern Gaza, wounded at least six people despite the ceasefire and the facility sitting in an Israeli-controlled "Green Zone"; Gaza's Health Ministry called it part of the "systematic targeting of health facilities" ([Al Jazeera, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/10/hospital-workers-wounded-in-israeli-drone-attack-on-gazas-kamal-adwan)).

WHO warned at the UN Geneva briefing that Sudan's cholera outbreak, declared 27 June, has spread from West Kordofan into North Kordofan and Central and South Darfur, with over 1,330 cases and 114 deaths (a 13.7% fatality rate). WHO fears besieged El Obeid could become "a second El Fasher"; its Sudan appeal is under 15% funded and the UN's 2026 response plan has received only US$930 million of the US$2.9 billion required ([UN Geneva, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.ungeneva.org/en/news-media/press-briefing/2026/07/un-geneva-press-briefing-1)).

WFP and the Government of South Sudan activated the country's first-ever drought anticipatory action plan, protecting more than 65,000 people in Budi and Kapoeta North counties, with 52,751 people receiving cash assistance ahead of forecast drought, funded by KOICA and Germany ([WFP, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-triggers-historic-anticipatory-action-plan-shield-people-severe-drought-impact-south-sudan); [UN News, 10 Jul 2026](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167915)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

UN Women's new report *Beyond the Breaking Point*, surveying 855 women-led organisations across 52 countries, finds at least one million women and girls have lost access to critical humanitarian services since January 2025 due to record aid cuts. Nearly nine in ten organisations can no longer meet demand, two in five expect to close within a year, and 86% report rising gender-based violence ([UN News, 10 Jul 2026](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167908); [UN Women, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.unwomen.org/en/news-stories/press-release/2026/07/at-least-one-million-women-and-girls-lose-access-to-critical-support)).

No other rest-of-world items could be verified as published within the last 24 hours; notable Ukraine, Haiti, and Venezuela developments this week trace to 6–9 July.

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Typhoon Bavi (Inday) — GDACS Red alert.** The season's second violent typhoon (peak intensity 287 km/h) has killed at least 15 in the Philippines and is passing extreme northern Luzon, Taiwan, and Japan's Sakishima Islands overnight 10–11 July, with up to 1 metre of rain possible in parts of Taiwan before an expected landfall on China's southeast coast around 12 July ([GDACS](http://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=38&eventtype=TC); [Rappler/PAGASA, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.rappler.com/philippines/weather/typhoon-inday-southwest-monsoon-update-pagasa-forecast-july-10-2026-5am/)).

**Missouri, USA — flash flood emergency.** A roughly 1-in-1000-year rainfall event (10–11 inches in ~12 hours) triggered catastrophic flash flooding; the Governor declared a state of emergency, more than 90 people were rescued, and an estimated 10–17 remain missing after a campground building collapsed into floodwaters in Reynolds County ([FOX Weather, 10–11 Jul 2026](https://www.foxweather.com/weather-news/30-million-brace-renewed-flood-threat-across-heartland-northeast); [St. Louis Public Radio, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.stlpr.org/news-briefs/2026-07-10/flooding-southern-missouri-kehoe-state-emergency)).

**Kanlaon Volcano, Philippines.** A moderately explosive eruption on 9 July sent an ash plume 2–3 km above the crater with ashfall reaching parts of Cebu, displacing at least 8 families; Phivolcs maintains Alert Level 2 ([Philippine Star, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.philstar.com/nation/2026/07/10/2541173/kanlaon-volcano-erupts-ashfall-reaches-cebu); [GDACS](http://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1000143&episodeid=1&eventtype=VO)).

**South Korea — monsoon flooding.** Heavy rain flooded central provinces (over 450 facilities damaged, 758 evacuated, one missing), with emergency evacuation alerts issued for residents near the Imjin River amid concern about a possible North Korean dam release upstream ([Korea Herald, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10804548); [NK News, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.nknews.org/2026/07/south-korea-watches-for-north-korea-dam-release-after-heavy-rain-near-border/)).

**Northwest India — flash flood watch.** IMD reported rainfall exceeding 21 cm in 24 hours in parts of Uttarakhand and Himachal Pradesh and issued flash flood warnings for six districts, with heavy rain forecast through 16 July ([O Heraldo, 10 Jul 2026](https://www.heraldgoa.in/goa/heavy-rain-to-continue-across-northwest-india-imd-issues-flash-flood-alert-for-uttarakhand/481356/); [GDACS](http://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103972&episodeid=6&eventtype=FL)).

No M6+ earthquakes affecting populated areas and no tsunami warnings in the window (verified against the [GDACS live alert list](https://www.gdacs.org/)).
