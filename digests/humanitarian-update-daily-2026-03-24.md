# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Tuesday, March 24, 2026

## 🇦🇺 Australian Alerts

- **Lebanon — Further Humanitarian Assistance (21 March):** Foreign Minister Penny Wong announced additional Australian humanitarian assistance for Lebanon, expressing grave concern over the displacement of more than one million civilians amid renewed hostilities since 2 March. Australia called on all parties to uphold international humanitarian law and protect civilian life. [Media Release](https://www.foreignminister.gov.au/minister/penny-wong/media-release/further-humanitarian-assistance-lebanon)

- **Myanmar — Australia-New Zealand 2+2 Consultations (17 March):** Australia and New Zealand reiterated calls for safe and unhindered humanitarian access in Myanmar at their joint Foreign and Defence Ministerial Consultations, signalling readiness to support efforts to alleviate humanitarian suffering. [Statement](https://www.minister.defence.gov.au/statements/2026-03-17/australia-new-zealand-foreign-defence-ministerial-consultations-22)

- **Middle East — Iran Humanitarian Visas (10 March):** Australia announced five humanitarian visas for members of the Iranian women's soccer team, as Australia continues to monitor the worsening regional crisis.

## What's New Today

- **Middle East (Iran/Lebanon/Gaza)** — With the US-Israel-Iran war entering Day 24, President Trump announced a five-day pause on US airstrikes against Iranian energy infrastructure, citing "very good and productive conversations" toward a resolution. Approximately 1.9–3.2 million Iranians have been temporarily displaced. In Lebanon, more than 1 million people remain displaced since hostilities renewed on 2 March. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167180) | [OHCHR](https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-briefing-notes/2026/03/middle-east-crisis-plays-out-worst-fears-talks-only-way-out)

- **Gaza** — Humanitarian crossings have been shut since Saturday, forcing aid partners to ration fuel and prioritise life-saving operations; 30 partner organisations are now providing healthcare services in northern Gaza, and 502 temporary learning spaces serve more than 297,500 children. [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/3/un-chief-warns-of-israeli-made-humanitarian-crisis-in-gaza-amid-war-on-iran)

- **Asia-Pacific — Energy Shock Spillover** — The Middle East conflict is rippling through Asia-Pacific: fertiliser benchmark prices have risen approximately 25% since hostilities escalated, impacting food security in import-dependent countries including Bangladesh, the Philippines, Pakistan and Sri Lanka. Fuel shortages in Myanmar have intensified, disrupting transport and humanitarian operations. [OCHA Asia-Pacific Snapshot](https://www.unocha.org/asia-and-pacific)

- **Sudan** — With only 5.5% of its $2.9 billion 2026 response plan funded, Sudan's crisis deepens: 33 million people are in urgent need, famine is confirmed in parts of Darfur and Kordofan, and WFP requires an urgent US$700 million to continue operations through June 2026. [WFP Sudan](https://www.wfp.org/emergencies/sudan) | [Action Against Hunger](https://www.actionagainsthunger.org/press-releases/sudan-becomes-the-worlds-hungriest-country-as-famine-spreads-to-two-new-areas-of-darfur/)

- **Myanmar** — OCHA's latest humanitarian update (No. 51, 9 March) confirms over 3.6 million people are internally displaced, 16 million require assistance in 2026, and severe underfunding (only 26% of 2025 plan received) has left millions without support. [OCHA Myanmar Update](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/myanmar-humanitarian-update-no51-9-march-2026)

- **Cuba** — Cuba experienced its third national power grid collapse this month; authorities have partially restored electricity after more than 10 million people lost power, as the US oil blockade exacerbates the ongoing humanitarian crisis. [The New Humanitarian](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/news-feature/2026/03/03/cuba-relentless-us-pressure-human-suffering)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

The broader Middle East conflict is now producing cascading humanitarian effects across Asia-Pacific through energy and food supply chain disruptions, placing tens of millions of additional people at risk of food insecurity. Simultaneously, the Gaza aid blockade and Sudan's catastrophic funding gap represent compounding emergencies with no immediate resolution in sight.
