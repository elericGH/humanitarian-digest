## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

NOAA officially declared El Niño conditions on Thursday 11 June 2026, confirming what climate scientists have warned for months — and the implications for Australia are significant. Models now project a 63% probability that this El Niño will surpass 3°C above the climatological baseline by November 2026–January 2027, which would make it the most intense event in over 70 years and rival the catastrophic episodes of 1982–83, 1997–98, and 2015–16. For eastern Australia, currently enduring an active GDACS-tracked drought event now entering its 15th week (since late February 2026), the forecast signals an elevated risk of deepening agricultural and hydrological stress through the Australian summer. Source: [GDACS Eastern Australia Drought DR-1018457](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=5&eventtype=DR); [Bangkok Post — El Niño arrives](https://www.bangkokpost.com/world/3269440/weather-pattern-el-nino-is-here-and-could-reach-historic-intensity)

The GDACS Eastern Australia-2026 drought event covers approximately 159,000 km² of agricultural land, with drought indicators showing anomalies consistent with mild-to-severe conditions. The Global Drought Observatory has flagged the region, and given the NOAA El Niño declaration on 11 June, Australia's Bureau of Meteorology is expected to update its seasonal outlook accordingly. Australian humanitarian and agricultural sectors should monitor relief planning for rural and farming communities likely to face water scarcity and crop failures in coming months. Source: [GDACS DR-1018457](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=5&eventtype=DR)

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

A magnitude 7.8 earthquake struck southern Mindanao in the Philippines on 7 June 2026 at 23:37 UTC (08 June 07:37 local time), triggering tsunami advisories across the Philippines, Japan, and Indonesia. The GDACS alert is rated Orange, indicating medium humanitarian impact. An estimated 3.6 million people were exposed to shaking at MMI ≥ VII, and a maximum wave height of 0.83 metres was observed. Reports confirmed structural collapses including a school roof in a primary school, airport closures at General Santos International Airport, and widespread power outages. Japan's Meteorological Agency issued advisories for Pacific-facing coasts from Ibaraki Prefecture to Okinawa. The earthquake generated a significant aftershock sequence, with multiple M5.5–6.5 events in the days following. Source: [GDACS EQ-1544720](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1544720&episodeid=1710268&eventtype=EQ)

A multi-country drought across South-East Asia, active since late January 2026, continues to affect China, Indonesia, Cambodia, Laos, Myanmar, Malaysia, Thailand, and Vietnam. Now in its 20th week and covering 446,000 km² of affected agricultural land, the GDACS event (DR-1018545) is rated Green with minor-to-moderate agricultural impact. The confirmation of El Niño on 11 June significantly elevates longer-term risk for this region, which is already experiencing below-normal monsoon forecasts. A joint report released 11 June 2026 by Himalayan climate researchers warned that the Hindu Kush-Himalayan (HKH) region faces dual threats this monsoon season: intense localised flash flooding despite an overall below-normal precipitation forecast, creating paradoxical drought and flood risks for communities across South and South-East Asia. Source: [GDACS DR-1018545](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018545&episodeid=5&eventtype=DR); [Outlook India — HKH monsoon report](https://www.outlookindia.com/international/hkh-region-faces-twin-threat-of-droughts-flash-floods-despite-weaker-monsoon-2)

Flooding in Myanmar's Ayeyarwady Region during 5–8 June 2026 resulted in at least one confirmed death and two people missing, according to Copernicus EMS GloFAS reporting on 11 June. The GDACS flood event (FL-1103937) is rated Green, indicating low immediate humanitarian impact, but the event occurs against the backdrop of Myanmar's ongoing humanitarian crisis under military rule, where existing vulnerabilities sharply compound the impact of any natural disaster on affected communities. Source: [GDACS FL-1103937](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103937&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL)

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Large-scale forest fires continue to burn across the Democratic Republic of Congo, with three major active events tracked by GDACS as of 10 June 2026. The three fires cover 12,059 ha, 15,673 ha, and a combined set of fires totalling over 55,000 ha in earlier episodes, ongoing since late May–early June. The DRC fires are occurring in a context of acute humanitarian crisis, with millions of internally displaced people and ongoing conflict in the country's east. Smoke and air quality impacts in the affected regions compound respiratory health risks for already vulnerable displaced populations. Source: [GDACS WF-1028832](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1028832&episodeid=3&eventtype=WF); [GDACS WF-1028770](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1028770&episodeid=5&eventtype=WF)

The East Africa drought (GDACS DR-1017863), now in its 45th week, continues to affect communities across the Horn of Africa and wider East African region. This prolonged drought, combined with the NOAA El Niño declaration of 11 June 2026, significantly raises concern for the 2026–27 seasonal outlook in the region. El Niño events are historically associated with suppressed rainfall over parts of East and Southern Africa, threatening further deterioration of food security conditions. The drought in Libya and Tunisia (now 11 weeks) adds to the regional climate stress picture across the continent. Source: [GDACS DR-1017863 East Africa](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1017863&episodeid=32&eventtype=DR); [Tribune Pakistan — El Niño arrives](https://tribune.com.pk/story/2612765/el-nino-is-here-and-could-reach-historic-intensity)

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

Two tropical storms remain active in the Eastern Pacific/Central America region. Tropical Storm BORIS-26, which tracked along Mexico's southern Pacific coast during 7–9 June 2026, brought heavy rain, flooding, and mudslide risk to coastal areas near Acapulco. GDACS rates it Green (low humanitarian impact) with no population in Category 1 or higher wind exposure. Tropical Storm CRISTINA-26, active 8–11 June across Honduras, El Salvador, and Nicaragua, similarly tracked as a Green alert tropical storm with 74 km/h maximum winds and 0.5 m storm surge potential, affecting high-vulnerability populations in El Salvador. Both storms have now dissipated. Source: [GDACS TC-1001274 BORIS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001274&episodeid=10&eventtype=TC); [GDACS TC-1001275 CRISTINA](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001275&episodeid=13&eventtype=TC)

NOAA's official El Niño declaration on 11 June 2026 carries global implications. Scientists note a 63% probability of a historically intense event by late 2026, with some models projecting anomalies exceeding 3°C — a threshold matched only by the 1982–83, 1997–98, and 2015–16 events, the last of which contributed to widespread famine, displacement, and excess mortality across multiple continents. The global humanitarian system should begin scenario planning for compounding crises across the Pacific, South and Southeast Asia, East Africa, and South America. May 2026 has also been confirmed by the Copernicus Climate Change Service as the second-warmest May on record globally. Source: [Daily Mail — El Niño arrives](https://www.dailymail.com/sciencetech/article-15892183/el-nino-global-phenomenon-arrives.html)

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Philippines M7.8 Earthquake & Tsunami — Active Response (7–12 June 2026):** The magnitude 7.8 earthquake off Mindanao on 7 June, with a 1 m tsunami recorded and a maximum observed wave height of 0.83 m, remains the most significant sudden-onset event of the past week. With 3.6 million people exposed to severe shaking and an extensive aftershock sequence (including M6.5, M6.1, M6.0 and multiple M5.5+ aftershocks through 11 June), the humanitarian response phase is ongoing. Airport closures, infrastructure damage, and power outages have been confirmed. Japan, Indonesia, and the Philippines all issued tsunami advisories, and the EC/ECHO has produced a dedicated daily situational map. Source: [GDACS EQ-1544720 + media](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1544720&episodeid=1710268&eventtype=EQ); [EC/ECHO Daily Map](https://erccportal.jrc.ec.europa.eu/ECHOProducts//Maps#/maps/5686)

**El Niño Declared (11 June 2026):** While not a sudden-onset event in isolation, the NOAA declaration of El Niño on 11 June represents a step-change in global hazard risk, with immediate implications for disaster preparedness planning for the Pacific, Australia, East Africa, and Central America.
