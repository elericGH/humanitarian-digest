## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia is among 30 co-signatories to a joint statement at the UN Human Rights Council 62 raising alarm over escalating atrocity risks in El Obeid, Sudan. The statement, issued 18 June 2026, condemned ten consecutive days of RSF drone strikes that killed at least 50 civilians across El Obeid and North Kordofan, with credible reports of ethnically targeted sexual violence. The 30-country coalition — including Australia, the UK, France, and Germany — called on the RSF to immediately cease its assault on the 500,000 civilians in the area, including 100,000+ internally displaced persons. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/un-human-rights-council-62-joint-statement-escalating-atrocity-risks-el-obeid-sudan))

The Emergency Action Alliance (EAA) continues to coordinate active appeals across four humanitarian emergencies: the Middle East Crisis, the Myanmar Earthquake response, the Hunger Crisis, and the Ukraine Emergency. No new datestamped news releases were published on the EAA website in the last 24 hours, but member organisations are scaling up responses across these appeals. ([emergencyaction.org.au](https://emergencyaction.org.au/about/disasters/))

GDACS reported multiple green-level wildfires active across Australia's Northern Territory as of 24 June 2026, with individual fires ranging from 5,000 to 14,000+ hectares. No deaths or mass displacement have been reported. ([gdacs.org](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1029048))

No new items were published by ACFID in the last 24 hours (most recent item dated 29 May 2026). DFAT's humanitarian pages were unreachable during this research run.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Timor-Leste floods** continue to affect communities, with 397 people displaced as of 24 June 2026. The flood began 19 June and remains active; green alert has been issued by GDACS. Given Australia's close development partnership with Timor-Leste, the situation warrants monitoring. ([gdacs.org](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103968))

**Papua New Guinea** continues to grapple with the humanitarian and livelihoods impact of the Titan Ridge volcanic eruption, which has blanketed parts of PNG's coastline in metres of floating pumice rock. Fishing communities dependent on ocean access have been severely disrupted, and authorities and aid organisations are assessing the extent of impact. ([thediplomat.com](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/floating-volcanic-rock-is-disrupting-life-in-papua-new-guinea/))

**Myanmar** remains in protracted humanitarian crisis with 3.8 million internally displaced and 1.62 million refugees and asylum-seekers outside the country as of end-May 2026. UNHCR projects cross-border displacement could reach 5.2 million by end-2026. In May, Tatmadaw operations displaced 25,000+ civilians in Sagaing Region, and a supply blockade in western Magway cut food and medicine to four townships. Diesel prices have risen 76.9% since February, threatening the monsoon planting season. ([reliefweb.int/myanmar-unhcr](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/route-based-snapshot-data-trends-refugees-myanmar-end-may-2026)) ([reliefweb.int/myanmar-monitor](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/myanmar-principled-action-monitor-edition-no-02-reporting-period-01-may-31-may-2026))

**Bangladesh** has begun fencing its border with Myanmar as the Arakan Army now controls the Myanmar side of the crossing, complicating humanitarian access for Rohingya refugees and cross-border aid operations. ([thediplomat.com](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/why-bangladesh-is-fencing-its-border-with-myanmar/))

**Afghanistan** faces its highest-ever recorded levels of acute malnutrition: 3.7 million children under five are affected, with nearly one million experiencing severe acute malnutrition. Transport costs have tripled due to Middle East conflict disrupting supply chains, and cash assistance for returnees from Iran and Pakistan has been halted at all border crossings due to funding shortfalls. WFP warns that one million children will be denied food assistance this year. ([reliefweb.int/wfp-afghanistan](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/wfp-afghanistan-country-brief-june-2026))

**Japan** experienced a M6.9 earthquake at 22:30 UTC on 24 June 2026 (depth 51.7km), with approximately 320,000 people within the MMI≥VI intensity zone. GDACS issued a green alert; the moderate depth reduces surface impact, but monitoring is ongoing. ([gdacs.org](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1548381))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Gaza** remains in severe humanitarian crisis despite a ceasefire that took effect in October 2025. OCHA's Under-Secretary-General Tom Fletcher briefed the UN Security Council on 18 June 2026 that over 67,000 Palestinians have been killed, with nearly 1,000 more deaths since the ceasefire. Gaza has moved from IPC Phase 5 famine to Phase 4 (severe crisis), with the share of households going to bed hungry falling from 92% to 36% — but only 11% of the $3.4 billion humanitarian appeal has been funded. Key crossings including Erez, Karni, and Kerem Shalom are not yet fully operational. ([reliefweb.int/ocha-fletcher](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/mr-tom-fletcher-under-secretary-general-humanitarian-affairs-and-emergency-relief-coordinator-briefing-security-council-humanitarian-situation-gaza-18-june-2026))

A UN Commission of Inquiry — only the second in UN history focused on violations against children — concluded that Israeli forces deliberately targeted Palestinian children, constituting genocide, crimes against humanity, and war crimes. Save the Children called the report a turning point for accountability. More than 20,000 Palestinian children have been killed and 44,000+ injured since October 2023. ([reliefweb.int/stc](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/no-child-should-ever-be-target-un-report-must-mark-turning-point-accountability-palestinian-children))

**Sudan** is simultaneously the world's largest displacement crisis and largest hunger crisis. Famine has been confirmed in 14 areas across North Darfur, South Darfur, and South Kordofan through September 2026, with 19.5 million people (41% of Sudan's population) in IPC Phase 3+ food insecurity. WFP assisted 3.4 million people in May 2026 — down from a 5 million target due to severe funding shortfalls — with rations cut to 70% for famine-risk areas. ([reliefweb.int/wfp-sudan](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/wfp-sudan-country-brief-may-2026-0)) ([reliefweb.int/fao-wfp-hotspots](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/hunger-hotspots-fao-wfp-early-warnings-acute-food-insecurity-june-november-2026-outlook))

RSF drone strikes on El Obeid (North Kordofan) have killed 23 people and injured 19 over two weeks, knocking out the city's main power station and disrupting water systems. El Obeid hosts 563,000+ civilians including 105,000 internally displaced persons. INGOs are warning of catastrophic humanitarian consequences and calling for unhindered access. ([reliefweb.int/ingos-el-obeid](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/sudan-ingos-warn-catastrophic-humanitarian-consequences-civilians-el-obeid))

**DRC** is contending with an Ebola outbreak in the Bundibugyo strain, with 896 confirmed cases and 232 deaths across 31 health zones as of 17 June 2026. More than 2 million forcibly displaced people are at heightened risk; the outbreak has spread to Uganda with 19 cases and 2 deaths. UNHCR is requesting $14 million for a July–November multi-country response. ([reliefweb.int/unhcr-drc-ebola](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/unhcr-warns-ebola-outbreak-threatens-displaced-communities-drc-and-beyond)) Multiple green-level wildfires have also been reported across DRC as of 24 June 2026. ([gdacs.org](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1029066))

**Somalia** faces a credible famine risk (IPC Phase 5) in the Bay-Bakool region through September 2026, according to FEWS NET. Acute malnutrition has already surpassed the Famine threshold in Buurhakaba District. Six million people (31% of Somalia's population) are in IPC Phase 3+ food insecurity, with 493,000 children experiencing severe acute malnutrition. Erratic May rains left 80% of planted farmland under moisture stress; a second consecutive failed crop could trigger full famine. ([reliefweb.int/fews-somalia](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/somalia-key-message-update-risk-famine-persists-and-around-bay-region-after-erratic-may-rains-may-september-2026))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

UNHCR's Global Trends 2025 Report, released 11 June 2026, recorded the first year-on-year decline in global refugee numbers in a decade: 41.6 million refugees globally (down 3%), with 14.7 million displaced people returning home in 2025 — the second-highest return figure in 60 years. However, 70% of refugees remain in long-term displacement, and resettlement arrivals collapsed by more than 50% year-on-year. UNHCR launched a "50 by 35" initiative to halve long-term displacement by 2035. The world's single largest IDP crisis remains Sudan, with 9.1 million internally displaced persons. ([reliefweb.int/unhcr-trends](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/7-10-refugees-living-long-term-displacement-unhcr-chief-calls-renewed-push-solutions))

A **M5.57 earthquake** struck northern California at 15:10 UTC on 24 June 2026 (depth 8.1km), with approximately 10,000 people in the MMI≥VII zone. GDACS issued a green alert. ([gdacs.org](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1548310))

**Honduras** is experiencing flooding that began 20 June 2026; no deaths or displacements have been reported at this stage. GDACS issued a green alert. ([gdacs.org](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103969))

**Syria** recorded a new wildfire that began 23 June 2026, burning approximately 5,068 hectares with around 917 people in the affected zone. GDACS issued a green alert; the event is notable given Syria's continued humanitarian fragility and limited firefighting capacity. ([gdacs.org](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1029038))

Central Asian nations, led by Kazakhstan, are emerging as critical logistics corridors for food, energy, and humanitarian aid into Afghanistan as direct routes remain constrained. Analysts warn of a worsening humanitarian crisis in Afghanistan if this corridor is not institutionalised. ([thediplomat.com](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/central-asia-is-becoming-europes-channel-to-afghanistan/))
