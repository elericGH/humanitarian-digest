# 🌍 Weekly Humanitarian Digest — March 6–12, 2026
*Produced 2026-03-12 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

**ACFID calls for stronger civilian protections amid Middle East escalation.** ACFID has issued an urgent statement urging the Australian Government to stand firmly behind international humanitarian law as conflict spreads from Iran across the region. The statement calls on Canberra to use diplomatic leverage to press all parties for civilian protection and unimpeded humanitarian access. [ACFID →](https://acfid.asn.au)

**Australia commits $50M in additional humanitarian assistance for Afghanistan.** DFAT confirmed a new $50 million commitment to Afghanistan humanitarian response this week, welcomed by ACFID as a meaningful contribution against a backdrop of major donor withdrawals globally. The funding will support food security, health services and protection for the 22 million Afghans in need. [DFAT →](https://www.dfat.gov.au/development/topics/development-issues/building-resilience/australias-humanitarian-assistance)

**EAA — No active appeal this week.** The Emergency Action Alliance has no new coordinated appeal active. Members are monitoring the Middle East escalation closely; an appeal may be triggered if displacement reaches a threshold requiring public fundraising. [EAA →](https://emergencyaction.org.au)

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Myanmar — Earthquake response ongoing.** Relief operations continue following the recent earthquake in central Myanmar, with access challenges hampering delivery to hard-hit rural areas. MSF and local partners report acute shortages of clean water and emergency shelter materials in Mandalay and Sagaing regions. [ReliefWeb →](https://reliefweb.int)

**Papua New Guinea — Highlands flooding displaces thousands.** Seasonal flooding in PNG's Highland provinces has displaced an estimated 12,000 people, damaging crops and cutting road access to remote communities. The PNG government has requested international assistance; Australia's DFAT is assessing response options through its Pacific development program. [ABC →](https://abc.net.au)

**Pacific — Climate resilience funding welcomed.** Australia's $550 million commitment to Pacific-led climate and development priorities continues to be welcomed across the region. Civil society organisations have called for co-design processes to ensure community-level priorities shape program delivery. [ACFID →](https://acfid.asn.au)

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Middle East — Regional war triggers major emergency.** US and Israeli strikes on Iran, and Iran's counterattacks, have sparked a rapidly widening regional crisis. Lebanon has seen 700,000 displaced in days; Gaza's border crossings are again shut. UNHCR has declared a major emergency, coordinating response across a region hosting 25 million existing refugees. [UN News →](https://news.un.org)

**Gaza — Border closures cut off humanitarian access.** UN Secretary-General Guterres has called urgently for Israel to reopen Gaza's crossings, closed since the Iran strikes. The WHO's $690M Eastern Mediterranean emergency appeal — needed before this escalation — is already 70% underfunded. [Al Jazeera →](https://aljazeera.com)

**Sudan — 1,000 days of war.** Sudan's civil war passed the 1,000-day mark this week. WHO reports 33.7 million people in need, 2 million at famine risk, and a third of health facilities non-functional. A new $1.6B UN regional refugee appeal has launched. [WHO →](https://www.who.int)

**South Sudan — Violence surges, aid access worsens.** Renewed fighting in South Sudan has sharply deteriorated humanitarian access, with direct attacks on aid operations reported. Over 10 million people — two-thirds of the population — require assistance, and 2.3 million children face acute malnutrition. [OCHA →](https://unocha.org)

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Afghanistan — 10% funded, 22M in need.** The $1.71B humanitarian appeal for Afghanistan is critically underfunded at just 10%, even as nearly half the population requires assistance. Taliban restrictions on female aid workers continue to hamper response. [OCHA →](https://unocha.org)

**Ukraine — Conflict continues into fourth year.** Humanitarian needs remain significant across Ukraine with ongoing displacement and infrastructure damage. International donor fatigue is a growing concern as the Middle East crisis absorbs political attention. [IRC →](https://rescue.org)
