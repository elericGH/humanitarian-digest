## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Lebanon** continues to dominate the humanitarian landscape. [OCHA's Flash Update #31](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-flash-update-31-escalation-hostilities-lebanon-1june-2026) (published 3 June) reports continued Israeli airstrikes and displacement orders across South Lebanon and Nabatieh governorates, placing severe pressure on collective shelters in Saida and surrounding areas. [UNHCR's Middle East Situation Weekly Update #19](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/unhcr-middle-east-situation-weekly-update-19-2-june-2026) (as of 2 June) notes that since late February, escalating hostilities in Iran and Lebanon have triggered regional humanitarian consequences, including refugee returns under adverse conditions to Afghanistan and Syria.

The International Rescue Committee [warned from Beirut](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanons-humanitarian-crisis-demands-lasting-ceasefire) that only a lasting ceasefire can create conditions for humanitarian recovery, with airstrikes, ground operations, and mass evacuation orders causing acute suffering. [International Medical Corps reported](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/middle-east-conflict-lebanon-response-situation-report-6-june-3-2026) that more than 1 million people remain displaced in Lebanon, including over 132,000 in 633 collective shelter sites, with escalating insecurity restricting humanitarian access and blocking safe returns. [Life for Relief and Development launched an emergency response](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/life-relief-and-development-life-launches-emergency-response-families-displaced-escalating-conflict-lebanon) targeting displaced families, focusing on health, shelter, and non-food items.

In **Gaza/oPt**, [MSF reported](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/one-year-later-msf-continues-treat-injuries-linked-gaza-humanitarian-foundation) that one year after the establishment of the Gaza Humanitarian Foundation's aid distribution mechanism, the organisation continues to treat Palestinians injured while attempting to access food, with suffering from these injuries outlasting the controversial operation itself.

**Yemen** faces a deepening food crisis. [WFP warned](https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/nearly-half-population-government-controlled-areas-yemen-face-acute-food-insecurity-humanitarian-support-sharply-declines) that 47 percent of the population in government-controlled areas now face acute food insecurity as humanitarian funding shortfalls sharply reduce aid operations. **Syria** saw [WFP publish a satellite-based assessment](https://reliefweb.int/report/syrian-arab-republic/wfp-satellite-based-assessment-euphrates-river-flooding) of flooding along the Euphrates River for humanitarian response planning.

The **Ebola outbreak in eastern DRC and Uganda** continues to draw multi-agency responses. [UNAIDS reported](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/unaids-supports-efforts-ramp-ebola-and-hiv-response-amid-evolving-ebola-situation-democratic-republic-congo-and-uganda) it is coordinating with WHO and the Health Cluster to maintain uninterrupted HIV treatment amid the evolving outbreak. [WHO published an updated operational presence map](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/cluster-sante-presence-operationnelle-des-partenaires-de-la-reponse-ebola-juin-2026) for the Ebola response in the DRC for June 2026. [South Sudan's second Ebola preparedness flash update](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/ebola-virus-disease-evd-preparedness-snapshot-flash-update-2-31-may-2026) assessed surveillance capacity and border monitoring readiness.

**Sudan** remains a major protection concern. [Insecurity Insight documented](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/attacks-health-care-sudan-13-26-may-2026) 755 attacks on health care since fighting began in April 2023, with 207 health workers killed, 15 kidnapped, and 127 arrested — over two-thirds of attacks attributed to the RSF. In **Libya**, [the government and UNICEF launched](https://reliefweb.int/report/libya/libya-launches-two-national-initiatives-transform-community-and-school-health-ministry-health-ministry-education-and-unicef-unveil-coordinated-package-national-community-health-program-and-national-strategy-school-health) two national health initiatives — the National Community Health Program and National Strategy for School Health — to transform community and school-based services. [OCHA published a flooding outlook](https://reliefweb.int/report/cameroon/west-and-central-africa-2026-flooding-outlook-02-06-june) for 2–6 June covering 13 countries across West and Central Africa, including Cameroon, CAR, Chad, Côte d'Ivoire, DRC, and Nigeria, supporting pre-positioning of humanitarian resources ahead of seasonal flooding.

[The International Rescue Committee released its Q1 2026 protection monitoring report for Italy](https://reliefweb.int/report/italy/irc-italy-protection-monitoring-report-january-march-2026), covering protection risks among people on the move through the country between January and March 2026, and documenting patterns of violence, exploitation, and rights violations affecting asylum seekers and migrants.

[EMPHNET researchers proposed a new multi-level framework](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/new-framework-reframing-modern-conflicts-global-health-system-shocks) in the Journal of Epidemiology and Global Health that redefines modern armed conflicts as shocks to global health systems rather than discrete public health emergencies, intended to guide health system resilience planning in conflict-affected contexts.
