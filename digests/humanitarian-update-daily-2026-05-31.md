## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

An ongoing drought affecting approximately 74,596 km² of eastern Australia has now reached 59 days duration, with GDACS classifying it as a minor-to-moderate agricultural drought event (active since late February 2026). The affected area spans parts of New South Wales and Queensland. Indicators suggest anomalies typical of mild to severe drought conditions, raising concerns for winter cropping seasons. ([GDACS DR 1018457](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=3&eventtype=DR))

A developing "Godzilla" El Niño — described by climate scientists as potentially the most intense in years — is being closely watched for its impact on Australian agriculture and the broader Asia-Pacific region. A 30 May report flagged the phenomenon as threatening crop yields from India to Australia, with analysts warning that 2027 could become the hottest year on record if the pattern intensifies. ([La Discussione, 30 May 2026](https://ladiscussione.com/442624/esteri/el-nino-minaccia-agricoltura/); [The Independent Singapore, 30 May 2026](https://theindependent.sg/nus-professor-warns-super-el-ni-o-could-deepen-asia-s-energy-and-economic-woes/))

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Tropical Cyclone JANGMI-26** is the most significant active hazard in the region today. As of 30 May 12:00 UTC, the storm is tracking toward Japan at Category 1 intensity with sustained winds of 148 km/h and a maximum storm surge forecast of 0.8 m. Approximately 2.3 million people are exposed in areas experiencing Category 1 or higher conditions. Known locally in the Philippines as Tropical Storm Domeng, the system was reported to have slightly intensified over the Philippine Sea on 29 May before continuing its northwestward track. GDACS rates the expected humanitarian impact as low given Japan's low vulnerability, but monitoring continues. ([GDACS TC 1001272](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001272&episodeid=14&eventtype=TC))

Flooding continues to affect multiple countries across the region. In **Sri Lanka**, heavy rains from 22 May triggered landslides and flooding across several provinces, with disaster authorities reporting 1 death and 3,485 people displaced as of 23 May. ([GDACS FL 1103900](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103900&episodeid=2&eventtype=FL)) In **Malaysia**, flooding in the Johor and Kedah states displaced 359 people — 312 across two evacuation centres in Pontian District and 47 in Kuala Muda District — with further rainfall forecast across Peninsular Malaysia. ([GDACS FL 1103884](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103884&eventtype=FL)) Ongoing flood events are also recorded in **Indonesia** ([GDACS FL 1103786](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103786&episodeid=54&eventtype=FL)) and the **Philippines** ([GDACS FL 1103880](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103880&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL)).

The Virtual OSOCC response to **Typhoon Sinlaku-26's** impact on the Federated States of Micronesia remains active following the April 2026 event. A South-East Asia drought alert (18 weeks ongoing) also adds to regional food security pressures. ([GDACS DR 1018545](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018545&episodeid=3&eventtype=DR))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Afghanistan** is experiencing widespread flash flooding across more than 14 provinces following heavy and sustained rainfall in May. GDACS records 17 confirmed deaths from the 20–22 May event in Baghlan and Badakhshan provinces, with a ReliefWeb situation report (25 May) indicating affected communities also in Helmand, Herat, Uruzgan, Samangan, Parwan, Kapisa, Kabul, Takhar, Daikundi, Ghor, Sar-e-Pul, Kandahar, and Maidan Wardak. Additional deaths were reported from thunderstorms and strong winds in Kandahar, Herat, Ghor, and Takhar on 22 May. ([GDACS FL 1103890](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103890&episodeid=1&eventtype=FL); [ReliefWeb, 25 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/afg-flood-05-2026-afghanistan-flash-flood-may-2026-2026-05-23))

A significant **Ebola Bundibugyo virus disease** outbreak in the **DRC and Uganda** is under active EU/ECHO monitoring as of 29 May, with DG ECHO publishing a daily map update. This is a rare Ebola subtype and the cross-border nature of the outbreak warrants close tracking by Australian and Pacific health authorities. (DG ECHO Daily Map, 29 May 2026)

In **Lebanon**, OCHA's Flash Update #30 (28 May) reported an escalation of hostilities, continuing a pattern of violence affecting civilian populations and humanitarian operations. Yemen's food security situation remains critical, with a WFP/FEWS NET Key Message Update (May–September 2026) noting that price fluctuations and above-average temperatures are negatively impacting household incomes and food access. Israeli military operations in **Gaza** over the Eid period resulted in at least 26 deaths, with forces pushing further into the territory according to a ReliefWeb-sourced report. A long-running drought across **Angola, Zambia, DRC, Congo, and Gabon** (ORANGE alert, 131 days) continues to drive food insecurity in central and southern Africa. ([GDACS DR 1018344](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018344&episodeid=11&eventtype=DR))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

Severe flooding in **Chongqing Municipality, China** from 25–26 May brought record-breaking torrential rain to Yongchuan District, killing at least 18 people and displacing 22,912. Landslides compounded the damage, and rescue operations were ongoing as of 27 May. ([GDACS FL 1103883](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103883&episodeid=6&eventtype=FL); [China News Service via ECNS, 27 May 2026](https://www.ecns.cn/hd/2026-05-27/detail-ihfewyzm7780496.shtml))

**Türkiye** remains on flood alert after repeated severe weather events through May. Flooding in Hatay Province in late May included landslides, with earlier events in Havza District (Samsun Province) on 12 May resulting in seven injuries and significant infrastructure damage. Authorities warn that spring rainfall levels are exceeding normal by more than 60%, and 3 deaths and 98 displacements have been recorded across the event period. ([GDACS FL 1103845](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103845&episodeid=9&eventtype=FL))

A **M6.9 earthquake** struck Chile on 25 May ([GDACS EQ 1542497](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1542497&episodeid=1707803&eventtype=EQ)), and a **M5.6 earthquake** affected Barbados on 30 May. A Latin America & Caribbean Weekly Situation Update was published by ReliefWeb on 29 May, summarising ongoing humanitarian conditions across the region. A Central-Eastern Europe drought alert (23 weeks, ORANGE) and a Madagascar drought (27 weeks) add to global slow-onset concerns. ([GDACS DR 1018332](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018332&episodeid=10&eventtype=DR); [GDACS DR 1018431](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018431&episodeid=3&eventtype=DR))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Tropical Cyclone JANGMI-26** (Category 1, 148 km/h sustained winds) is active and tracking toward Japan, with 2.3 million people in the exposure zone as of 30 May 12:00 UTC. Maximum storm surge forecast is 0.8 m. The system is expected to continue its trajectory with a forecast peak of up to 162 km/h per ECMWF models. ([GDACS TC 1001272](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001272&episodeid=14&eventtype=TC))

Two **M6.0 earthquakes** struck the Western Indian-Antarctic Ridge on 27 May within 10 minutes of each other (14:51 and 15:01 UTC), both in a remote ocean zone with no reported humanitarian impact. ([GDACS EQ 1542829](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1542829&episodeid=1708164&eventtype=EQ); [GDACS EQ 1542825](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1542825&episodeid=1708158&eventtype=EQ))

A **M5.6 earthquake** struck near Barbados on 30 May. No tsunami warning was issued and no humanitarian impact has been reported. A **M5.7 earthquake** hit Papua New Guinea on 22 May ([GDACS EQ 1541910](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1541910&episodeid=1707144&eventtype=EQ)) — no significant damage reported.
