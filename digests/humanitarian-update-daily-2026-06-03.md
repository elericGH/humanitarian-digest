## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**El Niño Developing, Raising Pacific Extreme Weather Risk.** The World Meteorological Organization (WMO) published its May 2026 El Niño/La Niña Update on 2 June, warning that El Niño conditions are developing, fuelled by unusually warm ocean waters in the tropical Pacific. The WMO says El Niño typically increases global temperatures and drives more extreme weather and rainfall patterns, with above-average temperature and precipitation risk projected over coming months — a direct threat to Pacific island nations already vulnerable to climate-related disasters. ([ReliefWeb / WMO, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/wmo-el-ninola-nina-update-may-2026))

**India Engages Myanmar Military Government Despite Humanitarian Concerns.** Indian Prime Minister Narendra Modi hosted Myanmar junta chief Min Aung Hlaing in New Delhi on 2 June, drawing criticism from human rights advocates. New Delhi has defended the decision to engage with the military leader who led the February 2021 coup, citing border security and regional stability interests, even as Myanmar's civil war continues to displace hundreds of thousands and UN agencies report a severe funding shortfall in the Myanmar Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan (HNRP) for 2026 as of 31 May. ([The Diplomat, 2 June 2026](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/indian-pm-hosts-head-of-myanmars-military-government-in-new-delhi/))

**Religious Violence Weaponised Against Gender Rights Defenders in Myanmar.** Analysis published on 2 June documents how Buddhist fundamentalism in post-coup Myanmar functions as an integral component of military authoritarianism, targeting gender rights defenders through religiously framed violence. The report illustrates the compounding protection risks facing civil society actors in the country. ([The Diplomat, 2 June 2026](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/unholy-violence-weaponizing-religion-against-gender-rights-defenders-in-myanmar/))

**Rohingya Refugees Mark Nine Years of Displacement.** UNHCR and partners called on the international community on 2 June not to forget the 1.2 million Rohingya refugees in Bangladesh, marking the approaching nine-year anniversary of the large-scale forced displacement from Myanmar. UNHCR spokesperson Babar Baloch warned that funding shortfalls and global instability are threatening essential services for the world's largest stateless population. ([ReliefWeb / UNHCR, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/unhcr-humanitarian-partners-ask-world-not-forget-rohingya-refugees-bangladesh-enar))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Ebola Outbreak in Central Africa Escalates, Spreads to Uganda.** The International Medical Corps released Situation Report #5 on 2 June confirming 321 confirmed cases and 116 suspected cases across the Democratic Republic of Congo (DRC) and Uganda, with 48 confirmed deaths and more than 240 suspected deaths. Cases of the Bundibugyo strain began in Ituri province and have since been confirmed in North Kivu, South Kivu, and across the border into Uganda, where nine cases and one death have been confirmed. Response is hampered by insecurity and limited laboratory access. ([ReliefWeb / International Medical Corps, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/ebola-outbreak-central-africa-situation-report-5-june-2-2026))

**IOM Warns Ebola Must Be Stopped at Borders Before It Crosses Them.** The International Organization for Migration on 2 June urged governments and partners to urgently strengthen cross-border coordination, warning that border closures alone risk driving population movement underground and increasing transmission risks. IOM noted that insecurity and displacement in eastern DRC are complicating containment, and called for a regionally integrated response framework. ([ReliefWeb / IOM, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/ebola-response-must-cross-borders-faster-virus-iom-warns))

**South Sudan on High Alert for Ebola Spillover.** A WHO and Health Cluster risk characterisation published on 2 June maps potential Ebola importation corridors into South Sudan from DRC, assessing health services functionality across border districts. The report highlights weak surveillance capacity and health infrastructure gaps that would complicate early detection and response. ([ReliefWeb / Health Cluster & WHO, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-evd-risk-characterization-potential-importation-corridors-health-services-functionality))

**Lebanon: 23 Children Killed Since Ceasefire, Humanitarian Alliance Launched.** A report published on 2 June by Qatar Charity notes that at least 23 children have been killed and 93 injured in Lebanon since the ceasefire came into effect earlier in 2026, with the total child casualty count reaching 1,006 since the escalation of hostilities in March — nearly 14 children killed or injured every day. Separately, IOM facilitated the launch of "Rooted for Lebanon," an international humanitarian alliance bringing together local and international NGOs under the Lebanese Ministry of Social Affairs to run a global fundraising campaign for families affected by conflict, displacement, and economic crisis. ([ReliefWeb / Qatar Charity, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-children-remain-risk-despite-ceasefire)) ([ReliefWeb / IOM, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/rooted-lebanon-launch-international-humanitarian-alliance-support-people-lebanon))

**Sudan Refugee Crisis: Rising Protection Risks at Chad Border.** UNHCR's Project 21 border monitoring for May 2026, published on 2 June, reports increased concentration of population flows into Chad from Sudan at specific entry points, with a rise in protection risks — including gender-based violence — reported by newly arrived households. The analysis highlights that security-related factors remain the predominant driver of displacement, with continued fighting making return impossible for most. ([ReliefWeb / UNHCR, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/chad/project-21-chad-sudanese-emergency-border-monitoring-key-trends-may-2026))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**UN and Partners Appeal for USD 710.5 Million for Rohingya.** The UN's Joint Response Plan (from May 20 2026, indexed 2 June) calls for renewed international support totalling USD 710.5 million to meet critical needs of Rohingya refugees in Cox's Bazar and Bhasan Char, and for Bangladeshi host communities. The appeal comes amid growing global instability, funding competition from other crises, and threats to the provision of essential services. ([ReliefWeb / UN, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/un-and-partners-appeal-usd-7105-million-meet-critical-needs-rohingya-refugees-and-bangladeshi-host-communities-enbn))

**Great Power Disruption Threatening Global Development Architecture.** East Asia Forum published analysis on 2 June examining the cascading impact of great power geopolitical competition on multilateral development frameworks, noting that wars in Ukraine, the Middle East, and Iran and the securitisation of trade are undermining the rules-based order that underpins international humanitarian and development cooperation. ([East Asia Forum, 2 June 2026](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/06/02/how-to-rebuild-what-the-great-powers-broke/))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**El Niño Developing — Elevated Extreme Weather Risk for Pacific and Beyond.** The WMO's May 2026 update published 2 June confirms El Niño conditions are developing with above-average sea surface temperatures in the tropical Pacific. While no acute sudden-onset events (cyclone, earthquake, tsunami, or major flood) have been recorded in the past 24 hours, the WMO warning signals an elevated risk period for Pacific island nations, East Africa, and parts of South and Southeast Asia over coming months. Humanitarian pre-positioning and early action planning are advised. ([ReliefWeb / WMO, 2 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/wmo-el-ninola-nina-update-may-2026))

No sudden-onset disaster alerts (earthquake ≥Mw6.0, active cyclone, tsunami, or major flash flood) were recorded in the GDACS system or Pacific Disaster Center in the 24-hour window.
