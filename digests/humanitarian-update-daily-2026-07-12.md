## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Typhoon Bavi made landfall in China's eastern Zhejiang province late Saturday night after authorities evacuated more than 1.7 million people, with Shanghai relocating a further 34,000 residents from high-risk areas. China's weather centre issued an orange typhoon alert and its first red rainstorm alert of the year, cancelling hundreds of flights and suspending high-speed rail services, while Beijing allocated 40 million yuan (about US$5.9 million) in central disaster relief funds for Zhejiang and Fujian ([PBS News/AP](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/typhoon-bavi-makes-landfall-in-eastern-china-as-more-than-1-million-are-evacuated)).

In the Philippines, at least 17 people were killed as monsoon rains intensified by Bavi (local name Inday) triggered landslides and flooding across Mindanao, including 10 villagers killed in Malapatan, Sarangani, and five in Calanogas, Lanao del Sur; about 11,000 people moved to 77 emergency shelters ([PBS News/AP](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/typhoon-bavi-makes-landfall-in-eastern-china-as-more-than-1-million-are-evacuated)). Philippine disaster officials reported nearly 50,000 people affected, 74 flooded areas across Northern Mindanao, Soccsksargen and BARMM, and 1,777 families sheltering in 22 evacuation centres, with many of the landslide victims being survivors of the recent magnitude 7.8 Soccsksargen earthquake ([The Manila Times](https://www.manilatimes.net/2026/07/11/podcasts/headlines-inday-brings-heavy-flooding-landslides-july-11-2026/2382460)).

Taiwan recorded at least 113 injuries as Bavi passed north of the island on Saturday, with more than 14,200 people evacuated, including from Hualien and Taichung, schools and offices suspended across most of the island, and around 29,000 soldiers placed on standby for relief operations. Japan's Okinawa prefecture saw more than 200 flights cancelled amid high waves and storm surges ([PBS News/AP](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/typhoon-bavi-makes-landfall-in-eastern-china-as-more-than-1-million-are-evacuated)).

GDACS issued a new flood alert for Bangladesh on 11 July as monsoon flooding develops, one of several active flood events in the region this week alongside India and the Philippines ([GDACS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1104015&episodeid=1&eventtype=FL)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

In the United States, record flash flooding in southeastern Missouri forced the helicopter evacuation of more than 200 children and staff from Camp Taum Sauk near Lesterville after surrounding roads were washed away, with the Army National Guard using Black Hawk helicopters and Governor Mike Kehoe declaring a state of emergency ([The Washington Times/AP](https://www.washingtontimes.com/news/2026/jul/11/200-people-summer-camp-helicoptered-safe-missouri-faces-flooding/)). The Black River crested at an unprecedented 28.7 feet after up to 12.25 inches of rain fell overnight Friday into Saturday, with hundreds of rescues across Reynolds and Iron counties and one woman still missing in Crawford County ([The Weather Channel](https://weather.com/2026/07/10/news/weather/black-river-record-flash-kentucky-flooding-missouri-rescues)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

Typhoon Bavi (BAVI-26) remains an active GDACS red-level tropical cyclone event, having reached peak sustained winds of 287 km/h before weakening ahead of its Zhejiang landfall; it is forecast to move northwest inland and weaken further ([GDACS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=44&eventtype=TC)).

A magnitude 6.4 earthquake struck the remote South Sandwich Islands region at 10:26 UTC on 11 July; given the location, no significant humanitarian impact is expected ([GDACS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1551279&episodeid=1717547&eventtype=EQ)). GDACS is also tracking new monsoon flooding in Bangladesh ([GDACS](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1104015&episodeid=1&eventtype=FL)).
