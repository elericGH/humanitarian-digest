## 🇦🇺 Australian Alerts

**Two Years Since Zomi Frankcom's Death** — Foreign Minister Penny Wong marked two years since Australian aid worker Zomi Frankcom was killed in an Israeli strike while delivering humanitarian aid in Gaza. Australia launched the Declaration for the Protection of Humanitarian Personnel, alongside more than 100 countries, at the United Nations, and provided $15 million through UN agencies and the Red Cross to strengthen protections for aid workers. [↗ Foreign Minister media releases](https://www.foreignminister.gov.au/minister/penny-wong/media-releases)

## What's New Today

- **Asia-Pacific — Strait of Hormuz Fallout** — OCHA's April 3 report confirms that Middle East escalation continues to drive food and fuel price spikes across Asia-Pacific, with WFP warning that up to 9.1 million additional people in Asia could face acute food insecurity if oil stays above $100/barrel through mid-2026. Fertilizer shortages are threatening spring planting in Bangladesh and Pakistan. [↗ OCHA](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/asia-and-pacific-humanitarian-impact-middle-east-escalation-3-april-2026)

- **Myanmar — Airstrikes Continue** — Military airstrikes on civilian areas in Sagaing, Rakhine, and Magway are continuing, with one recent strike on a trading junction in Magway killing more than 25 people. Over 16 million people require humanitarian assistance and at least 3.7 million remain displaced. [↗ UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167099)

- **Gaza — New Sea Route Delivers Aid, Famine Risk Looms** — WHO facilitated the first delivery of 106 tonnes of nutrition supplies via a new sea route through Cyprus and Ashdod port, offering a potential new corridor to reduce bottlenecks. However, aid agencies warn famine could return by mid-April if hostilities resume or international NGOs are expelled. [↗ UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/04/1167235)

- **Sudan — War Enters Fourth Year** — As Sudan's civil war approaches the four-year mark in April, 33.7 million people require humanitarian assistance — a 10% increase on 2025. Cholera is now reported in all 18 states, with dengue in 14 and malaria in 16. A UN refugee appeal of $1.6 billion has been launched for seven neighbouring countries. [↗ UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/02/1166979)

- **DRC — Ituri Violence Surges** — At least 30 civilians were killed on April 6 in armed attacks in Bafwakoa, Mambasa Territory, Ituri province, bringing the death toll to more than 100 since 11 March and 390 people abducted in the same period. The $1.4 billion humanitarian appeal remains only 30% funded. [↗ UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/04/1167248)

- **South Sudan — UN Experts Sound Alarm** — UN Human Rights Council-appointed experts warned on April 2 that South Sudan is evolving into a catastrophic human rights crisis, with over 267,000 people displaced in Jonglei state alone in 2026 and 300,000 students out of school due to conflict. [↗ Global Security](https://www.globalsecurity.org/military/library/news/2026/04/mil-260402-unnews03.htm)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

**DRC — Ituri Massacre:** With 30+ civilians killed on April 6 and 100+ killed since March 11 in Mambasa Territory, eastern DRC is experiencing one of its deadliest periods in recent months. The crisis is severely underfunded at 30% of the appeal.

**Gaza — Famine Deadline Approaching:** Aid agencies warn famine could return to Gaza by mid-April 2026 if hostilities resume or international NGOs are expelled. The Kerem Shalom crossing remains the sole cargo entry point and is creating critical bottlenecks.
