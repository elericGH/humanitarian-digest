## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Tropical Cyclone Maila response continues in Solomon Islands.** The Pacific Humanitarian Team released its fourth situation report on TC Maila on 22 May 2026, covering ongoing shelter and camp coordination operations across the affected islands. The response, coordinated by OCHA and partners, has been active since late April 2026 and continues to address displacement, protection, and recovery needs ([ReliefWeb / PHT, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/solomon-islands/tropical-cyclone-maila-pacific-humanitarian-team-situation-report-no4-19-may-2026)).

**Dengue outbreak persists in Tonga.** The Government of Tonga and Tonga Red Cross released their 22nd joint situation report on an ongoing dengue fever outbreak on 22 May 2026, tracking case numbers and the health sector response across the archipelago ([ReliefWeb, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/tonga/tonga-dengue-fever-outbreak-situation-report-22-22nd-may-2026-ento)).

**Myanmar displacement and conflict.** OCHA's Asia-Pacific weekly snapshot covering 5–18 May 2026, posted 21 May, highlights ongoing humanitarian needs across Myanmar and four other Asia-Pacific countries — including protection, drought, and earthquake-related needs ([ReliefWeb / OCHA, 21 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-weekly-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-5-18-may-2026)). A separate IOM/UNHCR route-based snapshot published 22 May continues to track large-scale refugee outflows from Myanmar along border routes through end-April 2026 ([ReliefWeb, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/route-based-snapshot-data-trends-refugees-myanmar-end-april-2026)).

**US engagement in the Pacific under strain.** An analysis published in The Diplomat on 19 May argues the Trump administration's inaction toward the Freely Associated States — Palau, the Federated States of Micronesia, and the Marshall Islands — is undermining both US strategic interests and the regional development aid and disaster response capacity that these partnerships underpin ([The Diplomat, 19 May 2026](https://thediplomat.com/2026/05/the-us-is-quietly-torpedoing-its-relationship-with-pacific-island-partners/)). Separately, The Diplomat reported on 20 May on the growing psychological toll experienced by deported Bhutanese refugees facing statelessness and family separation one year after removal ([The Diplomat, 20 May 2026](https://thediplomat.com/2026/05/1-year-later-deported-bhutanese-refugees-feel-the-psychological-toll-of-statelessness/)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Occupied Palestinian Territory.** OHCHR called on 22 May 2026 for Israel to immediately halt the forcible displacement of the Bedouin community of Khan Al Ahmar and to cease settlement expansion in the E1 area east of Jerusalem, citing violations of international humanitarian law ([ReliefWeb / OHCHR, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/israel-must-halt-forcible-displacement-khan-al-ahmar-and-settlement-expansion-e1-enar)). The UN Deputy Special Coordinator for the Middle East Peace Process briefed the Security Council on 21 May on the humanitarian and political situation, including access constraints and ongoing conflict dynamics ([ReliefWeb / UNSCO, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/ramiz-alakbarov-un-deputy-special-coordinator-middle-east-peace-process-briefing-security-council-situation-middle-east-21-may-2026)). WFP's Lebanon country brief for May 2026 highlights the continued fragility of food security and humanitarian operations in the country's post-conflict recovery context ([ReliefWeb / WFP, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/wfp-lebanon-country-brief-may-2026)).

**Yemen.** IPC's acute food insecurity snapshot for Yemen's government-controlled areas, published 22 May, projects deteriorating food security from March through December 2026 ([ReliefWeb / IPC, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/yemen-government-controlled-areas-ipc-acute-food-insecurity-snapshot-march-december-2026-enar)). IOM's rapid displacement tracking update for 10–16 May records continued conflict-driven population movements across the country ([ReliefWeb / IOM, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/yemen-rapid-displacement-tracking-update-10-16-may-2026)).

**DRC Ebola outbreak — multiple alerts.** The Ebola outbreak in Ituri Province, Democratic Republic of Congo, prompted a third situation report from the International Medical Corps on 22 May 2026, with an IFRC emergency appeal and an ACAPS analytical briefing also published the same day — together signalling a significant and escalating health emergency in the region ([IMC Sitrep 3](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/dr-congo-ebola-outbreak-ituri-province-situation-report-3-may-21-2026), [IFRC Appeal](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/africa-region-ebola-bvd-epidemic-emergency-appeal-no-mdrs1007), [ACAPS Briefing](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/acaps-briefing-note-drc-ebola-outbreak-22-may-2026)).

**West and East Africa.** A cholera outbreak in Borno State, northeast Nigeria reached its fourth situation report on 22 May, with WHO and the Health Cluster coordinating response operations ([ReliefWeb, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/nigeria/nigeria-health-sector-cholera-outbreak-situation-report-borno-state-no-04-21-may-2026)). WFP's northern Nigeria situation report for May 2026 underscores ongoing food insecurity in the Lake Chad Basin ([ReliefWeb / WFP, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/nigeria/wfp-northern-nigeria-situation-report-may-2026)). In South Sudan, REACH issued an alert on 22 May warning of increasing humanitarian needs in Unity State due to flooding and access constraints ([ReliefWeb / REACH, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/alert-south-sudan-increasing-humanitarian-needs-unity-state-amid-flooding-and-access-constraints-may-2026)).

**Sudan and Chad.** IOM's fifth Displacement Tracking Matrix update for Sudan, published 22 May, continues to document large-scale displacement in the context of the ongoing conflict ([ReliefWeb / IOM, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/dtm-sudan-displacement-and-return-snapshot-update-5-21-may-2026)). FEWS NET warned on 22 May that refugee influxes from Sudan are pushing food consumption in Chad toward IPC Phase 3 (Crisis) levels for the April–September 2026 outlook period ([ReliefWeb / FEWS NET, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/chad/chad-food-security-outlook-update-refugee-impacts-are-driving-income-and-consumption-toward-crisis-ipc-phase-3-april-september-2026)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Middle East regional mobility.** IOM's escalation mobility report covering 12–18 May 2026, published 22 May, tracks population movement across Iran and nine other countries affected by the broader Middle East escalation, documenting displacement, cross-border movement, and mobility restrictions ([ReliefWeb / IOM, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/escalation-middle-east-and-beyond-mobility-report-12-18-may-2026)).

**Somalia.** IOM's emergency trend tracking for Somalia (Week 61, 9–13 May 2026), published 22 May, covers displacement, access, and needs across five assessed districts, reflecting the country's protracted humanitarian crisis ([ReliefWeb / IOM, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/somalia-emergency-trend-tracking-ett-5-districts-assessed-week-61-09-13-may-2026)).

**Myanmar political dynamics.** The Diplomat published analysis on 21 May arguing that Myanmar's National Unity Government is gaining international moral legitimacy but faces growing operational pressures as the region trends toward accommodation of the military government — raising longer-term protection and humanitarian access concerns ([The Diplomat, 21 May 2026](https://thediplomat.com/2026/05/myanmars-national-unity-government-winning-the-moral-argument-but-running-out-of-time/)).
