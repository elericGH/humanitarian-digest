## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

GDACS monitoring detected twelve separate wildfire events across Australia's Northern Territory and Kimberley region of Western Australia on 7 May 2026. All alerts are rated Green (low humanitarian impact), with fires covering between 5,000 and 7,700 hectares each. Population exposure remains minimal — fewer than 15 people are affected across all events combined — reflecting the remote character of the affected areas. The largest cluster spans the NT region around latitude −13° to −19°, consistent with typical dry-season fire activity. Australian humanitarian organisations are not currently activating emergency responses for these fires. Monitoring continues via the Global Disaster Alert and Coordination System: [GDACS Australia Wildfire Alerts](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028603).

The Emergency Action Alliance (EAA), which brings together 15 leading Australian aid organisations, continues to run two active appeals: the **Middle East Crisis Appeal** for Gaza, Lebanon, the West Bank and Syria, and the **Myanmar Earthquake Appeal** supporting earthquake-affected communities since March 2025. Australian NGO members including World Vision Australia, CARE Australia, Oxfam Australia and others are sustaining field operations in both contexts. The EAA published a 6-month report on the Myanmar response earlier in 2026, and urges ongoing Australian public donations as both crises remain acute: [EAA Active Appeals](https://emergencyaction.org.au/).

ACFID, Australia's peak body for international development NGOs, issued no new media releases in the last 24 hours. Its most recent public statement (23 April 2026) highlighted the "far-reaching and long-lasting" impact of the US-Iran conflict on global aid volumes and called on the Australian Government to step up as global aid plummets. Australia has been consistently cited as having an opportunity to fill the leadership gap left by declining US foreign assistance across the Indo-Pacific: [ACFID News and Media](https://acfid.asn.au/news-and-media/).

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Myanmar's humanitarian crisis deepened further according to an updated Situation Analysis covering the period 20–26 April 2026, published on ReliefWeb on 4 May. An estimated 16 million people — nearly one in three citizens — require humanitarian assistance in 2026, with 3.6 million people internally displaced (up from 300,000 before the 2021 coup). The Arakan Army now controls approximately 90% of Rakhine State's 17 townships. The March 2025 earthquake aftermath continues to hamper communities: 24,200 houses were damaged, 3.5 million tonnes of debris block roads, and over 80% of the population lives in chronic poverty. The 2026 Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan appeals for USD 890 million but only 2.6 million of the most severely affected people can be prioritised given funding constraints. WFP warns that over 400,000 young children subsist on plain rice or watery porridge: [Myanmar Crisis Situation Analysis, ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/country/mmr).

A landmark interview with Arakan Army (AA) chief Twan Mrat Naing — the first with a foreign correspondent — was published in *The Diplomat* this week, offering rare insight into the Myanmar conflict's trajectory. Twan Mrat Naing stated that the military government "wants to negotiate from a position of strength but we will not succumb to such pressure," while confirming the AA now controls the vast majority of Rakhine State and is engaged in indirect talks with the junta. The interview also covers the AA's relationships with Bangladesh and India, and responds to human rights allegations. The AA's military consolidation complicates humanitarian access for hundreds of thousands of Rohingya IDPs: [Arakan Army Chief Interview, The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com/2026/05/arakan-army-chief-twan-mrat-naing-on-talks-with-the-myanmar-government-and-relations-with-india-and-bangladesh/).

One year after the brief India-Pakistan conflict of May 2025, border civilians in India-administered Kashmir continue to live in trauma, *The Diplomat* reports. Thousands of residents in Kupwara, Baramulla, Poonch and Rajouri describe children still reacting to loud sounds with panic, and families sheltering in self-made bunkers rather than returning to shelled homes. The four-day exchange of artillery in May 2025, which followed Indian strikes on suspected militant infrastructure after the Pahalgam attack killing 26 tourists, left civilian infrastructure severely damaged across the Line of Control. Aid and reconstruction access to the worst-affected areas remains constrained by ongoing security sensitivities: [Kashmir Border Civilians, The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com/2026/05/a-year-after-the-war-kashmirs-border-civilians-sit-between-shelled-roofs-and-self-made-bunkers/).

Mayon Volcano in the Philippines has been in an active eruptive phase since 2 May 2026, when a strong eruption generated a lava-flow collapse producing a pyroclastic density current along the Mi-isi Gully on the southwestern slopes of Albay Province, southern Luzon. Rockfalls and lahars have been recorded. The Philippines' Phivolcs has maintained an alert and evacuation perimeter. This is an ongoing volcanic hazard event listed as active on ReliefWeb's disaster tracker: [Philippines Mayon Volcano Disaster, ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/disasters). A separate magnitude 5.5 earthquake struck the Philippines on 6 May 2026 at 15:35 UTC, affecting an estimated 37,000 people in MMI intensity V shaking; no significant damage was reported but monitoring continues: [GDACS Philippines Earthquake](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1539398).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Humanitarian conditions in the Occupied Palestinian Territory remain dire, with OCHA's Humanitarian Situation Report of 1 May 2026 warning that only just over 10% of the USD funding required for critical operations in 2026 has been secured. In Gaza, two NGO workers were killed in separate incidents in late April, triggering the suspension of health and water services. Import restrictions on generators, engine oil and spare parts are causing widespread system failures in hospitals, sanitation infrastructure and debris-removal operations. In the West Bank, 2026 has already recorded the highest monthly average of demolitions due to lack-of-permit orders in over 17 years of UN records. Women in overcrowded displacement sites consistently report feeling unsafe due to inadequate lighting, significantly increasing gender-based violence risks at night: [oPt Humanitarian Situation Report, ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/country/pse).

Sudan's crisis against children gained fresh documentation on 7 May 2026, with the publication of a thematic report on the six grave violations against children in armed conflict. The report covers developments in 2025 and 2026, noting that the last country-level Children and Armed Conflict (CAC) verified-violations report was published in June 2024, leaving a critical monitoring gap. Analysis draws on approximately 80 publicly available secondary sources supplemented by key informant interviews with child protection responders. Verified violations include killing and maiming, recruitment and use of children, and attacks on schools and hospitals. Access constraints, active hostilities and community fear of reprisals are believed to result in significant under-reporting: [Sudan: Six Grave Violations Against Children, ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/sudan-six-grave-violations-against-children-during-armed-conflict-thematic-report-7-may-2026).

Armed group attacks in Mozambique's Cabo Delgado province triggered fresh displacement during 1–5 May 2026. Coordinated Non-State Armed Group (NSAG) attacks on 30 April in Muaja, Nacole and Minheuene villages in Ancuabe District involved looting, destruction of civilian infrastructure and abductions. New displacement arrivals during the reporting period include 280 families (1,213 individuals) to Muaja village, 20 families (132 individuals) to Majasse, and 15 families (90 individuals) to Nsanja village. IOM's Emergency Tracking Tool recorded the movements; while many families returned to their areas of origin, ongoing protection risks remain elevated: [Mozambique ETT Movement Alert #155, ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/mozambique/mozambique-ett-movement-alert-report-ancuabe-cabo-delgado-155-05-may-2026).

Regional tensions stemming from Iran-related escalation since late February 2026 continue to affect cross-border mobility and displacement in Iraq. Despite a ceasefire extension from 8 April to allow for further negotiations, IOM's April 27 update notes that Iraq faces "escalating pressures" exacerbating pre-existing vulnerabilities from the protracted ISIL conflict era. UNHCR is separately seeking USD 5.6 million in flexible funding to provide life-saving assistance to refugees and internally displaced people in Mali, where the Sahel security situation also continues to deteriorate. WFP's April 2026 brief for DRC confirms the organisation reached over 848,000 people in March 2026, with approximately 714,000 receiving life-saving assistance in the four conflict-affected eastern provinces: [Iraq Cross-Border Mobility Update, ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/country/irq).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

The International Rescue Committee launched **Sarah**, a new agentic AI-powered career coach designed to support survivors of human trafficking, on 7 May 2026. The tool is designed to help trafficking survivors navigate employment pathways, build CVs and prepare for job interviews. The launch represents one of the first humanitarian applications of agentic AI technology in case management, and comes amid growing sector interest in leveraging large language models for survivor-centred service delivery: [IRC Sarah Launch, ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/updates).

Haiti's humanitarian crisis remains acute, with OCHA publishing a "Haiti Crisis in Numbers" infographic dated 7 May 2026. The country continues to face gang violence, internal displacement, food insecurity and disrupted public services, with humanitarian operations severely constrained by access and security challenges. The infographic updates key operational statistics across protection, food security, health and water/sanitation clusters: [Haiti Crisis in Numbers, ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/country/hti).

A 2026 regional overview of Anticipatory Action in Latin America and the Caribbean confirms that, as of March 2026, the region has 45 active anticipatory action protocols — reflecting a decisive shift from piloting to institutionalised practice since 2025. The state of Anticipatory Action (AA) report, published by the GTAA LAC regional group, covers 16 countries and documents how pre-agreed funding triggers and early-action plans are being activated ahead of floods, droughts and other slow-onset shocks. Separately, a new IOM assessment of internal displacement in Bangladesh due to disasters (Round 1) provides the first nationwide estimate under the government's National Strategy on Internal Displacement Management: [Anticipatory Action Latin America 2026, ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/updates).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Australia — Wildfires (Northern Territory / Kimberley WA):** GDACS issued twelve Green-level wildfire alerts across remote NT and Kimberley WA locations on 7 May 2026. Fires range from 5,028 ha to 7,710 ha. Population impact is negligible (fewer than 15 people across all fires combined). This is consistent with the start of the dry season in northern Australia. No humanitarian response activation: [GDACS Australia Wildfire](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028603).

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano (Ongoing):** Active eruptive phase since 2 May 2026. Pyroclastic density currents along Mi-isi Gully and ongoing rockfalls. Evacuation zones maintained by Phivolcs. This remains the most significant volcanic hazard in the Pacific right now: [ReliefWeb Disasters](https://reliefweb.int/disasters).

**Kenya — Floods and Landslides:** DG ECHO's Daily Map of 7 May 2026 documented recent floods and landslides affecting Kenya. The event is consistent with the March–May long-rains season, which has produced severe flooding in multiple East African countries in 2026. Affected areas and casualty figures are still being assessed: [ReliefWeb Kenya](https://reliefweb.int/country/ken).

**Philippines — Earthquake M5.5 (6 May 2026):** A magnitude 5.5 earthquake at 15:35 UTC struck the Philippines (Mindanao region, depth 10 km), with approximately 37,000 people experiencing intensity level V shaking. GDACS rated the event Green; no significant casualties reported: [GDACS Philippines EQ](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1539398).

**Uganda — Flood Alert (6 May 2026):** GDACS issued a Green flood alert for Uganda on 6 May 2026. Population exposure data is limited; the event is being monitored: [GDACS Uganda Flood](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103869).
