*Produced Friday, 24 April 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The **Australian Council for International Development (ACFID)** published new analysis titled *"Development in Reverse: Far reaching and long-lasting impact of US-Iran conflict already being felt"* on 23 April 2026, warning that the need for humanitarian assistance is rising rapidly across the Middle East, Africa and Asia as a direct result of the US–Iran conflict, and that immediate impacts are already being felt across the Indo-Pacific. ACFID draws on real-time insights from Australian member organisations operating on the ground and notes the World Food Programme forecasts that fuel and supply shocks could push an additional 45 million people into acute food insecurity by mid-year. Read the full release on [ACFID's website](https://acfid.asn.au/development-in-reverse-far-reaching-and-long-lasting-impact-of-us-iran-conflict-already-being-felt/).

The same ACFID analysis highlights that **Pacific Island countries are especially exposed** as some of the world's most import-dependent economies, with disruption at the Strait of Hormuz already translating into fuel, food and fertiliser price spikes in Pacific markets — elevating electricity, water, transport and service-delivery costs and risking government budget and social stability. Australian agencies on the ground report delayed deliveries and mounting security risks for staff. The Australian-led [Emergency Action Alliance](https://emergencyaction.org.au/) (a coalition of 15 Australian aid charities) continues to carry active appeals including its Hunger Crisis, Myanmar Earthquake and Ukraine appeals, but has not launched a new appeal in the reporting window.

An OCHA **Asia and the Pacific Regional Humanitarian Snapshot** released on 23 April 2026 flags continuing concern for DFAT and Australian-funded programmes across the region, particularly in Afghanistan where cross-border hostilities with Pakistan have displaced more than 94,000 people ([ReliefWeb / OCHA, 23 Apr 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-1-20-april-2026)). Australian NGOs with Indo-Pacific programming are watching compounding shocks — conflict, drought, fuel-price inflation and cyclone recovery across Melanesia — that run through both the humanitarian and development portfolios Canberra funds.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

OCHA's **Asia and the Pacific Regional Humanitarian Snapshot (1–20 April 2026)**, posted to ReliefWeb on 23 April 2026, reports that fighting between Afghanistan and Pakistan has continued to cause civilian casualties and damage civilian infrastructure throughout April, with shelling, armed clashes, anti-aircraft fire and drone activity documented. A woman was killed in Kunar's Dangam district on 16 April, a school in Kunar was struck on 15 April, and three people were reportedly killed in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa from Afghan shelling the day before. UN road missions remain suspended and most border crossings are closed; displacement has exceeded 94,000 across Khost, Kunar and Nangarhar ([OCHA snapshot](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-1-20-april-2026)).

The **European Union** announced €175,000 (around 13 million Afghani) in humanitarian funding on 23 April 2026 to respond to recent floods across Afghanistan. The DG ECHO-funded operation, channelled through the IFRC's Disaster Response Emergency Fund and implemented by the Afghan Red Crescent, will reach approximately 12,000 people in Badghis, Farah, Ghor, Helmand, Herat, Kandahar, Logar and Uruzgan with cash assistance, emergency shelter, hygiene kits, safe water and primary health care ([ECHO via ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/european-union-releases-eur175-000-humanitarian-aid-response-recent-floods)).

In Southeast Asia, *The Diplomat* reports on 23 April 2026 that the Hormuz oil crisis has exacerbated Thailand's long-running fuel-smuggling problem, putting pressure on Prime Minister Anutin's government to act on what the piece calls a "national oil heist" ([The Diplomat, 23 Apr 2026](https://thediplomat.com/2026/04/thailands-fuel-smuggling-problem-surges-into-view/)). The fuel story compounds a Pacific-and-Asia pattern of energy-price transmission flagged in today's OCHA and ACFID analyses.

On the policy-development front, *East Asia Forum* published a piece on 23 April 2026 arguing that any India–Bangladesh reset requires institutional structure beyond political sentiment — a reminder that South Asia's bilateral politics will shape humanitarian access corridors across Bangladesh, the northeast of India and into Myanmar ([East Asia Forum, 23 Apr 2026](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/04/23/india-bangladesh-reset-requires-structure-beyond-sentiment/)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**OHCHR** condemned the killing of 14-year-old Aws Hamdi Naasan and 35-year-old Jihad Abu Naiem by Israeli settlers on 21 April in Al Mughayyir village in the occupied West Bank, as well as the killing of 25-year-old Odeh Awawdeh in a separate settler attack in Deir Dibwan, east of Ramallah. The OHCHR statement (23 April 2026) describes a growing pattern of "militarised and shielded" settler violence, with Israeli security forces detaining 30 Palestinian men during the Deir Dibwan incident ([OHCHR via ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/child-and-man-killed-near-school-al-mughayyir-settler-militarisation-intensifies)). OCHA's April 2026 West Bank movement-and-access fact sheet (also posted 23 April) documents 925 movement obstacles restricting 3.4 million Palestinians ([OCHA fact sheet](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/fact-sheet-movement-and-access-west-bank-april-2026)).

The **WFP Middle East Regional Escalation Emergency Response Sitrep #8** (23 April 2026) reports that despite the fragile ceasefire, nearly two months into the escalation, supply chain disruptions, port congestion and transport delays are still driving up costs and timelines for food, fuel and fertiliser across the region. WFP has assisted 611,200 displaced people in Lebanon with food and cash, reached 112,576 displaced people with date bars at Syria crossings, and is supporting 33,500 refugees across 20 Iranian settlements ([WFP Sitrep #8](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/wfp-middle-east-regional-escalation-emergency-response-external-situation-report-8-23-april-2026)). The **WHO Global Sitrep #5** on the same escalation (23 April 2026) notes that trauma injuries, interruptions to non-communicable disease care, and communicable disease risks from overcrowded shelters remain the dominant health concerns ([WHO Sitrep #5](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/world-health-organization-middle-east-escalation-conflict-global-situation-report-5-23-april-2026)).

**Islamic Relief** aid workers in Somalia's Gedo district have reported a malnutrition outbreak and at least one confirmed death at Iftin camp near Bardhere, as four consecutive failed rainy seasons compound with the regional fuel-price surge — reported to be up 134% in some areas since the Strait of Hormuz disruption — raising food prices and choking supply lines. Families are arriving at the camp after walking 50–60 km in search of aid ([Islamic Relief, 23 Apr 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/malnutrition-and-death-plagues-camp-somalia-drought-worsens)).

Two **IOM Displacement Tracking Matrix** reports were published on 23 April 2026: the **Yemen Q1 2026 update** records 827 newly displaced households (approximately 4,662 individuals), with 59% of displacement concentrated in Ma'rib and a declining monthly trend from January's peak of 492 households ([IOM Yemen](https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/yemen-rapid-displacement-tracking-update-first-quarter-report-january-march-2026)); and a **South Sudan Jonglei State event tracking report #105** covering 1 January–17 April 2026, noting that renewed fighting in Jonglei since early January has driven large-scale intra-state displacement on top of refugee inflows from Sudan ([IOM South Sudan](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-event-tracking-report-jonglei-state-105-01-january-17-april-2026)). OCHA's **Central African Republic Situation Report #64** (posted 23 April) reports on humanitarian assessments in Lim-Péndé and Ouham-Péndé and the distribution of emergency shelter and non-food items to 200 households ([OCHA CAR](https://reliefweb.int/report/central-african-republic/republique-centrafricaine-rapport-de-situation-ndeg64-au-21-avril-2026)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

A joint **EHP Colombia / GIFMM / OCHA flash update** (23 April 2026) describes a continuing deterioration of the humanitarian situation in rural areas of Tibú municipality in the Catatumbo sub-region of Norte de Santander between 20 March and 17 April 2026, driven by territorial disputes among non-state armed groups ([OCHA / EHP Colombia](https://reliefweb.int/report/colombia/colombia-flash-update-emergencias-humanitarias-en-tibu-norte-de-santander-23-de-abril-de-2026)).

The **European Union and Western Balkans partners** endorsed a new set of migration and asylum cooperation priorities running through 2030 on 23 April 2026, covering joint work on returns, international protection and migration governance — with downstream implications for onward migration from crisis-affected regions ([EU / ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/eu-and-western-balkans-agree-new-priorities-migration-and-asylum-cooperation-through-2030)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

The **Orange-alert M7.4 earthquake off Japan on 20 April 2026** (depth 35 km; ~70,000 people estimated in MMI VI shaking) remains the most significant recent sudden-onset event on the GDACS board and is still flagged as current ([GDACS EQ1536386](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1536386)). A subsequent **M5.6 earthquake in northern Japan on 21 April** (Green alert, 1.1 million in MMI III) and an **M5.5 earthquake in Timor-Leste on 21 April** (Green alert, 1 million in MMI IV) were both updated on GDACS in the last 24 hours but did not meet Orange thresholds ([GDACS Japan M5.6](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1536683); [GDACS Timor-Leste](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1536567)).

Ongoing **GDACS Orange-alert agricultural droughts** were re-scored today across Argentina–Uruguay, the Congo Basin (Angola–DRC–ROC–Gabon–Zambia), Kenya–Somalia, and Bolivia–Brazil–Peru–Paraguay — consistent with the Somalia malnutrition signal reported above ([GDACS Kenya–Somalia](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1017863); [GDACS Congo Basin](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018344)). Tropical Cyclone **Maila** is tracked for the Solomon Islands area as an open disaster record ([ReliefWeb TC Maila](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/tc-2026-000049-slb)). No active tropical cyclones meet GDACS Orange/Red thresholds in the Pacific or Indian Ocean basins in this window.
