## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

A magnitude 6.0 earthquake struck Eastern Samar in the Philippines on 4 May 2026 at 06:09 UTC (14:09 local time), with an epicentre approximately 20 km northeast of San Julian town at a depth of 73 km. The [GDACS report (EQ 1538909)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1538909&episodeid=1703833&eventtype=EQ) rates it Green with approximately 3,000 people exposed to MMI VI shaking; no significant casualties have been reported. The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (Phivolcs) confirmed the tectonic origin of the event.

Two further earthquakes were registered in the broader Pacific region within the past 24 hours: a M5.8 in New Zealand on 5 May at 08:00 UTC and a M5.8 in Indonesia on 5 May at 06:44 UTC, both tracked by [GDACS](https://gdacs.org/Alerts/default.aspx). Neither generated significant impact alerts, but both underscore ongoing seismic activity across the Pacific Rim. An earlier M5.7 in Papua New Guinea (30 April) and M5.8 in Taiwan (1 May) are also on the GDACS active monitoring list for the region.

The ongoing South-East Asia drought (now 15 weeks in duration per [GDACS drought tracking](https://gdacs.org/)) continues to place pressure on food systems and water resources across mainland and island Southeast Asia. The drought intersects with Mayon Volcano's active status in the Philippines (last updated 3 May 2026) and ongoing forest fire events in Nepal, India, Thailand, and Vietnam — all of which compound displacement and vulnerability in the Asia-Pacific corridor.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Flash floods in Afghanistan have now caused 16 confirmed fatalities, with two people still missing and approximately 3,600 individuals affected across seven provinces — Faryab, Badghis, Baghlan, Bamiyan, Ghor and Sar-e Pul — since 24 April 2026. The [GDACS Afghanistan flood report (FL 1103848)](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103848&eventtype=FL) notes that the IFRC reported 510 damaged houses as of 5 May, and that the EU/ECHO is supporting the Afghan Red Cross through the IFRC Disaster Response Emergency Fund (DREF) with €175,000. Humanitarian partners continue scaling up response efforts following a broader season of heavy rainfall and flash floods since late March.

Severe flooding struck Uvira in South Kivu province, eastern Democratic Republic of the Congo, on 1–2 May 2026, killing six people, displacing 414, and affecting a total of 79,000 residents. According to the [GDACS DRC flood report (FL 1103864)](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103864&eventtype=FL), the IFRC confirmed thirteen houses completely destroyed and 500 more flooded, with drinking water sources also compromised. Moderate to locally heavy rain is forecast to continue for northern and central DRC including South Kivu over the next 48 hours.

Heavy thunderstorms struck Gaziantep and Sanliurfa provinces in southern Türkiye on 3 May 2026, bringing flash floods, strong winds and casualties. EU/ECHO reporting cited one fatality in Sanliurfa (a solar panel torn from a roof) and 51 injuries, with 39 schools sustaining minor damage and multiple vehicles and buildings damaged across the Sahinbey, Sehitkamil, Nizip and Yavuzeli districts. Separately, Kenya experienced significant flooding as of 3 May ([GDACS FL 1103849](https://gdacs.org/)) and Algeria recorded a new flood event on 3 May.

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

Floods and landslides struck southwestern Kyrgyzstan in early May 2026, with the town of Gulcha in Osh Oblast hit on 28 April and the Batken District of Batken Oblast affected on 2–3 May. The [GDACS Kyrgyzstan flood report (FL 1103857)](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103857&eventtype=FL) notes that the IFRC reported 149 people displaced in Batken Oblast and 92 households affected in Gulcha. Peru recorded a new flood event on 5 May 2026 (GDACS FL 1103854), and Brazil continues to face multiple active flood events — including one ongoing since 3 May and a separate event from 30 April — highlighting persistent seasonal flood pressure across South America. The United States also reported active flooding as of 3 May (GDACS FL 1103841), likely associated with spring storm systems. In Central Asia, Tajikistan registered a new flood event on 3 May (GDACS FL 1103861).
