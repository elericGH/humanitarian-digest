# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Wednesday, 25 March 2026
*24-hour scan | Australian Interest Priority*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Alerts

- **Australia–FSM Maritime Partnership** — Foreign Minister Penny Wong welcomed FSM President Wesley Simina to Canberra on 24 March in the first official bilateral visit by an FSM head of state in nearly 30 years. Australia and the Federated States of Micronesia signed an MOU to enhance maritime security cooperation, reinforcing Australia's Pacific engagement strategy. [Source](https://www.foreignminister.gov.au/minister/penny-wong/media-release/strengthening-our-partnership-federated-states-micronesia)

- **Lebanon Aid Package (21 March)** — Australia announced a further $5 million in emergency funding for Lebanon: $3 million to WFP for food assistance and $2 million to UNICEF for nutrition and health services. This brings Australia's total humanitarian support for Gaza and Lebanon to over $135 million since October 2023. [Source](https://www.miragenews.com/further-humanitarian-assistance-for-lebanon-1641414/)

## What's New Today

- **Myanmar** — Fuel rationing introduced by the military junta amid the global oil supply shock is disrupting transport, businesses, and humanitarian operations across the country. Airlines have cancelled most domestic flights through April; food and transport costs are rising, putting further pressure on the 16 million people already in need of assistance. [OCHA](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-3-16-march-2026)

- **Lebanon** — The health system continues to deteriorate following the resumption of hostilities on 2 March. As of mid-March, 27 attacks on healthcare have been recorded, 49 primary health centres and five hospitals have shut, and over 1.2 million people have been displaced. A UN appeal for $308.3 million (March–May) is ongoing. [UNICEF Sitrep](https://www.unicef.org/media/179621/file/Lebanon-Humanitarian-Situation-Report-No.1(Escalation-of-Hostilities)-19-March-2026.pdf.pdf)

- **Gaza** — UNRWA reports humanitarian needs are deepening as access remains constrained, with 46% of essential medicines and 66% of medical consumables out of stock. All crossings except Kerem Shalom remain closed. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167159)

- **Sudan / East Darfur** — WHO has confirmed the death toll from the 20 March attack on Al-Daein Teaching Hospital rose to 64, including 13 children and multiple health workers. The hospital — the main referral facility for the entire state — is now non-functional. Total fatalities from health facility attacks in Sudan's war have surpassed 2,000. [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/3/21/who-says-attack-on-sudan-hospital-killed-64-including-13-children)

- **Ukraine / Donetsk** — A clearly marked evacuation vehicle operated by NGO Mission Proliska was struck by a Russian FPV drone near Oleksievo-Druzhkivka on 20 March, killing two elderly women evacuees. UNHCR condemned the attack, noting the rising threat posed by drone use to humanitarian workers near frontlines. [UNHCR Ukraine](https://www.unhcr.org/ua/en/news/press-releases/statement-unhcr-representative-ukraine-deadly-attack-during-evacuation)

- **Asia-Pacific / Middle East spillover** — The UN reports Middle East conflict is sending shockwaves through Asia-Pacific supply chains: benchmark urea prices up ~25%, wheat costs rising, and over 1.7 million Nepali migrant workers in the Gulf at risk of displacement. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167167)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

**Lebanon:** The humanitarian situation is deteriorating rapidly, with displacement now exceeding 1.2 million and systematic attacks on health infrastructure. WHO and UNICEF warn that the collapse of primary health services could trigger a public health emergency within weeks if funding gaps are not addressed.
