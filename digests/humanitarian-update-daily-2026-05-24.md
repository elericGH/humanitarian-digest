## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

The dominant humanitarian emergency of the reporting period is the multi-country Ebola Bundibugyo virus disease (BVD) outbreak centred in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo and Uganda. WHO declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC) on 17 May 2026 following confirmed cross-border transmission — the 17th Ebola outbreak in DRC history. Situation Report No. 1 was published on 23 May 2026. The response is severely complicated by ongoing armed conflict in Ituri Province and North Kivu, where 3.59 million internally displaced persons (98% conflict-driven) are present. WHO has designated DRC, Uganda, South Sudan, Burundi, and Rwanda as Priority 1 countries, with Angola, CAR, Ethiopia, Kenya, Republic of Congo, Tanzania, and Zambia as Priority 2. IOM has activated rapid response in all Priority 1 countries ([ReliefWeb — Ebola BVD Situation Report No. 1, 23 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/multi-country-ebola-bundibugyo-outbreak-situation-report-no-1)). The IFRC has also launched an emergency appeal covering the multi-country Ebola response ([ReliefWeb — IFRC MDRS1007, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/country/cod)).

South Sudan faces a dual humanitarian crisis: a new early-warning alert issued 22 May 2026 warns of increasing humanitarian needs in Unity State due to flooding and humanitarian access constraints, while a separate health sector concern relates to rising child marriage rates among refugees and host communities as noted in a report from 21 May 2026. A health cluster dashboard published 23 May 2026 provides situational data on the broader health response ([ReliefWeb — South Sudan alerts, May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/country/ssd)).

In Palestine, a statement published 22 May 2026 calls on Israel to halt the forcible displacement of residents of Khan Al Ahmar and to cease settlement expansion in the E1 corridor, adding to ongoing humanitarian pressure documented in UNRWA Situation Report #221 (20 May 2026) covering the Gaza and West Bank crisis ([ReliefWeb — Palestine, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/country/pse)).

Nigeria's cholera outbreak in Borno State in the northeast continues to escalate, with Situation Report No. 4 from the Health Sector published 22 May 2026. The outbreak is concentrated in communities already affected by the Boko Haram/ISWAP conflict ([ReliefWeb — Nigeria Cholera, Borno State, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/country/nga)).

Lebanon continues to receive multi-agency attention: WFP's Lebanon Country Brief for May 2026 was published 22 May 2026, and UNRWA's Lebanon Emergency Response Situation Report No. 10 was issued 20 May 2026, both indicating sustained humanitarian operations in the country ([ReliefWeb — Lebanon, May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/country/lbn)).

Sudan's humanitarian crisis continues to be tracked through market monitoring, with the Joint Market Monitoring Initiative (JMMI) Market Overview for 2–11 April 2026 published 22 May, providing data on purchasing power and food access amid ongoing conflict displacement ([ReliefWeb — Sudan, 22 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/country/sdn)).
