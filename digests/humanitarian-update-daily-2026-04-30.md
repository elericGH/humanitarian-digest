## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

GDACS satellite monitoring detected more than 20 active forest fire incidents across Australia between 27–29 April 2026, all assessed at Green (lower humanitarian impact) alert level. The largest single tracked event (GDACS WF 1028521) covered 6,368 hectares with a start date of 27 April and last detection on 29 April, with zero people reported in the direct burned area. Multiple additional fire hotspots were recorded across the continent on 28–29 April UTC, with updated notifications issued as recently as 29 April at 19:00–19:05 UTC. These are automated satellite detections coordinated through the EU-JRC Global Wildfire Information System and do not currently indicate catastrophic populated-area disasters; however the volume of simultaneous hotspots across Australia is notable for late April. [GDACS — Australian Wildfire Events, 27–29 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org/)

Monitored Australian-angle sources — ACFID, DFAT, the Emergency Action Alliance, and the Indo-Pacific Defense Forum — were not accessible during this automated run (see Sources Unreachable footer). No new Australian humanitarian funding announcements or advocacy releases could be confirmed.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Southern & Central Africa — Orange-level drought (ongoing).** GDACS continues to carry an Orange-level drought alert (DR 1018344) for Angola, the Democratic Republic of Congo, the Republic of Congo, Gabon, and Zambia, active since late June 2025 and last updated through 27 April 2026. Drought indicators show anomalies typical of severe to extreme conditions across this multi-country band. The EU's weekly Global Weather Hazards Summary (16–22 April 2026) confirmed that dryness and drought persist over Southern Africa. [GDACS — Orange Drought DR 1018344](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018344).

**East Africa — Orange-level drought (ongoing, Kenya & Somalia).** An Orange-level drought alert (DR 1017863) covering Kenya and Somalia has been active since May 2025 — 304 days as of 30 March 2026 — with medium agricultural impact assessed. Recent regional media carried a 28 April report on pastoralists in Kenya's Turkana County being pushed into irrigation farming as the drought depletes traditional grazing. The GDACS event page also linked a 29 April Bayer announcement of seed donations to affected African communities via NGO partners, underscoring ongoing food-security stress. [GDACS — Orange Drought DR 1017863](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1017863).

**West Africa — Green wildfires in Mali and Sierra Leone.** GDACS satellite monitoring flagged Green-level forest fire notifications in Mali and Sierra Leone on 29 April 2026. While assessed at low humanitarian impact, both countries face chronic humanitarian fragility. [GDACS — Mali WF 1028536](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028536); [GDACS — Sierra Leone WF 1028563](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028563).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Brazil — Green flood alert, 2 dead in Ceará State.** Heavy rainfall caused flooding across parts of Ceará State, north-eastern Brazil, from 27–29 April 2026, killing two people. GDACS (FL 1103852) assessed the event as Green-level (lower humanitarian impact) and notes that floods can have low impact due to the exposed population profile. Regional specialists cited by GDACS media links are proposing river renaturalisation as a long-term flood resilience strategy in Brazilian cities, where climate change and urbanisation are increasing flood frequency. [GDACS — Brazil Flood FL 1103852](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103852).

**Global wildfire activity elevated.** In addition to the Australian, Indian, and African events noted above, GDACS logged Green-level forest fire notifications on 29 April 2026 for the United Kingdom, Mexico, China, and the United States. All were assessed at Green level with no immediate mass-displacement figures. [GDACS global wildfire feed, 29 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org/).
