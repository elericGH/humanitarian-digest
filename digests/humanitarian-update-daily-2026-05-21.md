# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Thursday, 21 May 2026
*Produced Thursday, 21 May 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia's humanitarian funding posture remains under scrutiny following the May Federal Budget. The Australian Council for International Development (ACFID) welcomed the budget's decision to hold aid levels steady but called for stronger investment given an increasingly unstable world, warning that Australia's international development commitments are insufficient against the scale of global need. ACFID noted that global aid has plummeted from other donor nations, putting pressure on countries like Australia to step up their contribution. ([ACFID, 12 May 2026](https://acfid.asn.au/federal-budget-holds-for-aid/))

Australia faces direct economic exposure to the Strait of Hormuz crisis. The FAO warned on 20 May that the closure of the strait — a key artery for global energy and fertiliser flows — is triggering a systemic agrifood shock that could produce severe food price inflation within six to twelve months. Australia, as a major food commodity producer and trader, faces upstream cost pressures through higher fertiliser and fuel prices. FAO Chief Economist Maximo Torero urged immediate action by governments and international financial organisations to build supply chain resilience before the window for preventive action closes. ([ReliefWeb/FAO, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/strait-hormuz-conflict-threatens-global-food-prices-fao-warns-time-running-out))

The UN General Assembly voted on 20 May on a resolution to formally endorse an International Court of Justice advisory opinion establishing that states have binding legal obligations to combat climate change. Save the Children called the move a landmark moment for children's rights, noting that the ICJ had explicitly recognised climate change as a threat to children's fundamental rights to health and education. The resolution carries significant weight for Australia given its responsibility to Pacific Island neighbours facing existential climate threats. ([ReliefWeb/Save the Children, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/it-legal-responsibility-we-have-worlds-children-and-future-generations-un-votes-icj-climate-ruling))

GDACS recorded a green (low-severity) flood alert and green forest fire notifications across parts of Australia on 20 May 2026. No orange or red alerts were issued for Australian territory. ([GDACS, 20 May 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103887))

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

The Philippines faces compounding humanitarian pressures from its ongoing energy crisis and the forecast development of an El Niño event in 2026. A briefing note published on ReliefWeb on 20 May analyses how rising fuel and food prices — driven by the Middle East conflict — are exacerbating inflation and socioeconomic vulnerability across the country. The anticipated El Niño is expected to disrupt agricultural production and water availability, deepening food insecurity for already vulnerable communities. ([ReliefWeb, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/impacts-ongoing-energy-crisis-and-forecasted-el-nino-philippines))

Mayon Volcano in the Philippines remains under active monitoring. The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology published its 24-hour observation summary for 20 May 2026, covering volcanic activity and displacement risks for communities in the volcano's danger zone. Authorities continue to enforce exclusion zones and provide regular situation reports to coordinate the humanitarian response. ([ReliefWeb/PHIVOLCS, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/mayon-volcano-summary-24hr-observation-20-may-2026-1200-am-entl))

A joint UN and partner appeal for USD 710.5 million was launched on 20 May to meet the critical needs of Rohingya refugees and Bangladeshi host communities in Cox's Bazar. The appeal — 26% lower than the 2025 figure and described as covering only the bare minimum — includes USD 247.3 million for food and USD 128 million for shelter. Since early 2024, approximately 150,000 newly arrived Rohingya have strained already overcrowded camps and depleted limited humanitarian resources, intensifying pressure on Bangladesh's generosity as host nation. International assistance remains crucial to sustaining protection and services until a durable solution is found. ([ReliefWeb/UNHCR, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/un-and-partners-appeal-usd-7105-million-meet-critical-needs-rohingya-refugees-and-bangladeshi-host-communities-enbn))

In Myanmar, Burma Army air strikes and shelling continued to cause displacement and damage in Tnay Hsah Township, Hpa-an District. Reporting released on 20 May documents ongoing attacks from September to December 2025 that have driven communities from their homes and disrupted access to basic services. The conflict in Myanmar persists with little humanitarian access to affected areas. ([ReliefWeb, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/hpa-district-short-update-burma-army-air-strikes-and-shelling-caused-damage-and-displacement-tnay-hsah-township-september-december-2025))

Tonga's dengue fever outbreak continues, with Situation Report #21 released on 18 May 2026 recording 62 confirmed cases since the outbreak began, including two new cases in the reporting period. The majority of cases are among 11–14 year olds, predominantly male, and concentrated on Tongatapu Island. The Pacific Syndromic Surveillance System continues to monitor the situation across the broader Pacific region. ([ReliefWeb, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/tonga/tonga-dengue-fever-outbreak-situation-report-21-18th-may-2026-ento))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Humanitarian conditions in Gaza remain grave more than six months after the October 2025 ceasefire agreement. Human Rights Watch documented on 20 May that aid volumes entering Gaza have fallen far below required levels and that Israel has continued to restrict humanitarian access, killing civilians in the process. While the Board of Peace — established under UN Security Council Resolution 2803 — reported a 70% increase in aid distribution compared to pre-ceasefire levels, HRW noted this figure omits a sharp decline since early 2026 when all crossings were closed on 28 February as Israeli-US military operations against Iran began. As of early May, commercial trucks have resumed entering Gaza in limited numbers but total deliveries remain far short of what the population needs, with famine risk returning to parts of the strip. ([ReliefWeb/Human Rights Watch, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/gaza-israel-curbs-aid-kills-civilians-during-ceasefire-enar))

Lebanon's humanitarian situation deteriorated sharply between 15 and 18 May, despite a new 45-day ceasefire extension announced on 15 May following US-facilitated talks between Israel and Lebanon. OCHA Flash Update #27 reported that hostilities continued through the ceasefire period, with at least 2,988 people killed and 9,210 injured since the onset of hostilities on 2 March 2026. Six new displacement orders were issued for 27 localities during the reporting period, pushing the number of collective shelters to 634 sites hosting over 130,000 internally displaced persons. Attacks on healthcare continued to be recorded despite the ceasefire, with WHO documenting five incidents between 14 and 18 May. ([ReliefWeb/OCHA, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-flash-update-27-escalation-hostilities-lebanon-18-may-2026))

Nigeria is bracing for one of its worst lean seasons on record, with almost 35 million people facing acute food insecurity nationally. An advocacy note published on 20 May warns that millions of families will be forced to reduce meals, sell productive assets and withdraw children from school during the peak hunger period. The crisis is described as unprecedented in scale, driven by a combination of conflict, economic disruption and climate shocks. Humanitarian partners are calling for urgent funding and early action before conditions deteriorate further. ([ReliefWeb, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/nigeria/nigeria-2026-lean-season-unprecedented-food-and-nutrition-crisis-advocacy-note))

In the Democratic Republic of Congo, the humanitarian situation in North Kivu province remains dire. An OCHA situation report covering April 2026 documents 1.67 million internally displaced persons in the province as of 30 April, with deteriorating security in Masisi health zone having displaced more than 170,000 people alone in mid-April. Humanitarian access constraints continue to prevent partners from reaching the most vulnerable. Some 2.2 million people have returned to their areas of origin, but insecurity is hampering recovery. ([ReliefWeb/OCHA DRC, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/rd-congo-situation-humanitaire-dans-la-province-du-nord-kivu-rapport-de-situation-5-19-mai-2026))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

UN humanitarian convoys in Ukraine came under repeated attack in the week of 12–15 May 2026. A WFP food delivery truck was struck by a drone in the Dnipro region on 12 May, injuring the driver. On 14 May, a UN humanitarian vehicle was hit by a drone in Kherson city, followed by a second strike twenty minutes later on the same convoy. A World Central Kitchen van delivering hot meals was struck the same day, and on 15 May a Ukrainian NGO food delivery vehicle was hit, injuring two people — one critically. Between January and April 2026, three humanitarian workers were killed and ten injured in Ukraine. OCHA Director Edem Wosornu told the Security Council on 19 May that these incidents were "brazen" and not isolated, underscoring the erosion of protection for humanitarian operations. ([ReliefWeb/OCHA, 19 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/ms-edem-wosornu-director-crisis-response-division-ocha-behalf-mr-tom-fletcher-under-secretary-general-humanitarian-affairs-and-emergency-relief-coordinator-briefing-security-council-ukraine-19-may-2026))

Sexual violence in Haiti has reached a breaking point, according to the UN Special Representative on Sexual Violence in Conflict, Ms. Pramila Patten. Reports of rape and gang rape have surged since the start of 2025, overwhelmingly concentrated in areas under gang control where state presence is "virtually nonexistent." Ms. Patten called for the full deployment of the Multinational Security Support mission to reinforce Haitian national security forces and for the enforcement of UN Security Council sanctions targeting gang arms flows. The broader humanitarian situation across Port-au-Prince and beyond remains deeply unstable. ([ReliefWeb/UN, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/haiti/un-special-representative-patten-urges-immediate-action-sexual-violence-surges-amid-gang-violence-haiti-0))

At a UN Security Council open debate on 20 May, OCHA's Director of Crisis Response, Edem Wosornu, stated that the UN has documented civilian deaths in 20 armed conflicts in the current year, acknowledging that the real toll is "far higher" given documentation limitations in DRC, Sudan, Ukraine, occupied Palestinian territory and other theatres. The statement underscored the scale of global civilian suffering and the persistent gap between humanitarian need and available funding and access. ([ReliefWeb/OCHA, 20 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/ms-edem-wosornu-director-crisis-response-division-ocha-behalf-mr-tom-fletcher-under-secretary-general-humanitarian-affairs-and-emergency-relief-coordinator-open-debate-protection-civilians-armed-conflict-20-may-2026))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

GDACS recorded no red or orange sudden-onset hazard alerts in the last 24 hours. Three ongoing orange-level drought events are active: in Madagascar ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018431)), in the Central and Southern African cluster covering Angola, DRC, Republic of Congo, Gabon and Zambia ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018344)), and in the Southern Cone covering Argentina and Uruguay ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018327)). These are ongoing multi-month events rather than sudden onset.

Green (low-impact) seismic alerts were issued for a 5.9M earthquake in Japan (20 May, depth 42 km, 2 affected) and a 5.7M earthquake in Vanuatu (19 May, depth 27 km). Both are within the Pacific region of interest but at levels that do not currently indicate humanitarian impact. A 6.6M green-level earthquake was recorded in the Southern East Pacific Rise on 20 May in a remote marine location with no population exposure. ([GDACS RSS, 20 May 2026](https://gdacs.org/xml/rss.xml))

Australia has active green-level flood and forest fire notifications from GDACS dated 20 May, indicating monitoring-level activity only. No urgent action is triggered at this threshold. ([GDACS, 20 May 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103887))
