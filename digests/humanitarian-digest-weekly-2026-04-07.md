# 🌍 Weekly Humanitarian Digest — April 1–7, 2026
*Produced 7 April 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

**🇦🇺 EAA Middle East Appeal — Active Through Mid-April**
The Emergency Action Alliance (EAA), Australia's coalition of 15 leading aid charities, has an active Middle East Appeal running through 17 April 2026. Resources for community broadcasters and fundraising partners remain available to help mobilise Australian public donations in response to the ongoing humanitarian emergency in Gaza and Lebanon. [Emergency Action Alliance](https://emergencyaction.org.au/)

**🇦🇺 ACFID: $550 Million Pacific Commitment Welcomed**
ACFID has welcomed the Australian Government's announced $550 million commitment to Pacific-led climate and development priorities via the Australian Infrastructure Financing Facility for the Pacific (AIFFP). The statement reflects ongoing civil society advocacy for Australia to scale up its Pacific partnerships amid growing climate and displacement pressures in the region. [ACFID](https://acfid.asn.au/acfid-welcomes-550-million-commitment-to-pacific-led-climate-and-development-priorities/)

**🇦🇺 Australia's $50 Million Afghanistan Pledge**
Foreign Minister Penny Wong and International Development Minister Anne Aly jointly announced A$50 million in humanitarian aid for Afghanistan, with a focus on supporting women and girls. The announcement follows acute deterioration in food security, with 17.4 million Afghans projected to face food insecurity in 2026. [DFAT Humanitarian Assistance](https://www.dfat.gov.au/development/topics/development-issues/building-resilience/australias-humanitarian-assistance)

**🇦🇺 Australia–Indonesia Defence Diplomacy Deepens**
Australia and Indonesia formalised closer defence ties this week with a new Jakarta Treaty framework, shifting cooperation from functional collaboration to deeper strategic alignment. Indonesian Defence Minister Sjafrie Sjamsoeddin met with Australian Defence Minister Richard Marles in Jakarta in late March, emphasising joint commitment to Indo-Pacific security and a planned regional training hub on Indonesia's Morotai Island. [East Asia Forum](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/04/03/indonesia-and-australia-step-up-defence-cooperation/)

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Middle East Escalation Rippling Across Asia-Pacific Supply Chains**
A new OCHA report (3 April 2026) warns that Middle East conflict escalation is generating significant spillover effects across Asia and the Pacific. Maritime war-risk insurance has risen by 25–50%, disrupting regional supply chains, while higher fuel prices are increasing transport and agricultural input costs in import-dependent nations including Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh and the Philippines. WFP estimates that if the crisis persists through mid-2026 with oil above $100/barrel, up to 9.1 million additional people in Asia alone could face acute food insecurity. [OCHA Asia-Pacific Report](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/asia-and-pacific-humanitarian-impact-middle-east-escalation-3-april-2026)

**Myanmar: 12 Million Facing Hunger, Displacement Rising**
WFP has warned that more than 12 million people in Myanmar face acute hunger in 2026, with one million projected to reach emergency (IPC Phase 4) levels. Internal displacement has risen to nearly 4 million people. The Rohingya crisis in Bangladesh compounds the emergency, with Bangladesh hosting over 1.18 million refugees — and a new analysis in The Diplomat describes Bangladesh as stuck in an "exhaustion trap," cycling through crisis management without a durable solution. [WFP Myanmar](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-warns-myanmar-faces-rising-displacement-and-unacceptable-hunger-levels-2026) | [The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com/2026/04/the-graveyard-of-rohingya-diplomacy-is-bangladesh-stuck-in-an-exhaustion-trap/)

**Afghanistan–Pakistan Border Escalation: Humanitarian Access Blocked**
OCHA's 5 April situation update reports that sustained cross-border hostilities between Afghanistan and Pakistan since late February have cut off humanitarian access to approximately 100,000 people in Nuristan Province. Civilian casualties number in the hundreds, including children and one humanitarian worker. Nearly 150,000 Afghans have already returned from Pakistan to Afghanistan in 2026, adding to a staggering 2.9 million returnees in 2025. [OCHA Afghanistan Situation Update #2](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-situation-update-2-humanitarian-impact-afghanistan-pakistan-military-escalation-5-april-2026)

**Indonesia Strengthens Indo-Pacific Crisis Cooperation**
Indonesia is deepening regional crisis-response capacity through expanded defence diplomacy with Australia, Japan, Papua New Guinea and Singapore. A planned multinational training facility on Morotai Island would host joint exercises for disaster response and maritime security. The INDOMALPHI arrangement (Indonesia–Malaysia–Philippines) is entering a new phase of joint maritime patrols and predictive risk management in vulnerable zones. [Indo-Pacific Defense Forum](https://ipdefenseforum.com/2026/04/indonesia-strengthens-cooperation-with-indo-pacific-partners-to-boost-regional-crisis-response/)

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Gaza: Power Blackouts Deepen a Crisis of 77% Food Insecurity**
Power shortages in Gaza have triggered prolonged blackouts this week, severely limiting access to water, healthcare and sanitation. Approximately 77% of Gaza's 2 million+ population — some 1.6 million people — are facing high levels of acute food insecurity. Only one cargo crossing remains open, with daily aid trucks running far below the 600 required; 46% of essential medicines and 66% of medical consumables are currently out of stock. The 2026 Flash Appeal for occupied Palestinian territory seeks US$4.06 billion. [OCHA oPt Flash Appeal](https://www.ochaopt.org/content/flash-appeal-2026-glance) | [CGTN Gaza Crisis Report](https://news.cgtn.com/news/2026-04-05/VHJhbnNjcmlwdDg5OTk3/index.html)

**Lebanon and West Bank: UN Warns of Continuing Deterioration**
A UN roundup (3 April) confirms the humanitarian crisis continues to worsen across Lebanon and the West Bank as the broader Middle East conflict persists. Al Jazeera asked this week whether the crisis in Gaza is being systematically ignored by the international community, amid evidence of international fatigue reducing diplomatic and media pressure for access. [UN Roundup](https://en.gmw.cn/2026-04/03/content_38689702.htm) | [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/video/inside-story/2026/4/5/has-the-humanitarian-crisis-in-gaza-been-ignored)

**Sudan: 33.7 Million in Need, World's Largest Displacement Crisis**
Sudan's three-year conflict continues to generate the world's largest internal displacement crisis, with more than 9 million people displaced. The 2026 Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan (released March 2026) identifies 33.7 million people in need. Mass hunger has taken hold in the Darfur and Kordofan regions, and women and girls face acute risks including gender-based violence and forced marriage. ACFID has previously called on Australia to provide $50 million in new and additional funding for Sudan. [OCHA Sudan](https://www.unocha.org/sudan) | [Sudan HNRP 2026](https://humanitarianaction.info/plan/1514)

**DRC and Somalia: Severe Underfunding Triggers WFP Pipeline Breaks**
The Democratic Republic of Congo's humanitarian appeal is just 22% funded, while Somalia's stands at 24%. WFP has cut DRC assistance from a planned 2.3 million to just 600,000 people, with a complete pipeline break threatened. In Somalia, WFP reached just 350,000 people in late 2025 compared to 2.2 million the previous year. These collapses are directly linked to the dismantling of USAID and parallel cuts by European donors. [WFP Pipeline Breaks](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-warns-six-critical-operations-are-facing-significant-food-aid-pipeline-breaks-year-end) | [Global Humanitarian Overview](https://concernusa.org/media-room/media-room-gho-2026-press-release/)

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Afghanistan: 21.9 Million in Need, Funding Gap Severe**
Afghanistan remains one of the world's worst humanitarian crises in 2026, with 21.9 million people — 45% of the population — requiring assistance. The UN's 2026 appeal asks for US$1.7 billion to reach 17.5 million people. The crisis is compounded by the Taliban's ongoing restrictions on women, the return of millions from Pakistan and Iran, and fresh conflict along the Afghan-Pakistan border. [UN News Afghanistan](https://news.un.org/en/story/2025/12/1166684) | [OCHA Afghanistan Plan](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-humanitarian-needs-and-response-plan-2026-summary)

**Global Aid Architecture Under Pressure: Lancet Study Projects 9.4 Million Deaths**
One year on from the dismantling of USAID, a study published in The Lancet projects that global aid cuts could lead to at least 9.4 million additional deaths by 2030. Total global humanitarian funding has dropped precipitously from approximately US$27.6 billion in 2025 to around US$8 billion in 2026. Aid organisations are cutting staff and localising operations to adapt. The IRC describes 2026 as a year of "innovation vs cuts." [CNN/Lancet Report](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/04/world/lancet-usaid-global-aid-cuts-intl) | [IRC 2026 Analysis](https://www.rescue.org/article/innovation-vs-cuts-humanitarian-aid-2026)

**Ukraine: Sustained Needs Amid Global Attention Deficit**
While specific April updates were limited in this week's search, humanitarian organisations continue to warn that the war in Ukraine risks being overshadowed by multiple competing crises. Ongoing displacement and infrastructure damage continue to drive humanitarian needs that international response systems are increasingly unable to fund at required levels.
