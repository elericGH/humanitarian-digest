## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Tropical Cyclone Sinlaku-26 — which earlier peaked at category-equivalent winds of 287 km/h on 19 April — remains the regional centrepiece, with the European Commission's Copernicus Emergency Management Service activating mapping product EMSR872 over the Federated States of Micronesia. Multiple AOI grading maps were published over 22–23 April covering affected atolls, and the Virtual OSOCC for "Federated States of Micronesia – Typhoon Sinlaku, April 2026" remains active as of this update ([GDACS – Sinlaku-26](https://gdacs.org/)) ([Copernicus EMSR872](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR872/download)).

Northern Vietnam is digging out from severe weather that began on 21 April. The ASEAN Disaster Information Network reports one fatality in Quang Ninh from lightning, 768 affected people, one destroyed and 191 damaged houses, seven damaged schools across Lai Chau, Lao Cai, Quang Ninh and Thanh Hoa provinces, plus a major landslide in the Phu Ha area of Phu Tho that injured one person and forced the evacuation of 11 families. Heavy rainfall is forecast to continue over central and northern Vietnam in the next 72 hours ([GDACS / ECHO – Vietnam severe weather and landslide](https://gdacs.org/)).

Indonesia is also responding to widespread flooding triggered by 22 April rainfall. ADINet reports one injured and 600 affected in North Sumatra (with three houses destroyed and 150 damaged, plus a destroyed bridge and school); 16 people displaced in North Sulawesi; over 5,420 people affected by the Welang River overflow in East Java; and 2,513 affected in South Jakarta from the Ciliwung River. Additional moderate to locally heavy rain is forecast across large parts of Indonesia ([GDACS / ECHO – Indonesia severe weather and floods](https://gdacs.org/)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Coordinated armed assaults struck multiple cities in Mali on Saturday morning, with the army reporting it was fighting "terrorist groups" simultaneously in Bamako and at the Kati military base, as well as in Sevare, Kidal and Gao. Heavy weapons and automatic-rifle gunfire were heard near Modibo Keita International Airport, with helicopters patrolling adjacent neighbourhoods — the most serious escalation in the Sahel state in months ([Al Jazeera, 25 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/25/mali-army-says-armed-groups-launch-nationwide-attacks-gunfire-near-airport)).

Israeli strikes killed four people in southern Lebanon on 25 April amid an escalating series of post-ceasefire breaches, with the ICRC continuing to deliver water and essential lifelines as hostilities disrupt services around displacement sites ([Al Jazeera – Israel kills four in Lebanon](https://www.aljazeera.com/)) ([ICRC – Lebanon water lifelines, 22 Apr 2026](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/lebanon-maintaining-water-and-essential-lifelines-hostilities-disrupt-essential-services)).

In Gaza, residents of Deir el-Balah cast votes in municipal elections on 25 April, the first such poll under the present truce. Coverage focuses on a population still living amid rubble and mass displacement, with Al Jazeera also documenting growing public-health alarm about disease-carrying rats in unsanitary camps ([Al Jazeera – Gaza Deir el-Balah elections, 25 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2026/4/25/joy-and-desire-for-change-as-gazas-deir-el-balah-holds-elections)).

WFP, FAO and partners released the latest Global Report on Food Crises this week, warning that acute food insecurity and malnutrition remain alarmingly high as crises deepen — flagging Sudan, Gaza and the Sahel as the most severe trajectories ([WFP news release, 24 Apr 2026](https://www.wfp.org/news/acute-food-insecurity-and-malnutrition-remain-alarmingly-high-crises-deepen-un-eu-and-partners)). Three years into Sudan's war, ICRC reports continued mass displacement into Tawila, North Darfur, after fresh hostilities and violence in El Fasher ([ICRC – Sudan: human cost of three years of war](https://www.icrc.org/en/news-release/sudan-human-cost-three-years-war)).

Severe thunderstorms, heavy rain and strong winds battered Mombasa and Kwale counties in south-east Kenya from 21–22 April. Over 100 households were displaced in Mombasa with extensive dwelling damage; flash floods blocked transport routes in Nyali sub-county and disrupted health services in Tudor; in Kwale, one person remains missing and several villages have been cut off ([GDACS / ECHO – Kenya severe weather and floods](https://gdacs.org/)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

An explosion in southwest Colombia killed at least seven people, the state governor said on 25 April, with details still emerging on the cause ([Al Jazeera – Colombia explosion, 25 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/25/explosion-in-southwest-colombia-kills-at-leat-seven-state-governor-says)).

Diplomatic flashpoint: Donald Trump cancelled US envoys' planned trip to Pakistan after Iran's foreign minister Abbas Araghchi left the country, complicating the latest round of nuclear-and-Hormuz talks; Iran's authorities are projecting a hardened stance amid the ongoing US–Israel war on Iran, with cumulative toll trackers citing thousands killed in Iran, Lebanon and Gulf states ([Al Jazeera – Trump cancels envoys' trip, 25 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/25/trump-cancels-envoys-trip-to-pakistan-after-irans-araghchi-leaves-country)) ([Al Jazeera – Iran hardened stance](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/25/irans-authorities-project-hardened-stance-with-more-talks-on-horizon)).

Wildfires continue to burn across northern Florida and southern Georgia; FEMA reports four ongoing active wildfires across Levy, Putnam, Clay (FL) and Brantley, Clinch, Echols (GA) counties, with about 16,785 hectares burned to date, evacuation orders covering 940 people in Georgia and 223 in Florida, 83 destroyed and 70 damaged structures ([GDACS / ECHO – USA wildfires update](https://gdacs.org/)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

A magnitude 5.8 (later refined to M5.7 at depth 13 km) earthquake struck offshore approximately 25 km south-east of Goudoura, Lasithi, in south-eastern Crete, Greece, at 03:18 UTC on 24 April. There are no immediate reports of casualties or significant damage, but secondary aftershock monitoring is ongoing ([GDACS – Greece earthquake](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1537093)).

Tropical Cyclone Sinlaku-26 remains the most consequential active sudden-onset event in the Pacific theatre with a Virtual OSOCC still open for the Federated States of Micronesia ([GDACS – Sinlaku-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001270&episodeid=44&eventtype=TC)).

Active flood events in the GDACS feed include the United States (22 Apr), Argentina (21 Apr) and India (21 Apr) — within or just outside the 24-hour window. No active tsunami advisories are listed in the GDACS feed for the period ([GDACS](https://gdacs.org/)).
