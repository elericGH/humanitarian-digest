## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Tropical Cyclone Maila — Solomon Islands and Papua New Guinea (ongoing response)**

The Pacific Humanitarian Team's Situation Report No. 4, published 22 May 2026, describes a protracted response across Solomon Islands following Tropical Cyclone Maila. Forty-one health facilities remain only partially functional, approximately 180 expectant mothers require access to safe delivery services, and more than 11,000 children across 84 schools have been affected. Shelter remains the highest-priority need, with rising risks of gender-based violence and psychosocial distress among displaced populations. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/solomon-islands/tropical-cyclone-maila-pacific-humanitarian-team-situation-report-no4-19-may-2026))

In Papua New Guinea, the Disaster Management Team's Situation Report No. 8 (posted 19 May) recorded 256 wards assessed across five provinces, more than 128,000 people affected, 114,000 requiring humanitarian assistance, and 50 fatalities. The response is transitioning to a recovery phase. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/papua-new-guinea-severe-tropical-cyclone-maila-disaster-management-team-situation-report-no-08-16-may-2026))

**Tonga and Vanuatu Dengue Outbreaks**

WHO's Tonga Dengue Fever Outbreak Situation Report No. 23 (25 May 2026) recorded 66 confirmed cases since the previous report, with no dengue-related deaths. The predominant serotype is DENV-2, and children aged 11–14 are disproportionately affected. Two people were hospitalised during the reporting period. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/tonga/tonga-dengue-fever-outbreak-situation-report-23-25th-may-2026-ento))

In Vanuatu, eight dengue cases have been recorded on Efate since Epidemiological Week 1, with 88% from Pango Area Council. No deaths or hospitalisations have occurred; no outbreak has been formally declared but the situation is under active monitoring, with travel links to Fiji noted as a transmission risk. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/vanuatu/vanuatu-dengue-situation-update-01-18-may-2026)) The WHO Pacific Syndromic Surveillance System weekly bulletin for weeks 11–17 May (posted 26 May) continues to track disease trends across all 22 Pacific island nations and territories. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/pacific-syndromic-surveillance-system-weekly-bulletin-systeme-de-surveillance-syndromic-dans-le-pacifique-bulletin-hebdomadaire-w20-2026-may-11-may-17-enfr))

**Myanmar — Ongoing Crisis Escalation**

OCHA's Myanmar Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan Q1 2026 Dashboard (posted 27 May) shows that as of 31 March, humanitarian partners had reached 1.6 million people — 33% of the 4.9 million target. Within the hyper-prioritised caseload, only 832,000 of 2.6 million had been reached. Needs from the March 2025 earthquake remain unaddressed. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/myanmar-humanitarian-needs-and-response-plan-2026-quarter-1-dashboard-jan-mar-2026))

The Myanmar Crisis Situation Analysis for 11–17 May (posted 26 May) reports approximately 3.7 million internally displaced people — up from 300,000 before the 2021 coup — with cross-border displacement projected to reach 5.2 million. The Arakan Army now controls 14 of 17 Rakhine townships. Civilian violence in April 2026 included 185 events, 106 fatalities, and the bombing and subsequent occupation of a hospital in Hpapun Township. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/myanmar-crisis-situation-analysis-period-110526-170526))

**Bangladesh — Measles Outbreak and Rohingya Appeal**

Bangladesh's measles outbreak has reached 57,856 suspected cases and 8,067 confirmed cases across all 64 districts as of 20 May, with an average of more than 1,100 cases per day since 9 April. Children under five account for 81% of cases. Vaccination campaigns have reached 18.3 million children (102% of target). In Rohingya camps in Cox's Bazar, 595 suspected cases and 5 deaths have been recorded. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/bangladesh-situation-report-4-measles-outbreak-21-may-2026))

Separately, the UN and partners issued a joint $710.5 million appeal on 20 May to meet the critical needs of 1.2 million Rohingya refugees in Bangladesh, noting that 150,000 new arrivals have crossed from Myanmar since early 2024. The appeal is 26% lower than 2025, reflecting funding constraints rather than reduced need. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/un-and-partners-appeal-usd-7105-million-meet-critical-needs-rohingya-refugees-and-bangladeshi-host-communities-enbn))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**DRC and Uganda — Ebola Outbreak Declared a Global Health Emergency (MAJOR)**

The Democratic Republic of Congo and Uganda are at the centre of a rapidly escalating Ebola outbreak involving the Bundibugyo strain, for which no approved vaccine or treatment exists. WHO declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern on 17 May 2026, and the Africa CDC declared a Public Health Emergency of Continental Security on 18 May. As of 24 May, 906 suspected cases, 112 confirmed cases, 223 suspected deaths, and 11 confirmed deaths have been recorded. The virus has spread to Bunia and Goma in eastern DRC, and to Kampala in Uganda. UNICEF activated a Level 3 Corporate Emergency and released $5.75 million in initial funds. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/unicef-ebola-virus-disease-outbreak-flash-update-no-1-democratic-republic-congo-uganda-26-may-2026))

Save the Children reported on 27 May that one in four confirmed Ebola deaths are children under 15, with those under five making up 14% of deaths. Updated figures show 1,077 suspected cases, 238 suspected deaths, and 121 confirmed cases. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/drc-one-four-confirmed-ebola-deaths-are-children-underscoring-urgent-need-protect-them))

The IRC issued a flash alert on 26 May warning that the outbreak is spreading faster than the response, citing aid cuts that have weakened health systems significantly compared to the 2018–2020 outbreak — which itself killed more than 2,000 people. The IRC is calling for urgent international funding and coordination. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/irc-watchlist-flash-alert-ebola-outbreak-eastern-drc-spreading-faster-response))

South Sudan has activated preparedness measures, with 28 Points of Entry screening locations processing 13,897 travellers in a single 24-hour period. Uganda has closed its border with DRC and suspended air travel between the two countries. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/ebola-virus-disease-evd-preparedness-snapshot-flash-update-1-25-may-2026))

In Goma, the Jericho Road Wellness Clinic is receiving $2.5 million in PPE, medication and supplies from Direct Relief and is screening all patients for Ebola symptoms. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/facing-new-ebola-threat-goma-clinic-steps))

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano Ongoing Eruption**

PHIVOLCS published its 24-hour observation summary for Mayon Volcano on 27 May, noting continued activity. The eruption began on 2 May; as of 6 May it had affected 199,367 people, displaced 195,363, disrupted 132 schools, and generated lava flows up to 3.8 km in length with 17 or more pyroclastic flows recorded. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/mayon-volcano-summary-24hr-observation-27-may-2026-1200-am-entl))

**Global Food Security — Strait of Hormuz Warning**

FAO warned on 20 May that closure of the Strait of Hormuz could trigger a severe global food price crisis within 6–12 months. The FAO Food Price Index rose for the third consecutive month in April. Decisions on fertiliser, crop choices, and import contracts being made now will shape food security outcomes for vulnerable populations worldwide. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/strait-hormuz-conflict-threatens-global-food-prices-fao-warns-time-running-out))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Chongqing Floods — China (most significant active event)**

Record-breaking torrential rain has caused severe flooding and landslides in Chongqing Municipality, China, since 15 May. As of 27 May, 18 people have been confirmed killed and 22,912 displaced. Rescue operations are ongoing. A bridge collapse in Xiongan was captured on video on 25 May. Copernicus GloFAS issued a note on 26 May confirming the severity of the event. ([gdacs.org](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?episodeid=6&eventid=1103883&eventtype=FL))

**Tropical Cyclone JANGMI-26 — Western Pacific (Japan region)**

Tropical Cyclone JANGMI-26 was tracking toward Japan as of 27 May, with maximum sustained winds of 167 km/h (Category 2 equivalent). Approximately 320,000 people fall within the Category 1 or higher wind zone. GDACS rates the alert as Green given Japan's high adaptive capacity, but the system warrants monitoring as it approaches landfall. ([gdacs.org](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?episodeid=3&eventid=1001272&eventtype=TC))

**Türkiye — Ongoing Flooding**

Severe flooding and landslides continue in Hatay Province and the Black Sea region of Türkiye, with 3 deaths and 98 people displaced since 20 April. Rainfall in the northern Black Sea region is forecast to exceed seasonal norms by over 60%. Disaster response teams remain deployed. ([gdacs.org](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?episodeid=9&eventid=1103845&eventtype=FL))

**Texas Flooding — United States**

Heavy rain and flooding across Texas since 19 May has caused 1 death and significant infrastructure disruption. Automated vehicle services suspended operations in six major cities on 27 May due to floodwater incidents. ([gdacs.org](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?episodeid=5&eventid=1103888&eventtype=FL))

**Two M6.0 Earthquakes — Southern Indian Ocean**

Two M6.0 earthquakes struck the remote Western Indian-Antarctic Ridge within ten minutes of each other on 27 May (14:51 and 15:01 UTC). Both occurred at 10 km depth with zero population exposure. No tsunami advisory was issued. GDACS score: 0. ([gdacs.org](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?episodeid=1708158&eventid=1542825&eventtype=EQ))

No active tsunami warnings are in effect for the Pacific or Indian Ocean. No new significant volcano eruption alerts were issued in the past 24 hours.
