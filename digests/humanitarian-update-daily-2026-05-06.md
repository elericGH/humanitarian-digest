## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Myanmar: Earthquake survivors hit by compounding fuel and food price shocks (27 March 2026)**

One year after Myanmar's devastating earthquake, communities in Sagaing and Mandalay remain deeply fragile, with one in six households still facing moderate to severe food insecurity. WFP warns that the ongoing Middle East conflict is now compounding their hardship: fuel shortages are driving up food transport costs and threatening to double fertiliser expenses for monsoon crop planting. With 12.4 million people — nearly a quarter of Myanmar's population — already facing acute hunger, WFP is urgently seeking USD 150 million to sustain assistance to 1.5 million people in 2026. ([WFP](https://www.wfp.org/news/one-year-after-myanmar-earthquake-new-global-shocks-threaten-push-survivors-back-hunger))

**Asia-Pacific food systems under stress from global supply chain disruptions (17 March 2026)**

WFP analysis warns that the Middle East conflict's disruption of the Strait of Hormuz and Red Sea shipping lanes is having serious knock-on effects across Asia. Import-dependent countries in the region are projected to see a 24 percent increase in acute food insecurity if the conflict persists and oil prices remain above USD 100 per barrel. Countries such as Bangladesh and Pakistan — already dealing with humanitarian caseloads — are among the most exposed to rising food and fuel prices driven by the crisis. WFP and the Asian Development Bank have signed a landmark cooperation arrangement to strengthen food systems across Asia-Pacific. ([WFP](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-projects-food-insecurity-could-reach-record-levels-result-middle-east-escalation))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Gaza: Bread shortage deepens as Middle East conflict disrupts bakeries (5 May 2026)**

Seven months after the Gaza ceasefire, bread production across the Strip remains critically constrained. Damaged bakeries, restricted supply chains and surging costs — including cooking gas prices driven by the regional conflict — are limiting output. WFP supplies flour and fuel to 26 bakeries that together provide about one-third of Gaza's bread needs, with subsidised flatbread sold at three shekels (about USD 1) per bundle. Many bakeries rely on repaired equipment; one owner reported the cost of spare parts has risen eightfold since before the conflict. With around 80 percent of Gaza's workforce unemployed and USD 71.4 billion needed for reconstruction over the next decade, the food security situation remains deeply precarious. ([WFP](https://www.wfp.org/stories/middle-east-conflict-fuels-bread-shortage-hungry-gaza))

**Lebanon: Conflict escalation pushes nearly one in four into food crisis (29 April 2026)**

A new IPC analysis released by Lebanon's Ministry of Agriculture with FAO and WFP finds that 1.24 million people — nearly one in four of those assessed — are expected to face acute food insecurity between April and August 2026, a significant deterioration from 874,000 in the previous assessment period. The sharpest impacts are in conflict-affected districts including Bent Jbeil, Marjeyoun, Sour and Nabatiyeh. Syrian refugees face 36 percent acute food insecurity rates; Palestinian refugees, 45 percent; and newly arrived Syrians since 2024 face 52 percent rates. Agricultural damage, displacement of farming households, and rising input costs are compounding the crisis ahead of the spring planting window. Agencies warn conditions could deteriorate further without timely humanitarian and livelihood support. ([WFP](https://www.wfp.org/news/lebanon-new-ipc-analysis-shows-conflict-escalation-pushing-nearly-quarter-population-acute))

**South Sudan: Seven million face emergency hunger as conflict and flooding converge (30 April 2026)**

More than 7.8 million people across South Sudan — over half the population — face emergency levels of acute food insecurity, with more than 70,000 projected to reach catastrophic IPC Phase 5 hunger in Jonglei State. Escalating violence has cut river supply routes and displaced 300,000 people, forcing WFP to rely on expensive airdrops and airlifts in some areas. Some 2.2 million children under five are acutely malnourished, with two areas of the country, including Jonglei, carrying a credible risk of famine. Funding shortfalls have forced WFP to halve rations to just 300 grams per day; the agency faces a USD 193 million shortfall for the next six months. ([WFP](https://www.wfp.org/stories/there-no-food-multiple-crises-push-south-sudan-deeper-hunger))

**Sudan: Three years of war leaves 19 million acutely hungry (14 April 2026)**

As Sudan's civil war entered its fourth year, WFP reported that more than 19 million people face acute hunger — the world's largest humanitarian crisis, with nearly two-thirds of the population in urgent need of assistance. Famine continues in parts of Darfur and Kordofan where fighting is heaviest. The Middle East conflict is compounding Sudan's crisis: Red Sea shipping disruptions have driven fuel prices up by over 24 percent, inflating food prices and cutting purchasing power for millions. WFP is reaching 3.5 million people per month but urgently requires USD 600 million for the next six months to sustain operations, having already cut food assistance by 14 percent since January. ([WFP](https://www.wfp.org/news/three-years-war-sudans-people-abandoned-and-hungry))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Middle East conflict threatens to push 45 million more people into acute hunger globally (17 March 2026)**

WFP's global modelling warns that if the Middle East conflict persists and oil prices remain above USD 100 per barrel, up to 45 million additional people could fall into acute food insecurity worldwide — bringing the total to near-record levels last seen at the start of the Ukraine war in 2022. Sub-Saharan Africa and Asia face the steepest projected increases: West and Central Africa could see a 21 percent rise in food-insecure people; East and Southern Africa, 17 percent; and Asia, 24 percent. Countries like Sudan (which imports 80 percent of its wheat) and Somalia — already experiencing severe food crises — are highlighted as particularly vulnerable. WFP warns that without adequately funded humanitarian responses, the situation "could spell catastrophe." ([WFP](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-projects-food-insecurity-could-reach-record-levels-result-middle-east-escalation))

**Global humanitarian supply chains under severe strain (31 March 2026)**

WFP Director of Supply Chain Corinne Fleischer warned at a UN press briefing that disruptions to shipping in the Middle East region represent the most significant blow to humanitarian supply chains since COVID-19 and the Ukraine war. Delayed shipments and rising transport costs are directly impacting food aid deliveries to operations across Asia, Africa, and beyond. WFP warned that "today's supply chain challenges are tomorrow's hunger crisis," calling for urgent action to protect humanitarian logistics corridors. ([WFP](https://www.wfp.org/news/global-disruptions-supply-chains-are-driving-tomorrows-hunger-crisis))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

Multiple significant sudden-onset events were active as of 5 May 2026, per GDACS alerts. ([GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/))

**Afghanistan — Flash Floods (ongoing, updated 5 May 2026)**
Heavy rainfall since late April has triggered flash floods across seven provinces including Faryab, Badghis, Baghlan, Bamiyan, Ghor, and Sar-e Pul. As of 5 May, 16 people have been killed, 2 are missing, 9 injured, and approximately 3,600 people have been affected with 510 homes damaged. ECHO has contributed EUR 175,000 to support the response. The floods compound an already severe humanitarian situation in Afghanistan where WFP is also responding to ongoing conflict-related displacement.

**Kyrgyzstan — Floods and Landslides (updated 5 May 2026)**
Heavy rains on 28 April and again on 2–3 May triggered severe flooding and landslides in Gulcha (Osh Oblast) and Batken District. The IFRC reports 149 people displaced in Batken Oblast and 92 households affected in Gulcha. A state of emergency has been declared in affected areas. Further rainfall is forecast, raising concerns about continued impacts.

**Türkiye — Flash Floods (updated 5 May 2026)**
Severe thunderstorms on 3 May struck Gaziantep and Sanliurfa provinces, causing flash flooding that resulted in one death and 51 injuries. Thirty-nine schools sustained damage. Western Türkiye simultaneously experienced heavy snowfall and road closures. Emergency services have been mobilised across the affected provinces.

**Democratic Republic of Congo — Uvira Flooding (updated 5 May 2026)**
Flash floods in Uvira, South Kivu province, struck on 1–2 May, resulting in 6 deaths, 4 people missing, and approximately 79,000 people affected. Some 414 people have been displaced; 13 houses were completely destroyed and 56 partially damaged. Drinking water sources have been compromised, raising public health risks in an area already facing significant humanitarian vulnerabilities.

**Pacific and Southeast Asia — Seismic Activity (4–5 May 2026)**
Multiple significant earthquakes were recorded across the Pacific and Southeast Asia region over 4–5 May, including a M6.0 event in the Philippines (4 May), M5.8 events in Indonesia and New Zealand (5 May), and a M5.7 event in Mexico (4 May). Impact assessments are ongoing; no major humanitarian response has been triggered as of time of publication.
