# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Saturday, 14 March 2026
*24-hour scan | Australian Interest Priority*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Alerts

**Australia launches global Declaration for the Protection of Humanitarian Personnel** — After a year-long diplomatic effort, Foreign Minister Penny Wong launched the declaration at the United Nations in New York, joined by more than 100 countries. The ceremony honoured Australian aid worker Zomi Frankcom and the 674 humanitarian personnel killed since the start of 2024. The Albanese Government committed $15 million in support through UNDSS, OCHA, and the Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement. [DFAT](https://www.dfat.gov.au/development/topics/development-issues/building-resilience/australias-humanitarian-assistance/declaration-protection-humanitarian-personnel)

**Australia grants humanitarian visas to five Iranian footballers** — Five members of Iran's women's national football team were granted permanent protection humanitarian visas after refusing to sing the Iranian national anthem at a Women's Asian Cup match on the Gold Coast and fearing for their safety. Home Affairs Minister Tony Burke and PM Albanese confirmed the visa grants. [Prism News](https://www.prismnews.com/news/australia-grants-five-iranian-footballers-humanitarian-visas-after-anthem-protest)

**Australia criticised for silence on Israel's suspension of 37 aid organisations in Gaza** — Despite warnings from humanitarian groups, the Australian government has not publicly condemned Israel's failure to renew registrations for 37 international NGOs — including Oxfam and Save the Children — required to suspend operations by 1 March 2026. [Michael West Media](https://michaelwest.com.au/ngos-warn-of-catastrophic-impact-penny-wong-doesnt-care)

## What's New Today

- **Gaza** — Despite an Israeli High Court pause on the NGO ban, new humanitarian staff rotations remain blocked, with surgeons and medics denied entry. Aid operations by major organisations have shrunk significantly, local employment is at risk, and Israel continues to allow only half the fuel needed to sustain basic operations. [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/1/1/israel-faces-widespread-condemnation-as-ngo-ban-comes-into-effect) | [The National](https://www.thenationalnews.com/news/mena/2026/03/11/aid-workers-shut-out-of-gaza-despite-israeli-reprieve/)

- **Middle East / Iran conflict** — UNHCR's Emergency Flash Update #3 (9 March) warns of a rapidly expanding displacement crisis across the Middle East. Oil market shocks caused by ongoing military operations in Iran are driving up costs for humanitarian programs providing food, water, medicine, and shelter across multiple crises. [ReliefWeb / UNHCR](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/unhcr-middle-east-situation-emergency-flash-update-3-9-march-2026)

- **Lebanon** — A new emergency situation report (2–10 March) shows continued acute humanitarian need. The Iran conflict theater is generating fresh displacement pressures across Lebanon. [ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-emergency-response-sitrep-2-reporting-period-2-10-march-2026)

- **Sudan** — Intensified fighting between the Sudanese Armed Forces and the RSF in Kordofan and Blue Nile States continues. Drone attacks struck a medical facility in El Obeid, injuring 12 people including five medical staff. The UN reports pockets of famine throughout the country. [OCHA](https://www.unocha.org/news/todays-top-news-global-humanitarian-overview-2026-sudan-occupied-palestinian-territory) | [WHO Sudan Appeal](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/sudan-who-health-emergency-appeal-2026)

- **Asia-Pacific** — OCHA reports the Asia-Pacific region accounts for three-quarters of the world's disaster-affected people, with Myanmar's humanitarian situation worsening due to conflict, collapsing public services, and inflation. CERF allocations for the region in 2025 were published as of 10 March. [PINA](https://pina.com.fj/2026/03/11/asia-pacific-accounts-for-three-quarters-of-global-disaster-affected-people-ocha/) | [OCHA CERF](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/asia-and-pacific-overview-cerf-allocations-2025-10-march-2026)

- **Sahel / West Africa** — ReliefWeb's FSNWG analysis projects 53 million people in the Sahel, West and Central Africa will face food and nutrition insecurity in 2026. [ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/fsnwg-analysis-and-alert-note-executive-summary-53-million-people-sahel-west-and-central-africa-are-projected-face-food-and-nutrition-insecurity-2026-march-2026)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

**Gaza aid collapse deepening** — Despite Israel's High Court pausing the formal NGO ban, the operational shutdown of humanitarian capacity is proceeding in practice. Staff rotations are blocked, medical services are degraded, and fuel supply remains critically constrained. The situation is functionally equivalent to a full aid blockade.
