## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific
Super Typhoon BAVI-26 remains a GDACS Red-alert event, a Category 5 system with maximum sustained winds of 287 km/h that battered Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands before tracking west, with an estimated 44.1 million people exposed across Guam, the Northern Marianas, Japan, Taiwan and China ([GDACS event report](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=22&eventtype=TC)). Media monitored by GDACS in the past day report the system has entered the Philippine Area of Responsibility (where it takes the local name "Inday") and is now raising serious concern for Taiwan and eastern China, with forecasts of 16-metre waves and torrential monsoon-enhanced rainfall ([PhilStar](https://www.philstar.com/headlines/weather/2026/07/07/2540442/super-typhoon-bavi-may-enter-par-tuesday-be-named-inday); [3bmeteo, 8 July](https://www.3bmeteo.com/giornale-meteo/cronaca-meteo-super-tifone-bavi-entra-area-filippine-ora-fa-paura-taiwan-cina-orientale-100786625)).

The AHA Centre's weekly disaster update, posted to ReliefWeb late on 7 July, records 18 disaster events across the ASEAN region for the week to 5 July, including droughts, floods, landslides, storms and wind-related disasters in Indonesia, Thailand and Viet Nam ([ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/indonesia/asean-weekly-disaster-update-week-27-29-june-5-july-2026); [AHA Centre](https://ahacentre.org/weekly-disaster-update/weekly-disaster-update-29-june-5-july-2026/)).

Indonesia's flagship free school meals programme is facing allegations of corruption and waste, raising questions about the delivery of a scheme central to the country's child-nutrition agenda ([Al Jazeera, 8 July](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/8/indonesias-free-meals-programme-plagued-by-alleged-corruption-and-waste)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa
The US says it is conducting a new wave of strikes on Iran as the ceasefire falters, with explosions reported at Sirik, Qeshm and Bandar Abbas following attacks around the Strait of Hormuz; Iranian state media report a firefighter was killed in an attack on Iranshahr airport ([Al Jazeera, 8 July](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/8/us-says-conducting-new-wave-of-strikes-on-iran-as-ceasefire-falters-2); [live updates](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/8/iran-war-live-us-bombs-sirik-qeshm-bandar-abbas-over-hormuz-attacks)). The renewed hostilities deepen an already severe regional humanitarian and supply-chain crisis centred on the Strait of Hormuz ([Al Jazeera analysis, 8 July](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/8/why-have-us-iran-strikes-resumed-and-what-does-it-mean-for-peace-talks)).

A UN inquiry has urged the release of Dr Hussam Abu Safia, the Palestinian doctor jailed by Israel, amid continuing protests calling for his freedom ([Al Jazeera, 8 July](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/8/un-inquiry-urges-release-of-palestinian-doctor-jailed-by-israel)). Separately, the former Indian judge behind a damning UN report on the treatment of children in Gaza spoke about the risks involved in the inquiry's work ([Al Jazeera, 8 July](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/8/there-are-risks-ex-india-judge-behind-damning-un-report-on-gaza-children)). Inside Gaza, community elders are stepping in to settle disputes in place of police, a sign of strained civil administration ([Al Jazeera live update](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/8/iran-war-live-us-bombs-sirik-qeshm-bandar-abbas-over-hormuz-attacks?update=4751014)).

The United States has moved to delist Syria as a "state sponsor of terrorism", a step with significant implications for sanctions relief, reconstruction financing and humanitarian operations in the country ([Al Jazeera, 8 July](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/8/us-moves-to-delist-syria-as-a-state-sponsor-of-terrorism)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World
International agencies say the global economy has proven resilient to the shock of the Iran war so far, but warn the impact lingers, with elevated energy and food prices continuing to fall hardest on import-dependent low-income countries ([Al Jazeera live update, 9 July](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/8/iran-war-live-us-bombs-sirik-qeshm-bandar-abbas-over-hormuz-attacks?update=4750994)).

In Ukraine, Humanitarian Coordinator Matthias Schmale strongly condemned another wave of Russian attacks on energy infrastructure that killed and injured civilians and left thousands exposed as services were cut, in a statement posted among the latest updates on ReliefWeb ([ReliefWeb updates](https://reliefweb.int/updates)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards
Super Typhoon BAVI-26 (GDACS Red alert, Category 5, 287 km/h) is the most significant active hazard, having swept Guam and the Northern Mariana Islands and now threatening the northern Philippines, Taiwan and eastern China; an EC/ECHO daily map for the event was issued on 7 July ([GDACS](https://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=22&eventtype=TC)).

Response continues to the magnitude 7.2 and 7.5 earthquakes in Venezuela, with EC/ECHO producing updated daily mapping of the affected area ([GDACS/ERCC map](https://erccportal.jrc.ec.europa.eu/ECHOProducts//Maps#/maps/5714)). The June Philippines earthquake and tsunami disaster also remains listed as ongoing ([ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/eq-2026-000083-phl)).
