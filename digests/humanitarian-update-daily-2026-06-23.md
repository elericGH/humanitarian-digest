*Produced Tuesday, 23 June 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Eastern Australia continues to experience a drought event that has now lasted over 89 days, having begun in late February 2026. GDACS classifies the event as [Eastern Australia-2026 (DR 1018457)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=6&eventtype=DR), noting drought indicators consistent with mild to severe anomalies affecting approximately 159,232 km² — primarily in southeastern Queensland and northeastern New South Wales — with minor impact reported for agricultural land. The drought is ongoing with no current end date.

Adding longer-term concern, forecasters are warning of a potentially powerful El Niño developing in coming months. Multiple outlets reported on 19 June 2026 that UN Secretary-General described the possibility of a "Godzilla" super El Niño as "the urgent climate warning it is," with unusually strong Pacific conditions identified as a key driver ([aa.com.tr, 19 Jun 2026](https://www.aa.com.tr/en/environment/-godzilla-el-nino-scientific-warning-or-media-hype/3972261)). Near-record global grain stocks are seen as a partial buffer, though food security in Australia's already drought-affected regions could face compounding pressure ([The Cattle Site, 19 Jun 2026](https://www.thecattlesite.com/news/record-grain-stocks-seen-cushioning-blow-from-super-el-nino)).

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Typhoon MEKKHALA-26 reached Category 4 intensity (maximum sustained winds of 231 km/h) as it moved across Japan between 18 and 22 June 2026. GDACS assessed the humanitarian impact as low given Japan's high coping capacity, and issued a green alert ([GDACS TC 1001277](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001277&episodeid=13&eventtype=TC)). The storm exposed approximately 1.5 million people in Category 1 wind zones or higher, with a maximum storm surge of 0.5 m recorded. As of 22 June, PAGASA was monitoring a new Low Pressure Area inside the Philippine Area of Responsibility following separate Typhoon Francisco, signalling continued active weather in the western Pacific.

China experienced ongoing flooding between 6 and 20 June 2026, with 31,442 people displaced across affected provinces. GDACS rates the event as green (low humanitarian impact) but notes the scale of displacement ([GDACS FL 1103933](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103933&episodeid=7&eventtype=FL)). Heavy rain and flooding were also reported in Taiwan in mid-June, per Copernicus EMS GloFAS data aggregated by GDACS.

The Council on Strategic Risks published its Military Responses to Climate Hazards (MiRCH) roundup for February–May 2026 on 22 June, documenting 30 military deployments to address climate hazards in 22 countries. The report noted that in the first half of 2026 the Pacific experienced particularly challenging cyclones and extreme precipitation events ([MiRCH Roundup, 22 Jun 2026](https://councilonstrategicrisks.org/2026/06/22/mirch-roundup-february-2026-may-2026-militaries-respond-to-extreme-weather-events-in-22-countries/)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Pakistan's National Disaster Management Authority (NDMA) issued a nationwide critical weather alert on 22 June 2026, warning of thunderstorms, strong winds, heavy rainfall, urban flooding, and glacier-related flood risks across multiple provinces over a 12-to-24-hour window. The alert follows an earlier flood event in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Province (17–19 June) in which 5 people were killed, as recorded by GDACS ([FL 1103953](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103953&episodeid=1&eventtype=FL)). The NDMA urged vulnerable communities — particularly those in glacial flood-prone areas — to remain on high alert ([Daily Times, 22 Jun 2026](https://dailytimes.com.pk/1511593/ndma-warns-of-critical-weather-window-nationwide/)).

A deepening geopolitical dimension to Pakistan's water insecurity emerged on 22 June, when CNBC reported that Pakistan's defence minister warned water could become a cause for war if Islamabad's national interests under the Indus Waters Treaty are threatened. The tensions with India stem from disputes that escalated following the countries' most recent military conflict, and are now compounding humanitarian vulnerability for communities dependent on the Indus river system ([CNBC, 22 Jun 2026](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/06/22/india-pakistan-indus-waters-treaty-water-dispute-war-risk.html)).

Multiple large forest fires continued burning across the Democratic Republic of Congo as of 22 June 2026. The largest active fire (GDACS WF 1028934), monitored since 9 June, covered 15,215 ha with 289 people in the burned area. Several simultaneous fire events have been tracked by GDACS in recent weeks, with the total active DRC wildfire footprint exceeding 60,000 ha across multiple events. GDACS rates current humanitarian impact as low but continues monitoring ([GDACS WF 1028934](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1028934&episodeid=7&eventtype=WF)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

A potential "super El Niño" is drawing global attention from climate scientists and humanitarian planners alike. Articles published 19 June 2026 note that forecasters are warning of a powerful El Niño event, with UN University scientist Jack O'Connor describing it as "rare, but there is an elevated risk of potentially impactful weather changes." Near-record wheat and rice stocks are expected to cushion the immediate food security impact, but the event could significantly disrupt growing seasons in vulnerable nations across Sub-Saharan Africa, South Asia, and Central America ([Anadolu Agency, 19 Jun 2026](https://www.aa.com.tr/en/environment/-godzilla-el-nino-scientific-warning-or-media-hype/3972261)).

A M5.5 earthquake struck near St. Helena on 20 June 2026 at a depth of 10 km. GDACS assessed it as a green (low impact) event, with few people affected at MMI III intensity, given the island's small population ([GDACS EQ 1547584](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1547584&episodeid=1713427&eventtype=EQ)). No tsunami was generated.
