# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Wednesday, 27 May 2026
*Produced Wednesday, 27 May 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The Emergency Action Alliance's Myanmar Humanitarian Emergency appeal has secured significant Australian Government backing, with Australia committing $3.5 million for Australian NGOs to deliver food, water, sanitation, health and education aid following the devastating earthquake that killed over 3,600 people and injured more than 5,000. An additional $500,000 has been directed to the EAA appeal, $3 million for emergency supplies via the ASEAN Coordinating Centre, and an Australia Assists rapid response team has been deployed for on-ground coordination. The EAA has condemned ongoing military airstrikes in affected areas as further complicating aid delivery. ([Emergency Action Alliance](https://emergencyaction.org.au/australias-response-to-myanmar-earthquake/))

The Emergency Action Alliance's Middle East Humanitarian Emergency appeal remains active, with 15 Australian member organisations delivering aid across Gaza, Lebanon, the West Bank, and Syria to millions of people who urgently need shelter, food, and basic supplies as conflict continues to intensify. EAA charities are working through trusted local partners and UN networks to provide food parcels, clean water, safe shelter, hygiene kits, and medical support. Australians are urged to donate as the scale of need far outstrips current funding. ([Emergency Action Alliance](https://emergencyaction.org.au/middle-east-appeal/))

ACFID has called on the Australian Government to seize the opportunity to increase its global leadership on humanitarian aid as international budgets — particularly from the United States — continue to decline sharply in 2026. The sector peak body has highlighted Australia's moral and strategic interest in stepping into the gap left by US aid cuts, particularly across the Indo-Pacific. ([ACFID](https://acfid.asn.au/news-and-media/))

Australian NGOs and the Greens have jointly urged the Albanese Government to reform counter-terrorism laws that they argue are acting as an effective "handbrake" on humanitarian aid delivery in conflict-affected areas where listed organisations control territory. The campaign argues current laws create untenable legal risk for Australian aid workers and organisations, preventing timely and effective delivery of life-saving assistance. ([ACFID](https://acfid.asn.au/ngos-greens-urge-albanese-government-to-reform-terror-laws-warn-of-aid-handbrake/))

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Humanitarian partners continue scaling up the Tropical Cyclone Maila response in Solomon Islands and Papua New Guinea, shifting from immediate relief to stabilisation and early recovery, though significant gaps remain. Displacement is widespread with affected populations still in evacuation centres; 41 health facilities are only partially functional, and around 180 expectant mothers urgently require access to safe delivery services. Education disruption is affecting more than 11,000 children across 84 schools, while protection risks including gender-based violence and psychosocial distress are rising. ([ReliefWeb / OCHA Pacific Humanitarian Team](https://reliefweb.int/report/solomon-islands/tropical-cyclone-maila-pacific-humanitarian-team-situation-report-no4-19-may-2026))

Rising global fuel prices are hitting Pacific Island countries with particular severity, forcing families to choose between school transport and food. The Marshall Islands has declared a 90-day state of economic emergency and Tuvalu has declared a state of emergency amid daily blackouts; in Fiji, fuel prices rose suddenly with unleaded up 20%, diesel up 35%, and kerosene up 42%. Save the Children Australia warns the crisis — compounding ongoing disaster recovery from Tropical Cyclones Maila, Sinlaku, and Vaianu — will worsen child nutrition, school attendance, and protection outcomes across the region. ([ReliefWeb / Save the Children Australia](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/when-price-fuel-becomes-price-childhood-pacific-families-crisis))

Tonga's ongoing dengue fever outbreak recorded 66 confirmed new cases in its latest situation report (25 May 2026), with two people currently hospitalised. Cases are concentrated on Tongatapu Island and predominantly affect the 11-14 age group. OCHA Pacific continues monitoring the outbreak alongside broader epidemic and emerging disease alerts across the Pacific region as of 26 May 2026. ([ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/tonga/tonga-dengue-fever-outbreak-situation-report-23-25th-may-2026-ento); [OCHA Pacific disease surveillance](https://reliefweb.int/map/world/epidemic-and-emerging-disease-alerts-pacific-26-may-2026))

In Myanmar, approximately 3.7 million people are internally displaced — up from around 300,000 before 2021 — with cross-border displacement projected to approach 5.2 million. The UN's 2026 Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan projects over 16 million people will require humanitarian assistance this year; recovery from the March 2025 earthquake remains slow, with 24,200 houses damaged and 132 health facilities impaired. Armed clashes continue across Sagaing, Magway, Shan, Kayah, Kayin, Kachin, and Rakhine states. ([ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/myanmar-crisis-situation-analysis-period-110526-170526))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

The IRC is warning that the rapidly escalating Ebola outbreak in eastern DRC could become the deadliest on record without urgent international action, with over 900 suspected cases and at least 223 deaths reported across DRC and Uganda, including in major transport hubs such as Goma and Kampala. The IRC identifies three critical risk factors: the outbreak is spreading faster than the response; conflict and displacement are accelerating regional spread; and severe global aid cuts have left the frontline health system weaker now than during the catastrophic 2018-2020 outbreak that killed more than 2,000 people. The international community is urged to act immediately. ([ReliefWeb / IRC](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/irc-watchlist-flash-alert-ebola-outbreak-eastern-drc-spreading-faster-response))

Days 955-960 of the war in Gaza: renewed military escalation is causing increasing civilian casualties and displacement, with Israeli forces issuing multiple evacuation and shelter-in-place orders in areas west of the "Yellow Line" zone including Gaza City. UNRWA reports malnutrition admissions increased 21% between January-April 2026 compared to the same period in 2025, despite the earlier ceasefire, and is calling for unimpeded humanitarian access. The Israeli Minister of Finance is also advancing demolition orders against the Bedouin community of Khan al Ahmar, home to over 700 Palestine Refugees in the E1 corridor. ([ReliefWeb / UNRWA](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/unrwa-situation-report-223-humanitarian-crisis-gaza-strip-and-west-bank-including-east-jerusalem-enar))

Sudan remains the world's largest hunger crisis, with an estimated 19.5 million people experiencing acute food insecurity as the conflict between the Sudanese Armed Forces and the Rapid Support Forces enters its fourth year. Famine conditions have been confirmed in el-Fasher (North Darfur) and Kadugli (South Kordofan), and independent estimates suggest the true death toll may exceed 150,000 people. The crisis is compounded by the displacement of millions and ongoing obstruction of humanitarian access. ([ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/sudan-crisis-situation-analysis-period-110526-170526))

Lebanon's humanitarian situation remains fragile following a ceasefire between Israel and Hezbollah, with violations continuing and a further ceasefire extension coming into effect on 15 May 2026 following US-brokered talks. UNRWA reports 1,264 displaced people from 372 families are currently registered in its two emergency shelters, with the situation described as unpredictable and at continued risk of escalation. OCHA's weekly West and Central Africa update (19-25 May) also flags ongoing instability across Nigeria, Mali, Niger, and the broader sub-region. ([ReliefWeb / UNRWA Lebanon](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/unrwa-situation-report-11-lebanon-emergency-response-2026-21-may-2026-enar); [OCHA West Africa](https://reliefweb.int/report/nigeria/west-and-central-africa-latest-events-glance-19-25-may-2026))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

The UN and 98 humanitarian partners launched a USD 710.5 million appeal in Dhaka on 20 May 2026 for the Rohingya humanitarian crisis, covering minimum lifesaving assistance for up to 1.56 million people. Since early 2024, some 150,000 Rohingya have newly arrived, straining camps that already host 1.2 million refugees in Cox's Bazar and on Bhasan Char. The appeal is 26% lower than 2025 levels, and funding cuts are already impacting camp services, with 35% of households fully reliant on humanitarian food assistance. ([ReliefWeb / UNHCR](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/un-and-partners-appeal-usd-7105-million-meet-critical-needs-rohingya-refugees-and-bangladeshi-host-communities-enbn))

The Government of Zambia, WFP, and the UK Met Office have launched a USD 3.6 million initiative to strengthen climate monitoring and early warning systems, upgrading and automating 21 existing surface weather observation stations and installing four upper-air stations. The initiative responds directly to the El Niño-induced drought of 2023-2024 that left an estimated 5 million people facing severe hunger, and will help Zambia meet Global Basic Observing Network standards. ([ReliefWeb / WFP](https://reliefweb.int/report/zambia/government-zambia-wfp-and-uk-met-office-launch-usd-36-million-initiative-strengthen-climate-monitoring-and-early-warning-systems))

Flooding in Timor-Leste's Manatuto and Covalima municipalities on 23 May 2026 caused flooding and a landslide that blocked road access in Ainaro. Cruz Vermelha de Timor-Leste (CVTL), the Fire Department, and the Civil Protection Authority conducted rapid assessments of 152 affected families and supported evacuations, though road damage delayed access to some areas. No international assistance was requested. ([ReliefWeb / IFRC](https://reliefweb.int/report/timor-leste/tls-flood-05-2026-tls-flood-response-2026-05-26))

OCHA has published a new anticipatory action framework for cholera in Mozambique, establishing trigger mechanisms based on weekly epidemiological surveillance data to activate pre-agreed humanitarian actions before an outbreak escalates. The framework covers four pillars — surveillance and lab support, case management and infection prevention, WASH, and risk communication — recognising that cholera remains endemic in Mozambique due to limited safe water access and recurring climate shocks. ([ReliefWeb / OCHA](https://reliefweb.int/report/mozambique/mozambique-anticipatory-action-framework-cholera-2026))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

No active sudden-onset hazards of note reaching Red alert level. GDACS currently reports two Orange-rated drought alerts: one covering Angola, DRC, Republic of Congo, Gabon, and Zambia (272,473 km² of affected agricultural land), and one for Madagascar (223,673 km²). These reflect ongoing food security stress in Central and Southern Africa and compound existing humanitarian crises in those regions. ([GDACS — Central Africa drought](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018344); [GDACS — Madagascar drought](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018431))

Seismic activity in the last 48 hours includes a magnitude 6.9 earthquake in Chile (25 May, depth 101km, ~160,000 people in MMI VI zone, GDACS Green), a magnitude 5.6 earthquake in Indonesia's north Sulawesi region (25 May, Green), and a magnitude 5.5 earthquake in the Philippines (24 May, ~7,000 people affected, Green). Green-rated flood alerts are active for Afghanistan (17 deaths, 24 May), Malaysia (336 displaced, ongoing since 17 May), and Bulgaria (70 displaced, 25 May). No cyclone, tsunami, or volcanic events are currently active. ([GDACS Chile EQ](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1542497); [GDACS Indonesia EQ](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1542484); [GDACS Afghanistan flood](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103890))
