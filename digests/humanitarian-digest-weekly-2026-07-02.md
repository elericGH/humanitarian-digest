# 🌍 Weekly Humanitarian Digest — 25 June 2026 – 2 July 2026
*Produced Thursday, 2 July 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The 24 June double earthquake in Venezuela dominated the Australian humanitarian sector this week. ACFID launched a consolidated [Venezuela Earthquake Appeal](https://acfid.asn.au/venezuela-earthquake-appeal/) on 29 June, reporting that successive magnitude 7.2 and 7.5 earthquakes — the larger being the biggest recorded in Venezuela since 1900 — had left more than 400 people confirmed dead and over 4,300 injured as of 26 June, with more than 500 aftershocks recorded. ACFID notes the UN's Humanitarian Needs and Response Plan already estimated 7.9 million Venezuelans would require assistance in 2026, with the US$606 million plan only 23.23% funded as of June. Six ACFID members have verified Code-of-Conduct-compliant appeals open: Caritas Australia, UNICEF Australia, Save the Children Australia, Plan International Australia, Act for Peace, and Australian Red Cross, whose Global Emergency Fund donations from 26 June to 31 July will support the Venezuela response.

The [Emergency Action Alliance](https://emergencyaction.org.au/venezuela-earthquake/) is monitoring the Venezuela earthquake closely but has not yet launched a centralised joint appeal (page updated 2 July). EAA describes widespread devastation, families sleeping outside or in overcrowded shelters amid continuing aftershocks, and severely disrupted access to clean water, food, electricity and medical care. It links to five live member appeals — Caritas Australia, Plan International Australia, Australia for UNHCR, ADRA Australia, and Act for Peace — and says it will share a centralised appeal "if we can."

Separately, ACFID issued an [urgent appeal on the Ebola outbreak](https://acfid.asn.au/act-now-to-contain-the-ebola-outbreak/) (29 June) covering the Bundibugyo-strain outbreak in eastern DRC (North Kivu, South Kivu, Ituri) and neighbouring Uganda, which WHO has declared a Public Health Emergency of International Concern. There is no approved vaccine or therapeutic for this strain, and cross-border movement between DRC, Uganda and South Sudan raises regional spread risk; epicentre Ituri Province already has 1.9 million people needing humanitarian assistance. Responding members include UNICEF Australia (infection prevention supplies, PPE, WASH), Australia for UNHCR (border monitoring, Goma transit-centre prevention, screening and isolation scale-up in Uganda), and ActionAid Australia (protective kits, handwashing stations, awareness training, screening sites).

Closer to home, GDACS continues to track an ongoing [Eastern Australia drought](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018457&episodeid=6&eventtype=DR), now in its 18th week. DFAT's news feed could not be retrieved this run, so no departmental announcements are reflected this week.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

The Pacific Ring of Fire was active this week. GDACS logged a [magnitude 5.7 earthquake in Tonga](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549627&episodeid=1715727&eventtype=EQ) and a [magnitude 5.6 in Fiji](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549555&episodeid=1715635&eventtype=EQ) on 1 July, alongside a [magnitude 6.0 in Japan](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549565&episodeid=1715647&eventtype=EQ) the same day and a [magnitude 5.8 in Japan on 27 June](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1548889&episodeid=1714888&eventtype=EQ). No major humanitarian impact had been flagged for these events on the GDACS board as of publication.

The western Pacific typhoon season intensified. [Tropical Cyclone BAVI-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=5&eventtype=TC) was listed by GDACS at maximum sustained winds of 259 km/h on 2 July — the strongest storm on the board this week — and warrants close monitoring. [Tropical Cyclone MEKKHALA-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001277&episodeid=36&eventtype=TC) (Green alert) tracked toward Japan between 18 and 27 June, reaching Category 4 with 231 km/h winds and up to 1 m storm surge, though GDACS assessed low humanitarian impact with no population exposed at Category 1 or higher. Two further systems, [DOUGLAS-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001280&episodeid=4&eventtype=TC) and [TEN-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001281&episodeid=2&eventtype=TC), were active at 74 km/h on 2 July, and [HIGOS-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001278&episodeid=19&eventtype=TC) peaked at 102 km/h on 27 June. GDACS also lists ongoing drought alerts for [South-East Asia](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018545&episodeid=6&eventtype=DR) (23 weeks) and monsoon-season floods in [India (30 June)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103972&episodeid=2&eventtype=FL) and [China (27 June)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103933&episodeid=10&eventtype=FL).

On the social-protection front, East Asia Forum examined [Thailand's new lottery-backed pension scheme](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/06/30/thailand-rolls-the-dice-on-lottery-backed-pensions/) (30 June), which aims to draw the country's roughly 20.9 million informal workers — 52.4% of the labour force, of whom only 13.7% had joined the National Savings Fund by April 2026 — into retirement saving via digital tickets of up to 3,000 baht (US$92) a month, with the full spend banked into individual pension accounts. The World Bank has flagged the model as potentially relevant for other low- and middle-income countries, though equity and gambling-risk questions remain.

The economic aftershocks of the US–Iran conflict are also being felt across Asia. East Asia Forum's editorial ["Keeping the Strait of Hormuz open and free to shipping is a priority for Asia"](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/06/29/keeping-the-strait-of-hormuz-open-and-free-to-shipping-is-a-priority-for-asia/) (29 June) argues Iran's attempt to impose a de facto toll regime on Hormuz shipping threatens Asia's energy supply, with the burden of disruption falling disproportionately on smaller, trade-dependent Asian economies — a trend line worth watching for its knock-on effects on food and fuel prices in vulnerable countries (see also the companion piece of [28 June](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/06/28/irans-strait-of-hormuz-claim-must-not-become-a-precedent/)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

*Note: several primary sources (OCHA, WFP, UNHCR, ICRC, The New Humanitarian, Al Jazeera) were unreachable this run; the items below were sighted on the ReliefWeb front page on 2 July 2026, and exact publication dates could not be independently verified.*

In Gaza, the Shelter Cluster and NRC report that [Israel's restrictions on shelter materials have left nearly one million people — about 170,000 households — living in tents](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/gaza-israels-shelter-restrictions-force-nearly-million-people-endure-summer-heat-tents-nrc) as summer heat sets in. A further 5,000 households are sleeping outdoors and 52,000 households are in overcrowded shelters.

The joint FAO-WFP [Hunger Hotspots outlook for June–November 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/hunger-hotspots-fao-wfp-early-warnings-acute-food-insecurity-june-november-2026-outlook) warns that 13 hotspots face significant risk of worsening acute food insecurity. Sudan, South Sudan, Yemen and Palestine remain hotspots of highest concern, with northeast Nigeria and Somalia newly elevated to the highest-concern list as conditions deteriorate towards catastrophic levels. Consistent with that warning, MSF reports that [malnutrition admissions are overwhelming Bay Regional Hospital in Baidoa, Somalia](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/malnutrition-admissions-are-overwhelming-baidoa-hospital-somalia): 1,400 patients were admitted to its therapeutic feeding centre between March and May, and even after expanding from 50 to 125 beds the facility is being pushed beyond its limits.

In the Sahel, OCHA says [more than 24 million people urgently need assistance](https://reliefweb.int/report/mali/24-million-people-sahel-urgently-need-aid-and-world-needs-do-more), with violence in the Central Sahel spreading beyond traditional borders and spilling into coastal West Africa — making the region one of the main epicentres of violence on the continent.

The [DRC/Uganda Ebola outbreak](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/ep-2026-000071-cod) (ongoing since May 2026) continues to worsen — see the Australian Priority Alerts section for the ACFID appeal. The IRC warns that [women and girls face a double threat](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/women-and-girls-face-double-threat-ebola-outbreak-response-must-address-both-irc): they are usually the primary caretakers of the sick, and increased water-collection and care burdens force them to travel further on foot, raising exposure to violence. GDACS also logged repeated large [forest fires in DRC](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1029058&episodeid=8&eventtype=WF) during the week (14,816 ha on 29 June; 11,197 ha on 26 June), compounding pressure on an already stretched response.

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

The Venezuela earthquake is the week's defining global emergency. Beyond the casualty and funding figures in the Australian section, the international mapping response spun up rapidly: the Copernicus Emergency Management Service activated [rapid mapping (EMSR884)](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR884/download) with successive products released 26–28 June, an [EC/ECHO daily map](https://erccportal.jrc.ec.europa.eu/ECHOProducts//Maps#/maps/5710) was published 26 June, and a [Virtual OSOCC coordination page](https://vosocc.unocha.org/Report.aspx?page=FOaxxxplusc90yEam9aiSkfREX3Axxxequalxxxequal) opened 25 June — all tracked via [GDACS](https://gdacs.org/).

FAO and WFP launched a [joint US$202 million appeal](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/bracing-el-nino-fao-and-wfp-launch-joint-appeal-protect-88-million-people-extreme-weather-events) for anticipatory action ahead of El Niño-driven extreme weather, aiming to protect 8.8 million people in the most vulnerable regions through urgent, flexible funding before climate shocks hit food security, livelihoods and agriculture.

NRC published the 10th edition of its [World's Most Neglected Displacement Crises](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/worlds-most-neglected-displacement-crises-2025-10th-edition) list for 2025: Sudan, DR Congo, Colombia, Yemen, Afghanistan, Honduras, Ecuador, Cameroon, Nigeria and Mozambique — with DR Congo appearing for the tenth consecutive year, and three Latin American countries (Colombia, Honduras, Ecuador) underlining how far neglect extends beyond the headline conflicts.

Elsewhere on the GDACS board this week: a [magnitude 6.0 earthquake in Mexico](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549440&episodeid=1715504&eventtype=EQ) (30 June), a [magnitude 5.5 in the United States](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549176&episodeid=1715218&eventtype=EQ) (29 June), and floods in the [United States](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103888&episodeid=35&eventtype=FL) and [Ghana](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103954&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL) (both 30 June).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

The [Venezuela M7.5 earthquake](https://gdacs.org/) response remains the top sudden-onset emergency, with Copernicus EMS rapid-mapping activations continuing through 26–28 June and 400+ confirmed deaths (see Tier 3 and Australian sections).

[Tropical Cyclone BAVI-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=5&eventtype=TC) in the western Pacific reached 259 km/h sustained winds on 2 July and is the strongest active storm globally; its track and exposure should be reassessed daily. [MEKKHALA-26](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001277&episodeid=36&eventtype=TC) passed Japan as a Category 4 (Green alert, low expected impact) by 27 June.

New flood events were logged in [Ghana](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103954&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL), the [United States](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103888&episodeid=35&eventtype=FL) and [India](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103972&episodeid=2&eventtype=FL) on 30 June and in [China](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103933&episodeid=10&eventtype=FL) on 27 June. Moderate earthquakes struck Tonga (M5.7), Fiji (M5.6), Japan (M6.0 and M5.8), Afghanistan ([M5.5, 1 July](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549600&episodeid=1715684&eventtype=EQ)), Mexico (M6.0) and the United States (M5.5) during the week. Long-running drought alerts persist for Eastern Australia (18 weeks), South-East Asia (23 weeks) and [Madagascar (31 weeks)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1018431&episodeid=6&eventtype=DR).
