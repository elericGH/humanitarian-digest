## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia's humanitarian community continues to respond to crises across the Pacific and globally. GDACS monitoring indicates active wildfires across Australia on 12 June 2026, with multiple green-level alerts issued for fires of 5,000–10,000 hectares — consistent with early winter burn-off conditions ([GDACS Wildfire Alerts, 12 June 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028886)). These are not currently classified as humanitarian emergencies but are being monitored.

At the regional level, multiple seismic events in Australia's near neighbourhood — Papua New Guinea, Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, and the Philippines — continue to bear watch given Australia's role as a first responder in the Pacific. A magnitude 4.5 earthquake struck Papua New Guinea on 12 June, with approximately 2.2 million people within 100 km ([GDACS EQ Papua New Guinea, 12 June 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1545803)). No DFAT or ACFID emergency advisories were directly retrievable for the 12–13 June window due to source access limitations; DFAT and ACFID sites are listed as unreachable this run (see footer).

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

The Philippines is experiencing ongoing aftershocks following the devastating magnitude 7.8 Sarangani earthquake that struck Mindanao on 8 June 2026. As of 12 June, at least four further earthquakes (M4.5–M5.2) were recorded in the Philippines, with populations of up to 2.5 million within 100 km of the epicentres. Over 346,000 people (75,324 families) remain affected by the original quake, with 45,609 people in or near 32 evacuation centres. UN Women issued a Gender Alert warning that the disaster exacerbates pre-existing inequalities for women, girls, Indigenous Peoples, and LGBTQIA+ persons ([UN Women Gender Alert No.1 — Philippines, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/gender-alert-no-1-philippines-78-magnitude-sarangani-earthquake-12-june-2026)).

UNICEF's Sri Lanka humanitarian situation report (No. 13), released 12 June, provides an update on the ongoing recovery from Cyclone Ditwah, which struck Sri Lanka's Eastern coast on 28 November 2025, killing at least 646 people and affecting over 2.2 million. Approximately 1,337 people remain displaced in 18 safety centres. UNICEF has requested US$7.8 million for the response; a $1.2 million funding gap remains and response is transitioning to early recovery ([UNICEF Sri Lanka Sitrep No. 13, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sri-lanka/unicef-sri-lanka-humanitarian-situation-report-no-13-cyclone-ditwah-11-june-2026)).

The Solomon Islands and Vanuatu also registered seismic activity on 12 June — a M4.7 earthquake affecting 20,000 people near the Solomon Islands ([GDACS EQ Solomon Islands, 12 June 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1545926)) and a M4.5 quake near Vanuatu at significant depth ([GDACS EQ Vanuatu, 12 June 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1545800)). Both are green-level alerts with no reported casualties at this stage.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Lebanon continues to face severe humanitarian conditions despite a nominal ceasefire extension. An Israeli airstrike near Hiram Hospital in Tyre on 11 June injured 10 staff — the fifth reported incident at this hospital since March 2026. As of 11 June, 134,800 displaced people (35,700 families) are sheltering in 642 collective shelters, with nearly one million people displaced nationally. IOM reports over 48,000 migrants among the displaced population, facing additional barriers to assistance ([OCHA Lebanon Flash Update #34, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-flash-update-34-escalation-hostilities-lebanon-11-june-2026); [IOM Lebanon Flash Appeal, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/iom-lebanon-flash-appeal-crisis-response-march-august-2026)).

A major UN report released on 12 June documents the catastrophic toll of the Gaza conflict on children: over 21,000 children have been killed in Israeli attacks since October 2023. Even following the ceasefire announcement, a further 183 verified child deaths have occurred — including 48 in incidents where only children were killed. A 7-month-old infant was shot dead near a Hebron checkpoint on 6 June 2026 ([OHCHR/UN Women — Surviving Childhood Under Occupation, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/surviving-childhood-under-occupation-catastrophic-harm-facing-palestinian-children-enar)).

The Ebola Bundibugyo outbreak in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo is spreading in an already severely weakened context. UNICEF's Executive Director, visiting Bunia in Ituri Province, warned that more children will be affected in coming days. Over 50% of children under five in Ituri are chronically malnourished, and more than one in five have received no vaccinations. Families are avoiding hospitals out of Ebola fear, leading to preventable deaths from other causes. The EU's DG ECHO published a daily operational map tracking the outbreak's geographic spread into Uganda ([UNICEF DRC Ebola warning, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/we-may-see-more-children-affected-days-ahead-unicef-warns-ebola-spreads-eastern-drc); [EU DG ECHO DRC/Uganda Ebola map, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/map/democratic-republic-congo/democratic-republic-congo-uganda-ebola-bundibugyo-virus-disease-outbreak-and-eu-response-dg-echo-daily-map-12062026)).

In Afghanistan, the Taliban's Ministry for the Promotion of Virtue arrested a female MSF medical worker in Herat on 6 June on dress code grounds, detaining her for two days. On 9 June, a peaceful demonstration against restrictions on women was violently dispersed by police using firearms and whips. MSF warns these restrictions are causing women and children to avoid hospitals, resulting in preventable deaths ([MSF Afghanistan statement, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-msf-denounces-detention-its-staff-amid-growing-restrictions-women)).

Nigeria is experiencing a cholera outbreak in Borno State, with 7,233 suspected cases and 46 deaths (case fatality rate 0.6%) as of 4 June. Three cholera treatment centres are operational (MSF, Save the Children, FHI360), but the ongoing rainy season is expected to worsen transmission — driven by conflict-linked displacement, overcrowding, and unsafe water ([UNICEF Nigeria Cholera Flash Update, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/nigeria/unicef-nigeria-humanitarian-flash-update-cholera-outbreak-borno-state-07-june-2026)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

Haiti has opened its first state-supported safe houses for women and girls fleeing gang violence and sexual assault. In 2025, BINUH verified 1,863 sexual violence cases — a 163% increase on 2024. Gang violence has displaced 1.47 million people nationally, including 790,000 women and girls. Gender-based violence was reported in all 22 displacement sites surveyed in the country ([UN Women Haiti, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/haiti/haiti-opens-first-state-supported-safe-houses-women-and-girls-gangs-continue-weaponize-sexual-violence)).

OCHA's Latin America & Caribbean weekly situation update (12 June) covers ongoing displacement, food insecurity, and gang violence across Mexico, Haiti, Honduras, Nicaragua, and Venezuela ([OCHA LAC Weekly Update, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/mexico/latin-america-caribbean-weekly-situation-update-12-june-2026)).

Iraq is responding to a Crimean-Congo Hemorrhagic Fever (CCHF) outbreak across several governorates, reported as of 11 June by the Iraqi Red Crescent Society. CCHF has a case fatality rate of 10–40% and is spread through livestock contact or tick bites — a significant concern in rural and displaced populations ([IFRC/Iraqi Red Crescent CCHF update, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/iraq/irq-epidemic-05-2026-crimean-congo-hemorrhagic-fever-cchf-iraqircs-2026-06-11)).

Eastern Africa's humanitarian response continues at scale. WFP and OCHA's regional overview of cash and voucher assistance for January–March 2026 covers Sudan, Ethiopia, Kenya, South Sudan, Uganda, Somalia, Rwanda, and Burundi — reflecting the ongoing breadth of need across the Horn of Africa and Sudan conflict zone ([WFP/OCHA Eastern Africa CVA Overview, 12 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/eastern-africa-regional-cash-and-voucher-assistance-overview-january-march-2026)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Philippines — Ongoing Aftershock Sequence (M7.8 Sarangani Earthquake):** Multiple earthquakes (M4.5–M5.2) struck the Philippines on 12 June 2026, continuing the aftershock sequence from the 8 June M7.8 Sarangani event. Over 346,000 people remain affected; the aftershock sequence poses ongoing risk to displaced populations in evacuation centres ([GDACS EQ Philippines, 12 June 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1545967)).

**Papua New Guinea — M4.5 Earthquake (12 June 2026):** A magnitude 4.5 earthquake struck Papua New Guinea at 02:24 UTC, with approximately 2.2 million people within 100 km. Green alert level; no reported casualties. Relevant to Australian disaster response monitoring ([GDACS EQ PNG, 12 June 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1545803)).

**Nicaragua — Green Flood Alert (12 June 2026):** A new green-level flood alert was issued for Nicaragua on 12 June at 06:04 UTC, coinciding with the active Atlantic storm season and residual effects of Tropical Cyclone CRISTINA-26, which affected Honduras, El Salvador, and Nicaragua from 8–11 June ([GDACS Flood Alert Nicaragua, 12 June 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103938)).

**Australia — Wildfires (12 June 2026):** Multiple green-level wildfire alerts for Australia, with fires of 5,000–10,000 hectares recorded on 12 June. Not currently classified as humanitarian emergencies ([GDACS WF Australia, 12 June 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028886)).
