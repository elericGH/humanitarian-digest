## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Cyclone Maila continues to be the dominant humanitarian crisis in Australia's immediate neighbourhood. As of 30 April 2026, the UN Country Team in Papua New Guinea confirmed that assessments have now expanded to 140 wards across 23 Local Level Governments, spanning 8 districts and 5 affected provinces. Preliminary findings indicate over **83,000 people affected**, with more than **77,000 in need of humanitarian assistance** and **34 reported fatalities**. Priority needs include food assistance, shelter and non-food items (NFIs), WASH, and health services — with particular challenges reaching isolated and hard-to-access communities. The National Disaster Centre (NDC) is coordinating response through the National Emergency Operations Centre (NEOC). ([Source: UNCT PNG / ReliefWeb, 1 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/papua-new-guinea-severe-tropical-cyclone-maila-disaster-management-team-situation-report-no-06-01-may-2026))

The Australian Council for International Development (ACFID) currently has four active Consolidated Appeals open for donations from Australians: the Middle East Humanitarian Crisis Appeal, Asia Storms Appeal 2025, Sudan Consolidated Appeal 2025, and the Myanmar Earthquake Appeal. These reflect ongoing Australian civil society mobilisation across several simultaneous crises. ([Source: ACFID](https://acfid.asn.au/consolidated-appeal-middle-east-humanitarian-crisis/))

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Papua New Guinea — Cyclone Maila aftermath** remains the most acute crisis in the Pacific. The NDC/NEOC is beginning to transition from national-level coordination to sub-nationally led response and recovery operations, while humanitarian partners continue rapid assessments across the five affected provinces. ([Source: UNCT PNG / ReliefWeb, 1 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/papua-new-guinea-severe-tropical-cyclone-maila-disaster-management-team-situation-report-no-06-01-may-2026))

**Myanmar** — UNHCR published its Emergency Overview Map as of 27 April 2026, tracking the number of people displaced since February 2021 who remain displaced across Myanmar, Bangladesh, India, and Thailand. Separately, pre-monsoon downpours hit Cox's Bazar on 18 April, recording up to 43mm of rain, with conditions expected to worsen as the monsoon season intensifies in May–July. ([Source: UNHCR / ReliefWeb, 28 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/map/myanmar/myanmar-emergency-overview-map-number-people-displaced-feb-2021-and-remain-displaced-27-apr-2026))

**Pakistan** — The World Food Programme's April 2026 Market Monitor Report shows headline CPI-based annual inflation at 7.3% (up from 6.9% in February), with food inflation at 3.6% year-on-year. Higher transport, utility, and perishable food costs drove month-on-month increases, raising food security concerns. Separately, UNHCR and IOM published their 92nd Flash Update on the arrest, detention, and flow monitoring of Afghan nationals between September 2023 and April 2026. ([WFP Market Monitor](https://reliefweb.int/report/pakistan/pakistan-market-monitor-report-april-2026); [UNHCR-IOM Flash Update](https://reliefweb.int/report/pakistan/unhcr-iom-pakistan-flash-update-92-arrest-and-detentionflow-monitoring-15-sep-2023-25-april-2026))

**Philippines / International Justice** — The East Asia Forum published analysis on 1 May examining former President Rodrigo Duterte's ICC case, raising fundamental questions about whether international justice mechanisms can hold powerful figures accountable when domestic political dynamics resist cooperation. ([Source: East Asia Forum, 1 May 2026](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/05/01/dutertes-icc-case-questions-whether-justice-can-be-done/))

**Cambodia** — The FAO released its Agrometeorological Bulletin for 1–10 May 2026 for the Northern Tonle Sap Basin, providing early warning guidance on rainfall patterns and agricultural risks as the wet season approaches. ([Source: FAO / ReliefWeb, 1 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/cambodia/agrometeorological-bulletin-01-10-may-2026-across-northern-tonle-sap-basin-cambodia))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Gaza** — Save the Children warned on 1 May that plagues of rats, mice, and insects have swarmed displacement sites across Gaza as temperatures rise, escalating health risks for approximately **1.4 million people** in overcrowded sites. OCHA data from mid-April assessments shows over 80% of more than 1,600 displacement sites reported rodents or pests frequently visible; skin infections or rashes were found in nearly two-thirds of sites, lice in over 65%, and bedbugs in more than half. Children represent approximately 47% of Gaza's population, meaning around **680,000 children — about two in three** — are living in displacement sites affected by this infestation. Rodents are chewing through tents and contaminating food supplies with few safe storage facilities. ([Source: Save the Children / ReliefWeb, 1 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/gaza-two-three-children-risk-infection-due-plague-rats-and-pests))

**Lebanon** — OCHA published its Conflict Intensity Map covering 2 March to 30 April 2026, documenting the geographic distribution and severity of conflict activity across Lebanon over the two-month period. ([Source: OCHA / ReliefWeb, 1 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-conflict-intensity-2-march-30-april-2026))

**Somalia** — Radio Ergo's weekly feedback report (23–29 April 2026) captured a notable shift: more callers reported rainfall than drought, with some southern areas seeing water resource replenishment. However, flooding has become the new acute concern — in Garbaharey (Gedo region), heavy rainfall on the night of 27 April swept away two children who have not been found; flash floods were also reported in Middle Shabelle and along the Shabelle River in Hiran region. In Beledweyne, a caller reported children suffering from malnutrition, measles, and yellow fever. ([Source: Radio Ergo / ReliefWeb, 1 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/radio-ergo-weekly-feedback-report-issued-30-apr-2026))

**Madagascar** — The Health Cluster published its first bulletin for 2026 (January–March), monitoring disease trends and health system capacity. ([Source: Government of Madagascar / WHO / Health Cluster, ReliefWeb, 1 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/madagascar/madagascar-bulletin-cluster-sante-ndeg01-2026-edition-janvier-mars-2026))

**Afghanistan / Pakistan** — IFRC published its DREF Final Report on the Afghan nationals return from Pakistan operation (April 2025), closing out the emergency response phase. A separate IOM/UNHCR joint border monitoring report covering 1 March to 23 April 2026 provides updated data on population flows from Iran into Pakistan. ([DREF Report](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/dref-final-report-afghan-nationals-return-pakistan-april-2025-mdrpk027); [IOM/UNHCR Border Report](https://reliefweb.int/report/pakistan/pakistan-joint-iom-and-unhcr-border-monitoring-report-inflows-islamic-republic-iran-3-1-mar-23-april-2026))

**Gender / FGM** — The 2025 Annual Report of the Joint UNFPA-UNICEF Programme on FGM was released, documenting global progress toward elimination by 2030 across 20 countries including Burkina Faso, Egypt, Ethiopia, Kenya, Mali, Somalia, Sudan, and Uganda. ([Source: UNFPA-UNICEF / ReliefWeb, 1 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/2025-annual-report-fgm-joint-programme-race-2030-accelerating-action-end-female-genital-mutilation))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Latin America / Migration** — IOM released a new multi-country strategic outlook report on 30 April finding that migration in Latin America and the Caribbean is becoming increasingly complex and difficult to predict. Key drivers include organised crime-linked violence, economic shifts, evolving migration policies, increasing deportations and returns, and growing climate hazard impacts. The report identifies emerging trends likely to shape population movements in the years ahead. ([Source: IOM / ReliefWeb, 1 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/la-migration-en-amerique-latine-devient-plus-complexe-et-plus-difficile-prevoir-nouveau-rapport-de-loim))

**Ukraine — Regional Response Closure** — IFRC published a batch of Final Reports on 1 May for its Ukraine Emergency Appeal operations in neighbouring countries (Emergency Appeal MGR65002), formally closing the emergency phase of operations in Belarus, Hungary, North Macedonia, Czech Republic, Poland, and Romania. These reports document the humanitarian response to refugee and displacement flows from Ukraine. ([Belarus](https://reliefweb.int/report/belarus/belarus-ukraine-and-impacted-countries-emergency-appeal-final-report-mgr65002-march-2026); [Poland](https://reliefweb.int/report/poland/poland-ukraine-and-impacted-countries-emergency-appeal-final-report-mgr65002-march-2026); [Romania](https://reliefweb.int/report/romania/romania-ukraine-and-impacted-countries-emergency-appeal-final-report-mgr65002-march-2026))

**Costa Rica — Floods** — IFRC published its Early Action Protocol Annual Report for Costa Rica Floods (EAP2023CR02), covering the 2025–2026 cycle and documenting forecast-based financing actions taken in anticipation of flooding events. ([Source: IFRC / ReliefWeb, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/costa-rica/costa-rica-floods-early-action-protocol-annual-report-eap-no-eap2023cr02-operation-no-mdrcr024-30-april-2026))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Tropical Cyclone Maila — Papua New Guinea (MAJOR — ACTIVE)**: Severe Tropical Cyclone Maila is the most significant active sudden-onset hazard in the region. As of 30 April 2026, it has affected over 83,000 people across 5 provinces in PNG, causing at least 34 fatalities and leaving 77,000+ in need of immediate humanitarian assistance. Shelter, WASH, and food assistance remain critical priorities across the affected area. ([ReliefWeb Situation Report No. 06](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/papua-new-guinea-severe-tropical-cyclone-maila-disaster-management-team-situation-report-no-06-01-may-2026))

**Flash Flooding — Somalia**: Flash floods reported in Garbaharey (Gedo), Middle Shabelle, and Hiran regions following heavy rainfall on the night of 27 April 2026. At least two children reported swept away in Garbaharey. ([Radio Ergo / ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/radio-ergo-weekly-feedback-report-issued-30-apr-2026))

**Seismic Activity**: GDACS monitoring shows multiple active earthquake events with magnitudes ranging from M5.5 to M6.1 across the region.
