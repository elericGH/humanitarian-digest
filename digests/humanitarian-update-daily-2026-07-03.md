# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Friday, 3 July 2026
*Produced Friday, 3 July 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The Pacific Disaster Center's DisasterAware feed is carrying multiple active severe-weather warnings across Australia this morning (3 July AEST), including a [riverine flood warning east of Adelaide, South Australia](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=477333) and a [severe weather warning for Wollongong, New South Wales](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=475872), both refreshed within the past hour, alongside flood alerts near Tumut, Shepparton and Launceston ([PDC DisasterAware](https://www.pdc.org)).

PDC is also tracking an active [wildfire advisory for the Cook and Napranum areas of Cape York, Queensland](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=476690), updated within the last hour of this digest's production ([PDC DisasterAware](https://www.pdc.org)).

No new statements or media releases were published in the past 24 hours by ACFID, the Emergency Action Alliance or DFAT. ACFID's [Venezuela Earthquake Appeal](https://acfid.asn.au/venezuela-earthquake-appeal/) (last updated 29 June) and EAA's [Venezuela earthquake monitoring page](https://emergencyaction.org.au/venezuela-earthquake/) remain the most recent Australian-sector responses to the deadliest crisis of the week.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

[Typhoon Bavi](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=5&eventtype=TC) remains the most dangerous active storm in the region, with GDACS reporting sustained winds of 259 km/h as of 2 July — red-alert intensity for the northwest Pacific. PDC issued a fresh [advisory on Typhoon Bavi](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=476585) roughly four hours before this digest, alongside a [warning for Tropical Depression Maysak](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=477234) and an update on [Tropical Storm Douglas](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=476747).

A cluster of moderate earthquakes shook the Pacific in the past two days: [Tonga M5.7 (1 July, 21:13 UTC)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549627&episodeid=1715727&eventtype=EQ), [Fiji M5.6](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549555&episodeid=1715635&eventtype=EQ) and [Japan M6.0](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549565&episodeid=1715647&eventtype=EQ), all green-level alerts on GDACS with no major damage reported so far.

The response to the [June 2026 Philippines earthquake and tsunami](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/eq-2026-000083-phl) continues, with the disaster still listed as ongoing on ReliefWeb, as is the response to [Typhoon Sinlaku in the Federated States of Micronesia](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/tc-2026-000050-fsm).

PDC DisasterAware is also showing a broad band of active flood alerts across Indonesian Kalimantan, Maluku and Nusa Tenggara Barat in its current feed ([PDC DisasterAware](https://www.pdc.org)), a reminder that seasonal flooding continues to stress local response capacity across the archipelago.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

In Gaza, the Norwegian Refugee Council warns that [shelter restrictions are forcing nearly one million people to endure summer heat in tents](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/gaza-israels-shelter-restrictions-force-nearly-million-people-endure-summer-heat-tents-nrc) — about 170,000 households in tents, another 5,000 sleeping outdoors and 52,000 in overcrowded shelters (ReliefWeb latest headlines, 3 July AEST).

OCHA reports that [more than 24 million people in the Sahel urgently need aid](https://reliefweb.int/report/mali/24-million-people-sahel-urgently-need-aid-and-world-needs-do-more), with violence in the Central Sahel spreading into coastal West Africa and making the region one of Africa's main epicentres of conflict.

In Somalia, MSF says [malnutrition admissions are overwhelming Baidoa's Bay Regional Hospital](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/malnutrition-admissions-are-overwhelming-baidoa-hospital-somalia): 1,400 patients were admitted to its therapeutic feeding centre between March and May, and the facility is beyond its limits even after expanding from 50 to 125 beds.

In the DRC/Uganda Ebola outbreak, the IRC warns that [women and girls face a double threat as caretakers and water collectors](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/women-and-girls-face-double-threat-ebola-outbreak-response-must-address-both-irc), with increased exposure to both infection and violence.

In Syria, Al Jazeera examines [the deadly bombing of a crowded Damascus cafe](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/2/whats-behind-the-deadly-bombing-of-a-crowded-damascus-cafe) (2 July), while in Iran, [indirect US–Iran talks and the reopening of the Strait of Hormuz dominate the humanitarian-adjacent agenda ahead of the state funeral for Ali Khamenei](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/7/3/iran-war-live-tehran-slams-us-ahead-of-huge-funeral-for-ali-khamenei) (3 July).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

Venezuela's twin-earthquake toll has reached at least 2,295 dead, 11,267 injured and 13,000 homeless, and a [man was rescued alive after eight days under rubble in Catia La Mar](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/2/miracle-trapped-man-rescued-eight-days-after-venezuela-earthquakes) as attention turns to the humanitarian response (Al Jazeera, 2 July).

In Ukraine, Humanitarian Coordinator Matthias Schmale [strongly condemned another wave of Russian attacks on energy infrastructure](https://reliefweb.int/updates), which killed and injured civilians and left thousands without power (ReliefWeb updates, 3 July AEST).

OCHA's latest Latin America flash update reports [about 50,000 people evacuated due to wildfires in south-central Chile, $415M disbursed by the IMF for Hurricane Melissa recovery in Jamaica, and 50 deaths in armed clashes in Port-au-Prince](https://reliefweb.int/updates) (ReliefWeb updates, 3 July AEST). Separately, Al Jazeera asks whether [the end of TPS for Haitians will trigger a caregiving crisis in the US](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/2/will-the-end-of-tps-for-haitians-mean-a-caregiving-crisis-in-us) (2 July).

On the global outlook, FAO and WFP [launched a $202 million joint appeal to protect 8.8 million people from El Niño-driven extreme weather](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/bracing-el-nino-fao-and-wfp-launch-joint-appeal-protect-88-million-people-extreme-weather-events), and their new [Hunger Hotspots report warns of 13 hotspots at significant risk](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/hunger-hotspots-fao-wfp-early-warnings-acute-food-insecurity-june-november-2026-outlook) for June–November 2026, with Sudan, South Sudan, Yemen and Palestine of highest concern (ReliefWeb latest headlines, 3 July AEST).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

[Typhoon Bavi (259 km/h)](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001279&episodeid=5&eventtype=TC) is the standout active hazard, with [Tropical Storm Douglas](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001280&episodeid=4&eventtype=TC) and [Tropical Depression Maysak](https://disasteralert.pdc.org/disasteralert/?hazard_id=477234) also active in GDACS/PDC feeds as of 2–3 July.

The [M7.5 Venezuela earthquake response remains in full swing](https://gdacs.org), with Copernicus EMS rapid mapping (activation EMSR884) still producing damage assessments; recent moderate quakes in [Tonga](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549627&episodeid=1715727&eventtype=EQ), [Fiji](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549555&episodeid=1715635&eventtype=EQ), [Japan](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549565&episodeid=1715647&eventtype=EQ) and [Afghanistan](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1549600&episodeid=1715684&eventtype=EQ) (all 1 July) drew green alerts only. GDACS also lists active flood events in the [United States](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103888&episodeid=35&eventtype=FL), [Ghana](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103954&episodeid=3&eventtype=FL) and [India](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103972&episodeid=2&eventtype=FL) (30 June).
