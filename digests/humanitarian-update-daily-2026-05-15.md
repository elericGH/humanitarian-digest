## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia's aid sector is welcoming — but scrutinising — the 2026–27 Federal Budget's treatment of overseas development assistance. The Australian Council for International Development (ACFID) acknowledged the Government's commitment to protect the aid program from the sweeping global cuts being seen elsewhere, including increased investment in the Indo-Pacific and funding for Australian NGOs operating internationally. However, ACFID cautioned that Australia has reached an all-time low in generosity as a share of national income, with ODA now accounting for just 0.63 percent of GNI — a historic nadir at precisely the moment when global conflict, climate disruption, and food insecurity driven by the ongoing fuel crisis are mounting. ([ACFID — Federal Budget holds for aid, but stronger investment needed in an increasingly unstable world](https://acfid.asn.au/federal-budget-holds-for-aid/), 12 May 2026)

The ongoing US-Iran conflict and resulting crisis in the Strait of Hormuz is reverberating through Australia's near region and its own economy. Analysis published by the East Asia Forum warns that Asia is bearing the brunt of the Hormuz crisis, with nations heavily dependent on Middle East energy exports — including several of Australia's key trading partners — facing serious economic strain. The fuel crisis is also exacerbating humanitarian conditions across the Pacific, where a new brief from the Pacific region describes the price of fuel as having become "the price of childhood" for families pushed deeper into poverty. ([East Asia Forum — Asia bears the brunt of the Hormuz crisis](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/05/14/asia-bears-the-brunt-of-the-hormuz-crisis/), 14 May 2026; [ReliefWeb — When the price of fuel becomes the price of childhood: Pacific families in a crisis](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/when-price-fuel-becomes-price-childhood-pacific-families-crisis))

Australia's development diplomacy in the critical minerals space is also under new pressure. A dual-track approach to critical minerals supply chains — separating commercial flows from strategic stockpiling — is being scrutinised in the Indo-Pacific policy community, with analysts arguing it cannot adequately address coercion risks from dominant producers. This has direct implications for Australian policy as Canberra seeks to position the country as a reliable alternative supplier. ([East Asia Forum — A dual-track approach to critical minerals can't overcome coercion concerns](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/05/13/a-dual-track-approach-to-critical-minerals-cant-overcome-coercion-concerns/), 13 May 2026)

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Papua New Guinea continues to manage the aftermath of Severe Tropical Cyclone Maila, with the Disaster Management Team releasing its seventh situation report in early May. The cyclone caused widespread infrastructure damage and displacement across affected provinces, and recovery operations are ongoing. Humanitarian coordination in PNG remains complicated by pre-existing conflict dynamics, particularly in Enga Province. ([ReliefWeb — PNG: Severe Tropical Cyclone Maila — Situation Report No. 07](https://reliefweb.int/report/papua-new-guinea/papua-new-guinea-severe-tropical-cyclone-maila-disaster-management-team-situation-report-no-07-08-may-2026), 8 May 2026)

Indonesia experienced significant flooding across multiple provinces, prompting an ECHO Daily Flash advisory on 13 May. The flooding, attributed to ongoing heavy rainfall tracked by meteorological agencies BMKG and NOAA-CPC, has caused displacement and infrastructure damage. Indonesia's flood vulnerability during the wet season continues to strain local disaster management capacities. ([ReliefWeb — Indonesia - Floods (ECHO Daily Flash of 13 May 2026)](https://reliefweb.int/report/indonesia/indonesia-floods-adinet-bmkg-noaa-cpc-echo-daily-flash-13-may-2026))

Myanmar's protracted displacement crisis continues to affect the wider region. A new UNHCR Regional Bureau for Asia and Pacific situation update covering January to March 2026 tracks refugees and asylum-seekers from Myanmar across Bangladesh, India, Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand, highlighting the cross-border nature of the crisis and the pressure it places on neighbouring states — several of which are key Australian partners. Pakistan was also struck by severe weather, floods, and landslides, with a 13 May ECHO Daily Flash reporting the latest update from provincial disaster authorities. ([ReliefWeb — Pakistan - Severe weather, floods and landslides (ECHO Daily Flash of 13 May 2026)](https://reliefweb.int/report/pakistan/pakistan-severe-weather-floods-and-landslides-update-pdma-pmd-echo-daily-flash-13-may-2026))

ASEAN's financial system is facing structural stress as central banks across the bloc confront risks that existing monetary tools were not designed to handle. East Asia Forum analysis identifies the need for new instruments to cope with oil-price volatility, currency spillovers from the Hormuz crisis, and climate-related supply shocks. The Philippines' Mindanao peace process also received attention in a newly published update covering the September–December 2025 period, tracking civil society normalization efforts under the peace accords. ([East Asia Forum — ASEAN central banks need new tools for new risks](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/05/13/asean-central-banks-need-new-tools-for-new-risks/), 13 May 2026; [ReliefWeb — The Peace Accords Matrix-Mindanao (PAM-M) Update](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/peace-accords-matrix-mindanao-pam-m-and-civil-society-normalization-update-report-september-december-2025))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Lebanon's humanitarian situation remains dire despite a ceasefire that has proven deeply fragile. The Norwegian Refugee Council reported that nearly 600 people have been killed since the ceasefire was agreed, and OCHA's Flash Update #25 — covering escalating hostilities as of 11 May — documents continued attacks on civilian infrastructure. Food, shelter, and protection needs are acute for populations in the south, Bekaa Valley, and southern suburbs of Beirut. ([ReliefWeb — Lebanon: Flash Update #25 - Escalation of hostilities](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-flash-update-25-escalation-hostilities-lebanon-11-may-2026), 13 May 2026; [ReliefWeb — Lebanon: Nearly 600 killed since fragile ceasefire agreed | NRC](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-nearly-600-killed-fragile-ceasefire-agreed-nrc), 13 May 2026)

In the occupied Palestinian territory, a West Bank Protection Cluster Snapshot covering February through April 2026 documented severe and worsening protection conditions. Settlement expansion, movement restrictions, and settler violence are the primary drivers of harm identified across the reporting period. In Syria, WFP has been forced to scale back food assistance amid a serious funding shortfall, leaving hundreds of thousands of conflict-affected Syrians with reduced or no rations. ([ReliefWeb — West Bank Protection Cluster Snapshot](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/west-bank-protection-cluster-snapshot-protection-monitoring-reporting-period-1-feb-30-apr-2026), 13 May 2026; [ReliefWeb — WFP scales back food assistance in Syria amid funding shortfalls](https://reliefweb.int/report/syrian-arab-republic/wfp-scales-back-food-assistance-syria-amid-funding-shortfalls))

Sudan's civil war between the Sudanese Armed Forces and the Rapid Support Forces continues to generate one of the world's largest and most underfunded humanitarian crises. ACAPS published a fresh thematic risk analysis on 13 May, warning of compounding protection and food security risks as the conflict enters its third year with no political resolution in sight. In South Sudan, the International Rescue Committee warned on 13 May that renewed fighting in Akobo state is producing an alarming humanitarian crisis, with fresh displacement, interrupted aid access, and mounting civilian casualties. ([ReliefWeb — ACAPS Sudan Risk Analysis [2026] Thematic Report](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/acaps-sudan-risk-analysis-2026-thematic-report-13-may-2026), 13 May 2026; [ReliefWeb — South Sudan: Renewed fighting in Akobo state | IRC](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-renewed-fighting-akobo-state-leading-alarming-humanitarian-crisis-warns-irc), 13 May 2026)

Yemen's humanitarian needs remain among the most severe globally. A newly published Humanitarian Action for Children 2026 report for Yemen outlines the scale of child-specific vulnerability, including acute malnutrition, disrupted education, and protection risks from ongoing conflict. In the Democratic Republic of Congo, a monthly displacement report for April 2026 documents continued large-scale movement of civilians as fighting persists in eastern provinces. ([ReliefWeb — Humanitarian Action for Children 2026 - Yemen](https://reliefweb.int/report/yemen/humanitarian-action-children-2026-yemen); [ReliefWeb — DRC Monthly Displacement Report - April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/monthly-displacement-report-april-2026))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

Ukraine continues to suffer widespread civilian harm from Russian attacks. The Ukraine Health Cluster published an update on 13 May on partner emergency responses to attacks, while AMDA released its 56th emergency relief brief documenting ongoing medical and humanitarian needs. UNHCR's Ukraine Operational Update of 12 May tracks the displacement situation as the war enters its fifth year. ([ReliefWeb — Ukraine Health Cluster Partner Emergency Response to Attacks](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/ukraine-health-cluster-partner-emergency-response-attacks-30-april-2026), 13 May 2026; [ReliefWeb — AMDA Emergency Relief #56: Crisis in Ukraine](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/amda-emergency-relief-56-crisis-ukraine-13-may-2026), 13 May 2026; [ReliefWeb — UNHCR Ukraine Operational Update - 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/unhcr-ukraine-operational-update-12-may-2026))

Haiti's gang violence crisis continues to produce acute displacement. Emergency tracking data published on 13 May recorded displacement of civilians in Cité Soleil, Ouest department, following armed clashes between 10–12 May. The displacement follows the collapse of large parts of the capital's governance and security infrastructure, with humanitarian access severely constrained. ([ReliefWeb — Haiti - Emergency Tracking Tool (ETT) 93 — Displacement following armed clashes in Cité Soleil](https://reliefweb.int/report/haiti/haiti-emergency-tracking-tool-ett-93-displacement-following-armed-clashes-cite-soleil-10-12-may-2026), 13 May 2026)

The Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre released its Global Report on Internal Displacement 2026 on 12 May, providing the most comprehensive annual snapshot of conflict- and disaster-driven internal displacement worldwide. A weekly disease surveillance bulletin covering outbreaks and other emergencies was also published, and IOM/UNHCR/ICVA launched a new platform to map services and identify service gaps on mixed migration routes globally. ([ReliefWeb — Weekly Bulletin On Outbreaks and other Emergencies](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/weekly-bulletin-outbreaks-and-other-emergencies); [ReliefWeb — Protection in Danger Monthly News Brief - April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/protection-danger-monthly-news-brief-april-2026))
