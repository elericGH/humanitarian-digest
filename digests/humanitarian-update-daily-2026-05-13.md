# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Wednesday, 13 May 2026
*Produced Wednesday, 13 May 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

ACFID (the Australian Council for International Development) released its response to the 2026–27 Federal Budget on 12 May 2026, acknowledging that Australia's Official Development Assistance budget was maintained but calling for stronger investment in aid and development given the increasingly unstable global humanitarian environment. The statement calls on the Australian Government to do more as global needs outpace current commitments. ([ACFID, 12 May 2026](https://acfid.asn.au/federal-budget-holds-for-aid/))

ACFID has also highlighted the far-reaching and long-lasting humanitarian impact of the US-Iran conflict, noting that the conflict is reversing hard-won development gains across the region with consequences felt well beyond the immediate conflict zone — including in communities supported by Australian aid programs. ([ACFID Analysis](https://acfid.asn.au/development-in-reverse-far-reaching-and-long-lasting-impact-of-us-iran-conflict-already-being-felt/))

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Tonga — Dengue Outbreak Continues:** The Government of Tonga and Tonga Red Cross Society issued Situation Report #19 on 12 May 2026, confirming 60 new confirmed dengue cases since the previous report. The majority are in the 11–14 age group on Tongatapu Island, with Dengue Virus Serotype 2 (DENV-2) identified. No hospitalisations or deaths in this reporting period. The outbreak is part of a broader 2025–2026 Pacific dengue surge. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/tonga/tonga-dengue-fever-outbreak-situation-report-19-12th-may-2026-ento))

**Timor-Leste — Flash Flood Threat:** Moderate-to-heavy rainfall on 10–12 May caused river overflow and inundation in Dotic Village, Manufahi Municipality, cutting off transportation links between the village and surrounding areas. National meteorological authorities have forecast heavy to extreme rainfall through 14 May, with a high likelihood of further flash flooding and river overflow in the coming days. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/timor-leste/tls-flood-05-2026-early-action-flood-manufahi-tls))

**Myanmar — One Year Since Deadly School Attack:** The UN Independent Investigative Mechanism for Myanmar issued a statement on 12 May marking one year since the Myanmar military's attack on Oe Htein Kwin village school in Sagaing Region, which killed more than 20 students. The IIMM continues to investigate this and more recent incidents of children killed in bombardments of schools, hospitals and homes, stating that deliberate airstrikes targeting civilians violate international humanitarian law. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/one-year-after-deadly-school-attack-myanmar-children-continue-be-victims-militarys-air-campaign))

**Pacific Disease Surveillance:** The World Health Organization published its Pacific Syndromic Surveillance System Weekly Bulletin (Week 18, 27 April–3 May 2026) on 12 May, covering disease trends across Papua New Guinea, Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, Fiji, Samoa, Tonga, Kiribati and other Pacific island nations. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/pacific-syndromic-surveillance-system-weekly-bulletin-systeme-de-surveillance-syndromic-dans-le-pacifique-bulletin-hebdomadaire-w18-2026-apr-27-may-03-enfr))

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano Active Monitoring:** The Philippine Institute of Volcanology and Seismology (PHIVOLCS) continued issuing 24-hour observation bulletins for Mayon Volcano on 10, 11 and 12 May 2026. The volcano remains under active monitoring. ([ReliefWeb disaster page, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/disaster/vo-2026-000065-phl))

**IFRC Strengthens Asia-Pacific Preparedness:** The International Federation of Red Cross and Red Crescent Societies reported on 12 May that its "Becoming Ready for Disasters" programme (July 2025–April 2026) has significantly accelerated the transition from reactive crisis response to a coordinated regional preparedness system across Asia-Pacific, as humanitarian needs grow faster than available resources. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/lifrc-renforce-la-preparation-aux-catastrophes-en-asie-pacifique-optimisant-limpact-des-investissements-humanitaires-ifrc))

**Nepal — Severe Weather Casualties:** Heavy rain and lightning across Nepal in recent days have killed one person in Sindhuli District and injured three others in Tehrathum and Rolpa Districts. Rain-triggered landslides are blocking roads across the country, and moderate-to-heavy rainfall is forecast to continue, especially in eastern regions. ([ReliefWeb ECHO Daily Flash, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/nepal/nepal-severe-weather-ndrrma-noaa-cpc-echo-daily-flash-12-may-2026))

**Pakistan — Severe Weather in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa:** Heavy rain, strong winds and lightning struck six districts in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Province on 11 May, killing four people in Shangla, Battagram, Haripur and Kolai Pallas Kohistan, and injuring eight more. Further severe weather is expected across the province. ([ReliefWeb ECHO Daily Flash, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/pakistan/pakistan-severe-weather-update-pdma-noaa-cpc-echo-daily-flash-12-may-2026))

**Bangladesh — Measles Emergency in Rohingya Camps:** The International Rescue Committee launched an emergency vaccination response in Cox's Bazar Rohingya refugee camps in late April 2026 as measles spread across Bangladesh in one of the most serious outbreaks in decades. As of 28 April, over 34,600 suspected cases had been recorded across 58 of 64 districts, with more than 200 confirmed deaths. The overcrowded Cox's Bazar camps face acute risk. ([ReliefWeb, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/bangladesh/irc-launches-emergency-measles-response-20000-children-rohingya-refugee-camps-bangladesh-outbreak-spreads))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Lebanon — Children Killed Despite Ceasefire:** Save the Children reported on 12 May that in the first 25 days of a temporary ceasefire beginning 17 April, 22 children were killed and 89 injured in Lebanon — more than four per day. The total number of children killed since hostilities renewed on 2 March now approaches 200, with approximately 2,900 people killed overall. More than one million people — one in six of Lebanon's population — have been displaced. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/over-four-children-killed-or-injured-average-day-during-so-called-ceasefire-lebanon))

**Occupied Palestinian Territory — West Bank Violence:** Save the Children and the UN reported on 12 May that at least one Palestinian child has been killed on average every week in the West Bank since January 2025, with 70 children killed in that period — 93% by Israeli forces — and 850 more injured, mostly by live ammunition. OCHA noted that March 2026 saw the highest number of Palestinians injured by settler attacks in 20 years. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/demolition-homes-schools-and-childhood))

**Gaza — War Crimes Investigation Called For:** Amnesty International issued a statement on 12 May calling for investigation of Israel's systematic destruction of high-rise civilian buildings in Gaza as war crimes of wanton destruction and collective punishment. Despite an October 2025 ceasefire, reconstruction remains impossible amid ongoing operations. The report details devastating consequences for displaced Palestinian families. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/israelopt-israels-systematic-destruction-high-rise-buildings-must-be-investigated-war-crimes-wanton-destruction-and-collective-punishment-enar))

**Sudan — Critical Shelter Gap Ahead of Rains:** The CCCM, Protection and Shelter Clusters reported on 12 May that only US$35.3 million of the required US$179.4 million has been secured for emergency shelter in Sudan — just 19.7% funded. Some 15.4 million people need assistance, but only 1.8 million can be targeted under current constraints. With 6.91 million IDPs exposed to medium-to-very-high flood risk across 183 localities, and the rainy season imminent, the crisis is described as critical. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/shelter-failure-sudan-protection-risk-urgent-funding-needed-rains-may-2026))

**Sudan — Famine Confirmed:** FEWS NET published an updated food security outlook on 12 May confirming that conflict and high prices are driving Emergency (IPC Phase 4) conditions and a risk of famine through September 2026. Famine has been confirmed in el-Fasher (North Darfur) and Kadugli (South Kordofan), with famine conditions present in over 20 additional locations. Over 24.6 million people face acute food insecurity in what is now described as the world's largest displacement and hunger crisis. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/sudan-food-security-outlook-update-conflict-and-high-food-and-fuel-prices-drive-emergency-and-risk-famine-april-september-2026))

**Mali — Post-Attack Humanitarian Situation:** OCHA published a situation report on 12 May on the humanitarian consequences of coordinated armed group attacks on 25 April across five Malian cities including Kati (near Bamako), Sévaré, Gao, Mopti and Kidal. Some 600 people have been newly registered as refugees in Niger, with over 100 households internally displaced. Humanitarian operations are gradually resuming. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/mali/humanitarian-situation-following-attacks-25-april-2026))

**Mozambique — Cabo Delgado Attacks and Displacement:** The EU ECHO Daily Flash of 12 May reported heightened non-state armed group activity in Cabo Delgado province in late April and early May. In Ancuabe District, armed groups looted villages, burned homes, killed and kidnapped civilians, committed sexual violence and destroyed a health centre and Catholic mission buildings. At least 11,000 people have been displaced from Ancuabe and neighbouring Montepuez. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/mozambique/mozambique-coordinated-attacks-and-rising-displacements-cabo-delgado-dg-echo-mozambique-rapid-response-mechanism-acled-un-echo-daily-flash-12-may-2026))

**Somalia — Flash Floods and Drought Simultaneously:** Somalia is entering the Gu rainy season (April–June) with flash flooding already affecting multiple regions. The Shabelle River has overflowed, displacing approximately 400 households in Jowhar, while over 250 families have been displaced and two people killed in Togdheer/Buuhoodle. Simultaneously, the Somali Red Crescent Society and IFRC activated a Drought Early Action Protocol on 11 May, with a CHF 530,533 DREF allocation, following failure of the Gu rains in some areas. ([Flash floods: ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/somalia-severe-rains-and-flash-floods-un-ocha-echo-daily-flash-12-may-2026); [Drought EAP: ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/somalia-drought-early-action-protocol-activation-report-eap-no-eap2024so01-operation-no-mdrso019-11-may-2026))

**South Africa — Severe Storms, Multiple Deaths:** A series of severe storms, heavy rain and flooding struck South Africa from 5–10 May, affecting Western Cape, Eastern Cape, Northern Cape, North West, Free State and Mpumalanga provinces. At least five deaths were confirmed: three in Kenilworth (Cape Town) and two in the Northern Cape. A second storm struck Cape Town and the Western Cape on 10 May, compounding the damage. ([ReliefWeb ECHO Daily Flash, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-africa/south-africa-severe-weather-and-floods-update-media-noaa-cpc-ndmc-saws-echo-daily-flash-12-may-2026))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Global — Internal Displacement Doubled Over a Decade:** The Internal Displacement Monitoring Centre released its flagship 2026 Global Report on Internal Displacement on 12 May, finding that conflict and violence are now the leading driver of displacement worldwide, with conflict displacements up 60% compared to 2024 driven by increasing international conflicts and attacks on urban areas. Total internally displaced persons have doubled over the past decade — from 38.9 million in 2015. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/2026-global-report-internal-displacement-enar))

**Ukraine — Deadliest Winter for Women and Girls:** The UN Human Rights Monitoring Mission in Ukraine reported on 12 May that January–March 2026 was the deadliest quarter for women and girls since the start of the full-scale invasion — 199 women and girls killed in Q1 2026, more than the same period in 2025, 2024 and 2023. Casualties among women increased by 27% in 2025 compared to 2024. The war has now lasted more than 1,500 days. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/war-ukraine-becomes-deadlier-women-and-girls-more-1500-days-after-full-scale-invasion))

**Haiti — Armed Group Clashes Displace Thousands:** The EU ECHO Daily Flash of 12 May reported that on 10 April, fighting erupted between armed groups for control of Cité Soleil and Croix des Bouquets in Port-au-Prince, forcing the closure of Fontaine Hospital and displacing thousands of civilians. MSF's Drouillard hospital admitted 40 gunshot-wounded patients on 11 April alone as fighting intensified, with 800 people seeking refuge. A stray bullet struck an MSF security guard as clashes surrounded the hospital. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/haiti/haiti-armed-groups-confrontation-port-au-prince-cause-mass-displacement-dg-echo-partners-msf-media-echo-daily-flash-12-may-2026))

**Afghanistan — Deportation Concerns:** The IRC warned on 29 April that governments ramping up deportations of Afghan refugees risk returning people to a country where 40% of the population faces hunger, climate-driven displacement continues, and women and girls face systemic barriers to education, employment and healthcare. Afghans remain the largest group arriving in Europe via the Balkans. ([ReliefWeb, 30 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/irc-warns-looming-deportations-risk-afghans-returning-country-where-one-third-population-cannot-feed-their-families))

**Colombia — Flooding in Urabá, Antioquia:** Heavy rainfall and overflow of the Vijagual River caused flooding in Apartadó and Carepa in the Urabá region of Antioquia on 12 May, affecting approximately 1,600 families (1,500 in Apartadó, 100 in Carepa). Roads, bridges and basic services have been impacted. Further moderate-to-heavy rain is forecast. ([ReliefWeb ECHO Daily Flash, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/colombia/colombia-floods-noaa-cpc-antioquiagov-media-echo-daily-flash-12-may-2026))

**Dominican Republic — Flood Emergency:** Torrential rains and severe flooding are displacing thousands in the Dominican Republic, with Direct Relief coordinating medicines, medical supplies, cold chain equipment, nutrition assistance and personal care items through local partner FSDNJ in the Puerto Plata region, in coordination with the Ministry of Health. ([ReliefWeb, 12 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/dominican-republic/thousands-displaced-seeking-medical-aid-following-torrential-rain-flooding-dominican-republic))

**IOM — Nearly 8,000 Migrant Deaths in 2025:** The IOM's Missing Migrants Project Annual Report published in April 2026 recorded nearly 8,000 migrants dead or missing in 2025, bringing the total since 2014 to over 82,000, with at least 340,000 family members directly affected. Migration routes are shifting rather than becoming safer. ([ReliefWeb, 22 April 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/iom-missing-migrants-project-2025-annual-global-report-missing-migrants-and-their-families-left-behind))
