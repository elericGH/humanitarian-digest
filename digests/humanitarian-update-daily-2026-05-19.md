# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Tuesday, 19 May 2026
*Produced Tuesday, 19 May 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

ACFID welcomed the Australian Government's 2026–27 Federal Budget for maintaining the existing aid envelope but called for stronger investment in an increasingly unstable world. The peak body for Australian NGOs argued that flat-lining aid in the face of surging global humanitarian need — driven by conflicts in the Middle East, Sudan, and the Pacific — amounts to a declining commitment in real terms, and that Australia has both the opportunity and responsibility to step up as traditional donors, particularly the United States, continue to retreat from global aid commitments. ([ACFID, 12 May 2026](https://acfid.asn.au/federal-budget-holds-for-aid/))

ACFID has also published an analysis of the far-reaching humanitarian consequences of the US-Iran conflict, warning of "development in reverse" across the broader region. The report flags deepening food insecurity, displacement, and damage to fragile health systems as second-order effects are already being felt across the Middle East and beyond. A companion analysis document examines the specific mechanisms by which the conflict is undermining decades of development gains. ([ACFID, 23 April 2026](https://acfid.asn.au/development-in-reverse-far-reaching-and-long-lasting-impact-of-us-iran-conflict-already-being-felt/))

A key global aid-policy moment directly relevant to Australia arrives today: the Global Partnership Conference (London, 19–20 May 2026, 50+ countries) is examining the structural shift in global ODA as US and other Western donor funding contracts sharply. ACFID has previously called on Australia to step into the leadership vacuum, noting Australia's regional standing and values-based foreign policy give it both the credibility and the strategic interest to expand its humanitarian and development footprint in the Indo-Pacific. ([ACFID, 10 April 2026](https://acfid.asn.au/australia-opportunity-to-lead_global-aid-plummets/))

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Typhoon Sinlaku's devastating impact on the Federated States of Micronesia continues to worsen. Situation Report No. 9 (covering 2–8 May) confirms that all municipalities in Chuuk State and two in Yap State remain severely affected, with access to safe water critically compromised, food reserves depleting rapidly, and outer islands increasingly isolated as maritime supply lines remain constrained. A compounding threat has now emerged: Tropical Storm Hagupit is threatening Yap State, adding to an already acute humanitarian crisis. ([ReliefWeb / CDEOC, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/micronesia-federated-states/federated-states-micronesia-cdeoc-situation-report-no-9-tropical-storm-sinlaku-chuuk-state-may-08-2026))

Heavy flooding across Peninsular Malaysia over the past 48 hours has forced the evacuation of 359 people: 312 in Pontian district (Johor) and 47 in Kuala Muda district (Kedah). Further heavy rainfall is forecast across the whole of Peninsular Malaysia over the next 24 hours, and GDACS has recorded a Green-level flood alert for the event (event ID 1103884, active since 16 May). ([EU ECHO Daily Flash, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/malaysia/malaysia-floods-adinet-met-malaysia-echo-daily-flash-18-may-2026))

Severe weather across multiple Indian states has killed at least 136 people in the past week, with heavy rainfall, lightning, hailstorms, and thunderstorms striking Uttar Pradesh (122 dead since 13 May), Maharashtra, Andhra Pradesh, Uttarakhand, Himachal Pradesh, and Chhattisgarh. India's National Emergency Response Centre (NDMI) confirms national response mechanisms are active. The scale of deaths from what is largely a meteorological event underscores the compounding vulnerability of populations across South Asia entering the pre-monsoon period. ([EU ECHO Daily Flash, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/india/india-severe-weather-and-floods-update-ndmi-media-imd-echo-daily-flash-18-may-2026))

A magnitude 5.2 earthquake struck southern Myanmar on 18 May at 02:05 UTC, 35 km south-east of Yangon. USGS PAGER estimates indicate up to 42,000 people were exposed to strong shaking and 238,000 to moderate shaking. Several buildings were evacuated as a precaution in Yangon and neighbouring towns, and tremors were felt as far away as Bangkok and Chiang Mai. No casualties or structural damage have been confirmed at this stage, though assessments are ongoing in a country already experiencing severe conflict-driven humanitarian crisis. ([EU ECHO Daily Flash, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/myanmar-earthquake-gdacs-usgs-media-echo-daily-flash-18-may-2026))

The IFRC published results from its Asia-Pacific National Society Preparedness and Readiness Programme (July 2025–April 2026), reporting accelerated progress in shifting from reactive crisis response toward preparedness-led, regionally coordinated readiness across National Societies. IFRC Director Alexander Matheou noted that every Swiss franc invested in preparedness saves more lives with fewer resources, a finding with direct relevance to Australia's Pacific aid investment strategy and its support for regional early warning and response capability. ([ReliefWeb / IFRC, 7 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/ifrc-strengthens-disaster-readiness-across-asia-pacific-delivering-better-value-humanitarian-investment))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

The WHO has declared the rapidly escalating Ebola outbreak in eastern DRC a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC). At least 88 people have died and 336 suspected cases have been identified, involving the rare Bundibugyo strain of Ebola for which there are no licensed vaccines or targeted treatments. The IRC is launching an emergency response covering infection prevention and control, frontline health worker support, and community outreach, amid fears of wider spread to Uganda and neighbouring countries — a situation with global health security implications. ([ReliefWeb / IRC, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/irc-launches-emergency-response-rare-ebola-strain-no-vaccine-spreads-across-eastern-drc-and-uganda-irc))

A joint FAO, WFP, and UNICEF statement warns that nearly 19.5 million people — two in five Sudanese — are currently facing crisis-level acute food insecurity (IPC Phase 3 or above), including nearly 135,000 in Catastrophic food insecurity (IPC Phase 4+) and more than 825,000 children at risk of death from severe acute malnutrition in 2026. The IPC Partnership has confirmed risk of famine in 14 areas across three Sudanese states, stating: "This report does not show progress; it shows the world has failed to act." Conflict, mass displacement, and severely constrained humanitarian access are the primary drivers. ([ReliefWeb / FAO, WFP, UNICEF, 15 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/joint-news-release-wfpfaounicef-risk-famine-persists-nearly-195-million-people-face-acute-food-insecurity-sudan))

Somalia faces an alarming deterioration in food security, with the latest IPC analysis showing that six million people — approximately one in three Somalis — are projected to be in acute food insecurity by June 2026. Burhakaba District is now at likely risk of famine, and 1.88 million children under five will be affected by acute malnutrition by December 2026 — representing a 9% increase in overall food insecurity and an 18% increase in IPC Phase 4 compared to February projections. ([EU ECHO Daily Flash, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/somalia-deterioration-food-insecurity-and-malnutrition-ipc-ipc-integrated-food-security-phase-classification-dg-echo-echo-daily-flash-18-may-2026))

In the Occupied Palestinian Territory, the head of UN Human Rights Ajith Sunghay presented a major report in Geneva covering 19 months of large-scale violations of international law, including alleged atrocity crimes, from October 2023 through end of May 2025. Despite a ceasefire in October 2025, killings and destruction of infrastructure have continued on an almost daily basis, the overall humanitarian situation remains dire, and the rate of forcible displacement of Palestinians in the West Bank is described as "unseen in decades." A separate OPT Humanitarian Situation Report (7 May) documents 38 attacks on healthcare in a single week and rising disease burden. ([ReliefWeb / UN Human Rights, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/remarks-ajith-sunghay-head-un-human-rights-occupied-palestinian-territory-press-geneva-launch-new-report-enar))

South Sudan faces compounding crises: tens of thousands of people are returning to Akobo, Jonglei State, from displacement in Ethiopia, while a credible risk of famine persists in four counties across Upper Nile and Jonglei States under worst-case conflict scenarios. Humanitarian access is deteriorating sharply, with aid worker detentions, supply confiscation, and movement restrictions reported across Upper Nile, Jonglei, and Central Equatoria states. ([ReliefWeb / OCHA South Sudan, 17 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-humanitarian-update-1-15-may-2026))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

A new IDMC dataset analysed by Save the Children reveals that 2025 saw an all-time high in child displacements due to conflict and violence — averaging 35,000 per day, or approximately 13 million child displacement events across the year. For the first time in recorded data, conflict overtook climate as the primary driver of global displacement. The finding is a stark indicator of the direction of global humanitarian need heading into 2026. ([ReliefWeb / Save the Children, 14 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/conflicts-force-record-35000-child-displacements-every-day-2025-new-data-shows))

Published on the eve of the Global Partnership Conference (London, 19–20 May), The HALO Trust warns the world is in "one of the most dangerous and politically volatile periods in decades" as wars spread, alliances fracture, climate shocks intensify, and foreign aid becomes more politicised. HALO Director James Denselow warns that "just as humanitarian needs are accelerating, global military spending is surging while aid budgets shrink" — a structural contradiction that the Global Partnership Conference is convening to address, with 50+ countries represented. ([ReliefWeb / HALO Trust, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/perfect-storm-global-conflict-drives-record-humanitarian-needs-aid-funding-stalls))

At the 79th World Health Assembly (WHA79) in Geneva, the International Rescue Committee urged delegates to make sustained investments in health systems in fragile and conflict-affected states, citing the DRC Ebola PHEIC, hantavirus concerns, and the broader collapse of health infrastructure due to conflict and funding cuts as drivers of mass preventable death across multiple crises. ([ReliefWeb / IRC, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/irc-world-health-assembly-close-global-health-equity-gap-worlds-benefit))

A Global Protection Cluster advocacy note flags a "deadly convergence" of ancient unlawful warfare methods — siege tactics, conflict-related sexual violence — with modern advanced technologies, particularly armed drones, driving expanding levels of civilian harm across active conflict zones globally. The GPC calls for renewed IHL compliance efforts, strengthened accountability mechanisms, and protection-centred humanitarian response frameworks. ([ReliefWeb / GPC, 18 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/poc-advocacy-note-civilian-protection-2026-deadly-convergence-advanced-technology-and-ancient-warfare-practices))

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

No active Red or Orange-level sudden-onset hazard alerts are recorded in the GDACS system as of 18–19 May 2026. All 58 currently active GDACS events are Green-level. Noteworthy Green-level events from the past 48 hours include: a M5.2 earthquake near Yangon, Myanmar ([GDACS EQ-1541257](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1541257&eventtype=EQ)); a M5.0 earthquake in Guangxi, China with 2 dead and 7,000+ displaced ([GDACS EQ-1541314](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1541314&eventtype=EQ)); earthquakes in the Philippines ([GDACS EQ-1541333](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1541333&eventtype=EQ)), Japan ([GDACS EQ-1541332](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1541332&eventtype=EQ)), Papua New Guinea ([GDACS EQ-1541226](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1541226&eventtype=EQ)), Tonga ([GDACS EQ-1541163](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1541163&eventtype=EQ)), and New Zealand ([GDACS EQ-1541116](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1541116&eventtype=EQ)); and active floods in Malaysia ([GDACS FL-1103884](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1103884&eventtype=FL)). India's severe weather event (136+ dead) is being managed through national response mechanisms and does not appear as a GDACS Red/Orange alert. No active tropical cyclone alerts in the Pacific or Indian Ocean.
