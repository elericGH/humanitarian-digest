# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Sunday, 14 June 2026
*Produced Sunday, 14 June 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

The Emergency Action Alliance (EAA) — which unites 15 leading Australian aid organisations including ADRA Australia, Caritas Australia, Oxfam Australia and Save the Children — continues to run its active [Middle East Crisis Appeal](https://emergencyaction.org.au/middle-east-appeal/), with EAA charities urgently scaling up their work in Gaza, Lebanon, the West Bank and Syria as conflict intensifies. The appeal notes millions of people are in urgent need of shelter, food and basic supplies, and that more funds are critically needed.

EAA's [Myanmar Earthquake appeal](https://emergencyaction.org.au/appeal/myanmar-earthquake/) remains active, with a 6-month report recently published. Member organisations are on the ground in Myanmar providing lifesaving food, shelter, safe drinking water and emergency supplies following the devastating earthquake that struck in early 2025.

GDACS has flagged an ongoing drought alert for **Eastern Australia** (now in its 16th consecutive week), raising concerns about food system pressures and agricultural impacts domestically. The drought is part of a broader South-East Asia drought pattern now in its 20th week. No new DFAT humanitarian alerts were available at time of publication.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

A magnitude 5.5 earthquake struck the Philippines on 11 June 2026, prompting the activation of the [Copernicus Emergency Management Service (EMSR882)](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR882/download) for satellite mapping. This follows a major M7.8 earthquake that struck the Philippines in early June, for which EC/ECHO daily maps remain active and response operations are ongoing. The southern Philippines region is managing multiple seismic events in quick succession.

Tropical Cyclone **CRISTINA-26** is currently active in the Pacific, tracked at 74 km/h as of 11 June 2026. It follows earlier cyclones BORIS-26 and AMANDA-26 in what has been an active June for cyclone activity in the Pacific basin. These cyclones are affecting communities already under pressure from ongoing climate stresses. [GDACS event tracking](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001275&episodeid=13&eventtype=TC) is available for the latest storm positions.

The World Food Programme and the Government of Nepal [launched the Adaptation Fund Climate Innovation Accelerator (AFCIA)](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-and-government-nepal-launch-innovation-accelerator-climate-and-food-security) on 8 June 2026 in collaboration with Nepal's Ministry of Agriculture, Forests and Environment. The initiative marks a step forward in strengthening climate resilience and food security in Nepal, supporting communities facing increased weather-related shocks.

UNHCR and humanitarian partners [called on the world not to forget Rohingya refugees in Bangladesh](https://www.unhcr.org/news/briefing-notes/unhcr-humanitarian-partners-ask-world-not-forget-rohingya-refugees-bangladesh) (2 June 2026), as funding pressures increasingly threaten programs supporting the world's largest refugee camp. GDACS also flags ongoing flooding in Indonesia (now in its 56th episode) and a continuing drought across South-East Asia.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

The World Food Programme warned on 5 June that the escalating Middle East crisis — a war that began in February 2026 — is already pushing millions into hunger across the region and beyond. New WFP analysis shows the fallout is having [deep and long-lasting effects in some of the world's most vulnerable countries](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-warning-becomes-reality-millions-middle-east-crisis-pushes-poorest-families-further-hunger). Lebanon is among the worst affected, where WFP [scaled up its response on 1 June](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-scales-response-lebanon-displacement-crisis-enters-third-month-and-rising-costs-push-food) as more than one million people remain displaced and food prices surge beyond reach of vulnerable families.

In the Democratic Republic of the Congo, the Ebola outbreak in Ituri Province continues to demand emergency response. The International Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement [expressed serious concern on 6 June](https://www.icrc.org/en/news-release/international-red-cross-and-red-crescent-movement-expresses-concern-over-attack-red) after Red Cross volunteers carrying out Ebola burials were attacked in Bunia. WFP is simultaneously [scaling up its Ebola emergency response](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-scales-emergency-ebola-response-eastern-drc-contain-risk-regional-crisis) to prevent a health emergency from becoming a broader humanitarian catastrophe. Large forest fires are also burning in DRC, with GDACS reporting multiple events exceeding 14,000 hectares active as of 12 June.

In Sudan, nearly 19.5 million people — two in every five Sudanese — face crisis-level acute food insecurity (IPC Phase 3+), with famine risk persisting across multiple areas. More than 825,000 children face severe acute malnutrition, at 12 times the risk of death compared to well-nourished children. A joint AfDB-WFP [USD 87 million agriculture project (BOOST)](https://www.wfp.org/news/african-development-bank-launches-project-boost-agriculture-sudan) was signed on 5 June to begin rebuilding Sudan's food systems. In Somalia, the [African Development Bank Group, WFP and UNOPS launched a USD 11.8 million resilience initiative](https://www.wfp.org/news/somalia-african-development-bank-group-wfp-and-unops-partner-build-resilience-drought-affected) on 8 June, even as UN agencies warn famine risk is emerging with 6 million people facing critical food insecurity.

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

UNHCR released its flagship [Global Trends 2025 report](https://www.unhcr.org/news/press-releases/7-10-refugees-living-long-term-displacement-unhcr-chief-calls-renewed-push) on 11 June 2026, revealing that global forced displacement has decreased for the first time in a decade — but remains at an unacceptably high 41.6 million refugees. High Commissioner Barham Salih warned that 7 in 10 refugees are trapped in long-term displacement and called for a paradigm shift to reduce by more than half, over the next decade, the number of refugees in protracted exile relying on humanitarian assistance. Refugee returns were the second highest since records began (14.7 million returned in 2025), with significant returns to Afghanistan, Sudan and Syria — though many occurred under pressure.

In Yemen, [nearly half of the population in government-controlled areas faces acute food insecurity](https://www.wfp.org/news/nearly-half-population-government-controlled-areas-yemen-face-acute-food-insecurity) (3 June), with malnutrition risks rising and humanitarian funding sharply declining. WFP has also been forced to [scale back food assistance in Syria by 50 per cent](https://www.wfp.org/news/wfp-scales-back-food-assistance-syria-amid-funding-shortfalls) due to critical funding shortfalls. Major flooding is active in the United States (GDACS, 12 June 2026) and Honduras (10 June), with ongoing flood events across China and Myanmar.

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Active events requiring monitoring:**

A **magnitude 5.5 earthquake** struck the southern Philippines on 11 June 2026 (UTC), triggering a [Copernicus Emergency Management Service activation (EMSR882)](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR882/download) for satellite damage mapping. This follows the significant **M7.8 Philippines earthquake** earlier in June, for which the EC/ECHO is still producing daily situation maps ([latest map](https://erccportal.jrc.ec.europa.eu/ECHOProducts//Maps#/maps/5693)).

Tropical Cyclone **CRISTINA-26** remains active at 74 km/h (as of 11 June 2026, [GDACS tracking](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1001275&episodeid=13&eventtype=TC)), following active cyclone season activity across the Pacific basin. Cyclone Boris-26 and Amanda-26 have also been active this June.

Active flooding is being monitored in the **United States** (GDACS reports 12 June 2026) and **Honduras** (10 June 2026). An ongoing drought alert covers **Eastern Australia** (16 weeks) and **South-East Asia** (20 weeks), per GDACS monitoring.
