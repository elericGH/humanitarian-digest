*Produced Monday, 11 May 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia's international development sector was focused on two concurrent themes this week: regional disaster response in the Pacific and the federal budget's implications for the aid program.

**ACFID and the 2025–26 Federal Budget**: The Australian Council for International Development (ACFID) published analysis titled "Federal Budget: A vital opportunity to keep regional relationships on track," calling on the government to treat the aid budget as a strategic asset for Australia's regional standing. ACFID's position, consistent with its *Elevating Development* campaign, is that the aid program should reflect Australia's leadership responsibilities in the Indo-Pacific, particularly as Pacific island nations face compounding climate and humanitarian pressures. Members and sector observers have been urged to engage with the budget analysis and to amplify advocacy on maintaining and growing the aid budget. Source: [ACFID Federal Budget Analysis](https://acfid.asn.au/federal-budget/)

**ACFID Consolidated Appeals**: ACFID's consolidated emergency appeals remain active for three ongoing crises with clear Australian-donor implications. The Consolidated Appeal for the Israel and Palestine humanitarian crisis continues to raise funds through member agencies for Gaza and the broader Middle East response. The Consolidated Appeal for Sudan 2025 supports Australian NGO responses to one of the world's worst displacement emergencies. The Asia Storms Appeal 2025, which covered Typhoon and cyclone responses across the region, also remains active. These appeals channel Australian public giving through ACFID-accredited member NGOs, ensuring funds meet quality standards. Sources: [ACFID Middle East Appeal](https://acfid.asn.au/consolidated-appeal-middle-east-humanitarian-crisis2026/), [ACFID Sudan Appeal](https://acfid.asn.au/consolidated-appeal-sudan-2025/)

**Pacific Response — Cyclone Maila**: Australia's Pacific humanitarian responsibilities are front and centre this week as Tropical Cyclone Maila continues to affect Papua New Guinea (see Tier 1 for full coverage). The Pacific Humanitarian Team (PHT) — in which Australian agencies and DFAT play key roles — issued its third situation report on 8 May 2026. With over 107,000 people in need of humanitarian assistance across five PNG provinces, the scale of the response will require sustained Australian government and NGO engagement over the coming weeks. Source: [ReliefWeb TC Maila search](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=cyclone+maila)

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Tropical Cyclone Maila — Papua New Guinea (Ongoing)**

Tropical Cyclone Maila — which struck Papua New Guinea and the Solomon Islands in late April 2026 — remained a major active response as of this digest. The PNG National Disaster Centre's Situation Report No. 07, released 8 May, reported that assessments have now expanded to 230 wards across 28 Local-Level Government areas, covering 10 districts and 5 affected provinces. Preliminary findings indicate more than 119,000 people affected, with over 107,000 people in need of humanitarian assistance and 49 confirmed fatalities. Priority needs span food assistance, shelter and non-food items (NFIs), water sanitation and hygiene (WASH), health services, and logistics support to reach isolated communities. Multi-sector response is actively underway in East New Britain, Milne Bay, Bougainville (AROB), and New Ireland, with expansion planned to West New Britain. The Pacific Humanitarian Team's SitRep No. 3 (8 May) reflects the broader regional coordination picture, including the residual impact on the Solomon Islands (particularly Ranongga and Simbo Islands in Western Province, where building damage assessments were completed). Logistics remain constrained due to damaged infrastructure and difficult sea conditions, with operations reliant on air and maritime transport. Sources: [PNG DMT SitRep 07](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=papua+new+guinea+maila+disaster+management+team), [Pacific Humanitarian Team SitRep 3](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=cyclone+maila)

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano and Eastern Samar Earthquake**

Compounding events struck the Philippines this week. On 2 May, Mayon Volcano in Albay Province experienced increased activity, releasing dangerous pyroclastic flows and thick ashfall that forced evacuations across the region. As of 7 May, more than 1,400 families remained displaced and were sheltering in evacuation centres or with host families. Separately, on 4 May, a 6.1-magnitude earthquake struck near San Julian in Eastern Samar, causing strong shaking across Samar Island and neighbouring provinces. No casualties were officially confirmed from the earthquake, but damage assessments and aftershock monitoring are ongoing. CARE Philippines raised concerns about the compounding effects on women and girls, including loss of privacy, increased care burdens, and disrupted access to health services in evacuation settings. The organisation committed to a locally led response approach in coordination with community-based organisations and women-led groups. Source: [CARE Philippines Report, 7 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/care-highlights-stress-displacement-and-well-being-concerns-after-mayon-eruption-earthquake-philippines)

**Tonga — Dengue Fever Outbreak**

Tonga's dengue fever outbreak, now the subject of 18 consecutive situation reports from the national health authorities (SitReps 16, 17, and 18 were all issued between 5–8 May), reflects a sustained epidemic that shows no signs of resolution. The outbreak is part of a broader Pacific dengue pattern documented under a regional ReliefWeb disaster file dating to 2025–2026. Alerts on epidemic and emerging disease in the Pacific were issued on 5 May 2026 covering multiple island nations. The intensity of reporting (daily SitReps) signals a health system under strain. Sources: [Tonga SitRep 18](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=tonga+dengue+2026), [Pacific epidemic alerts](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=tonga+dengue+2026)

**South China Sea — Strategic Tensions**

The East Asia Forum published two pieces of direct relevance to regional stability this week. On 7 May, EAF ran analysis arguing that "joint development in the South China Sea is a strategic trap," cautioning states against development-based engagement frameworks that may entrench rival claims rather than manage them. This is particularly relevant for Australia's Pacific engagement given Beijing's diplomatic pushes in the region. On 8 May, EAF published a piece on ASEAN's green energy ambitions outpacing its workforce skills, with implications for climate-related development cooperation. Sources: [EAF, 7 May 2026](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/05/07/joint-development-in-the-south-china-sea-is-a-strategic-trap/), [EAF ASEAN, 8 May 2026](https://eastasiaforum.org/2026/05/08/aseans-green-ambitions-are-outpacing-its-skills/)

**Indonesia — Volcanic Activity and Landslide**

On 8 May, the EU's DG ECHO daily flash map recorded volcanic activity and a landslide in Indonesia, adding to the region's multi-hazard pressure. Details were limited as the week closed but Indonesia's geological risk profile means such events regularly trigger localised displacement and require monitoring. Source: [ECHO Daily Flash, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=indonesia+volcanic+landslide+ECHO+2026)

**Rohingya Camps — Bangladesh Measles Outbreak**

Although the initial IRC emergency declaration was in late April, the Bangladesh measles outbreak in the Rohingya refugee camps around Cox's Bazar continued to deepen into this digest period. On 4 May, MSF confirmed a response to the measles outbreak and a Bangladesh Red Cross DREF operation was activated (MDRBD039). The IRC's emergency response for 20,000 children in the camps underscores the ongoing public health vulnerability created by aid funding cuts that have hollowed out routine immunisation programs. Source: [IRC Measles Response, 30 Apr 2026](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=rohingya+measles+camps+2026)

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Somalia — On the Brink Again**

The World Food Programme's Assistant Executive Director, Matthew Hollingworth, used a Geneva press briefing on 8 May to deliver an urgent warning: Somalia is once again approaching famine-level conditions, and the window to push back is shrinking. Hollingworth, who had just returned from a week on the ground, described families facing the "cumulative effects of repeated extreme weather shocks, conflict, and economic pressure." Somalia has now experienced three consecutive failed rainy seasons. Across the country — particularly in Puntland — water resources have completely dried up, markets are severely strained, and recovery from previous crises has proven impossible for many communities. This is a crisis that has received sustained international attention for over two decades, but each cycle of emergency undermines long-term resilience. WFP warned that the response window before another famine classification is narrow. Earlier in the week, a multi-million Euro EU funding package was announced to support drought-stricken Somali communities (30 Apr), offering some relief — but observers noted this is far below what is needed. Source: [WFP Somalia, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=somalia+food+drought+hunger)

**South Sudan — Humanitarian Needs Escalating**

The week brought two important South Sudan updates. On 8 May, ReliefWeb headlines confirmed South Sudan's humanitarian needs "continue to rise" — an alarming but unfortunately predictable headline for a country in its third consecutive year of compounding crisis. An earlier ReliefWeb headline (27 April — within the digest window) reported that more than 100 women and girls are being displaced every hour in South Sudan, with some going days without food. South Sudan's humanitarian update 18 (30 April) also falls within this week's range. The trend lines are deeply concerning: displacement is accelerating, food access is worsening, and protection risks — particularly for women and girls — are intensifying. Sources: [ReliefWeb South Sudan Headlines](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=south+sudan+humanitarian&view=headlines)

**Lebanon — Hostilities Escalating Under Fragile Ceasefire**

Lebanon's fragile ceasefire was under serious stress this week. OCHA's Flash Update #24 on the escalation of hostilities in Lebanon was issued on 7 May 2026, and a conflict intensity snapshot covering the period 2 March – 7 May was released the same day. A separate IPC analysis from 29 April (within the digest window) found that conflict escalation is pushing nearly a quarter of Lebanese into acute food insecurity. The killing and displacement of women and girls in Lebanon "ongoing under a fragile ceasefire" was a featured headline on ReliefWeb on 8 May. UNHCR and IOM joint border monitoring on inflows from Iran (covering March–April) also signalled continued regional displacement pressures converging on Lebanon. Sources: [Lebanon Flash Update 24](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=lebanon+hostilities+ceasefire), [ReliefWeb Lebanon headlines](https://reliefweb.int/updates?view=headlines)

**Mali — Armed Conflict Escalation**

Mali saw a significant deterioration in security conditions in late April and early May 2026. According to the IRC, coordinated armed group attacks and growing insecurity along key transport routes have disrupted movement of people and goods, including fuel and food into Bamako itself. The IRC is scaling up and adapting programs and conducting ongoing assessments to determine safe program delivery. Mali hosts an estimated 5.1 million people in need of assistance in 2026, including more than 415,000 internally displaced people. Women, children, older people, and persons with disabilities face heightened protection risks alongside reduced service access. IRC described the situation as "one of the world's most underreported humanitarian crises." Source: [IRC Mali, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/mali/irc-responds-escalating-violence-mali-insecurity-disrupts-access-food-and-healthcare-irc)

**Myanmar — Attacks on Healthcare**

On 8 May, Insecurity Insight published its bi-monthly report on attacks on health care in Myanmar, covering the period 15–28 April 2026. MSF issued a statement in late April saying medical staff "deserve more than empty words" — a direct rebuke to statements of concern that have not been matched by action to protect medical facilities and personnel. Separately, a Crisis Situation Analysis covering 20–26 April was published on 4 May. These reports reflect a systematic pattern of attacks on health infrastructure in conflict zones across the country. Source: [Attacks on Healthcare Myanmar, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=myanmar+attacks+health+2026)

**Sudan — North Kordofan Protection Concerns**

A Protection Cluster Note from El Obeid, North Kordofan was issued on 8 May 2026, reflecting ongoing documentation of civilian protection violations in Sudan's conflict areas. This was issued in the same week that broader reporting on "three years into Sudan's war" tracked the cumulative toll: three children born into war every minute, rising maternal deaths, and collapsing health services (mid-April reporting, within the digest window). Sudan's trajectory remains dire, with millions displaced and the humanitarian system operating under severe access and funding constraints. Source: [ReliefWeb Sudan/South Sudan](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=south+sudan+humanitarian)

**Haiti — 1,600+ Killed in First Quarter 2026**

A human rights report published on 8 May 2026 confirmed that more than 1,600 people were killed in Haiti in the first quarter of 2026 alone, with the human rights situation described as "extremely worrying." Gang violence continues to ravage Port-au-Prince and surrounding areas, with humanitarian access severely constrained. This statistic — more than 17 deaths per day — reflects the scale of violence in a country that has received inadequate international attention compared to other crises of similar severity. Source: [ReliefWeb Haiti, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/updates)

**Gaza / Palestine — One Year After Ceasefire**

An April report (within the window: 10 April falls outside this week's strict range, but the ongoing Gaza situation merits coverage given the 8 May Palestine Human Rights Bulletin): the OHCHR's Palestine Human Rights Bulletin for April 2026 was published on 8 May, covering the period 1–30 April. The bulletin documents ongoing rights violations under a "ceasefire in name only," with a final Gaza Rapid Damage and Needs Assessment (published 21 April) estimating USD $71.4 billion will be needed for recovery and reconstruction — a figure that places Gaza's rebuilding challenge among the largest post-conflict recovery efforts in recent history. Sources: [Palestine Human Rights Bulletin, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/updates), [Gaza Damage Assessment](https://reliefweb.int/updates?view=headlines)

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Protection of Civilians Week — May 2026**

OCHA launched Protection of Civilians Week on 8 May 2026 with a dedicated report package. The timing underscores how normalised civilian harm has become across active conflict zones — from Gaza and Lebanon to South Sudan, Sudan, Myanmar, Haiti, and Mali. This year's focus on accountability comes as humanitarian access restrictions have tightened in multiple theatres simultaneously. Source: [PoC Week, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=protection+civilians+week+2026)

**Press Freedom at 25-Year Low**

ReliefWeb's 4 May headline report noted that press freedom has fallen to a 25-year low globally. This has direct humanitarian implications: reduced press access correlates with reduced accountability, impaired early warning, and greater impunity for actors who restrict humanitarian access or commit atrocities. Source: [Press Freedom, 4 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/updates?view=headlines)

**Ukraine — WFP Country Brief, April 2026**

WFP's Ukraine Country Brief covering April 2026 was published on 8 May, providing a monthly snapshot of food assistance operations in a country now entering the fifth year of full-scale war. WFP continues large-scale food distribution to conflict-affected populations in Ukraine. Source: [WFP Ukraine, 8 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/updates)

**Afghanistan — Flash Floods and Nutrition**

Afghanistan continued to face compounding shocks this week. DG ECHO flash updates on 5 May and 8 May documented ongoing flash floods. The Afghanistan Nutrition Cluster published its Q1 2026 performance snapshot on 9 May, giving the first systematic look at nutrition outcomes for the year. With food insecurity rising for the sixth consecutive year and the looming deportation of Afghans from neighbouring countries back into a nation where one-third of people cannot meet their food needs (per ReliefWeb, 30 April), the humanitarian situation shows no signs of stabilising. Sources: [ECHO Flash Floods Afghanistan](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=afghanistan+flash+floods+2026+ECHO), [Nutrition Cluster Q1](https://reliefweb.int/updates?search=afghanistan+nutrition)
