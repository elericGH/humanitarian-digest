## 🇦🇺 Australian Alerts

**Civil Society Campaign to Protect Australian Aid Launches at Parliament** — A coalition led by the National Council of Churches in Australia (NCCA) has launched a 'living' banner campaign at the Australian Parliament calling on the government to protect the aid budget ahead of the upcoming federal budget. The campaign kicks off this week (March 23–30) alongside the Micah Women's Network Tour, where 800+ women are expected to sign on in support of maintaining Australia's overseas development assistance. [NCCA Statement](https://www.ncca.org.au/statement-of-support-to-protect-australian-aid-ahead-of-the-budget/)

**Australia Redirecting Aid to Indo-Pacific at 40-Year High** — In response to widespread cuts to global aid from traditional donors, Australia is directing 75% of total overseas development assistance directly to the Indo-Pacific region — the highest proportion in 40 years. [Lowy Institute Analysis](https://www.lowyinstitute.org/publications/future-australian-aid)

## What's New Today

- **Afghanistan–Pakistan Conflict (Week 3)** — The armed conflict between Afghanistan and Pakistan is now entering its third week with no sign of abating. OCHA's 18 March situation update records 289 civilian casualties (76 killed, 213 injured) since hostilities began on 26 February, including 59 women and 104 children. Over 40,000 people across 75 assessed villages have been displaced, with 318+ shelters destroyed or damaged. [OCHA Situation Update #2](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-situation-update-2-humanitarian-impact-afghanistan-pakistan-military-escalation-18-march-2026)

- **Myanmar — Airstrikes Kill 25+ in Magway Region** — Fresh military airstrikes on a trading junction in central Magway region killed more than 25 people and wounded 20 others. This follows earlier strikes in early March that killed 30+ civilians in Nyaunglebin Township and 19 in southeast Myanmar. The UN notes the humanitarian situation "continues to worsen with each passing year." [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167099)

- **Indonesia — Flooding and Volcanic Lahar Disasters** — Floods across at least 35 Indonesian districts and cities are affecting approximately 23,000 people with thousands of homes damaged. Separately, volcanic lahar floods from Mt. Merapi in Magelang have killed three people, injured five, left two missing, and destroyed dozens of houses and bridges. [OCHA Asia-Pacific Snapshot](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-3-16-march-2026)

- **Gaza — Crossings Closed, Medicine Stocks Critical** — All crossings into Gaza except Kerem Shalom remain closed. UNRWA's latest situation report (Sitrep #213) notes 46% of essential medicines and 66% of medical consumables are out of stock, while 10 additional UNRWA installations now fall within Israel's expanded militarised buffer zone. Cumulative casualties since October 2023 stand at 72,135 killed and 171,830 injured. [UNRWA Sitrep #213](https://www.unrwa.org/resources/reports/unrwa-situation-report-213-humanitarian-crisis-gaza-strip-and-occupied-west-bank)

- **Sudan — Drone Strikes Hit Medical Facility in El Obeid** — Intensified fighting between the Sudanese Armed Forces and Rapid Support Forces continues in Kordofan and Blue Nile States. A drone attack struck a medical facility in El Obeid (North Kordofan capital), injuring 12 people including five medical staff. OCHA has called on all parties to protect civilians and allow humanitarian access. [UN News Brief](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167069)

- **Lebanon — Emergency Response Mechanism Active** — UNICEF's Rapid Response Mechanism has been reactivated since 2 March to address ongoing displacement waves and reach families on the move in Lebanon amid continued regional tensions. [ReliefWeb Flash Sitrep](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/lebanon-theater-emergency-flash-sitrep-2-march-2026)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

**Afghanistan–Pakistan conflict** is deepening rapidly, with 115,000 people now displaced according to OCHA's update, the border with Pakistan closed, and the alternative trade route through Iran increasingly uncertain. UN High Commissioner for Human Rights Türk has pleaded for dialogue, warning the conflict is "piling misery on misery" for Afghan civilians already facing one of the world's worst humanitarian crises. [OHCHR Statement](https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-releases/2026/03/turk-says-pakistan-afghanistan-conflict-piles-misery-misery-pleads-dialogue)
