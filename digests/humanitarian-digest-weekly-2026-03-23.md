## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

**🚨 EAA Launches Middle East Emergency Appeal (17 March 2026)**

Australia's Emergency Action Alliance (EAA) united on 17 March 2026 to launch a joint Middle East Appeal, raising funds for people in Lebanon, Syria, Gaza and the West Bank facing an escalating humanitarian crisis following military action by the US and Israel against Iran in early March. The coalition of 15 Australian charities — including Save the Children Australia, Plan International Australia, Oxfam Australia, CARE Australia, Caritas Australia, ADRA, and Australia for UNHCR — held a media conference led by CEOs Susanne Legena (Plan International Australia), Matt Tinkler (Save the Children Australia) and Morgana Ryan (CARE Australia). Campaign resources remain available through to 17 April 2026. [Source: RadioInfo Australia](https://radioinfo.com.au/news/how-community-broadcasters-can-support-the-emergency-action-alliance-middle-east-appeal/) | [B&T](https://www.bandt.com.au/australian-charities-unite-in-joint-appeal-amid-escalating-middle-east-emergency/) | [EAA](https://emergencyaction.org.au/)

**ACFID — Gender Equality Resolution & Pacific Climate Commitment**

ACFID's General Council unanimously passed a resolution calling on Australian leaders to advance gender equality at Women Deliver 2026. Earlier in the year, ACFID welcomed Australia's additional $550 million commitment to Pacific-led climate and development priorities through the Australian Infrastructure Financing Facility for the Pacific (AIFFP). [Source: ACFID](https://acfid.asn.au/)

**DFAT — Further Humanitarian Assistance for Gaza**

The Australian Foreign Minister confirmed further humanitarian assistance for Gaza this week, consistent with Australia's stated $709 million humanitarian assistance envelope for 2025–26. Australia pledged A$50 million in humanitarian aid for Afghanistan in January 2026, and continues to prioritise the Pacific and Asia-Pacific in its aid allocation. [Source: foreignminister.gov.au](https://www.foreignminister.gov.au/minister/penny-wong/media-release/further-humanitarian-assistance-gaza) | [DFAT](https://www.dfat.gov.au/development/topics/development-issues/building-resilience/australias-humanitarian-assistance)

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Myanmar: Compounding Crises Persist as UN Scales Response**

OCHA's Myanmar Humanitarian Update No. 51 (9 March 2026) confirmed that humanitarians are now targeting the 2.6 million people with the most severe needs in the country, amid persistent armed conflict, economic collapse, and the lingering aftermath of the 2025 earthquake. Significant underfunding, inflation, and access restrictions continue to leave large gaps in essential services. [Source: OCHA](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/myanmar-humanitarian-update-no51-9-march-2026)

**Asia-Pacific Regional Snapshot: 69 Disaster Events in ASEAN in a Single Week**

OCHA's Asia and Pacific Regional Humanitarian Snapshot (3–16 March 2026) reported 69 disaster events across the ASEAN region in week 10 alone, including floods, landslides, and storms in Indonesia, Thailand and Viet Nam, affecting more than 84,000 people with over 1,000 displaced. The region remains the world's most disaster-prone and accounts for more than three-quarters of global disaster displacements. [Source: OCHA](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-3-16-march-2026) | [ReliefWeb](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-3-16-march-2026)

**Pacific Islands: Preparedness Progress Amid Ongoing Vulnerability**

Pacific Islands News Roundup (March 12–18, 2026) noted that the Federated States of Micronesia opened its first sovereign humanitarian pre-positioning warehouse in Palikir — a positive step toward regional disaster readiness — even as Pacific island nations remain among the most vulnerable globally to climate-linked disasters and food insecurity. [Source: PI Report](https://pireport.org/2026/03/19/pacific-islands-news-roundup-march-12-18-2026/)

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Lebanon: 2026 War Displaces One Million People**

Since 2 March 2026, a new war between Israel and Hezbollah in Lebanon has killed more than 1,000 people and displaced nearly one million — 19% of the country's population. Airstrikes have struck Beirut, South Lebanon, and the Bekaa Valley, shutting 49 primary health care centres and five hospitals. WHO warned on 11 March that the conflict is deepening a health crisis across the entire Middle East. [Source: WHO](https://www.who.int/news/item/11-03-2026-conflict-deepens-health-crisis-across-middle-east--who-says) | [Time](https://time.com/article/2026/03/16/lebanon-israel-hezbollah-displaced/)

**Gaza: Hunger Crisis Deepens, Crossings Remain Closed**

As of 19 March 2026, OCHA's Humanitarian Situation Report documented that 72,135 Palestinians have been killed in Gaza since 7 October 2023, with 171,830 injured. More than 1.6 million people — 77% of the population — face crisis-level hunger, including over 100,000 at starvation level. All crossings except Kerem Shalom remain closed. UN WFP warned that the Middle East conflict risks causing the worst disruption to lifesaving humanitarian work since COVID-19. [Source: OCHA oPt](https://www.ochaopt.org/content/humanitarian-situation-report-19-march-2026) | [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167147)

**Sudan: Crisis Spills Across Borders**

Almost 4.5 million Sudanese have now fled to neighbouring countries — Chad, Egypt, Ethiopia, South Sudan and Uganda — placing critical strain on already fragile systems across the region. Sudan itself remains the largest humanitarian crisis in the world, with 24.6 million people (roughly half the population) facing acute food shortages, including 637,000 at catastrophic hunger levels. UNHCR's $1.6 billion Sudan Situation Appeal for 2026 remains critically underfunded. [Source: IFRC](https://www.ifrc.org/press-release/ifrc-warns-sudan-crisis-spills-across-borders-humanitarian-crisis-worsens) | [UNHCR](https://data.unhcr.org/en/documents/download/121184)

**DRC: M23 Consolidates Control as Displacement Record Set**

The Rwanda-backed M23 armed group has seized the DRC's largest eastern cities, establishing parallel administration and signalling long-term ambitions. The country now hosts the highest number of internally displaced people ever recorded within its borders — over 7.3 million — while the humanitarian response remains severely underfunded and access for aid workers is increasingly restricted. [Source: IRC](https://www.rescue.org/watchlist)

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Ukraine: Fifth Year of War, 10.8 Million in Need**

Ukraine enters its fifth year of full-scale war with 10.8 million people still requiring humanitarian assistance. The UN and partners have appealed for US$2.3 billion to reach 4.1 million prioritised individuals in 2026, but funding gaps are widening as major donor nations cut their aid budgets. [Source: UN Ukraine](https://ukraine.un.org/en/308290-relentless-war-ukraine-grinds-humanitarian-partners-aim-reach-41-million-people-2026)

**Afghanistan: 22 Million in Need, Aid Access Under Threat**

Afghanistan remains one of the world's largest humanitarian crises with 22 million people in need and 4.27 million Afghans displaced abroad. The termination of many USAID-funded medical programs, including nutrition and maternal health services, has caused severe service disruptions. [Source: IRC](https://www.rescue.org/article/top-10-crises-world-cant-ignore-2026)

**Global Aid Architecture: Landmark Study Projects 9.4 Million Deaths from Cuts by 2030**

One year on from the dismantling of USAID, a study published in *The Lancet* projects that the global aid cuts could cause at least 9.4 million additional deaths by 2030. Global humanitarian funding has collapsed from approximately $27.6 billion to around $8 billion as US cuts have been followed by reductions from the UK, Germany, Canada, and others. At the IRC alone, more than 6 million clients have experienced service interruptions, with 2 million losing services entirely. [Source: CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2026/02/04/world/lancet-usaid-global-aid-cuts-intl) | [Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/my-europe/2026/03/20/after-a-year-of-trumps-cuts-to-foreign-aid-has-humanitarian-funding-suffered) | [GBH](https://www.wgbh.org/news/local/2026-03-09/a-year-after-usaid-cuts-local-groups-say-impact-on-humanitarian-work-has-been-devastating)
