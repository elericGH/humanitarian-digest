# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Monday, March 30, 2026
*24-hour scan | Australian Interest Priority*

## 🇦🇺 Australian Alerts

**Declaration for the Protection of Humanitarian Personnel** — Australia has formally launched the Declaration for the Protection of Humanitarian Personnel, with more than 100 countries joining at a ceremony at the United Nations. Foreign Minister Penny Wong honoured Australian aid worker Zomi Frankcom and the 674 humanitarian personnel killed since the start of 2024. The Albanese Government is contributing $15 million to practical protection measures through the UN Department of Safety and Security, OCHA, and the Red Cross and Red Crescent Movement. [Foreign Minister Media Release](https://www.foreignminister.gov.au/minister/penny-wong/media-release/australia-launches-global-declaration-protect-aid-workers)

**Humanitarian Program 2026–27 Discussion Paper** — The Department of Home Affairs has released the discussion paper for Australia's Humanitarian Program 2026–27, seeking public submissions. The Community Refugee Integration and Settlement Program (CRISP) pilot is being made permanent with an initial 200 places. [Home Affairs](https://www.homeaffairs.gov.au/reports-and-publications/submissions-and-discussion-papers/australias-humanitarian-program-2026-27)

## What's New Today

- **Lebanon** — Since the resumption of hostilities on March 2, more than one million people have been forced to flee their homes, with over 134,000 now in collective shelters. Airstrikes have impacted Beirut, South Lebanon, and the Bekaa Valley, damaging homes, health facilities, and critical infrastructure. [OCHA](https://www.unocha.org/asia-and-pacific) | [Al Jazeera](https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2026/3/28/israels-unending-attacks-in-lebanon-push-countrys-population-to-the-brink)

- **Gaza / Occupied Palestinian Territory** — UN human rights experts warn that Israel's aid restrictions are inflicting severe gender-specific harm, with women and girls bearing a disproportionate share of deprivation across food, healthcare, shelter, water, and sanitation. All crossings except Kerem Shalom remain closed, with 46% of essential medicines out of stock. [OHCHR](https://www.ohchr.org/en/press-releases/2026/03/aid-restrictions-deepen-crisis-occupied-palestinian-territories-women-and) | [UNRWA](https://www.unrwa.org/resources/reports/unrwa-situation-report-213-humanitarian-crisis-gaza-strip-and-occupied-west-bank)

- **Myanmar** — Ongoing military airstrikes continue to kill and displace civilians across multiple regions. A strike on a market in Let Pan Hla village, Mandalay Region, killed at least 27 civilians including young children; further strikes hit a monastery in Sagaing Region killing 17. An estimated 3.6 million people remain internally displaced. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167099) | [HRW](https://www.hrw.org/news/2026/03/27/a-year-on-from-myanmars-earthquake)

- **Sudan** — Famine conditions are confirmed in Al Fasher and Kadugli, with famine risk in 20 additional areas. The 2026 Sudan response plan ($2.9 billion) has received only 5.5% of required funding; the IFRC warns the crisis is now spilling across borders into Chad, Egypt, Ethiopia, South Sudan, and Uganda as food rations in refugee camps are cut by half. [IFRC](https://www.ifrc.org/press-release/ifrc-warns-sudan-crisis-spills-across-borders-humanitarian-crisis-worsens) | [Action Against Hunger](https://www.actionagainsthunger.org/press-releases/sudan-becomes-the-worlds-hungriest-country-as-famine-spreads-to-two-new-areas-of-darfur/)

- **Asia-Pacific — Middle East Ripple Effects** — ESCAP and UN News warn that Middle East escalation is disrupting fuel and supply chains across import-dependent Asia-Pacific nations including Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Bangladesh, and the Philippines. Benchmark urea fertiliser prices have risen ~25% since the conflict escalated, threatening food security. Tens of thousands of migrant workers — including 1.7 million Nepalis in the Gulf — are stranded or at risk. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167167)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

Lebanon's humanitarian situation has entered what aid workers describe as a significantly worse phase than 2024: more than one million people displaced since early March, with collective shelters overwhelmed and civilian infrastructure suffering sustained damage from ongoing airstrikes. Funding and access constraints mean the response is severely under-resourced relative to need.
