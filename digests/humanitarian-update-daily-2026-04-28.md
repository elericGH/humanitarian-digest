## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Afghanistan — Multi-province floods kill at least 13.** Heavy rain and thunderstorms across ten Afghan provinces (Kabul, Bamiyan, Paktia, Ghor, Badghis, Balkh, Samangan, Sar-e Pol, Jawzjan and Baghlan) have caused floods that killed at least 13 people and injured nine, with around 200 houses damaged or destroyed and 17 water-supply networks plus 12 km of roads affected. Moderate to locally heavy rainfall is forecast over northern and north-eastern Afghanistan in the next 48 hours, per the EU's ERCC daily report relayed via [GDACS (27 Apr 2026)](https://gdacs.org/).

**Bangladesh — 14 dead from lightning in 26 April thunderstorms.** Severe thunderstorms hit several Bangladeshi districts on 26 April, with 14 fatalities reported across seven districts (including five in Gaibandha, two each in Sirajganj, Thakurgaon and Jamalpur, and one each in Bogura, Natore and Panchagarh) and at least 12 injured. Moderate thunderstorms with continued lightning are forecast across the whole country on 27–28 April. [GDACS / ERCC, 27 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org/).

**Japan — Iwate wildfires force ~3,000 evacuations.** Wildfires in the Kozuchi and Kirikiri districts of Otsuchi (Iwate Prefecture, Honshu) have burned more than 700 hectares, prompting more than 3,000 evacuations and destroying around eight buildings. About 700 personnel and aerial assets including helicopters are deployed; fire-danger forecasts for the next 72 hours are low. [GDACS / ERCC, 27 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org/).

**Federated States of Micronesia — Tropical Cyclone Sinlaku-26 mapping continues.** Copernicus EMS released new rapid-mapping products (EMSR872) and an EC/ECHO daily map for SINLAKU-26 on 27 April, supporting ongoing impact assessment in Micronesia after the storm earlier peaked at category-equivalent winds of 287 km/h. [Copernicus EMS map via GDACS, 27 Apr 2026](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR872/download).

**Pakistan–Afghanistan — Ceasefire under strain over cross-border attacks.** Both Islamabad and Kabul reported cross-border incidents on 27 April that placed the recent ceasefire at risk, with civilian-protection implications for displaced and border-area communities. [Al Jazeera, 27 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/27/ceasefire-at-risk-as-pakistan-and-afghanistan-report-cross-border-attacks).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Sudan — Thousands reportedly held by RSF in el-Fasher.** An NGO operating in North Darfur reports thousands of civilians being held by paramilitary Rapid Support Forces in el-Fasher, intensifying concerns over civilian protection as Sudan's war enters a fourth year. [Al Jazeera, 27 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/27/thousands-reportedly-held-by-paramilitary-rsf-in-sudans-el-fasher). The ICRC continues to flag mass displacement into nearby Tawila, North Darfur, as people flee hostilities in al-Fasher. [ICRC – Sudan, news series, 27 Apr 2026 archive](https://www.icrc.org/en/news-release/sudan-human-cost-three-years-war).

**Lebanon — Four killed in southern Lebanon as Israeli strikes resume.** Al Jazeera's live war tracker reported four people killed in Israeli strikes in southern Lebanon on 27 April, alongside fresh Hezbollah–Israel cross-border threats. The ICRC's most recent operational update on 22 April warned that hostilities continue to disrupt water and essential services in Lebanon. [Al Jazeera live blog, 27 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/liveblog/2026/4/27/iran-war-live-araghchi-to-meet-putin-trump-says-tehran-can-call-for-talks); [ICRC, 22 Apr 2026](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/lebanon-maintaining-water-and-essential-lifelines-hostilities-disrupt-essential-services).

**Iran — Tehran proposes Hormuz deal as US-Israel war continues.** Iran's foreign minister broadened diplomatic outreach, offering a Strait of Hormuz arrangement decoupled from nuclear talks while the war with the US and Israel continues into its third month. Casualty trackers compiled by Al Jazeera report 3,375 dead in Iran, 2,509 in Lebanon and 28 in Gulf states. The ICRC delivered its first batch of emergency relief items to the Iranian Red Crescent in mid-April and reiterated its FAQ on its work in Iran. [Al Jazeera, 27 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/27/iran-offers-hormuz-deal-without-nuclear-talks-as-it-seeks-broader-buy-in); [ICRC, 14 Apr 2026](https://www.icrc.org/en/news-release/iran-icrc-delivers-emergency-relief-items-iranian-red-crescent-society).

**Democratic Republic of the Congo — Paramilitary guard for mines.** The DRC announced plans on 27 April to establish a US- and UAE-backed paramilitary guard force for its mining operations, raising civilian-protection and accountability concerns in conflict-affected eastern provinces. [Al Jazeera, 27 Apr 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/27/dr-congo-to-establish-us-uae-backed-paramilitary-guard-for-mines).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**United States — Texas and Oklahoma tornado damage.** A series of tornadoes struck Texas and Oklahoma between 23–25 April, killing two and injuring six in Wise County, northern Texas, with 20 families displaced; another tornado in Garfield County, northern Oklahoma injured 10 and damaged 40 homes. More severe thunderstorms are forecast over the next 48 hours in the affected and central US areas. [GDACS / ERCC, 27 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org/).

**Paraguay — Severe weather flooding affects 1,730+ people.** Since 15 April, intense rainfall has caused multiple flooding events in Paraguay, with the IFRC reporting 1,730 people affected as of 27 April — including 1,500 in the Paraguayan Chaco region after the Pilcomayo River overflowed between 21–26 April. Further rainfall is forecast across most of Paraguay over the next 24 hours. [GDACS / ERCC, 27 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org/).

**Global food security — Acute hunger remains alarmingly high.** WFP, FAO and partners flagged on 24 April that acute food insecurity and malnutrition remain at alarming levels as overlapping crises deepen, with 318 million people facing acute hunger globally and WFP citing a USD 13 billion funding need. [WFP news release, 24 Apr 2026](https://www.wfp.org/news/acute-food-insecurity-and-malnutrition-remain-alarmingly-high-crises-deepen-un-eu-and-partners).
