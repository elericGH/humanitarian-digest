## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Pacific and Indigenous community leaders are urging Australia to lead by example on climate change as it takes the driving seat at COP31 in Turkey, where Climate Change Minister Chris Bowen will serve as president of negotiations. The Government has pledged more than $150 million for November's global summit, but Yuin woman Takesa Frank of the Oceania First Voices workshop warned Australia has "so far to go" on climate action and must not overlook First Nations voices. Ahead of COP31, Pacific leaders will host a Pre-COP gathering to give delegates first-hand experience of climate impacts on island nations, where sea surface temperatures are rising at three times the global average and climate disaster costs have soared above $7 billion in the past decade ([AAP via PACNEWS/Islands Business, 16 July 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-one-16-july-2026/)).

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Senior officials from Pacific Islands Forum member countries are meeting in Nadi, Fiji, from 15 to 17 July for the Climate Finance Access and Mobilisation Strategy (CFAMS) Preparatory and Prioritisation Workshop. Forum Deputy Secretary General Desna Solofa warned that despite growing global commitments, Pacific countries still face lengthy approval processes, fragmented funding and complex financing requirements, calling climate change "an existential challenge" for the region. The workshop is building a pipeline of investment-ready climate and resilience projects ahead of the inaugural Pacific Climate Finance Investment Forum in 2027 ([PIFS via PACNEWS/Islands Business, 16 July 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-one-16-july-2026/)).

Nearly 70 young climate leaders from across the Pacific gathered for the Second Pacific Youth Talanoa Series Dialogue, led by the Pacific Youth Council with the UN Pacific Office, to strengthen youth engagement on the road to the Pacific-led Pre-COP31 that Fiji hosts this October. Fiji's chief climate negotiator Dr Sivendra Michael urged young people to become actively involved in international climate processes ([Fiji Government via PACNEWS/Islands Business, 16 July 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-one-16-july-2026/)).

Researchers convening in Auckland next week for the International Coral Reef Symposium — held in New Zealand for the first time — warn that the forecast El Niño event this summer will hit South Pacific coral reefs particularly hard, with marine heatwaves expected to drive mass bleaching. Experts urged South Pacific governments to send delegates, noting reef islands provide the only habitable land for more than 700,000 people in nations such as Tuvalu, Kiribati and the Marshall Islands ([Science Media Centre NZ via PACNEWS/Islands Business, 16 July 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-one-16-july-2026/)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Israeli attacks killed at least five Palestinians across the Gaza Strip as Israel continues near-daily violations of the October ceasefire. The humanitarian crisis remains largely unchanged owing to continuing Israeli restrictions on the volume of aid entering the enclave, with only about a third of promised trucks entering daily ([Al Jazeera, 16 July 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/7/16/israeli-attacks-kill-at-least-five-palestinians-in-gaza-despite-ceasefire)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

An opinion piece in The New Humanitarian argues the recent WFP cyber-attack — which exposed data of hundreds of thousands of Gaza households — shares a common root with global visa-processing scandals: states and agencies outsourcing public functions, from refugee registration to humanitarian aid, to intermediaries a step removed from domestic scrutiny and accountability ([The New Humanitarian, 16 July 2026](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/opinion/2026/07/16/what-wfp-cyber-attack-has-common-visa-scandals)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

A Flash Flood Emergency is in effect in Central Texas (USA), where the National Weather Service warned a "large and deadly flood wave" is moving down the Pedernales River through Blanco and Gillespie counties, with further emergencies declared for parts of Kendall, Kerr and Uvalde counties. At least one death has been confirmed, helicopter rescues are under way, and additional shelters have been opened ([KXAN, 16 July 2026](https://www.kxan.com/news/texas/flash-flood-emergency-in-texas-thursday-july-16-2026/)).
