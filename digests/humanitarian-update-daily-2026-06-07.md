## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Pacific nations adopt landmark climate relocation framework.** In a major humanitarian and climate policy development for the region, Pacific governments have agreed to the world's first regional framework for planned climate relocation, providing guidance for communities facing displacement driven by rising seas, coastal erosion, and intensifying storms. The framework creates shared standards for how Pacific Island nations approach — and fund — the managed retreat of vulnerable communities. [The Diplomat, 6 June 2026](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/pacific-nations-adopt-first-regional-climate-relocation-framework/)

**Dengue spreading across 17 Pacific countries.** Between 1 January and 4 June 2026, 3,521 confirmed dengue cases were reported across the Pacific, with 156 hospitalisations and 3 deaths. The Pacific Syndromic Surveillance System additionally logged 7,234 dengue-like illness reports across 17 countries and territories. Humanitarian actors and health systems across the region face increased pressure as the outbreak continues. [ReliefWeb / OCHA, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215025)

**Sumatra floods linked to governance failure.** A new analysis argues that deforestation, enabled by weak oversight and opaque licensing regulations, transformed seasonal rainfall in Sumatra into a large-scale catastrophe. The piece has direct relevance for disaster risk reduction practitioners operating in the Asia-Pacific, highlighting systemic factors that convert hazards into humanitarian emergencies. [The Diplomat, 6 June 2026](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/how-government-inaction-turned-sumatras-rains-into-a-national-catastrophe/)

**Myanmar junta deepens Russia ties amid humanitarian crisis.** Myanmar's military is seeking Russian technical assistance for a stalled cyber infrastructure project, adding complexity to the operating environment for humanitarian organisations in the country. Conflict-driven displacement and access constraints continue to affect millions of civilians. [The Diplomat, 5 June 2026](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/myanmars-military-is-turning-to-russia-to-revive-its-stalled-cyber-dream/)

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Lebanon Flash Appeal revised as crisis deepens.** UN Humanitarian Coordinator Imran Riza addressed the launch of a revised Flash Appeal for Lebanon, more than three months into severe conflict. Communities face "an appalling toll" including widespread casualties, repeated displacement, destruction of housing and basic infrastructure, and widespread psychological trauma. In southern Beirut's Dahiyeh suburb — hit by hundreds of airstrikes since the 2 March 2026 escalation — healthcare centres have lost staff, been damaged, or shut down entirely. [ReliefWeb / OCHA, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215016) | [Lebanon health brief, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4214998)

**WFP: Middle East conflict now pushing millions into hunger.** Three months after warning the crisis could push 45 million people into acute food insecurity, WFP confirmed the scenario is now unfolding. With oil prices near US$100/barrel affecting supply chains and livelihoods across the region, WFP's Director of Food Security Analysis stated: "We warned that this crisis could push millions more people into hunger; now we are seeing it happen." The Lebanon conflict intensity snapshot for 2 March to 4 June 2026 was also released. [ReliefWeb / WFP, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4214982) | [Lebanon snapshot, 6 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215081)

**Gaza: Protection cluster flags deepening displacement risks.** The May 2026 Gaza Protection Cluster Snapshot documents how risks for displaced populations continue to worsen, shaped by deepening food insecurity, deteriorating living conditions, and shrinking humanitarian access. Communities face increasing challenges with civil documentation, digital assistance systems, and reduced access near military zones. [ReliefWeb / OCHA, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4214975)

**Ebola outbreak in DRC declared third-largest on record.** The Bundibugyo Ebola outbreak in eastern DRC — for which there is no approved vaccine or treatment — is now the third largest on record. Underfunding is creating critical supply gaps for health workers. IOM's Situation Report No. 3 (26 May to 2 June) confirms the outbreak is evolving in both DRC and Uganda, with WHO maintaining both as Priority 1a countries and flagging South Sudan, Burundi, and Rwanda at Priority 1b. [ReliefWeb / WFP, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215030) | [IOM Sitrep 3, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215031)

**Sudan: Displacement crisis and new $87M resilience investment.** UNHCR's June 2026 tracking confirms large-scale cross-border displacement from Sudan into six or more neighbouring countries. Separately, the African Development Bank and WFP signed the USD 87 million BOOST project to restore agricultural production and farmer incomes over four years. WFP noted that farmers engaged in pre-war resilience activities "did not require food assistance even during the war." [UNHCR, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4214996) | [AfDB/WFP BOOST, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215010) | [Chad-Sudan logistics snapshots, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215028)

**Burkina Faso: WFP reached 415,000 in April amid access constraints.** In April, WFP assisted 415,351 people in Burkina Faso with 3,590 metric tonnes of food — slightly down from March due to operational constraints. Ongoing insecurity continues to severely limit humanitarian access across large parts of the country. [ReliefWeb / WFP, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4214978)

**Syria flooding mapped by EU's ECHO.** The EU humanitarian mapping office released an updated daily map tracking active flooding in Syria as of 5 June 2026, compounding an already severe humanitarian situation. [ReliefWeb / ECHO, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215064)

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Haiti displacement hits record 1.5 million.** IOM figures confirm nearly 1.5 million people are now displaced in Haiti — a historic high. Recent violence in Cité Soleil alone displaced over 18,000 people, pushing Port-au-Prince's internally displaced population above 300,000 for the first time. More than half of those displaced are women and girls. OCHA's Latin America and Caribbean weekly update additionally flagged 20,000 confirmed measles cases across the region in 2026 — four times the rate of the same period in 2025. [ReliefWeb / IOM, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215009) | [OCHA LACRE weekly, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215071)

**Ukraine: WFP warehouse struck by ballistic missile.** Intensified long-range attacks on Kyiv, Kharkiv, and Dnipro are increasing constraints on humanitarian mission delivery in Ukraine. A WFP-contracted warehouse was directly hit by a ballistic missile during May, underscoring the extreme risks facing humanitarian logistics. Separately, OCHA's access team reported increasing barriers to mission delivery across front-line areas. [ReliefWeb / WFP Ukraine Brief, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/wfp-ukraine-country-brief-june-2026-reporting-period-may-2026-enuk) | [OCHA Coordination Meeting, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215017)

**Myanmar: Sustained attacks on healthcare documented.** Insecurity Insight's latest report documents the devastating impact of violence on Myanmar's health system since the February 2021 coup, with attacks on health workers and facilities continuing to undermine disease surveillance and healthcare resilience. The reporting period covers 13 to 26 May 2026. [ReliefWeb / Insecurity Insight, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215014)

**FAO food prices broadly stable but cereals rise.** The FAO Food Price Index averaged 130.8 points in May 2026 — down 0.2% from April but 2.9% higher year-on-year. Rising cereal prices underscore vulnerability to weather-related risks and disruptions in energy and input markets, with continued uncertainty affecting key trade routes. [ReliefWeb / FAO, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215000)

**Start Fund identifies Sudan among June's highest-risk situations.** The Start Fund's June 2026 Risk Bulletin flagged Sudan as a high-priority anticipated hazard, noting over 740,000 refugees have fled to neighbouring countries under harrowing conditions, with more than 185,000 internally displaced within Sudan since the conflict began. [ReliefWeb / Start Network, 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/node/4215001)

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

**Germany — Flooding (Green, updated 6 June 2026).** A flood that began 29 May in Germany remained active as of 6 June, causing 2 deaths and 30 displaced persons. This is the only GDACS-tracked event with an update timestamp within the 24-hour window. [GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103919)

**South Africa — Flooding (Green, 5 June 2026).** A flood alert was issued 5 June for South Africa, beginning 3 June, with 764 people displaced and no deaths reported. [GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103929)

**Honduras — Flooding (Green, 5 June 2026).** A flood alert was issued 5 June for Honduras, beginning 2 June, resulting in 1 death. [GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103928)

**Tropical Cyclone AMANDA-26 — Dissipated 6 June 2026.** TC Amanda-26 was active in the East Pacific from 2 to 6 June 2026 as a Tropical Depression (max 74 km/h winds). It affected no populated areas and has now dissipated. Green alert. [GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=TC&eventid=1001273)

**Balleny Islands Region — M5.6 Earthquake, 5 June 2026.** A magnitude 5.6 earthquake struck the uninhabited Balleny Islands Region (Southern Ocean) at 20:58 UTC on 5 June 2026. No people within 100 km. Green alert. [GDACS](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1544408)
