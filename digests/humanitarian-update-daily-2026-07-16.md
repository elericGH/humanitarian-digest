## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Papua New Guinea has reshuffled the ministries responsible for disaster management. Prime Minister James Marape swore in six new ministers at Government House on Tuesday, moving Health Minister Elias Kapavore to Defence while Dr Billy Joseph takes over the Health Ministry and retains the National Disaster Ministry, which formerly sat under Defence. Marape said the changes are aimed at repositioning the government ahead of national elections ([PACNEWS via Islands Business, 15 July 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-one-15-july-2026/)).

Tuvalu has called for stronger United Nations support to tackle security threats facing Pacific island states, in a bulletin that also reports New Zealand's Pacific Islands Forum budget could face a NZ$10 million squeeze, according to Official Information Act disclosures — a development with potential flow-on effects for regional programming ([PACNEWS via Islands Business, 15 July 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-3/)).

Fiji's Defence Minister Pio Tikoduadua outlined wide-ranging security sector reforms in Parliament, including strengthening the national search and rescue framework, advancing Fiji's maritime security strategy, and developing the country's first national peacekeeping strategy — reforms directly relevant to the region's disaster response architecture ([PACNEWS via Islands Business, 15 July 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-one-15-july-2026/)).

In a food security and maritime enforcement development, the Tuvalu Police Service, patrolling aboard the Sea Shepherd vessel Allankay, boarded and arrested the Chinese longliner Lu Rong Yuan Yu 138 for suspected illegal, unreported and unregulated fishing in Tuvaluan waters. The vessel was fishing at night without navigation lights while transmitting false AIS positions, and its FFA fishing authorisation had expired; it is detained in Funafuti pending investigation ([Sea Shepherd via PACNEWS/Islands Business, 15 July 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-one-15-july-2026/)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Humanitarians in Gaza continue to face significant challenges reaching communities near the "Yellow Line" in northern Rafah, OCHA reported. The UN and partners consulted representatives of 17 displacement sites hosting some 3,000 families following reports of Israeli force movements and disruptions to services; initial reports indicated one Palestinian was killed and three injured at one site, and insecurity is severely disrupting delivery of water, food, bread and hygiene support. UNICEF says 1.1 million children face uncertain daily access to water and 82 per cent of households are water insecure ([UN News, 14 July 2026](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167939); [OCHA](https://www.unocha.org/news/todays-top-news-occupied-palestinian-territory-lebanon-sudan-democratic-republic-congo)).

Sudan's cholera outbreak continues to expand amid multiple drone attacks across the Darfur and Kordofan regions, OCHA said. New suspected cases were confirmed in Kampala displacement camp in South Darfur and a first suspected case in Tawila, North Darfur, while West Kordofan recorded six new suspected cases and two deaths. WHO reported 1,330 confirmed cases and 114 deaths as of 7 July. Drone strikes hit a fuel station in El Obeid and trucks on the El Fasher–Um Kadada road; UNICEF delivered health supplies to El Fula on 12 July to support nearly 430,000 people ([UN News, 14 July 2026](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/07/1167939)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

The United Nations has defended the International Criminal Court as "a critical cog" in the international justice system after US Secretary of State Marco Rubio said he wants to dismantle the Court. UN Spokesperson Stéphane Dujarric said the ICC "helps bring accountability for serious crimes" and that international law is "under threat and under attack" — a debate with direct implications for accountability in humanitarian crises ([UN News Centre via PACNEWS/Islands Business, 15 July 2026](https://islandsbusiness.com/pacnews/pacnews-one-15-july-2026/)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

Response and recovery operations continue following Typhoon Bavi (locally Inday). In the Philippines, the combined effects of Bavi and the enhanced southwest monsoon left 20 people dead and 16 missing, with about 654,000 people affected nationwide and roughly 20,100 in evacuation centres, according to the NDRRMC ([SunStar, 13 July 2026](https://www.sunstar.com.ph/manila/typhoon-inday-death-toll-climbs-to-20-16-still-missing); [The Watchers, 13 July 2026](https://watchers.news/2026/07/13/20-reported-dead-16-missing-as-bavi-enhanced-monsoon-triggers-floods-and-landslides-philippines/)).

Bavi subsequently made landfall in China's Zhejiang province, described by Chinese officials as the strongest July typhoon to strike Zhejiang since 1949, with orange rainstorm and typhoon alerts renewed and elevated risk of flash floods and urban waterlogging ([Hong Kong Free Press, 12 July 2026](https://hongkongfp.com/2026/07/12/typhoon-bavi-makes-landfall-in-china-as-death-toll-in-philippines-rises-to-18/)). No new red-level sudden-onset alerts were confirmed in the past 24 hours.
