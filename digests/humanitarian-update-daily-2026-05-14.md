## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia's 2026–27 Federal Budget, handed down on 12 May 2026, has maintained overseas aid funding at broadly the same level as previous years, but the peak civil society body ACFID has called for stronger investment given mounting global instability. ACFID noted that holding aid flat in real terms amounts to a declining contribution as crises deepen in the Middle East, Sudan, and the Pacific. The organisation urged the government to use Australia's relative stability and international relationships to step into the growing leadership vacuum left by retreating traditional donors. ([ACFID, 12 May 2026](https://acfid.asn.au/federal-budget-holds-for-aid/))

GDACS is tracking an ongoing drought alert for Australia, a long-running condition affecting parts of inland Australia that has continued through autumn 2026. Separately, multiple low-severity (Green) forest fire notifications were issued for sites in Australia on 13 May, with no population displacement reported. ([GDACS, 13 May 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=DR&eventid=1018457))

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Two green-level earthquakes struck the southwest Pacific within hours of each other on 12 May. A magnitude 5.7 event at a depth of 154 km was recorded near Tonga, with approximately one thousand people in MMI-III shaking zones — no tsunami warning was issued. A separate magnitude 5.6 earthquake struck near the Solomon Islands at 68 km depth, affecting an estimated 10,000 people within 100 km; both events were assessed as low-impact by GDACS. ([GDACS – Tonga EQ](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1540431); [GDACS – Solomon Islands EQ](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1540398))

The Ibu volcano on Halmahera Island, Indonesia, remains in an active eruptive phase as of 11 May 2026, with GDACS continuing to monitor the situation. Ibu has been intermittently active throughout 2025–26, and Indonesian authorities have maintained local exclusion zones. While the alert level remains informational, continued activity could affect communities near the volcano if eruptions intensify. ([GDACS – Ibu Volcano](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=VO&eventid=1000141))

A ReliefWeb update published on 13 May covers the Peace Accords Matrix for Mindanao (PAM-M), documenting civil society normalization progress in the southern Philippines through December 2025. The report highlights fragile but sustained gains under the Bangsamoro peace framework, an area of ongoing Australian development partnership interest. ([ReliefWeb – Philippines, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/peace-accords-matrix-mindanao-pam-m-and-civil-society-normalization-update-report-september-december-2025))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

Lebanon continues to experience a deeply fragile ceasefire that took effect on 17 April 2026. OCHA's Flash Update #25, published on 13 May, reports ongoing escalation of hostilities despite the nominal halt, with civilian casualties mounting. The Norwegian Refugee Council issued a stark assessment the same day: nearly 600 people have been killed since the ceasefire was agreed, while more than one million people remain internally displaced across the country. ([ReliefWeb – OCHA Lebanon Flash Update #25, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-flash-update-25-escalation-hostilities-lebanon-11-may-2026); [ReliefWeb – NRC Lebanon, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/lebanon-nearly-600-killed-fragile-ceasefire-agreed-nrc))

South Sudan is facing an alarming deterioration in Akobo state, where renewed fighting since December 2025 has triggered mass displacement. The International Rescue Committee warned on 13 May that communities are being pushed dangerously close to IPC Phase 5 (Catastrophe) malnutrition levels — the most severe category. The IRC's warning comes alongside a South Sudan Health Cluster dashboard for March 2026, also published on 13 May, which tracks a healthcare system under severe pressure across the country. ([ReliefWeb – IRC South Sudan, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-renewed-fighting-akobo-state-leading-alarming-humanitarian-crisis-warns-irc); [ReliefWeb – South Sudan Health Cluster, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-health-cluster-dashboard-march-2026))

Syria's food security situation has worsened significantly following a WFP funding shortfall. On 13 May, WFP announced it has cut emergency food assistance by 50 percent — from 1.3 million beneficiaries to 650,000 — and has halted a nationwide bread subsidy programme that previously supported millions daily. The funding crisis reflects broader global humanitarian financing pressures and threatens to reverse fragile post-conflict stabilisation gains. ([ReliefWeb – WFP Syria, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/syrian-arab-republic/wfp-scales-back-food-assistance-syria-amid-funding-shortfalls))

Sudan's humanitarian emergency continues to deepen. ACAPS published a thematic risk analysis on 13 May warning of compounding vulnerabilities across the country after more than two years of civil war. Separately, OCHA's Situation Report #66 for the Central African Republic, also issued on 13 May, describes continued displacement and access constraints affecting humanitarian operations. An OCHA-backed protection cluster snapshot for the occupied West Bank, covering February to April 2026, documents ongoing demolitions and settler violence against Palestinian civilians. ([ReliefWeb – ACAPS Sudan, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/acaps-sudan-risk-analysis-2026-thematic-report-13-may-2026); [ReliefWeb – CAR Sit Rep, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/central-african-republic/republique-centrafricaine-rapport-de-situation-ndeg66-au-12-mai-2026); [ReliefWeb – oPt West Bank, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/west-bank-protection-cluster-snapshot-protection-monitoring-reporting-period-1-feb-30-apr-2026))

Mozambique's cholera outbreak remains active, with the government's daily bulletin for 12 May published on 13 May via ReliefWeb tracking case counts and response activities across affected provinces. ([ReliefWeb – Mozambique Cholera, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/mozambique/boletim-diario-da-colera-data-12052026))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

Ukraine remains under sustained humanitarian pressure. The Health Cluster published on 13 May an update on partner emergency responses to attacks through 30 April 2026, documenting health facility damage and disrupted medical supply chains. The Association of Medical Doctors of Asia (AMDA) also released Emergency Relief Bulletin #56 covering Ukraine on 13 May, noting ongoing medical support operations in conflict-affected areas. ([ReliefWeb – Ukraine Health Cluster, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/ukraine-health-cluster-partner-emergency-response-attacks-30-april-2026); [ReliefWeb – AMDA Ukraine, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/amda-emergency-relief-56-crisis-ukraine-13-may-2026))

Spain is managing a hantavirus outbreak, with the European Commission's DG ECHO Daily Map for 13 May documenting evacuations and civil protection support deployed through the EU Civil Protection Mechanism. The situation reflects the broadening scope of public health emergencies requiring humanitarian-grade coordination in Europe. ([ReliefWeb – Spain Hantavirus, 13 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/map/spain/hantavirus-evacuations-and-support-through-ucpm-dg-echo-daily-map-13052026))

Pakistan received a new green-level flood alert from GDACS on 13 May, following monitoring of hydrological conditions. While assessed as low-severity at this stage, Pakistan's vulnerability to seasonal flooding — particularly given its weakened post-2022 infrastructure and ongoing economic fragility — warrants continued monitoring heading into the monsoon season. ([GDACS – Pakistan Flood, 13 May 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=FL&eventid=1103877))
