# ⚡ Daily Humanitarian Update — Saturday, 25 April 2026
*Produced Saturday, 25 April 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia's Emergency Action Alliance — the 15-member coalition that channels Australian public donations into rapid international response — is currently running joint appeals on the Middle East, the Myanmar earthquake, the hunger crisis and Ukraine, with the Middle East appeal flagged as the most urgent in light of intensifying violence in Gaza, Lebanon, the West Bank and Syria. EAA notes that members are scaling up but require additional funding to meet escalating needs ([Emergency Action Alliance](https://emergencyaction.org.au/)).

Tropical Cyclone Sinlaku — which peaked at 287 km/h winds and made landfall in the Federated States of Micronesia — has triggered a Copernicus Emergency Management Service activation (EMSR872) and a Virtual OSOCC mission update dated 14 April but with continuing situation reports through 24 April. The system is the largest active tropical cyclone hazard in Australia's near region this run, and is being closely monitored by Pacific partners ([GDACS](https://gdacs.org/) / [Copernicus EMS](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR872/download)).

The Pacific cyclone, regional flooding in Indonesia and Vietnam, and a magnitude 5.8 earthquake offshore Crete (Greece) — all reported in the GDACS 24 April 2026 situation update — represent the principal Australian-region humanitarian watch points overnight, with Australian NGOs in the EAA network well-placed for any escalation ([GDACS news 24 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org/)).

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Northern Vietnam continues to feel the effects of severe weather and a major landslide. As of 24 April, the ASEAN Disaster Information Network reports one fatality in Quang Ninh province from lightning, 768 affected people, one destroyed and 191 damaged houses, and seven damaged schools across Lai Chau, Lao Cai, Quang Ninh and Thanh Hoa. A landslide on 21 April in Phu Ha (Phu Tho) injured one person and forced 11 families to evacuate, with further heavy rainfall forecast over the next 72 hours ([GDACS 24 Apr 2026 — Viet Nam](https://gdacs.org/)).

Heavy rainfall on 22 April triggered widespread flooding across Indonesia, with damage reported in North Sumatra (one injured, 600 affected, three houses destroyed, plus a destroyed bridge and school), North Sulawesi (16 displaced, 236 affected), East Java (5,420 people across 1,355 houses on the Welang River), and South Jakarta (2,513 people affected by Ciliwung River overflow). Additional moderate-to-heavy rain is forecast over the next 48 hours ([GDACS 24 Apr 2026 — Indonesia](https://gdacs.org/)).

A magnitude 5.6 earthquake struck Japan on 21 April, following a magnitude 7.4 event on 20 April; a magnitude 6.1 quake hit Tonga on 19 April; and a magnitude 5.8 quake near the Solomon Islands occurred on 18 April. While no mass-casualty reports have emerged, the cluster of seismic activity across the Pacific Ring of Fire is being tracked by GDACS and partners ([GDACS earthquakes feed](https://gdacs.org/)).

ICRC continues its multi-month operational update on the Myanmar earthquake response, marking one year since the disaster and detailing displacement and recovery work with the Myanmar Red Cross Society ([ICRC — Myanmar earthquake response: One year on](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/myanmar-earthquake-response-one-year-on)).

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

The UN Secretary-General has called for attacks in southern Lebanon to "stop", as Israel continued air strikes on southern Lebanon on 24 April despite an extended ceasefire, with the UN warning that the strikes may violate international law ([Al Jazeera 24 Apr 2026 — UN warning](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/24/israeli-attacks-on-lebanon-may-violate-international-law-un-warns)). ICRC is supporting life-saving medical services and maintaining water and essential lifelines as hostilities disrupt essential services in Lebanon ([ICRC — Lebanon water, 22 Apr 2026](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/lebanon-maintaining-water-and-essential-lifelines-hostilities-disrupt-essential-services)).

In Gaza, Al Jazeera reports Israel killed at least 12 Palestinians on 24 April amid the ceasefire, while features highlight a "silent suffering" of children losing speech and a public-health emergency over disease-carrying rats in displacement camps ([Al Jazeera 24 Apr 2026 — Gaza ceasefire](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/24/israel-kills-at-least-12-palestinians-in-gaza-amid-ceasefire); [Al Jazeera — children losing speech](https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2026/4/24/silent-suffering-why-children-in-gaza-are-losing-their-ability-to-speak)). ICRC has published a story on a Gazan child's recovery journey ([ICRC — Gaza: Aseel's journey, 23 Apr 2026](https://www.icrc.org/en/article/gaza-aseels-journey-back-hope)).

ICRC has delivered a first batch of emergency relief items to the Iranian Red Crescent Society as a regional response continues ([ICRC — Iran emergency relief, 14 Apr 2026, updated home feature](https://www.icrc.org/en/news-release/iran-icrc-delivers-emergency-relief-items-iranian-red-crescent-society)). Al Jazeera carries explainers on the Strait of Hormuz crisis and analysis of the looming "May 1 deadline" tied to the US War Powers Act ([Al Jazeera 24 Apr 2026 — Hormuz](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/24/iran-iraq-tanker-war-redux-why-the-strait-of-hormuz-crisis-is-different); [Al Jazeera 24 Apr 2026 — May 1 deadline](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/24/trumps-may-1-deadline-can-he-continue-war-on-iran-after-that)).

In Sudan, displaced families continue to arrive in Tawila, North Darfur, after fleeing hostilities and violence in Al-Fasher; ICRC has framed Sudan's war, now entering its fourth year, as carrying an unsustainable human cost ([ICRC — Sudan: human cost of three years of war](https://www.icrc.org/en/news-release/sudan-human-cost-three-years-war)). WFP's lead story for the day frames the Sudan war's compounding effect — "shattered dreams, soaring hunger, shrinking aid" ([WFP — Sudan three years on](https://www.wfp.org/stories/sudans-war-three-years-shattered-dreams-soaring-hunger-shrinking-aid)).

Severe thunderstorms and flooding battered Mombasa and Kwale counties in south-east Kenya on 21–22 April. In Mombasa, more than 100 households were displaced with extensive damage; in Kwale, one person remains missing and several villages were cut off, with smallholder farmers facing significant losses. Further moderate scattered rain is forecast across the south-east, north-east and south-west ([GDACS 24 Apr 2026 — Kenya](https://gdacs.org/)).

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

WFP, FAO, the European Union and partners released a joint global report on 24 April warning that acute food insecurity and malnutrition remain alarmingly high as crises deepen, citing protracted conflict, climate shocks and shrinking funding ([WFP news release, 24 Apr 2026](https://www.wfp.org/news/acute-food-insecurity-and-malnutrition-remain-alarmingly-high-crises-deepen-un-eu-and-partners)).

WFP also published a feature on how refugees and host communities in Chad are moving "from aid to autonomy" through resilience and food-security programming, a notable counterpoint to the headline crisis figures ([WFP — Chad, 23 Apr 2026](https://www.wfp.org/stories/aid-autonomy-how-refugees-and-host-communities-are-building-food-security-chad)).

In the Americas, wildfires continued across northern Florida (Levy, Putnam, Clay) and southern Georgia (Brantley, Clinch, Echols) since 21 April. FEMA reports four active uncontained fires totalling roughly 16,785 hectares burned, one injured person, evacuation orders for 223 people in Florida and 940 in Georgia, plus 83 destroyed and 70 damaged structures. JRC GWIS forecasts moderate-to-very-high fire danger over the next 72 hours ([GDACS 24 Apr 2026 — USA wildfires](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=WF&eventid=1028506)).

Colombian President Gustavo Petro has become the first foreign president to visit Venezuela following Maduro's reported abduction, a development with potential humanitarian-access implications across the Venezuela border crisis ([Al Jazeera 24 Apr 2026 — Petro to Venezuela](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/24/petro-becomes-first-president-to-visit-venezuela-since-maduro-abduction)).

A US appeals court rejected the Trump administration's ban on asylum seekers, while the administration also moved to end legal status for people who entered the US under the CBP One app — both decisions carry direct consequences for protection caseloads at the US southern border ([Al Jazeera 24 Apr 2026 — asylum ruling](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/24/us-appeals-court-rejects-trumps-ban-on-asylum-seekers-teeing-up-appeal); [Al Jazeera 24 Apr 2026 — CBP One](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/24/trump-to-again-end-legal-status-of-people-who-entered-us-with-cbp-one-app)).

## 🚨 Sudden Onset Hazards

A magnitude 5.8 earthquake struck offshore south-eastern Crete (Greece) on 24 April at 03:18 UTC, with no immediate reports of casualties or damage, per the ECHO Daily flash via GDACS ([GDACS — Greece M5.8, 24 Apr 2026](https://www.gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventtype=EQ&eventid=1537093)).

Tropical Cyclone Sinlaku (peak 287 km/h) remains the most consequential active sudden-onset hazard in Australia's near region, with Copernicus EMS rapid-mapping products published on 22–23 April for affected areas in Micronesia ([GDACS — SINLAKU-26](https://gdacs.org/); [Copernicus EMSR872](https://rapidmapping.emergency.copernicus.eu/EMSR872/download)).

Wildfires in the south-eastern United States (Florida and Georgia), severe weather and floods in Indonesia and Vietnam, and severe weather and flooding in Kenya are all rated as active and worsening over the next 48–72 hours per the JRC/ECHO daily flash via GDACS ([GDACS news, 24 Apr 2026](https://gdacs.org/)).
