## 🇦🇺 Australian Alerts

**ACFID — Middle East Consolidated Appeal (Updated 18 March 2026)**
ACFID's Emergency Action Alliance (EAA) has an active consolidated appeal for the Middle East humanitarian crisis, with more than one million people forcibly displaced across Gaza, Lebanon, Syria, and the West Bank and communities losing access to food, clean water, and medical care. Funds are being directed to Al Ahli Hospital in Gaza through the EAA's 15-member coalition. [ACFID Middle East Appeal](https://acfid.asn.au/consolidated-appeal-middle-east-humanitarian-crisis/)

**Australia silent on Israel's ban of 37 aid organisations in Gaza**
The Australian government has not publicly condemned Israel's decision to ban 37 international NGOs from operating in Gaza, despite calls from the humanitarian sector. DFAT declined to comment on the record; Foreign Minister Wong did not respond to media requests. NGOs cite safety concerns as the reason for non-compliance with Israel's new registration requirements. [Michael West Media](https://michaelwest.com.au/ngos-warn-of-catastrophic-impact-penny-wong-doesnt-care/)

**Australia launches Declaration to Protect Aid Workers**
After a year-long diplomatic effort, Australia formally launched the *Declaration for the Protection of Humanitarian Personnel* at the UN in New York, with over 100 co-signatory countries. The Albanese Government has committed $15 million to support implementation through OCHA, UNDSS, and the Red Cross/Red Crescent Movement, honouring Australian aid worker Zomi Frankcom among the 674 humanitarian personnel killed since January 2024. [Foreign Minister Media Release](https://www.foreignminister.gov.au/minister/penny-wong/media-release/australia-launches-global-declaration-protect-aid-workers)

**⚠️ Severe Tropical Cyclone Narelle — Queensland coastal communities on alert**
Emergency alerts have been issued for multiple Cape York communities including Aurukun, Pormpuraaw, and Napranum, warning that Severe Tropical Cyclone Narelle is expected to bring destructive winds from today (Friday 20 March). [QLD Police Emergency Alert](https://mypolice.qld.gov.au/news/2026/03/19/emergency-alert-for-aurukun-at-4pm-thursday-19-march-2026/)

## What's New Today

- **Indonesia** — Flooding across at least 35 districts and cities has affected approximately 23,000 people; separate landslides on Java have killed four and damaged hundreds of homes. [OCHA Asia-Pacific Snapshot, 3–16 March 2026](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-3-16-march-2026)

- **Myanmar** — Large-scale military operations in Nyaunglebin Township, eastern Bago Region, have reportedly killed more than 30 civilians and displaced thousands since early March; airstrikes continue across multiple regions. [OCHA Asia-Pacific Snapshot](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-3-16-march-2026)

- **Afghanistan–Pakistan** — Cross-border conflict is entering its third week with no sign of abating; airstrikes and attacks have affected at least 10 Afghan provinces including Kabul, with 115,000 people forced to flee and more than 300 shelters destroyed or damaged. [OCHA Asia-Pacific Snapshot](https://www.unocha.org/publications/report/myanmar/asia-and-pacific-regional-humanitarian-snapshot-3-16-march-2026)

- **Gaza** — All border crossings remain closed except Kerem Shalom; aid stocks are depleted and the UN can now bring in only half the fuel needed to sustain basic operations; medical evacuations remain on hold. [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167069)

- **Sudan** — Intensified SAF–RSF fighting in Kordofan and Blue Nile states continues to cause civilian casualties; a drone strike hit a medical facility in El Obeid, injuring 12 people including five health workers. [OCHA / UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/03/1167069)

## 🔴 Escalation Watch

**Gaza — imminent humanitarian collapse:** The closure of all crossings except Kerem Shalom, combined with depleted aid stocks and UN fuel supplies cut by half, signals an acute risk of full humanitarian system breakdown in Gaza over the coming days unless crossings reopen.
