## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia's peak international development peak body, ACFID, continues to coordinate consolidated member appeals for several ongoing crises. Active appeals this week cover the **Middle East Humanitarian Crisis** (launched March 2026, covering Lebanon and Gaza), **Sudan 2025**, **Afghanistan Earthquake 2025**, and the **Myanmar Earthquake**. The Middle East appeal — published 18 March 2026 and updated in April — focuses on the escalating hostilities in Lebanon, where ACFID members are supporting displaced families and those cut off from health services. More than 60–80% of households in affected Lebanese districts are now unable to afford health services, according to OCHA assessments. Australian NGOs operating under the ACFID umbrella have been scaling food, shelter, and cash-assistance programs in response.

The **Ebola Bundibugyo (BVD) outbreak** declared in the Democratic Republic of Congo on 15 May 2026, and elevated by WHO to a Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC) on 17 May, carries potential implications for Australian-funded programs in the region. Several ACFID member organisations operate in eastern DRC and neighbouring Uganda, where cross-border transmission has now been confirmed. The outbreak is unfolding in a complex security environment in Ituri and North Kivu provinces, where insecurity already hinders health surveillance and response. Australian development and humanitarian partners with programs in that region should review operational continuity plans.

The **Pacific Dengue Outbreak** (active since 2025) continues to affect multiple Pacific Island nations, an area of central focus for Australia's regional aid programming. The outbreak spans Tonga and multiple Pacific neighbours, with no sign of abating as of May 2026. Australian humanitarian health partners in the Pacific should maintain monitoring.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Philippines — Mayon Volcano Ongoing Emergency**

Mayon Volcano in Albay Province, southern Luzon, has been in a sustained eruptive phase since 2 May 2026. A pyroclastic density current on 2 May along the Mi-isi Gully triggered widespread ashfall and rockfalls affecting Guinobatan, Camalig, and Ligao City. By mid-May, the disaster had affected more than 30,000 families across 87 barangays (villages), with more than 14,000 individuals displaced into evacuation centres. The towns of Camalig, Guinobatan, and Ligao City were the hardest hit. As of the most recent updates, lava flows continued in the barangays of Basud, Bonga, and Misi. The ongoing nature of the eruption — now entering its fourth week — is placing sustained pressure on local emergency response systems and stretching shelter capacity in the province.

- ReliefWeb Disaster Profile: https://reliefweb.int/disaster/vo-2026-000065-phl

**South Asia — 2026 Monsoon Season Forecast**

The EU's DG ECHO issued its 2026 multi-model monsoon forecast for South Asia on 22 May, covering the June–September season. The forecast is relevant to anticipatory action planning across Bangladesh, India, Nepal, and Afghanistan, all of which experienced significant flood events in 2025. Humanitarian actors operating in flood-prone areas of South Asia have a narrow window to pre-position supplies and activate contingency frameworks ahead of the expected monsoon onset in June. Afghanistan, already facing cross-border conflict-related displacement, faces compounded risk if this year's floods materialise at scale.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**DRC/Uganda — Ebola Bundibugyo PHEIC (Major Alert)**

The most significant acute emergency of the week is the Ebola Bundibugyo virus disease (BVD) outbreak in the Democratic Republic of Congo and Uganda. On 15 May 2026, the Institut National de Recherche Biomédicale (INRB) in Kinshasa confirmed the outbreak in Ituri Province. WHO declared it a **Public Health Emergency of International Concern (PHEIC)** on 17 May — this is the 17th Ebola outbreak in DRC history, and marks the first time BVD has crossed DRC's borders. By 20 May, national authorities and international monitors reported 60 confirmed cases, more than 670 suspected cases, 105 probable cases, and 160 deaths linked to the Bundibugyo strain. Confirmed figures remain lower than actual infections due to severely limited testing capacity in affected areas.

The outbreak is centred in Ituri Province (particularly Bunia, Bambu, Fataki, Logo, Mangala, Mongwalu, Nizi, Nyankunde, and Rwampara) with additional cases in North Kivu and South Kivu. By 19 May, one person had died in Kampala, Uganda, and a second case was confirmed there. The Bundibugyo strain is particularly dangerous at this time because there are **no currently licensed vaccines or specific therapeutics** — unlike the better-known Zaire strain, for which effective vaccines (rVSV-ZEBOV) exist. Response is therefore entirely dependent on community engagement, isolation, supportive care, and contact tracing, all of which are severely complicated by active armed conflict and insecurity in Ituri and North Kivu.

OCHA, IOM, IFRC, WHO, ACAPS and partner organisations have all mobilised response frameworks. The IFRC launched an Emergency Appeal (No. MDRS1007) covering both DRC and Uganda. IOM's first multi-country situation report warned of significant undetected transmission risk driven by extensive cross-border mobility and trade patterns. ACAPS issued a Briefing Note on 22 May warning that Bunia's role as a major transport hub significantly amplifies transmission risk within DRC and across borders.

**Lebanon — Hostilities Escalate Despite Ceasefire Extension**

Lebanon's humanitarian crisis deepened this week despite successive ceasefire extension announcements. OCHA's Flash Update #28 (21 May) confirmed that hostilities continued with airstrikes and new displacement orders affecting at least 14 new localities in the week. Since the resumption of conflict on 2 March 2026, at least **3,089 people have been killed and 9,379 injured**. An airstrike on 19 May damaged the Tibnine Hospital in South Lebanon, adding to a pattern of attacks on health infrastructure.

Health assessments across 15 affected districts found that **60–80% of households cannot afford health services**. More than 618,000 people have received emergency multi-purpose cash assistance through Government-led response activities since March. Humanitarian partners have provided 10.3 million hot and cold meals for displaced families to date. The Lebanon Flash Appeal stands at only **54% funded**, and a revised extension of the appeal is planned for launch in the first week of June 2026. WFP's May Country Brief further documented the acute food security deterioration among displaced and host communities.

- WFP Lebanon Country Brief May 2026: https://reliefweb.int/report/lebanon/wfp-lebanon-country-brief-may-2026

**Occupied Palestinian Territory — Khan Al Ahmar Forcible Displacement Order**

The UN Office of the High Commissioner for Human Rights (OHCHR) issued a statement this week calling on Israel to immediately halt plans to forcibly displace the Khan Al Ahmar Bedouin community in the E1 area of the occupied West Bank. On 19 May, Israeli Finance Minister Bezalel Smotrich directed the Israeli Civil Administration to evict the community "as soon as possible." OHCHR characterised a forced transfer as a war crime. The E1 area is also the site of approved plans for 3,401 settlement units that would connect the settlement of Maaleh Adumim to occupied East Jerusalem and displace 18 Palestinian communities. The planned construction would further disrupt the territorial continuity of the occupied West Bank.

- OHCHR Statement: https://reliefweb.int/report/occupied-palestinian-territory/israel-must-halt-forcible-displacement-khan-al-ahmar-and-settlement-expansion-e1-enar

**Afghanistan — Cross-Border Conflict Driving Humanitarian Crisis**

OCHA's March–April 2026 humanitarian update for Afghanistan documented the continued impact of cross-border hostilities between Afghanistan and Pakistan. Between January and March 2026, cross-border armed violence resulted in **769 civilian casualties — 372 killed and 397 injured** — including 72 women, 554 men, 48 girls and 95 boys. Pakistan's announcement of Operation Ghazab lil-Haq on 26 February led to a surge in airstrikes that drove the majority of casualties. A ceasefire reached in October 2025 had temporarily reduced violence but has not held. Displacement, disruption to essential services, and access constraints for humanitarian actors continue to compound pre-existing vulnerabilities from drought, earthquakes, and the ongoing Taliban-era restrictions on women and girls.

- OCHA Afghanistan Humanitarian Update: https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-humanitarian-update-march-and-april-2026
- Afghanistan Humanitarian Access Snapshot April 2026: https://reliefweb.int/report/afghanistan/afghanistan-humanitarian-access-snapshot-april-2026

**Nigeria — Cholera Outbreak Expands in Borno State**

The cholera outbreak in Borno State, Nigeria continued to expand this week. As of 20 May 2026, a cumulative **2,081 cases and 23 deaths** had been recorded across five local government areas (LGAs), representing a case fatality rate of 1.1%. The Health Cluster's Situation Report No. 4 (published 21 May) noted the geographic expansion of the outbreak into a newly affected Monguno LGA, where access and WASH infrastructure constraints make response challenging. Borno State remains one of Nigeria's most conflict-affected areas, with a population already weakened by years of displacement and food insecurity.

- Nigeria Cholera SitRep No. 4 (21 May): https://reliefweb.int/report/nigeria/nigeria-health-sector-cholera-outbreak-situation-report-borno-state-no-04-21-may-2026

**Somalia — Drought Insurance Payout Activated**

Somalia's African Risk Capacity (ARC) drought insurance payout for 2025–2026 was activated and published on 24 May, confirming a trigger event linked to the drought insurance mechanism. This signals that drought conditions in Somalia have crossed agreed thresholds for pre-arranged, rapid financial disbursement to enable early response — a mechanism designed to shorten the lag between crisis onset and funding. The payout, coordinated through Start Network and the Government of Somalia, is intended to fund anticipatory action before the worst humanitarian impacts materialise.

- Somalia ARC Drought Insurance Payout Activation Bulletin: https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/somalia-arc-drought-insurance-payout-activation-bulletin-2025-2026

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**Iraq/Iran — Displacement Continues After Ceasefire**

IOM's Iraq mobility update (18 May) documented continued cross-border displacement impacts linked to regional instability stemming from escalatory military activity in and around Iran since late February 2026. A fragile ceasefire has been in place since 8 April, but Iraq continues to face cross-border population movements and internal displacement compounded by pre-existing ISIL-linked vulnerabilities. IOM is monitoring displacement and cross-border movements to support operational planning and preparedness.

- IOM Iraq Update: https://reliefweb.int/report/iraq/iraq-update-cross-border-mobility-and-internal-displacement-iraq-18-may-2026

**Iran — Afghan and Pakistani Migrant Protection Risks**

A Mixed Migration Centre infographic published via ReliefWeb in May 2026 documented protection risks faced by Afghan and Pakistani refugees transiting Iran en route to Türkiye. The findings are stark: **39% of Afghan respondents personally experienced physical violence in Iran**, compared to 11% of Pakistanis. The most common reasons for stopping in Iran were waiting for transport (31%), earning money for the next leg of the journey (29%), and locating smugglers (28%). The report highlights Tehran, Isfahan, and Urmia as locations of greatest unmet assistance need, and points to a widening protection gap for mixed migrants in one of the world's busiest transit corridors.

- Mixed Migration Through Iran: https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/mixed-migration-through-iran-may-2026

**Latin America & Caribbean — Mexico Displacement and Heat Deaths**

OCHA's weekly Latin America and Caribbean situation update (22 May) recorded at least **2,000 people newly displaced** across Guerrero, Michoacán, and Durango states in early May due to criminal violence — adding to an estimated 390,000 people already living in displacement in Mexico at end-2025. Separately, OCHA flagged that an estimated **13,000 heat-related deaths occur annually** across 17 countries in the region, a figure likely to rise with climate change. In the Caribbean, approximately 3,384 people across eight communities in southern Suriname were affected by flooding. Venezuela saw 739,000 people reached by humanitarian response between January and April 2026.

- OCHA Latin America & Caribbean Weekly Update: https://reliefweb.int/report/mexico/latin-america-caribbean-weekly-situation-update-22-may-2026

**Ukraine — Health Cluster Evacuation Response**

The Ukraine Health Cluster published its April 2026 partner response report for health evacuations on 22 May. Health system evacuation support remains a critical need as conflict continues, with partner organisations providing medical transport, trauma care, and facility-to-facility patient transfer services across conflict-affected oblasts.
