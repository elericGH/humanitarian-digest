# 🌍 Weekly Humanitarian Digest — 15 June 2026 – 22 June 2026
*Produced Monday, 22 June 2026 | Australian Interest Priority Edition*

---

## 🇦🇺 Australian Priority Alerts

Australia was among 29 co-signing states on a joint statement delivered at the UN Human Rights Council's 62nd Session, condemning ten consecutive days of RSF drone strikes on El Obeid, Sudan's North Kordofan capital. The statement warned that approximately 500,000 civilians face large-scale atrocity risk and documented ethnically targeted violence, including sexual and gender-based violence, with at least 50 civilians killed in the strikes. Australia's co-signature signals continued diplomatic engagement on the Sudan crisis, now approaching its fourth year, amid a deeply underfunded humanitarian response. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/un-human-rights-council-62-joint-statement-escalating-atrocity-risks-el-obeid-sudan))

Australia's humanitarian footprint in the Pacific also surfaced in technical publications this week. A Cash Delivery Mechanism Assessment produced by the Pacific Regional Cash Working Group — with an embedded Australia Assists Cash Specialist at WFP Pacific and supported by the DFAT-funded Disaster READY programme through the Australian Humanitarian Partnership (AHP) — evaluated financial service providers across Fiji, Solomon Islands, Vanuatu, Papua New Guinea, Tonga, and other Pacific Island countries. The assessment identifies critical gaps in cash-based humanitarian interventions across the region, relevant as Pacific disaster frequency and severity continues to rise. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/fiji/cash-delivery-mechanism-assessment-pacific-regional-cash-working-group-updated-june-2026))

No new public announcements were identified on acfid.asn.au or dfat.gov.au during this period; the most recent ACFID news available was from May 2026.

## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

**Philippines: M7.8 Sarangani Earthquake Response Ongoing**

The Philippines earthquake response continued to dominate Pacific humanitarian operations this week. The 8 June magnitude 7.8 event off Maasim, Sarangani, Mindanao had by 18 June affected over 1.5 million people, with 86,000+ still displaced, 68 confirmed deaths, and 1,300 injuries. Infrastructure damage exceeded US$16.5 million. More than 3,000 aftershocks had been recorded by the end of the reporting period. WFP published its first situation report on 18 June, detailing food pack distributions and mobile kitchen deployments alongside Philippine government agencies. Fifteen humanitarian organisations also conducted a consolidated rapid needs assessment covering shelter, food security, health, and protection across affected municipalities. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/philippines/wfp-philippines-response-78-magnitude-earthquake-sarangani-situation-report-1-18-june-2026))

**Indonesia: M6.7 Earthquake, Central Sulawesi**

A separate magnitude 6.7 earthquake struck Central Sulawesi on 16 June, causing 3 fatalities, 128 injuries, and affecting approximately 8,820 people. More than 2,660 homes were damaged or destroyed, along with 90 religious buildings, 31 schools, 6 health facilities, and 2 bridges, with 1,256 aftershocks recorded. Indonesia's disaster management agency BNPB declared a state of emergency. This followed IFRC documentation published the same week of the Indonesian Red Cross response to the late-2025 floods and landslides in three provinces, in which over 86,000 people received health services, electricity was cut for 47 days, and MSF deployed Starlink satellite units to maintain communications — a response that exceeded original targets tenfold. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/indonesia/asean-weekly-disaster-update-week-25-15-21-june-2026))

**Myanmar: Attacks on Healthcare and Economic Deterioration**

The situation in Myanmar continued to deteriorate across two dimensions this week. Insecurity Insight documented attacks on healthcare between 27 May and 9 June, bringing the cumulative total since the 2021 coup to at least 1,939 incidents — effectively one attack per day for more than five years. Of these, 65% are attributed to the Myanmar Armed Forces; 175 health workers have been killed and 931 arrested. A drone strike on a functioning hospital in Kachin State on 4 June killed three people. Separately, the World Bank's June 2026 Myanmar Economic Monitor found tentative economic stabilisation being undermined by a new fuel price shock, with GDP growth projected at just 2% for FY2026/27 amid ongoing conflict and humanitarian access restrictions. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/myanmar/attacks-health-care-myanmar-27-may-09-june-2026))

**Vanuatu: Dengue Outbreak, South Efate**

The Pacific Community (SPC) published Situation Report #4 on a dengue outbreak on South Efate Island, Shefa Province. As of epidemiological week 24 (ending 15 June), 17 cumulative confirmed cases had been recorded — exceeding the alert threshold — concentrated in the Pango Area Council. Response efforts are focused on surveillance, vector control, and community awareness. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/vanuatu/vanuatu-dengue-outbreak-situation-report-4-south-efate-shefa-province))

**Regional: ASEAN Week 25 Disaster Update**

The AHA Centre's weekly disaster report recorded 30 events across ASEAN during 15–21 June, spanning droughts, earthquakes, floods, landslides, and storms in Indonesia, Thailand, and Vietnam. Typhoon MEKKHALA was actively intensifying over the Philippine Sea, with a second tropical disturbance (INVEST 94W) tracking toward northern Luzon. El Niño conditions are forecast to strengthen through August–September 2026, raising the risk of compounding disasters across the region. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/indonesia/asean-weekly-disaster-update-week-25-15-21-june-2026))

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

**Gaza: Ceasefire Faltering, Civilian Toll Mounts**

Six months into the Gaza ceasefire, UN Emergency Relief Coordinator Tom Fletcher briefed the UN Security Council with a stark assessment: nearly 1,000 Palestinians killed since the ceasefire was declared; around 600 aid workers killed since October 2023; 70% of the population lacks proper shelter; no hospital is fully operational; and 1.1 million children face daily water uncertainty. Hunger has decreased — from 92% to 36% of households going to bed hungry — but Gaza remains in IPC Phase 4 (Emergency). Fletcher urged the Council: "Civilians cannot wait for diplomacy." ([ochaopt.org](https://www.ochaopt.org/content/un-relief-chief-tells-security-council-gaza-civilians-cannot-wait-diplomacy))

OCHA's fortnightly report covering 8–15 June documented Israeli forces advancing toward the "Yellow Line" in eastern Gaza City, triggering fresh displacement; critical fuel shortages hampering operations; and over 420,000 people receiving general food assistance in the first half of June. The 2026 OPT Flash Appeal remains less than 25% funded. A personal account published by The New Humanitarian from Gaza resident and freelance journalist Mohamed al-Astal described spending over $10,000 to construct a nylon tent shelter for his family of seven in al-Mawasi displacement camp, while cement and construction materials remain blocked under Israeli import restrictions. ([ochaopt.org](https://www.ochaopt.org/content/humanitarian-situation-report-19-june-2026)) ([thenewhumanitarian.org](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/opinion/first-person/2026/06/16/father-gaza-new-home-i-am-building-my-children-nylon-tent))

Adding a digital protection dimension, civil society groups including Access Now called on WFP this week to publish an independent investigation into a 14 May cyberattack that exposed sensitive data belonging to 600,000 households in Gaza — potentially the largest humanitarian beneficiary data breach on record. WFP delayed notifying affected Gazans for 17 days. Advocates warned the exposed data "fuels the targeting of civilians." ([thenewhumanitarian.org](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/news/2026/06/19/iran-us-ceasefire-ebola-cases-surge-aid-sector-crisis-cheat-sheet))

**Lebanon: Airstrikes Continue; War Crime Finding**

Despite a US-Iran memorandum of understanding signed at Versailles aimed at ending the US-Israel war against Iran (which began 28 February 2026), Israeli airstrikes killed 18 people in southern Lebanon overnight 18–19 June. Amnesty International separately released a report finding that Israel's mass evacuation orders in Lebanon constitute "unlawful transfer" under the Geneva Conventions, amounting to a war crime. A three-month impact report on the broader Middle East emergency (March–May 2026) documented more than 1 million people remaining displaced in Lebanon, over 389,000 Iranians having crossed into neighbouring countries, and more than 107,000 Lebanese refugees entering Syria. ([thenewhumanitarian.org](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/news/2026/06/19/iran-us-ceasefire-ebola-cases-surge-aid-sector-crisis-cheat-sheet)) ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/middle-east-emergency-three-month-impact-report-march-may-2026))

**US-Iran MoU: Fragile Hope**

The US and Iran signed a 14-point MoU at Versailles on 19 June, opening a 60-day ceasefire window for the regional war that has killed at least 7,300 people in Iran and Lebanon since late February. However, JD Vance's planned implementation talks in Switzerland were abruptly cancelled the same day, signalling significant fragility in the agreement. ([thenewhumanitarian.org](https://www.thenewhumanitarian.org/news/2026/06/19/iran-us-ceasefire-ebola-cases-surge-aid-sector-crisis-cheat-sheet))

**DRC: Ebola Outbreak Escalates**

The DRC's Bundibugyo Ebola virus outbreak has become the third largest ever recorded in the country, with 782 confirmed cases and 181 deaths within its first month. Multiple agencies published warnings this week that the true scale is likely being severely under-estimated. MSF warned of dangerous gaps in surveillance, diagnosis, contact tracing, and community engagement, noting that treatment centres in Ituri province are overwhelmed and that "no one knows the true scale or exactly where the disease is spreading." ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/drc-one-month-msf-warns-dangerous-gaps-persist-ebola-disease-response))

Save the Children documented that at least 52 children — including 16 toddlers and infants — have contracted Ebola, with 19 child deaths confirmed. Oxfam noted only 20% of residents in Mongbwalo (population ~140,000) have clean water access, and only one in five health facilities in Ituri province has sufficient clean water for effective infection prevention. UNHCR warned the outbreak threatens to spread through displacement corridors into neighbouring countries. WHO published a formal Disease Outbreak News bulletin confirming spread into Uganda. IOM reported crossing one million health screenings at border points and displacement sites across the DRC and neighbouring countries. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/unhcr-warns-ebola-outbreak-threatens-displaced-communities-drc-and-beyond)) ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/disease-outbreak-news-ebola-disease-caused-bundibugyo-virus-democratic-republic-congo-uganda-19-june-2026))

**Somalia, South Sudan, Nigeria**

In Somalia, MSF reported malnutrition admissions overwhelming its Baidoa hospital: nearly 1,400 patients admitted to the inpatient therapeutic feeding centre between March and May 2026, forcing expansion from 50 to 125 beds — still beyond capacity. In South Sudan, UNFPA documented widespread conflict-related sexual violence in Jonglei State following the March SSPDF offensive on Akobo that drove 100,000+ people into Ethiopia; MSF reported residents returning to an Akobo "stripped of services," with the humanitarian response still "falling far short." In Nigeria, Borno State is experiencing one of its most severe cholera outbreaks in years: confirmed 1 May, now spread to 16 LGAs with more than 12,000 suspected cases and at least 95 deaths. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/somalia/malnutrition-admissions-are-overwhelming-baidoa-hospital-somalia)) ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/south-sudan/south-sudan-residents-return-akobo-town-stripped-services)) ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/nigeria/medecins-du-monde-mobilizes-rapid-emergency-response-severe-cholera-outbreak-escalates-across-borno-state))

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

**World Refugee Day 2026: 117.8 Million Forcibly Displaced**

World Refugee Day on 20 June marked the 75th anniversary of the 1951 Refugee Convention, observed under the theme "Until Everyone Is Safe." UNHCR's end-of-2025 figures show 117.8 million people forcibly displaced globally — roughly one in every 70 people on the planet. The figure underscores an unbroken trend of year-on-year growth in displacement, driven by conflict, climate, and economic shocks. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/world-refugee-day-2026-75-years-protection-until-everyone-safe))

**Global Hunger: 318 Million in Acute Food Insecurity**

WFP and World Vision joint research released 16 June found 318 million people across 68 countries experiencing acute food insecurity, with 41.1 million in Emergency (IPC Phase 4+) conditions or worse. The research noted that funding cuts since 2025 have left over 64% of surveyed households relying on external humanitarian assistance. The FAO-WFP Hunger Hotspots report (17 June) independently warned that acute food insecurity is expected to worsen across 13 countries between June and November 2026, with Sudan, South Sudan, Yemen, and Palestine designated the most critical hotspots, and northeast Nigeria and Somalia newly elevated. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/hunger-worsens-new-research-shows-what-protects-children)) ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/sudan/hunger-hotspots-fao-wfp-early-warnings-acute-food-insecurity-june-november-2026-outlook))

**US Announces US$1 Billion for WFP and UNICEF**

Against the backdrop of global funding warnings, the United States announced more than US$1 billion in humanitarian assistance on 17–18 June — over US$800 million to WFP and US$218 million to UNICEF — through macro awards covering more than 40 countries. WFP's Acting Executive Director confirmed the funding will reach 38 million of the most vulnerable people across at least 37 countries. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/united-states-announces-more-1-billion-assistance-unicef-and-world-food-program-address-global-humanitarian-needs))

**FAO-WFP Joint Anticipatory Action Appeal: US$202 Million**

FAO and WFP launched their first-ever Joint Anticipatory Action Appeal, seeking US$202 million to protect nearly 9 million people across 22 high-risk countries ahead of anticipated El Niño climate shocks forecast to intensify food security crises globally from August onwards. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/bracing-el-nino-fao-and-wfp-launch-joint-appeal-protect-88-million-people-extreme-weather-events))

**OCHA's Tom Fletcher at ECOSOC: System "Overstretched, Underfunded and Under Attack"**

UN Emergency Relief Coordinator Tom Fletcher opened the 2026 ECOSOC Humanitarian Affairs Segment warning the humanitarian system is operating in environments "more complex, more constrained and more dangerous than ever before." His remarks at ECOSOC followed a similar briefing to the Security Council on Gaza, painting a consistent picture of a system at structural breaking point. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/mr-tom-fletcher-under-secretary-general-humanitarian-affairs-and-emergency-relief-coordinator-opening-remarks-2026-ecosoc-humanitarian-affairs-segment-17-june-2026))

**IOM on Climate Mobility; ICRC Delivers Supplies to Iran**

IOM Director General Amy Pope addressed the Climate Mobility Forum in Berlin, calling for stronger partnerships for Small Island Developing States and stressing that climate-driven displacement is "no longer a future challenge; it is a present reality." Separately, the ICRC delivered more than 18 metric tonnes of emergency humanitarian supplies — including medical items and CBRN personal protective equipment — to the Iranian Red Crescent Society in support of the ongoing Middle East emergency response. ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/world/iom-chief-urges-action-small-island-states-climate-mobility-forum)) ([reliefweb.int](https://reliefweb.int/report/iran-islamic-republic/iran-icrc-delivers-18-tonnes-humanitarian-supplies-support-emergency-response))

GDACS recorded flood events across Mexico (17 June), Pakistan (19 June), China (20 June), Ghana, and the United States (21 June). Active flood watches were also in place across Indonesia, Guatemala, Venezuela, Papua New Guinea, and Russia. ([gdacs.org](https://www.gdacs.org/default.aspx))

**Tropical Cyclone ARTHUR-26 (18 June)**
Tropical Cyclone ARTHUR-26 was active at 74 km/h wind speed as recorded by GDACS. ([gdacs.org](https://www.gdacs.org/default.aspx))

**Central Mid-Atlantic Ridge — M6.6 Earthquake (17 June)**
A magnitude 6.6 earthquake was recorded along the Central Mid-Atlantic Ridge; no humanitarian impact reported. ([gdacs.org](http://gdacs.org/report.aspx?eventid=1547100&episodeid=1712898&eventtype=EQ))
