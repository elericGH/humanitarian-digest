## 🌏 Tier 1 — Pacific & Asia-Pacific

Pacific nations reached a landmark milestone this week with the adoption of the world's first regional framework for planned climate-driven community relocation. According to [The Diplomat (5 June 2026)](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/pacific-nations-adopt-first-regional-climate-relocation-framework/), the framework creates new guidance for communities facing displacement from rising seas, coastal erosion, and intensifying cyclones — establishing legal and logistical pathways that Pacific governments have been developing for years.

The dengue outbreak across the Pacific continues to expand. A [Pacific Community/WHO situation report published via ReliefWeb on 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/fiji/dengue-pacific-multicountry-situation-04-june-2026) recorded 3,521 confirmed cases, 156 hospitalisations, and 3 deaths across 17 territories between 1 January and 4 June 2026 — including Fiji, Vanuatu, Tonga, Samoa, Tuvalu, Kiribati, French Polynesia, and others. The Pacific Syndromic Surveillance System logged 7,234 dengue-like illness cases over the same period, though the trend shows a lower trajectory compared to 2025.

In Indonesia, The Diplomat reported on 5 June that [Sumatra's seasonal rains have turned into a national catastrophe](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/how-government-inaction-turned-sumatras-rains-into-a-national-catastrophe/) due to systemic government failures — decades of deforestation enabled by weak oversight, opaque licensing, and regulations favouring extractive industries have destroyed watershed protection and made flooding far more severe and deadly than it would otherwise be. Indonesia's flagship food assistance program is also under scrutiny: the [Free Meal Program](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/indonesias-free-meal-program-to-target-efficiency-after-arrest-of-former-head/), which costs nearly $15 billion in 2026, is undergoing restructure following the arrest of its former head on corruption-related charges.

UNHCR published a [2026 mid-year funding update for the East Asia and Pacific region](https://reliefweb.int/report/australia/unhcr-east-asia-and-pacific-2026-funding-update-31-may-2026) (as of 31 May 2026) noting ongoing protection and resettlement needs across the region. Myanmar continues to feature in regional analysis, with [The Diplomat examining India's neighbourhood policy](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/how-myanmar-fits-into-indias-troubled-neighborhood-policy/) following Myanmar military chief Min Aung Hlaing's visit to Delhi — a visit that has angered pro-democracy movements while Delhi continues to seek strategic and commercial arrangements with the junta.

## 🌍 Tier 2 — Middle East & Africa

The Bundibugyo Ebola outbreak in eastern Democratic Republic of Congo is the dominant story in African humanitarian response this week, with multiple major agencies publishing situation reports on 5 June 2026. The outbreak is now the [third largest Ebola outbreak on record](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/ebola-outbreak-further-strains-drcs-underfunded-health-system-leaving-health-workers-limited-critical-supplies) (CARE via ReliefWeb), driven by the Bundibugyo strain for which no approved vaccine or treatment currently exists. CARE warns that healthcare workers face acute shortages of PPE, hygiene supplies, and diagnostic tools, and that 10 days were lost waiting for prepositioned supplies to be released — time during which the virus continued to spread. Women and girls face compounded risks in affected communities.

[IOM's Situation Report No. 3 (covering 26 May–2 June 2026)](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/iom-multi-country-bundibugyo-ebola-virus-disease-outbreak-situation-report-no-3-26-may-2-june-2026) confirms that cross-border transmission into Uganda remains an active concern. WHO classifies DRC and Uganda as Priority 1a; South Sudan, Burundi, and Rwanda as Priority 1b. IOM is supporting surveillance at points of entry and monitoring population movements along key transport corridors. Africa CDC and WHO simultaneously published a [six-month Continental Preparedness and Response Plan (June–November 2026)](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/bundibugyo-ebola-virus-continental-preparedness-and-response-plan-june-november-2026) identifying Angola, CAR, Ethiopia, Kenya, Republic of Congo, Tanzania, and Zambia as Priority 2 preparedness countries. [WFP also published its Ebola response situation report](https://reliefweb.int/report/democratic-republic-congo/wfp-ebola-response-external-situation-report-05-june-2026) for 5 June 2026.

Flood conditions in Syria remain active, with the EU's DG ECHO publishing a [daily map on 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/map/syrian-arab-republic/syria-floods-dg-echo-daily-map-05062026) tracking the situation. Burkina Faso continues to host a large displaced and refugee population: [UNHCR's statistical report as of 31 May 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/burkina-faso/unhcr-burkina-faso-rapport-statisitiques-des-refugies-et-demandeurs-dasile-au-31-mai-2026) details the ongoing protection caseload in a country severely affected by Sahel insecurity.

## 🌐 Tier 3 — Rest of the World

Ukraine's humanitarian situation deteriorated further in May 2026. [WFP's Ukraine Country Brief for June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/wfp-ukraine-country-brief-june-2026-reporting-period-may-2026-enuk) reports that a WFP-contracted warehouse was directly hit by a ballistic missile, and a WFP convoy was struck by an FPV drone, causing mission delays and cancellations across WFP's operational area. Despite these conditions, WFP signed an agreement with Ukraine's Ministry of Education and Science to provide school meals to 200,000 children in the 2026–27 academic year. [Logistics Cluster coordination meeting minutes from Kyiv on 4 June](https://reliefweb.int/report/ukraine/ukraine-coordination-meeting-minutes-kyiv-4-june-2026) noted increased long-range attacks on Kyiv, Kharkiv, and Dnipro, with Ukrainian security services launching new northern operations.

In the Latin America and Caribbean region, [OCHA's weekly situation update as of 5 June 2026](https://reliefweb.int/report/haiti/latin-america-caribbean-weekly-situation-update-5-june-2026) highlights a fourfold surge in measles cases — 20,521 confirmed cases across the region in 2026 compared to the same period in 2025 — alongside 1,800 people displaced in Haiti's Artibonite department due to gang violence on 29 May, and 12,600 people facing mobility restrictions from armed clashes in Colombia's Chocó region. Deteriorating dry conditions in Central America's Dry Corridor are increasing food insecurity pressure ahead of the primera planting season.

Diplomatically, [Kyrgyzstan has been elected to the UN Security Council](https://thediplomat.com/2026/06/kyrgyzstan-elected-to-un-security-council-for-first-time-in-countrys-history/) as a non-permanent member for the first time in the country's history — only the second Central Asian state to achieve UNSC membership, with support from neighbouring states, Türkiye, and Muslim-majority countries. The election carries implications for how Central Asian and Pacific humanitarian issues may be represented in Security Council deliberations.
